Summary,  W E D NE S D A Y, September 18, 

     Boulogne-sur-Mer - Interest in exhibition of Slovak
glass-artists in France, opened in June 1996, was enormous. By
September, more than 14,000 visitors have come to see the
exhibition. This is a record attendance, when taking all summer
exhibitions into consideration, said Francoise Poiret, curator
of the Musee Chateau Boulogne-sur-Mer.

     Bratislava - The Slovak parliament continues its 17th
session on Wednesday with a discussion on the governmental bill
on prosecution.

      Bratislava - The fifth congress of the European
Association of Gynecological Endoscopy began in Bratislava, on
Wednesday. It its for the first time, when an event of a similar
significance in this field of medicine, will take place in
Slovakia, or in former Czecho-Slovakia, at all. 
    
    Novy Cas: New Kocnars 
      Bratislava - According to the latest events, the
disintegration of Association of Slovak Workers (ZRS) progressed
to the next chapter. After the establishment of the United
Worker's Party of Slovakia (ZRSS) by Mazar (adviser to ZRS
Chairman Jan Luptak) and Kocnar (Luptak's former colleague),
this disintegration moved to the parliament as well. On Tuesday
September 17th two ZRS deputies, Kolnik and Poliak, announced
their departure at the ZRS Club session.

      Bratislava - The members of the Coordination Committee for
Women's Issues returned from the international conference
entitled "Implementation of Action Platform in Central and
Eastern European Countries." The conference took place between
September 12-14 in the Romanian capital Bucharest.

      Bratislava - Parliamentary Chairman Ivan Gasparovic
received Arpad Bogsch, Director General of the World
Intellectual Property Organization (WIPO), in Bratislava on
Wednesday. During the meeting Gasparovic informed his guest
about the activities of the Slovak parliament and the process of
transformation in Slovak society and economy. 

    Pravda: Trade Unions' Flag in the Wind
      Bratislava - Leaders of KOVO, the largest Slovak trade
union, make no bones about their plans to withhold support for
the candidature of the Confederation of Trade Unions's (KOZ)
leadership for continued tenure at the KOZ congress in October.
The cause is sustained discontent over the current state of the
central body of trade unions. Eugen Skultety, Vice-Chairman of
KOVO, nominated for the post of the KOZ Vice-President,
clarified the main reasons (for their displeasure). "In our eyes
the Confederation is acting like a flag in the wind. For more
than two years we've been pointing to the shortcomings in its
performance. However, we are well-aware that that is not
enough," The reality, acknowledged by KOVO members, is simply
that the country's trade unions of today are not a real force,
one that could express as well as carry out their views.

      Bratislava - Ivan Simko, Christian-Democratic Movement
deputy, announced during the 17th session of the Slovak
Parliament on Wednesday, that a group of 36 deputies have
submitted a new proposal to the parliament to discuss the
no-confidence vote in Ivan Hudec, the Minister of Culture, next
parliamentary session summoned immediately after the on-going
session.

      Medzev, September 18, (SLOVAKIA) - An international
seminar devoted to the research, preservation, and use of caves
that have been made accessible (for public viewing), began in
Medzev, the district of Kosice-Surroundings on Wednesday.

      Bratislava - Slovak Premier Vladimir Meciar received Betty
Boothroyd, Speaker of the British House of Commons, in
Bratislava, on Wednesday.

      Bratislava - Slovak Culture Minister Ivan Hudec presented
three distinguished personalities, Drahoslav Machala, Dusan
Caplovic and Jan Brtek, with the Honor of Culture Minister award
on Wedneday.

      Brno - Slovakia has its National Day on Wednesday at the
38th International Engineering Fair in Brno. It was opened by
Slovak Economy Minister Karol Cesnek. In his speech Cesnek said
that the engineering industry has an important position in the
Slovak economy. In 1995, its portion increased to 17.7 per cent
and continues to increase in 1996. Cesnek stressed that Slovak
exhibitors traditionally visit the Brno fair. In 1996, along
with Germany, Slovakia is the largest foreign exhibitor with 118
participants and 3,900 square meters of exhibition space.

       Bratislava- Slovak Premier Vladimir Meciar received Polish
Foreign Affairs Ministry Dariusz Rosati on Wednesday in
Bratislava. After the meeting, Rosati said that it is in the
interest of Poland and Slovakia, as well as other central
European countries, to become members of European Union (EU) and
NATO. He said that in addition to illustrating mutual support,
it is necessary to fulfill all the conditions required by these
Euro-Atlantic structures. 

       Bratislava - Slovak Premier Vladimir Meciar received
Turkish Ambassador to Slovakia Aydin Sahinbas at the conclusion
of his diplomatic mission.

       Bratislava - Slovak Minister of Transportation, Postal
Services, and Telecommunications Alexander Rezes's pacemaker
will soon be changed, Cardiovascular Surgery Clinic (UKVCH)
Director Viliam Fischer told Slovakia. He, at the same time,
refuted the Novy Cas daily information indicating that Rezes's
cardiovascular disorder calls for complicated heart surgery.

       Budapest - An official meeting of Slovak and Hungarian
premiers, Vladimir Meciar and Gyula Horn and parliamentary
chairs Ivan Gasparovic and Zoltan Gal will be held this year in
Slovakia.

       Banska Bystrica - General Director of World Intellectual
Property Organization (WIPO) Arpad Bogsch, on Wednesday, took
over a honorary doctorate at the Matej Bel University' (UBM) Law
School. Constitutional Court Chair Milan Cic and other state
administration representatives attended.

       Bratislava - The 4th international conference of
architects called DOCOMOMO International was kicked off in
Bratislava on Wednesday. The participants are scheduled to visit
objects built in functionalistic style and located at various
places in Slovakia - - in Sliac, Svit, Vysne Hagy, Piestany and
Bratislava. The event is attended by 180 participants from more
than 30 countries.

       Bratislava - The British House of Commons Speaker Betty
Boothroyd concluded her four-day visit to Slovakia on Wednesday.

       Bratislava - A press conference at the Slovak National
Gallery (SNG) provided information on Slovak architects'
participation in the 4th International Architecture Exhibition
in Venezia, Italy, being held from September 15 to November 16.

       Kosice - Juh Kosice District Mayor Jaroslav Hlinka served
more than 500 civil marriages but he will have to repeat the
Wednesday's wedding ceremony during which Maria Horvathova and
Marian Zlamal from Kosice said their Yes. As he told SLOVAKIA
correspondent the repetition will be held over the Atlantic on
September 19 on a plane via Vienna-New York. By this act a
life-dream nourished by Zlamal family will be fulfilled thanks
to the Slovak TV show Let's Dream With Us.

       Banska Bystrica - Branislav Petroch, the Director of the
Agrobanka's Banska Bystrica- headquartered branch office, told
SLOVAKIA that the Czech National Bank (CNB) will provide a full
guarantee for Agrobanka (AGB) clients in the Czech Republic and
Slovakia.
      The move is in response to the CNB's decision on temporary
receivership for the Prague-headquartered AGB, currently the
fifth largest financial house in the Czech Republic, prompted by
an investigation of the collapse of the Credit Bank a.s.,
headquartered in Plzen, the Czech Republic.

       Bratislava - With the participation of Ivan Hudec Culture
Minister, the premiere of the 30 minutes documentary on Jozef
Ciger-Hronsky, a Slovak writer, took place at the Koliba a.s.
Bratislava studio. The documentary's director is Ivan
Petrovicky.
       Bratislava - The Dutch insurance company
Nationale-Nederlanden a.s., daughter company of ING Group,
entered the Slovak insurance market on Wednesday.
      At a press conference, the general director of the Slovak
branch Ed Teer told journalists that 25 experts and 150 agents
will work at the branch covering all of Slovakia -- the only
central and east European country without a
Nationale-Nederlanden branch.

      Bratislava - Several computer servers in Slovakia connected
to the Internet network were under attack this week by a hacker
group called Syrup Emergency Response Team (SERT). The hackers
erased all illegal software from the servers and installed 
documents promoting fight against software piracy.	

      Bratislava - Ladislav Slobodnik, Head of the Slovak
Environment Ministry Office, received a World Bank Mission led
by its senior expert for the environment and economy Helmut
Schreiber.
     The mission is a preparation of the Slovak delegation's
participation in the World Bank General Assembly and World
Environment Fund. Slovak Finance Minister Sergej Kozlik will
lead the Slovak mission.

      Budapest - Slovak Foreign Minister Pavol Hamzik said his
negotiations in Hungary, the first since his installment as
minister, were very open and valuable. He paid a one-day visit
to Budapest on Wednesday.
     Hamzik and his Hungarian counterpart Laszlo Kovacs
discussed aspects of European and trans-Atlantic integration.
Kovacs stressed mutual support for integration efforts, Hamzik
said. "The basic thing is that both sides are interested in
settling controversial issues. We don't like to be pressured,
but we know very well that the European Union welcomes any
mutual progress," Hamzik said, adding that regarding this staff
there is much room for improvement.

    Bratislava 18. septembra (TASR) -  O slovenskej ucasti na
VI. medzinarodnej vystave architektury v Benatkach, ktora sa
kona od 15. septembra do 16. novembra, informovali dnes
novinarov na tlacovej besede v Slovenskej narodnej galerii v
Bratislave.
    Ako povedala narodna komisarka vystavy Henrieta Moravcikova,
prezentuje sa na nej 32 narodnych expozicii najroznejsimi
architektonickymi projektmi vratane nestandardnych, radikalnych
aj varujucich. Ceny - Benatskych zlatych levov ziskal americky
architekt Philip Johnson, Ignazio Gardella z Talianska a Oscar
Niemayer z Brazilie. Dalsich troch Zlatych levov udelili
autorom, ktori najlepsie interpretovali temu - architekt ako
seizmograf. Ziskali ich autori z Francuzska, Spanielska a
skupina mladych finskych architektov. Ako najlepsiu narodnu
reprezentaciu ocenili japonsky pavilon.
    Slovensku republiku prezentuje na vystave velky model
kostolika vo Vojkovciach, okres Spisska Nova Ves, v mierke 1:10.
Velkoplosne fotografie a texty priblizuju navstevnikom pribeh
jehho vzniku. Obyvatelia Vojkoviec sa usilovali o jeho vystavbu
od sestdesiatych rokov a vybudovali ho z vlastnych zdrojov v
spolupraci s architektom a vytvarnikmi po revolucnych zmenach v
novembri 1989. Stavba netradicneho kostolika v tvare rotundy s
malymi okruhlymi oknami posobi zaujimavym dojmom. Po skonceni
vystavy v Benatkach maju expoziciu instalovat v Bratislave.
    Riaditel SNG Juraj Zary dodal, ze slovenska expozicia je
vhodne realizovana v priestoroch spolocneho Cesko-Slovenskeho
pavilonu v Benatkach. Je dostatocne velky na to, aby sa v nom
mohli prezentovat obe krajiny. Doteraz vsak nie je doriesene
rozdelenie tohto spolocneho majetku. Preto bude SNG apelovat na
Ministerstvo kultury SR, aby vyvinulo snahu vyriesit tento
problem. Bola by totiz velka skoda prist o takuto vyznamnu
moznost prezentacie slovenskej kultury v zahranici.

telekomunikacii SR Alexandrovi Rezesovi v najblizsich dnoch

prednosta Kliniky kardiovaskularnej chirurgie UKVCH na

kde je pacient od vcera hospitalizovany. Zaroven vyvratil dnesnu

z desattisic pacientov s kardiostimulatorom.

kardiostimulatora sa sice narusila, ale na druhej strane doslo k
zlepseniu funkcie a vykonnosti lavej srdcovej komory ministra.
    Doc. V. Fischer konstatoval, ze vymena kardiostimulatora nie
je narocny vykon na srdci. Ide iba o 10 az 15-minutovu podkoznu
operaciu, po ktorej moze pacient na druhy den opustit operaciu.

 Zo stretnutia u predsedu NR SR 


    Zo stretnutia u predsedu NR SR
    
    Bratislava 18.septembra (TASR) - Predseda Narodnej rady SR
Ivan Gasparovic prijal dnes v Bratislave ministra zahranicnych
veci Polskej republiky Dariusza Rosatiho. Na brifingu po
skonceni rozhovorov obaja politici zdoraznili, ze hovorili spolu
predovsetkym o otazkach europskej integracie a moznostiach
spoluprace oboch krajin pri zaclenovani sa do bezpecnostnych a
hospodarskych struktur kontinentu.
    Sef polskej diplomacie za vyznamu povazuje diskusiu ako
mozno zvysit sance Polska i Slovenska na urychleny vstup do
Europskej unie a NATO. Obaja sa zhodli na tom, ze Ruska
federacia - odhliadnuc od nasich integracnych snah - zostane pre
obidve krajiny dolezitym partnerom. D.Rosati je presvedceny, ze
Rusko sa musi podielat na konstrukcii bezpecnosti Europy.
    I.Gasparovic zdoraznil, ze diskutovali o problemoch, ktore
region stednej Europy potrebuje riesit. Ide o mnohostrannu
spolupracu, ale aj bilateralne kontakty. Integraciu oboch krajin
do europskych a transatlantickych struktur oznacil za predpoklad
politickej stability a hospodarskeho rozvoja. Za dolezite pri
realizacii tychto snah povazuje stretnutia exekutivy i
predstavitelov parlamentov. "Kazdy musi sam riesit problemy,
ktore vyplyvaju z Bielej knihy EU. Pokial sme na rovnakej
startovacej ciare a urovni v plneni uloh a povinnosti, potom je
vzajomna podpora opodstatnena a musi byt jednoznacna. O tom sme
sa vzajomne ubezpecili", dodal predseda parlamentu.

 O slovensko-lotysskych privatizacnych vztahoch 


    O slovensko-lotysskych privatizacnych vztahoch
    
    Bratislava 18. septembra (TASR) - Rozpad sovietskeho imperia
vyrazne poznacil ekonomicke vazby Lotysska s krajinami byvaleho
Sovietskeho zvazu. Aj to je dovod, preco Lotyssko hlada v
suvislosti s ekonomickou transformaciou kontakty so zahranicnymi
partnermi. Na dnesnom slovensko-lotysskom rokovani o
privatizacii v zahranici to povedal T. Linkaits. Zdorazil, ze
privatizaciu v Lotyssku vyrazne ovplyvnuju aj zmeny v podobe
novelizacii znacneho poctu zakonov. Tie vsak zahranicnych
investorov nediskriminuju.
    T. Linkaits potvrdil, ze privatizacia v oblasti drevarskej
vyroby sa v Lotyssku skoncila a Lotysska privatizacna agentura
ma za ulohu zabezpecit do konca roka privatizaciu 75 percent z
celkoveho poctu statnych podnikov.
    V krajine pouzivaju aj privatizacnu metodu prostrednictvom
certifikatov - ta je obdobou kuponovej privatizacie a hodnota
ich bodu suvisi s vekom majitela tohto dokladu.
    Sucasna vlada povazuje za nevyhnutne riesit vyuzitie odpadu
dreva pre vyrobu elektrickej energie, vybudovanie exportneho
pristavu pre vyrobky z celulozy a gulatiny, vystavbu a
rekonstrukciu ciest a vyuzitie geotermalnej energie. Prvy statny
investicny programu bol uz vytvoreny pre obdobie 1995 - 1997.
Kazdy rok sa reviduje s vyhladom na trojrocne obdobie. Len
projekty zaradene do tohto programu sa mozu financovat zo
statneho rozpoctu, alebo z uverov garantovanych vladou. Podla
odhadu Svetovej banky sa zdroje pouzitelne na statne investicie
pohybuju pre obdobie 1995-1977 okolo troch percent hrubeho
domaceho produktu, t.j. okolo 200 milionov lotysskych latosov.
    Medzi SR a Lotysskom neboli zatial uzatvorene dohody o
zamedzeni dvojakeho zdanenia a zabraneni danovemu uniku v odbore
dani z prijmov a majetku a dohoda o podpore a vzajomnej ochrane
investicii. Uzatvorenie tychto zmluv medzi SR a Lotysskom sa
planuje na rok 1997.
