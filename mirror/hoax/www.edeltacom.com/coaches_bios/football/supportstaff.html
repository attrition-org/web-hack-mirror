Support Staff
 
Kevin Yoxall
Head Strength and Conditioning Coach

Joined AU Staff:
January, 1999

Responsibility:
Supervises all strength and conditioning programs for men's and women's athletics.

Experience:
Came to Auburn from UCLA where he was head strength and conditioning coach from 1995-98. Was head strength and conditioning coach for football at the University of Minnesota from 1992-95. Began his coaching career at Texas Christian University (1987-92). Served as assistant strength and conditioning coach from 1987-89, and was promoted to head strength and conditioning coach in 1989. Was a collegiate regional record-holder for power lifting in 1982, and was named a Collegiate All-American power lifter in 1983. The Pac-10 strength and conditioning coach of the year in 1998.

Personal Information:
Born on Aug. 3, 1959. He and his wife Nancy have two children, Collin, 8 and Marlee, 3. Earned his bachelor's of science degree in Special Education from East Texas State University in 1983 and his master's of science in Physical Education from Texas Christian University in 1989.

Tommy Stucky
Assistant Strength and Conditioning Coach

Joined AU Staff:
April 2000

Responsibility:
Assistant Strength and Conditioning Coach for football, baseball and other sports.

Experience:
Served as the Head Speed, Strength and Conditioning Coach at Oklahoma State three years. Helped implement strength and conditioning programs for football, softball and baseball at OSU. Graduate assistant at University of Tennessee for two and a half years.

Personal Information:
Born Nov. 3, 1972 in Salina, Kan. Married to former Angie Redell with one daughter, Ashton Paige, 1. Played defensive end for Hutchinson Junior College. Graduated with Associate's Degree in 1993. Worked as student assistant while attending Kansas State University. Graduated with B.S. in Kinesiology in 1995 from Kansas State. Graduated with M.S. in Sports Administration from the University of Tennessee in 1997.

Andrew Lutz
Assistant to the Head Coach

Joined AU Staff:
Dec. 1, 1998

Responsibility:
Serves as an administrative assistant to Head Coach Tommy Tuberville. Organizes the Spring coaching clinic, football camps and fantasy camp.

Experience:
Worked as the assistant to the athletics director for recruiting at the University of Mississippi, where he coordinated the recruiting process for all sports from 1996-98. Was promoted to this position from the position of graduate assistant coach in charge of linebackers and on-campus recruiting (1995-96). Before going to Ole Miss, was a graduate assistant in charge of the secondary and on-campus recruiting at the University of Nevada-Reno. Also was a graduate assistant at the University of the Pacific, his alma mater, where he worked with the linebackers and secondary. Was involved in recruiting, as well as being video coordinator and coordinator of the UOP Tiger Passing Camp. Worked at the Frank Glazier Football Clinics from 1990-98 as the clinic director.

Personal Information:
Born Oct. 24, 1968 in Lodi, Calif. Earned his bachelor's degree in Public Relations from the University of the Pacific in 1991. Received his master's degree in Educational Counseling and Psychology in 1994. A member of the American Football Coaches Association.

Eldon Hawley
Director of Football Operations

Joined AU Staff:
Dec. 2, 1998

Responsibility:
Directs the day to day operations of Auburn football.

Experience:
Spent 32 years as an assistant at the high school, junior college and college levels. Coaching career began as an assistant at Wynne High School in Arkansas in 1960. Came to Auburn after spending four years as director of football operations at Ole Miss. Before going to Ole Miss, spent two years as recruiting coordinator at Oklahoma State.

Personal Information:
Born on Sept. 27, 1938 in Forrest Park, Ark. Earned his bachelor's degree from the University of Central Arkansas in 1960 and master's degree from Texas Tech in 1963. Married to the former Rebecca O'Donell.

Rev. Chette Williams
Team Chaplain

Joined AU Staff:
Fall 1999

Responsibility:
Serves as the Auburn team chaplain. Also is the Auburn campus director for Fellowship of Christian Athletes and the State Director for Urban Ministries for FCA.

Experience:
Former president of IMPACT Ministries, Spartanburg, S.C. (1995-99). Served as pastor of New Covenant Baptist Church in New Orleans, La. (1993-95). Co-pastor of New Song Baptist Church in Mobile, Ala from 1991-93. Licensed (1987) and Ordained minister (1988).

Personal Information:
Born on Sept. 3, 1963. Married to former Lakeba Hibbler. Two daughters, Lauren Denise, 4, and Caitlyn Mae, 3, and one son, Chette LaRue Jr., born March 2000. Received bachelor of science from Auburn in Adult Education in 1985. Received master of divinity from New Orleans Baptist Theological Seminary in 1993. Three-year letterman on Auburn football team from 1982-84. During his career Auburn won two Sugar Bowls, a Citrus Bowl and a Liberty Bowl.
