Adam Schmidt

Assistant Coach
 

Adam Schmitt enters his second season with the Tigers. Schmitt’s vast experience with international swimming has had an immediate impact on the Auburn sprint program.

Included in his general coaching duties for both the men’s and women’s programs, Schmitt works closely with the strength and conditioning program for both teams and serves as the men’s recruiting coordinator.

Prior to Auburn, the Houston native coached in Long Beach, Calif. for four years. His coaching experience includes head coach at Long Beach City College, assistant coach at Shore Aquatics in Long Beach, and head coach of Calvary Chapel High School Swim Team.

Schmitt graduated from LSU in ‘88 where he was a nine-time All-American and a three-time SEC 50 freestyle champion. He was a member of the LSU SEC Championships Team in 1988 and was a two-time runner-up in the 50 free at NCAAs.

Schmitt competed for the United States in events such as the ‘86 Goodwill Games, the ‘87 World University Games and the ‘91 Pan-American Games. He was world ranked from 1986-1992 in the 50 freestyle. In 1988 he was an Olympic Trial finalist in the 50 free.

The 32-year-old Schmitt is married to former Stanford swimming All-American, Aimee Berzins. She is currently involved in motivational talks and published her own logbook. The couple has a one-year old daughter named Hannah.
