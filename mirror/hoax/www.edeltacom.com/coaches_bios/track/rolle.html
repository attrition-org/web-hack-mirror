Henry Rolle

Assistant Coach
 

Henry Rolle begins his first year as an assistant coach for the Auburn track and field program after serving as a graduate assistant for Coach Ralph Spry and the Tigers during the 1997-98 season. Rolle's dedication and hard work as a graduate assistant for the Tiger's solidified Spry's decision to make Rolle a full-time assistant coach this season. Rolle's coaching duties include over-seeing the horizontal jumps, 400-meter hurdles and multi-events. He will also take on the day-to-day responsibility of recruiting coordinator.

A graduate of Arkansas State University, Rolle previously served as the head track coach at St. John’s College in the Bahamas from 1995-97. While at St. John’s, Rolle led the program to a national championship in 1997. In just his first year at St. John’s his squad improved 13 places at nationals, jumping from 16th to third.

Rolle was also a successful club coach while in the Bahamas, as he coached five Bahamian national teams, including the 1997 Junior Pam American team and the 1998 Caribbean and Central American team in Maracaibo, Venezuela. Among the athletes Rolle coached includes current Tigers’ Osborne Moxey and Avard Moncur.

Following his stint at St. John's, Rolle returned to the States to serve as an assitant coach at Oral Roberts University in Tulsa, Oklahoma. While at Oral Roberts, Rolle was responsible for coaching the hurdles.

A native of Freeport, Bahamas, Rolle was a five-time Bahamian National team competitor, earning two silver medals in the high jump and 4x400-meter relay at the Caribbean and Central America Games in 1988.

The 26 year-old Rolle was a two-year member of the track and field team at Arkansas State, competing in the 200-meter and 400-meters. Rolle received his bachelor’s degree from Arkansas State in Political Science and Health and Human Performance and is currently, he is pursuing his master’s degree in Exercise Science at Auburn.
