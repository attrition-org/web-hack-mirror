Auburn Golfer Kevin Haefner Qualifies For U.S. Amateur: Will Swift 1st Alternate

August 3, 2000

AUBURN- Auburn All-American golfer Kevin Haefner shot a 73-66–139 in the Birmingham qualifier for the 2000 United States Amateur August 21-27 at Baltusrol Golf Club in Springfield,N.J. Senior Will Swift, who shot a two-day total of 140, won a playoff for the first alternate position.

"I am very happy for Kevin," Auburn head golf coach Mike Griffin said. "He had a great season last year for us, and he has continued his stellar play this summer. It will be a great experience for him to play at Baltusrol, a former U.S. Open site.

"Will has had a very impressive summer and just missed qualifying. With a little luck, he could find himself playing in the US Amateur since he is the first alternate."

Haefner, a junior from Pittsford, N.Y., had a 73.81 stroke average last season and finished 15th in the NCAA Championship. Swift, a senior from Selma, Ala., was redshirted last year. Swift has had an outstanding summer with a 70.6 stroke average for 18 rounds.
