Auburn Volleyball Defeats Florida A&M 3-1 In Home Opener

September 12, 2000

AUBURN- The Auburn volleyball team fought off a stingy Florida A&M team to capture its fifth victory of the season, defeating the Rattlerettes 3-1 in the home opener Tuesday night at the Auburn Student Activities Center.

Auburn improves to 5-2 on the season while Florida A&M falls to 1-1.

Leading 2-1 in the match, the Tigers jumped out to a 9-5 lead in game four. The Tigers extended their lead to 14-8 before Brooke McCarley gave the Tigers the match win with her 23rd kill of the night.

"Florida A&M is an extremely athletic team," Auburn head coach Kevin Renshler said. "They are a quality opponent. We had a mental breakdown in the third game, but continued to play hard and got the win."

In the third game, Florida A&M fell behind 6-3 before rallying for an 9-6 advantage in the game. Auburn pulled within 9-8 before the Rattlerettes stopped the rally and increased their lead to 10-8. Florida A&M then reeled off two more points to push their lead to 12-8 before the Tigers regained serve and narrowed the deficit to 12-9.

Auburn continued the rally, pulling within 12-11 before Florida A&M regained serve and held on for the 15-11 victory.

In game two, the Tigers jumped out to a 12-5 lead and cruised to a 15-5 win. McCarley again led Auburn with seven kills, while Melanie Wille recorded four kills. Traci Price led Auburn with 13 assists in the game.

The Tigers opened the match with a tough 15-13 win in the first game of the match. Brooke

McCarley led Auburn with seven kills, while Jaunelle Hamilton and Crystal Bluth recorded five kills apiece. Price recorded 17 assists to lead the Tigers in the game.

The Tigers will host the Cheeburger, Cheeburger Invitational Friday and Saturday. Clemson, Navy and Appalachian State will participate in the two day tournament.

Auburn will open the match against Navy at 1 p.m. Friday and then take on Clemson at 7 p.m.

The Tigers will finish the final day of the tournament with a match against Appalachian State at 1 p.m. Saturday.
