Auburn Opens Season With Wyoming

August 31, 2000

AUBURN- The Auburn Tigers open the 2000 football season Thursday against the Wyoming Cowboys. Airtime on the Auburn Network is 5:00 pm.

85,612

Jordan-Hare Stadium’s new capacity this year is 85,612 after seating 85,214 since renovations to the stadium in 1987.

The increase comes during off-season construction of new home and visitors’ locker rooms and a tunnel in the middle of the south end zone. While seats were removed with the addition of the tunnel in the south end zone, seats were added near the old tunnel in the southwest corner and an additional row of seat were added to the top of the north end zone.

With a capacity of 85,612, Jordan-Hare Stadium now becomes the sixth largest on-campus facility, surpassing Stanford’s 85,500-seat Stanford Stadium in Palo Alto, Calif.

New Locker Rooms

During the off-season, new home and visitors’ locker rooms were constructed in Jordan-Hare Stadium.

At a cost of $1.5 million, Auburn’s locker room increased in size from 1,300 square feet to 3,223 square feet. The new facility, also includes a media room, photographers dark room, training room and equipment room.

The signature piece to the locker room is an AU logo centered in the carpet with a large AU logo hanging directly overhead.

A visible change to fans will be the addition of a tunnel centered in the south end zone. Instead of entering and exiting the stadium from the southwest corner of Jordan-Hare Stadium as was done in years past, the Tigers will now use the new entrance to enter and leave the stadium.

Currently under construction, the second phase of the locker room project will include a recruiting lounge and a coaches locker room.

The previous locker room facilities were originally built in 1963, then renovated in 1970.

Visiting teams will also have a new locker room facility this season. Previously housed in the south end zone, the visiting locker rooms are now in the north end zone, complete with a coaches’ locker room, training room and media room. Opposing teams will now enter the field from the northeast corner of Jordan-Hare Stadium.

Auburn in Openers

Auburn has an 83-22-2 record in season-opening games. Auburn has won 12 of its last 14 season openers. The Tigers defeated Appalachian State, 22-15, in last season's opener.

Auburn has won 14 of the last 15 season openers played in Jordan-Hare Stadium dating back to 1976. Overall, the Tigers are 40-4-1 when kicking off the season at home.

Tuberville in Openers

Tommy Tuberville is 4-1 in season openers, including a 1-0 mark in openers at Auburn. In his first game as head coach at Auburn last season, Tuberville's team defeated Appalachian State, 22-15, at Jordan-Hare Stadium. In fact, Tuberville has won four consecutive season openers dating back to 1996, including three at Ole Miss and one at Auburn.

Non-Conference Action

Auburn has won 25 of the last 26 regular-season non-conference games dating back to 1991. Auburn has won five consecutive non-conference games dating back to the 1998 season. All five of those wins came in the confines of Jordan-Hare Stadium. In 1999, the Tigers were 3-0 against non-SEC teams, with wins over Appalachian State, Idaho, and Central Florida.

Tuberville Vs. Non-Conference Opponents

Tommy Tuberville is 16-0 as a head coach versus non-conference opponents. Tuberville won all 13 non-conference games at Ole Miss and all three in his first season on the Plains.

Versus The Mountain West

This is Auburn's first game against a Mountain West Conference opponent. In his five prior seasons as a head coach, Tommy Tuberville has not faced a Mountain West foe.

Dye Donned Cowboy Colors

Former Auburn coach Pat Dye was Wyoming's head coach in 1980 prior to his 12-year career as head coach of the Tigers. Dye posted a 6-5 mark during his brief stint in Laramie.

Mazzone’s Faced ‘Em

Auburn offensive coordinator Noel Mazzone has coached on two teams that have faced Wyoming. As a graduate assistant in 1980 at New Mexico, the Lobos lost to Pat Dye's Wyoming squad, 24-21. As an assistant at Colorado State from 1982-86, Mazzone coached against the Cowboys in four consecutive years.

Auburn on Thursday Nights

The Wyoming game marks Auburn's third Thursday evening game in the last four years. Auburn opened the 1997 season with a 28-17 win at Virginia. The Tigers and Cavaliers began the 1998 campaign with a Thursday night game., falling to Virginia, 19-0.

In 1994, Auburn defeated Kentucky, 41-14, on a Thursday night game in Jordan-Hare Stadium and the Tigers and Ole Miss Rebels opened the 1993 season on Thursday night in Auburn. Auburn won the contest, 16-12.

ESPN was slated to televise the Tigers 1995 game against Mississippi State, however Hurricane Opal ripped through Lee County on the Wednesday before the game, causing the contest to be moved to Saturday due to massive power failures throughout the region.

Auburn in August

The August 31st opener is the fourth game in Auburn's history that has been played during the month of August. The Tigers are 2-1 in August, with victories in Jordan-Hare Stadium over UAB (29-0) in 1996 and Georgia Southern (32-17) in 1991. Auburn's lone August loss was to Miami, 20-18, in the 1984 Kickoff Classic in East Rutherford, N.J.

This is Auburn's second earliest beginning to a season. The 1984 Kickoff Classic was played on August 28, with Auburn's 1991 and 1996 August games also being contested on August 31st.

History Lesson 101

Auburn’s opener with Wyoming marks the second time in school history that the Tigers have played a school nicknamed the Cowboys. Auburn defeated the Hardin-Simmons Cowboys, 35-12, on Oct. 3, 1959, in the Tigers’ home opener in then Cliff Hare Stadium.

What was also so significant about the Hardin-Simmons game in 1959? It marked the birth of Aubie, Auburn’s popular mascot. Aubie’s existence began as a cartoon charater that appeared on the game progam that day in 1959. Birmingham Post-Herald artist Phil Neel created the cartoon Tiger who continued to adorn Auburn program covers for 18 years.

He’s Got His Degree

Starting quarterback Ben Leard graduated Aug. 14 with a degree in Mass Communications. Leard spent this past summer interning with the Auburn Network and the Hughston Clinic.

Leard is now concentrating on a master’s degree, majoring in higer education administration with a minor in sports administration.

Prime Time

Sophomore wide receiver Ronney Daniels stole the spotlight as a freshman while playing on ESPN and ESPN2. In five televised games on the two all-sports networks, Daniels had 27 catches for 596 yards and six touchdowns for an average of 5.4 catches and 119.2 yards per contest.

Quarterback Ben Leard posted big numbers as well on the two networks last season in three games against LSU, Georgia, and Alabama. Leard was 66-of-94 (70.2%) for 957 yards with nine touchdowns and one interception.

Semesters Are Here

Besides the new-look locker rooms in Jordan-Hare Stadium this season, another new look will be the increased Auburn student attendance for its early season home games. For the first time in over 50 years, Auburn University has switched to the semester system instead of the quarter system used in previous years. The fall semester at Auburn began on Tuesday, August 22.

Dillard Connection

Since former coach Pat Dye signed TB Otis Mounds from Dillard High School in Ft. Lauderdale, Fla., in 1990, Auburn has had a pipeline of players from the south Florida school.

This season, Dillard products DB Junior Rosegreen and WR Joe Walkins are freshmen on Auburn’s team. Rosegreen and Walkins are two of nine recent AU players to have hailed from Dillard.

Dillard alums that played at Auburn include Mounds, WR Frank Sanders, RB James Bostic, DB Calvin Jackson, DB Brian Robinson, DL Charles Dorsey and DL James Roscoe.

Bostic, Sanders, Jackson, Robinson and Mounds all played at Auburn during the Tigers’ perfect 11-0 season in 1993.

Bostic and Sanders both became All-Americans at Auburn and today continue professional careers in the NFL.

Pre-Season All-SEC

Auburn wide receiver Ronney Daniels and defensive back Larry Casher were named to the 2000 Preseason All-SEC team. The team was chosen by media members at the SEC Media Days in July.

Quarterback Ben Leard and punter Damon Duval were tabbed to the second team.

A sophomore, Daniels set Auburn’s single-season record with 1,068 receiving yards in 1999. The Lake Wales, Fla., native was a freshman All-American and the SEC Freshman of the Year.

A senior from Prichard, Ala., Casher was an Associated Press All-SEC pick last season after notching 41 tackles.

Leard set four Auburn single-season records as a junior last season. He threw for 1,423 yards and 12 touchdowns despite missing four games due to injuries.

Duval, a sophomore from Chattanooga, Tenn., was second in the SEC and 18th nationally in punting as a freshman.

On the Tube

Three of Auburn’s games will be nationally televised by ESPN and ESPN2. The Tigers Sept. 9 game at Ole Miss will be televised by ESPN2 and the LSU game on Sept. 16 will be aired by ESPN.

Auburn has appeared on ESPN 41 times in the last 16 seasons. ESPN has televised at least two of the Tigers games in each of the last six seasons with the Auburn-Alabama game appearing on ESPN the last five consecutive years.

ESPN2 meanwhile has televised five Tiger games since 1996, with Auburn holding a 5-0 record in those contests.

The Tigers are 22-18-2 all-time on ESPN.

Auburn has had 10 consecutive SEC and 17 of its last 18 conference games televised live by either CBS, ESPN, ESPN2, Fox Sports South or Jefferson Pilot.
