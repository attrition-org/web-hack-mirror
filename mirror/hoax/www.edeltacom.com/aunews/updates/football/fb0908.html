Tigers Head To Oxford For SEC Opener

August 8, 2000

AUBURN- The Auburn Tigers are headed to Oxford, Miss. to take on the 19th-ranked Ole Miss Rebels. The Tigers (1-0, 0-0 SEC) and Rebels (1-0, 0-0 SEC) kick off at 6:00 pm CT. Airtime on the Auburn Network is 4:00 pm CT. Be sure to join Paul Ellen, along with Jim Fyffe, Charlie Trotman and Quentin Riggins for the Jeep Tiger Tailgate Show. This week Paul welcomes Auburn Director of High School Relations Jimmy Perry. Following the Tiger Tailgate Show ... Auburn vs. Ole Miss in an SEC Western Division battle.

Auburn in SEC Openers

Auburn is 34-27-5 in SEC openers winning seven straight conference openers dating back to 1993. This marks the ninth time since 1989 that Auburn has opened conference play with Ole Miss. Auburn was 5-1 in the 1990’s when opening conference play on the road.

In its last three SEC openers, Auburn has outscored its opponents, 77-16. The Tigers defeated Ole Miss, 19-9, in 1997, and shutout the Rebels, 17-0, in 1998, while beating LSU last season, 41-7.

Looking for 2-0 Start

Since 1983, Auburn has not lost its second game of the season after winning its season opener. In the last 16 years, Auburn has opened the season 2-0, 13 times. The last time the Tigers won their opener and dropped their second game was the 1983 season.

Auburn opened the 1983 season with a win over Southern Miss before falling to Texas, 20-7.

Tuberville Returns to Oxford

Tommy Tuberville will make his first return to Oxford since serving as Ole Miss’ head coach from 1995-98.

Tuberville posted a 25-20 mark in four years at Ole Miss. Coaching in Vaught-Hemingway Stadium, Tuberville is 14-7 all-time. Tuberville has posted a 17-7 mark in the state of Mississippi.

New Faces

A total of 62 players saw action in the Tigers’ season opening victory over Wyoming, including 26 first-timers. Of those players that played in their first game at Auburn, there were five junior college transfers, one sophomore, 11 redshirt freshmen, and nine true freshmen.

Seven of those 26 had their first start in an Auburn uniform, including six on defense. Starting in their first college game included: Rudi Johnson (TB), Whit Smith (LE), DeMarco McNeil (LT), Josh Weldon (RT), Reggie Torbor (RE), Mark Brown (LB), and Rashaud Walker (ROV).

Thirty-two of the 62 that played against the Cowboys were underclassmen.

Rudi Steps on the Scene

Junior college transfer Rudi Johnson made a big splash in his first game at Auburn, rushing for 174 yards on 27 carries with three touchdowns against Wyoming. His 174-yard performance was the most yards ever by an Auburn player in his Auburn debut. It also ranked as the 28th most yards all-time in a single-game.

His 174 yards were the most by an Auburn player since Stephen Davis ran for 186 yards (32 carries) against Georgia in 1994. Johnson gained more rushing yards (174) against Wyoming than the Tigers had gained as a team in each of the last 18 games dating back to the 1998 Tennessee game when Auburn rushed for 181 yards.

Johnson became the first Auburn runningback to rush for 100 yards since 1998 when Demontray Carter rushed for 102 yards against Alabama.

His three TD’s also marked the first time an Auburn runningback scored three touchdowns in a game since Stephen Davis had four against UTC in 1995. Johnson’s 70-yard fourth quarter touchdown run is tied for the 27th longest run in school history.

Johnson is the 22nd player in school history to rush for 150 or more yards in a game. Bo Jackson and James Brooks each ran for 150+ yards in a game eight times during their college careers.

For his efforts, the Auburn coaching staff named Johnson the offensive player of the game.

Big Plays Make Difference

In Auburn’s 35-21 victory over Wyoming, big plays made a difference in the Tigers’ 14-point victory. The Tigers tallied 11 plays of 10 yards or more, including five of 30 yards or more. Two plays of 50 yards or more resulted in two Auburn scores.

The 11 plays of 10 yards or more resulted in 347 of Auburn’s 443 total yards. The 347 yards equated into 78 percent of the Tigers offense.

20+ Points Equals Victory

Under Tuberville, Auburn is 6-0 when scoring 20 or more points in a game and is 0-6 when scoring less than 20 points.

Dating back to the 1995 season, Auburn is 31-7 when scoring 20 or more points in a game.

Lincoln Continues Where He Left Off

Senior linebacker Alex Lincoln, the team’s leading tackler in 1999 with 92, continued where he left off in the opener against Wyoming. The Mobile, Ala., native stopped a potential Wyoming score in its first possession, intercepting a Cowboy pass on the three yard line. Lincoln also added five tackles, including one for loss.

Lincoln was the Auburn coaching staff ‘s defensive player of the game last week.

Tiger IV Makes Flying Debut

Tiger IV, Auburn’s 21-year-old golden eagle that graces the sidelines in Jordan-Hare Stadium made history against Wyoming as she flew from the south goal posts to the 50-yard line prior to the game.

The flight will continue to be a part of Auburn’s pre-game festivities and occurs approximately 15 minutes before kickoff.

Under the care of the Southeastern Raptor Rehabilitation Center, Tiger IV makes appearances at Auburn athletic events and visits schools promoting conservation education about eagles and other endangered species.

Prime Time

Sophomore wide receiver Ronney Daniels stole the spotlight as a freshman while playing on ESPN and ESPN2. In six televised games on the two all-sports networks, Daniels had 28 catches for 662 yards and seven touchdowns for an average of 4.7 catches and 110.3 yards per contest. Against LSU, Daniels had one catch for 66 yards and one touchdown.

Quarterback Ben Leard posted big numbers as well on the two networks last season in three games against LSU, Georgia, and Alabama. Leard was 78-of-113 (69.0%) for 1,223 yards with 11 touchdowns and one interception. Against Wyoming, Leard was 12-for-19 with 266 yards and two touchdowns.

Dillard Connection

Since former coach Pat Dye signed TB Otis Mounds from Dillard High School in Ft. Lauderdale, Fla., in 1990, Auburn has had a pipeline of players from the south Florida school.

This season, Dillard products DB Junior Rosegreen and WR Joe Walkins are freshmen on Auburn’s team. Rosegreen and Walkins are two of nine recent AU players to have hailed from Dillard.

Dillard alums that played at Auburn include Mounds, WR Frank Sanders, RB James Bostic, DB Calvin Jackson, DB Brian Robinson, DL Charles Dorsey and DL James Roscoe.

Bostic, Sanders, Jackson, Robinson and Mounds all played at Auburn during the Tigers’ perfect 11-0 season in 1993.

Bostic and Sanders both became All-Americans at Auburn.

He’s Got His Degree

Starting quarterback Ben Leard graduated Aug. 14 with a degree in Mass Communications. Leard spent this past summer interning with the Auburn Network and the Hughston Clinic.

Leard is now concentrating on a master’s degree, majoring in higher education administration with a minor in sports administration.

Pre-Season All-SEC

Auburn wide receiver Ronney Daniels and defensive back Larry Casher were named to the 2000 Preseason All-SEC team. The team was chosen by media members at the SEC Media Days in July.

Quarterback Ben Leard and punter Damon Duval were tabbed to the second team.

A sophomore, Daniels set Auburn’s single-season record with 1,068 receiving yards in 1999. The Lake Wales, Fla., native was a freshman All-American and the SEC Freshman of the Year.

A senior from Prichard, Ala., Casher was an Associated Press All-SEC pick last season after notching 41 tackles.

Leard set four Auburn single-season records as a junior last season. He threw for 1,423 yards and 12 touchdowns despite missing four games due to injuries.

Duval, a sophomore from Chattanooga, Tenn., was second in the SEC and 18th nationally in punting as a freshman.

On the Tube

Three of Auburn’s games will be nationally televised by ESPN and ESPN2. The Tigers Sept. 9 game at Ole Miss will be televised by ESPN2 and the LSU game on Sept. 16 will be aired by ESPN.

Auburn has appeared on ESPN 42 times in the last 16 seasons. ESPN has televised at least two of the Tigers’ games in each of the last six seasons with the Auburn-Alabama game appearing on ESPN the last five consecutive years.

ESPN2 meanwhile has televised five Tiger games since 1996, with Auburn holding a 5-0 record in those contests.

The Tigers are 23-18-2 all-time on ESPN.

Auburn has had 10 consecutive SEC and 17 of its last 18 conference games televised live by either CBS, ESPN, ESPN2, Fox Sports South or Jefferson Pilot.

Tiger Walk

Coach Tommy Tuberville will announce each Tuesday at his weekly press conference who will walk to the traditional pregame Tiger Walk, that starts at the corner of Samford and Donahue and ends at the south end of Jordan-Hare Stadium.

Former Tigers James Owens and C.H. "Babe" McGehee led the team in Tiger Walk prior to the Wyoming game. Owens, a fullback from 1970-72, helped lead Auburn to a 10-1 record in 1972. An end for the Tigers from 1939-40, McGehee scored Auburn's first touchdown in what was then Auburn Stadium against Florida on Nov. 30, 1939.

Tuberville allows two people "who have done something special for Auburn University" to join the Tigers in the emotional event at each home game.
