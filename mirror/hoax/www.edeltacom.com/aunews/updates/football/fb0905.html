Auburn Football Practice Report

September 5, 2000

AUBURN-The Tigers spent the first 30 minutes of practice indoors because of light rain showers before finishing the final hour and a half outdoors in damp, soggy conditions behind the Auburn Athletic Complex Tuesday afternoon.

"It wasn't a great day for practice," Auburn head coach Tommy Tuberville said. "We had too much rain. Plus, we've got several guys fighting the flu. We've got three or four guys that are really down in the weather, but it's something we'll just have to deal with.

"Hopefully, we'll be able to get through this and get some of these guys back on the field for practice. Jeremy Toungett didn't even go out there today. Josh Weldon, Javor Mills, Marcus White and some other guys just didn't have any energy."

Tuberville said part of practice was spent working on special teams, kickoff and punting in particular.

"We worked on assignments in the kicking game. We didn't get to have any contact because of the weather. We know we're going to see the running game on Saturday and hopefully we'll be able to get a good day in tomorrow," Tuberville said.

Auburn will continue to practice Wednesday and Thursday in preparation for Saturday's nationally televised match-up against No. 18 Ole Miss in Oxford. The game is slated for a 6 p.m. kickoff and will be televised by ESPN2.
