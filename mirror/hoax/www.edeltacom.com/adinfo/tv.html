Television Advertising

 

The Auburn Network produces three high-quality television programs that bring Auburn Tiger athletics into viewers’ homes each week during football and basketball seasons.

Phil Snow and Coach Tommy Tuberville on the set of 'The Auburn Football Review'
The Auburn Football Review is an extremely popular show that airs on Sundays throughout football season and features Head Football Coach Tommy Tuberville. Highlights from Auburn’s game the day before are reviewed as Coach Tuberville gives his insights and comments about the Tigers’ performance and what lies ahead. Exclusive postgame lockerroom video is a popular part of the weekly show. The half-hour show airs in all Alabama markets, selected Georgia markets and throughout the Southeast on regional cable. Click here for a list stations that air The Auburn Football Review.

A half-hour magazine show, The Auburn Football Preview features Auburn assistant coaches who give a scouting report on the upcoming opponent and analyze the Tigers’ week of preparation. In addition, player features, interviews and a weekly visit with Coach Tommy Tuberville are also included. The show airs on Saturdays prior to network college football throughout the fall in all Alabama markets. Click here for a list stations that air The Auburn Football Preview.

Coach Cliff Ellis’ views and comments on Auburn Basketball and the SEC are featured each week on The Auburn Basketball Review. A look at the previous week’s basketball action, various features and fast-moving packages give viewers an inside look at Auburn Basketball. The half-hour show airs Saturdays throughout the SEC basketball season in all Alabama markets and is also available throughout the Southeast via regional cable. Click here for a list of stations that air The Auburn Basketball Review.

College Sports Southeast airs Auburn athletic events throughout the year. Events will be shown both live and tape-delayed with most events being re-aired at later dates resulting in multiple and long-term exposure. All eleven Auburn football games and the spring football game will be aired as well as several men’s basketball, women’s basketball and baseball games. A limited number of softball games, volleyball matches and gymnastics meets will also be aired. CSS programming airs in more than 675,000 homes in Alabama and the region.
