HISTORY

Founded by Dr. Jules Stein in 1960, RPB has been identified with virtually every major scientific advance in eye research. RPB conducted the first and only exhaustive survey of eye research in the U.S. and sponsored the first public opinion poll indicating that Americans fear blindness more than any other disability. The organization spearheaded efforts to establish the National Eye Institute (NEI) and was a major influence in stimulating legislative interest and support on its behalf.

Since its founding, RPB has channeled $166 million into eye research at medical institutions throughout the U.S. It financed initial efforts to adapt the laser for use in ophthalmology and fostered development of new drugs that prevent blindness and intraocular lenses used in cataract surgery that help improve and/or restore vision to more than one million Americans each year. RPB has helped create eye institutes in every geographical area of the country to conduct research, enhance ophthalmic education and provide improved vision care.

 

MISSION

Research to Prevent Blindness (RPB) is committed to stimulate, sustain and intensify a concerted research assault, with the goal of developing preventives, cures and effective treatments for all diseases of the visual system that damage and destroy sight.

RPB mobilizes financial resources in support of eye research making available essential laboratory space, scientific personnel and advanced technological equipment in its mission which seeks to preserve vision and restore sight.
