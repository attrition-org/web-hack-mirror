UK Minister's four-state Australian tour
Britain’s Minister for Consumers and Corporate Affairs, Dr Kim Howells, is having talks with Australian political and business leaders during a six-day Australian visit in which he has crossed the continent from east to west. Dr Howells has visited Brisbane, Melbourne, Sydney and Perth on his visit, which saw him open the Millennium Products exhibition at the Customs House, Sydney, with the Premier of NSW, Bob Carr. The exhibition opened on 7 December and will run till 27 December
NHS Direct website launched by Prime Minister
A major expansion of a health advice service which helps people get medial help at home via the Internet will be launched by Tony Blair today.  The Prime Minister will unveil details of an Internet version of the NHS Direct Service and an extended telephone advice line during a visit to Liverpool
ANZTAC Trade mission to Australia
A group of 14 strong companies will visit our shores from 7th -10th December 1999, on a trade mission organised by the Australia & New Zealand Trade Advisory Committee (ANZTAC), one of the Local Area Advisory Groups to British Trade International.
 Mission Co-ordinator, Christopher Heighway said, "Britain is Australasia's most important European trading partner. The Sydney Olympics, the 2001 Australian Federation and the Americas Cup are on the horizon and British companies are well placed to produce the products and services that these and other organisations within the Australasian market need."
Millennium Products Exhibition - Sydney
Innovative and pioneering designs at the sharp end of British Industry will be on show in Sydney next month in the Millennium Products exhibition, on the final leg of its world tour. Millennium Products is a Design Council (UK) initiative to promote originality in business and showcases the best of Britain. It provides insight into current trends and future issues in design and offers examples of how ideas can be applied to day-to-day products.
J.D.Pringle - 2000 British award for journalism
The British High Commission and the National Press Club are pleased to invite entries for the 2000 John Douglas Pringle Award, the British Prize for Journalism. The award is made to promote a deeper understanding of modern Britain among Australian journalists. The award is open to Australian print, radio, television or multimedia journalists working in Australia who have at least two years’ professional experience. The award winner will receive a study tour to the United Kingdom for up to 10 days to research a particular area of professional interest.

 

British Servicemen ending East Timor deployment
British service personnel will begin returning from East Timor to their home bases from today, when lead elements of the contingent from the 2nd Battalion, Royal Gurkha Rifles, return to their base in Brunei. The Gurkhas were among the first international troops to arrive in East Timor when the Australian-led Interfet force began its deployment in the territory. They were among the first troops to patrol outside the capital, Dili, brought the first humanitarian aid to communities east of the capital, and provided the bulk of the initial deployment into the Ambeno enclave.
British passport fee increases overseas
In parallel with the Home Office announcement on 6 December 1999 on upgrading passport agency services, the Foreign and Commonwealth Office announced an increase in the cost of passports issued overseas which will recover the cost of providing a passport issuing service overseas. It will also cover the cost of providing those consular protection services for which no fee is charged. This effectively represents an insurance premium on the price of a passport.
