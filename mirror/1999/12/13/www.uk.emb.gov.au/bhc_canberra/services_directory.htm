In Australia, the British High Commissioner represents the British Government in its relations with the Australian Government. The High Commissioner, Mr Alex Allan, arrived in Australia in November 1997. His tasks are to present British policies to the Australian Government and to Australians, to report and explain Australian policies and views to his own Government in London, and to promote British interests. He is based at the British High Commission in Canberra but travels regularly to the States and Territories.

In carrying out these tasks the British High Commissioner and his staff deal with political, commercial, defence and economic questions of interest to Britain and Australia. The High Commissioner is also responsible for press and cultural relations, and the issue of British passports, entry clearances and visas through the High Commission Consular Section.

The High Commissioner is supported in this work by the Deputy High Commissioner, Dr Andrew Pocock.

In addition to the British High Commission in Canberra, there are also British Consulates-General in Brisbane, Melbourne, Perth and Sydney, where the senior officer in charge has the title of "British Consul-General" and is responsible to the High Commissioner. A Consular office was established in Adelaide in 1995. In Tasmania the British Government is represented by an Honorary Consul.

The duties of the Consuls-General relate to the promotion of trade and investment between Britain and Australia. They also keep the High Commissioner in touch with political, economic and other developments in the States and Territories.

In addition to their trade and commercial role, the Consulates-General are responsible for the consular protection of British nationals who are temporary residents in, or visitors to, their consular district. Brisbane covers both Queensland and the Northern Territory, while Melbourne is responsible for Victoria, South Australia and Tasmania.

Since Britain and Australia are both members of the Commonwealth of Nations, the Consuls-General and their Consular officials do not hold exequatur status (ie they do not perform the functions of similar British officials in non-Commonwealth countries, such as administering oaths, registering births and deaths, keeping a record of UK citizens in the country, drawing up and attesting legal documents etc).

It is, however, a function of the British High Commission to issue entry certificates (visas), where necessary, to Australians planning to visit Britain, or other British dependent territories. All UK passport, entry clearance and visa applications are dealt with in Canberra by the Consular Section. The Consulates-General do not handle these matters, although application forms are available at their offices.

Entry clearance advisory leaflets and application forms are available on-line. You can contact the Entry Clearance section, Passports Section and the BHC Information Library on line if you require information that is not included in the BISA Passports and Entry Clearance section.

British commercial work or trade promotion in Australia is overseen by the High Commissioner in Canberra (covering the whole of Australia), the British Consulates-General in Sydney (NSW & ACT), Melbourne (VIC & TAS), Perth (WA & NT), Brisbane (QLD) and by the Consulate in Adelaide (SA).

The Consul-General in Sydney is also the Director General of Trade & Investment Promotion (DGTIP) and is responsible for co-ordinating the British trade and investment promotion effort throughout Australia. DGTIP oversees liasion with the Department of Trade & Industry (DTI) in London.

BISA provides news and background material about British policies on national and international issues.

The Information Section based at the British High Commission in Canberra offers a wide range of services, including answers to enquiries on:

  • British Government Departments
  • The British Media
  • British politicians and business leaders
  • Project work for schools and community groups
  • VAT, customs duty, addresses and telephone numbers, British Government pension problems etc.

BISA also loans photographic displays, flags, videos and music tapes to community groups and schools.
