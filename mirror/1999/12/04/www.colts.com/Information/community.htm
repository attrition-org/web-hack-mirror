The Indianapolis Colts take great pride in playing an active part in our community. We realize the importance and responsibility to give back to our community. Our commitment is to be active in programs that support and improve the quality of life of our fans and neighbors in and around Indiana. This is a commitment from the entire organization—our players, coaches, front office staff. Our efforts combine financial support, in-kind donations and assistance with many community programs.

The following are just some of the ways the Colts are making positive "hoofprints" in the community:

FUNDRAISING EVENTS

The annual Colts Classic Golf Tournament is our largest fundraising event. Over 30 players and coaches team up with corporate foursomes for a day on the links "down under" sponsored by Outback Steakhouse. The proceeds of the event benefit both Methodist Hospital and Noble of Indiana. Over $250,000 has been raised since the inaugural tournament.

PLAYER TICKET BLOCKS

Many of the Colts players and their families have donated time and resources to helping less fortunate young Colts fans attend games. In 1998, more than 20 players sponsored ticket blocks of at least 20 tickets per game to not-for-profit agencies. The players and organization realize the importance of providing a positive, unique and exciting experience for our Indiana youth—our future fans. If you would like your organization to be considered for tickets through the program, please contact and/or send information about your group to the Colts Community Relations Department.

NEWSPAPER IN EDUCATION PROGRAM

The Colts have teamed up with The Star and The News since 1985 to provide educational programs to schools throughout central Indiana. Through the Gridiron Geography program, students in grades 3-8 follow the Colts through the sports pages of the newspaper to learn various school subjects—primarily geography. 17 players participated in the program last year.

COMMUNITY OUTREACH

Auction/Raffle Donations

Throughout the year, contributions of Colts’ memorabilia are given to Indiana charity organizations, community fundraisers, school functions and more. Items donated are typically used as auction items, raffle/door prizes as well as school incentive prizes. Please note that a written request for such items must be received at least six weeks in advance of the event for consideration.

Public Service Announcements and Campaigns

The visibility afforded to professional athletes gives our players an extraordinary opportunity to reach out and make an impact on the community. Public Service Announcements (PSA’s) are one way we help accomplish this. Throughout each season, players and coaches help non-profit organizations promote their services by putting together PSA’s for both radio and television.

COLTS APPEARANCES

Colts Cheerleaders are available for personal appearances. They frequently attend special community events, school assemblies, hospital visits, company events, golf outings, fundraising benefits and more. Please contact Pam Watkins at the Colts office for more information.

When schedules permit, Colts players, coaches and management staff make public appearances for charity benefits, autograph sessions, speaking engagements and other various events. Front office representatives are also available to speak at career sessions and school programs.

COLTS CAR-A-VAN
The Indianapolis Colts Car-a-van will stop at over 40 events and festivals all over the State of Indiana this summer. The Car-a-van is an interactive mobile unit which offers Colts fans the opportunity to meet Colts Cheerleaders, the chance to win Colts prizes as well as provide information about the upcoming season and events. If you would like your events or festival to be considered for a Car-a-van stop, please contact Kristi Dorman at the Colts office.

TOURS AND MEETINGS

The Colts Training Facility will be available this fall to groups and organizations for purposes of tours or a possible meeting site. Please contact the Community Relations Department for additional information.

 Please note that any and all of these services provided by the Colts Community Relations Department are subject to availability and require at least six weeks advance written notice.
