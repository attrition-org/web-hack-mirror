The U.S. Botanic Garden

Established by Congress in 1820, the United States Botanic Garden is the oldest botanic garden in North America. The garden's first greenhouse was constructed in 1842; since 1849 the Garden has been located at the eastern end of the Mall.

NOTICE: On September 1, 1997, at 5 p.m. the U.S. Botanic Garden Conservatory officially closed for a period of about three years to undergo extensive renovation and reconstruction. Educational programs previously announced in the 1997 Calendar of Events, including the fall and winter flower shows, have been canceled. Information about off-site programs will be presented here as it becomes available.
