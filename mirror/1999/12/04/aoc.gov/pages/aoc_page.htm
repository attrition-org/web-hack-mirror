The United States Capitol

The Architect of the Capitol

The Architect of the Capitol is both the person and the title of the agency responsible to Congress for the structural and mechanical care of the Capitol, the legislative office buildings, the Library of Congress buildings, the Supreme Court building, the surrounding grounds, and other elements of the Capitol Complex. The Architect is also responsible for the care and conservation of numerous works of art.

Available information includes a description of the responsibilities of the Architect and brief biographies of the men who have held the position:

  • William Thornton (1793)
  • Benjamin Henry Latrobe (1803-1811, 1815-1817)
  • Charles Bulfinch (1818-1829)
  • Thomas Ustick Walter (1851-1865)
  • Edward Clark (1865-1902)
  • Elliott Woods (1902-1923)
  • David Lynn (1923-1954)
  • J. George Stewart (1954-1970)
  • George Malcolm White, FAIA (1971-1995)
  • Alan M. Hantman, AIA (1997-)
