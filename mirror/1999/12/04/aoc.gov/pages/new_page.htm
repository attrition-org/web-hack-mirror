The United States Capitol

What's New at This Site

As mentioned on our home page, this site is being implemented in phases, and new material will be added as time and resources permit. Present plans include more information about works of art in the Capitol Rotunda and a set of pages geared to younger users.

The most recent update to this site (December 3, 1999) includes

  • Information about the arrival of the 1999 Capitol Grounds Holiday Tree.

The update of November 2, 1999, consisted of

  • New information about the 1999 Capitol Grounds Holiday Tree.
  • The addition of information about the Peace Monument to the Art in the U.S. Capitol section.

The update of September 30, 1999, consisted of

  • An update about progress toward construction on the Renovation of the U.S. Botanic Garden Conservatory.
  • The removal of outdated information about concerts on the Capitol Grounds.

The update of August 23, 1999, consisted of

  • The addition of information about Music at the Capitol--free concerts on the Capitol Grounds (removed on September 30, 1999).

The update of August 16, 1999, consisted of

  • The addition on the For Further Information menu page of links to the virtual tour of the Capitol on the Web site of the United States Senate and information about opportunities for employment with the Architect of the Capitol on the Office of Personnel Management's USAJobs agency search page.

The update of July 21, 1999, consisted of

  • Information about the conservation of the Peace Monument on the Capitol Grounds.
  • A new photograph of the Capitol Rotunda with safety netting in place (56k .jpg) in connection with the rehabilitation of the U.S. Capitol dome.

The update of June 9, 1999, consisted of

  • Information about the installation of safety netting in the Capitol Rotunda in connection with the rehabilitation of the U.S. Capitol dome.

The update of May 25, 1999, consisted of

  • Information about the closing of the Capitol Rotunda from May 18 through June 10, 1999, in connection with the rehabilitation of the U.S. Capitol dome.
  • Information about "Kids View the World of Plants," a new exhibit of more than 215 botanical pictures created by children from 41 states and the District of Columbia, which was installed at the U.S. Botanic Garden in April 1999.

The update of March 2, 1999, consisted of

  • An update about progress toward construction on the Renovation of the U.S. Botanic Garden Conservatory.
  • New information about the orchid exhibition and orchid festival presented jointly by the Botanic Garden and the Smithsonian Institution.
  • New information about a tour of the Botanic Garden Production Facility on March , 1999.

The update of December 10, 1998, consisted of

  • Information about the decoration and lighting of the 1998 Capitol Holiday Tree and the history of the Capitol Holiday Trees.
  • Updated information about the rehabilitation of the U.S. Capitol dome.
  • Additional photographs of student murals on the U.S. Botanic Garden construction fence.
  • Maps showing tour bus routes from the Capitol to Parking Lots A and B near the Capitol Grounds.

The update of November 17, 1998, consisted of

  • An update about progress toward construction on the Renovation of the U.S. Botanic Garden Conservatory.

The update of August 13, 1998, consisted of

  • An update to the page about Those Who Have Lain in State or in Honor in the Capitol Rotunda.
  • An update about progress toward construction on the Renovation of the U.S. Botanic Garden Conservatory.

The update of August 5, 1998, consisted of

  • Additional photographs of student murals on the U.S. Botanic Garden construction fence.

The update of May 26, 1998, consisted of

  • An addition to the Works of Art section, presenting information about the Relief Portrait Plaques of Lawgivers in the House of Representatives Chamber.
  • Additional information about student murals on the U.S. Botanic Garden construction fence.

The update of March 23, 1998, consisted of

  • Information about a ceremony and program to honor the artists of the student murals on the U.S. Botanic Garden construction fence.

The update of March 23, 1998, consisted of

  • Additional information about student murals on the U.S. Botanic Garden construction fence.

The update of February 26, 1998, consisted of

  • Updated information about offsite programs of the U.S. Botanic Garden.

  • Updated information about the Renovation of the U.S. Botanic Garden Conservatory.
