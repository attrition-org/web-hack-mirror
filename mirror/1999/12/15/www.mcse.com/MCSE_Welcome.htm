About MCSE Certification

In today's highly competitive marketplace Microsoft Certified Systems Engineers are in great demand, and that demand is not being met. Independent industry surveys indicate that there are more than 346,000 Information Technology positions vacant at companies with at least 100 employees and an estimated 1.3 million new jobs being created through 2006. University of Phoenix can help prepare you for the Microsoft certification exams so you can become a MCSE.

Because in the IT industry there’s no such thing as overqualified, adding an MCSE certification to your list of professional qualifications could be a major factor in your career advancement.

University of Phoenix is now offering a series of concentrated courses that will help prepare you for the Microsoft certification exams. These courses are designed specifically to enhance and refine the skills you need in today’s rapidly changing world of technology.

For network professionals, Microsoft offers the Microsoft Certified Systems Engineer (MCSE) credential. MCSEs are qualified to effectively plan, implement, maintain, and support information systems in a wide range of computing environments using the Microsoft Windows NT Server and the Microsoft BackOffice integrated family of server products.
