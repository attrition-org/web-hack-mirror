The Dudley Deal Back to About the Council

Dudley Council provides services for the 312,000 people of the Borough. Services include the collection of rubbish, maintaining roads, social services and the provision of schools.

We are committed to providing a high quality of service for all people living, visiting and working in the Borough. We believe that people using all of these services should know what standards to expect.

We call these standards THE DUDLEY DEAL.

These pages tell you more about it.

When you contact the Council

You will be treated with dignity and respect, and we will deal with your enquiries in a friendly and efficient way.
All staff, in offices or visiting you at home, will carry a Council identify card.
We will try to answer your telephone calls within six rings.
We will reply to your letters to departments within 14 days.
We will welcome your enquiries, comments and complaints about our services.

Dudley Council tries hard to meet these objectives, but realise that sometimes things go wrong.

If you want to complain about a service, we will acknowledge your complaint within seven days and complete investigations or send you a progress report within 28 days.

If you would like to know more about any of the Council services mentioned, please contact us using one of the numbers listed at the end of this publication.

How you can have your say

Councillors represent the interests of their local residents and help decide how the Borough is run. They make important decisions such as how to spend the Council's money. You can influence these decisions when you vote for your local councillors. Dudley has 72 councillors, three for each of the 24 wards of the Borough.

Every home in the Borough will receive an electoral registration form in September, with clear instructions on how to fill it in. Returning this form will entitle you to vote in all elections (including General and European).

The Council publishes the draft Register of Electors in November each year and the Register of Electors in February. You can inspect them at the Council House and all main post offices and libraries.

Everyone on the Register of Electors will receive details of where and when to vote.

We will arrange postal or proxy votes for certain people who can't get to their Polling Station on the day of an election. You will have to apply in advance.

The Council holds Committee meetings regularly. You are welcome to attend, but you may have to leave whilst private matters are discussed. The dates of meetings are available from the Council House, and agendas and minutes can also be seen at main libraries.

Paying for your Council Services

To pay for local services, the Council receives money from the Government and it collects local taxes from council tax payers and business rate payers. Local tax payers can be sure that Dudley Council is determined to collect all taxes due from everyone liable to pay.

We promote easier methods of payment including the use of Direct Debit.

We can make reasonable payment arrangements with council tax payers, business rate payers and debtors in difficulty.

We will try to ensure that no one waits more than ten minutes to be seen at our Helpdesk or cash counter in the main banking hall in Dudley.

Claiming your benefits

The Council supports and encourages people to claim the benefits they are entitled to.

We operate a benefits advice service in Dudley town centre where you can find out how to claim.

We process at least 80% of all new claims for housing benefit, rent allowance and council tax benefit within ten working days of all information being received.

We process all claims for free school meals within three working days.

We try to visit all claimants who, due to illness or disability, are unable to come to our offices and need assistance with benefits matters, within seven working days.

We take all reasonable steps to detect fraudulent benefits claims and recover benefit overpayments.

Education

Individual school head teachers and governors have responsibility for the school budget, staff and day to day management. The Council supports school staff, pupils and parents by providing central services including special education, personnel advice, in-service training and arranging catering and cleaning contracts. The Council also pays student grants and provides non-vocational adult education courses.

Parents have a right to express a preference for a particular school for their child to attend. The Council will support parents in obtaining a school place. If the school is full, parents whose child is refused entry may appeal against this decision. A booklet entitled 'Information for Parents' is available from schools and libraries.

We provide support for children with special educational needs. You can get a booklet called 'Special Educational Needs - Dudley', giving further details - call (01384) 814393.

In addition to new statutory responsibilities with regard to teaching the national curriculum, schools will

Provide new parents a handbook or prospectus detailing school policies and the structure of the school.

Hold parents meetings and give regular reports on pupil progress.

Hold at least an annual parents meeting when the governors will present a report on their work over the last school year.

Provide a summary of any inspection of the school.

Sport and Leisure

The Council runs five leisure centres - at Brierley Hill, Coseley, Dudley, Halesowen and Stourbridge. These provide facilities for a wide range of sporting activities. Equipment hire is available.

All instructors and coaches at our centres are fully qualified.

Swimming pool water will be checked to make sure that it is of good quality, and will be maintained at a comfortable temperature.

All pool staff will be trained and hold a recognised qualification in life saving; there will always be a member of staff qualified in first aid in the centre.

Changing rooms will be cleaned regularly. If you report that they are not clean, we will take action immediately.

The Council maintains a large number of parks, nature reserves and public open spaces. The nature reserve at Saltwells has won two national awards.

We sweep and inspect all children's play areas three times each week.

We mark all hired football pitches every fortnight during the football season.

We will inspect any tree on Council land that is causing a nuisance within seven days.

The Council operates a number of community centres, aimed at young people, which are open for a minimum of four nights each week.

Libraries, Museums, Art Galleries and Events

The Council provides a wide range of library services including the loan of books, compact discs and videos. There are two museums, plus a wide variety of events on offer at halls in Stourbridge, Brierley Hill, Halesowen and Dudley.

We offer free library membership and loan of up to eight books at a time.

Books may be renewed by telephone.

Library services are available to people who are housebound.

Your Social Services

The Council provides support to people needing assistance in the community. This may be through home care, meals on wheels, day care, residential placements or involvement with families. To get assistance, please contact your local district office - Sedgley, Netherton, Dudley, Coseley, Brierley Hill, Halesowen or Stourbridge.

In urgent situations, assessment and provision of services will begin at once.

Once the assessment process has begun, it will be completed within a maximum of two weeks.

We try to provide services within three weeks of the completion of your assessment.

During your assessment, you may choose to have a relative, friend, or advocate with you to support or speak on your behalf.

We will give you accurate information about the range of services and what choices you have.

A more complete description of services and standards is provided in the Social Services Community Care Charter - available from district offices.

Services for Householders

The Council collects almost one tonne of household waste from each of the Borough's homes each year. It provides 60 recycling sites and over 3,500 tonnes of waste are recycled each year. The Council also deals with industrial air noise and land contamination and ensures that industrial developments do not harm the environment.

We will collect your rubbish on the same day each week, and tell you in advance of any changes to collection days.

We collect missed rubbish on the same day if you report this before 12 noon, or the next day if you call after 12 noon.

We provide a free special collection service twice a year to collect bulky waste including garden rubbish.

We will remove large items of rubbish e.g. sofas and fridges within eight working days of your request (a charge will be made for this service).

We will provide a pest control service on the next working day for rats, mice, cockroaches and bed bugs (free), wasps and fleas (for a charge).

We will try to clear blocked drains on the next working day (a call-out charge will be made for this service).

We aim to collect stray dogs within four hours of you contacting us (between 8am and 2pm, Monday to Friday).

We will investigate your complaints about industrial and commercial air and noise pollution and tell you what we are doing within five working days.

We will monitor the air quality in Dudley; air quality reports are available each week.

On planning issues

We will publicise planning applications so that people who may be affected by a proposed development can have their views taken into account.
We will make decisions on at least 70% of all planning applications within eight weeks.
We will let you know the result of your application within two working days of a decision being made.
We will respond to complaints about possible breaches of planning controls within seven working days.

Services for Consumers

The Council protects your interests by providing consumer advice, enforcing health and safety legislation for your protection, inspecting all food businesses and licensing all taxis and places for public entertainment.

We give advice on any trading standards matter during office hours. We will give a response to telephone and personal callers either immediately or on the following working day.

In appropriate cases we will investigate alleged breaches of criminal law and take necessary action.

We will investigate complaints about health and safety in commercial premises e.g. shops, restaurants and warehouses, within seven days.

We will look into any accident requiring investigation which happens in commercial premises within a maximum of seven days.

We will inspect on a regular basis all restaurants and shops selling food to ensure that they comply with the law.

We will promote your safety when determining licence applications for taxis and private hire vehicles, to make sure they are roadworthy, in good condition and clean.

We decide hackney carriage fares after consultation with the trade. The cost of your journey will be displayed on the meter in the car. It is an offence for the driver not to use the meter. The Council does not set rates for private hire vehicles; a price for the journey should be agreed at the time of booking.

We issue permits to door to door or street collectors. Before giving donations, ask to see the permit or Home Office exemption certificate.

The Council licences all places offering public entertainment. We check that the building is safe and suitable for this purpose.

Services for Businesses

The Council provides a wide range of services to businesses based within the Borough.

We offer a trade waste collection service to any shop, office or business within the Borough (a charge will be made for this service).

We can arrange a 12 month pest control contract for any non-food business in the Borough (a charge will be made for this service).

We offer advice on all aspects of trading standards and consumer legislation to any business trading in the Borough within five days (free of charge).

We offer advice and guidance on health and safety, food safety legislation and licensing to commercial premises such as shops, restaurants, offices and warehouses within seven days (free of charge).

The Borough's Roads

The Council maintains 865 kilometres of roads and 30,000 street lights, and is responsible for all traffic signals and winter gritting.

Dangerous potholes will be made safe or repaired within 24 hours of you telling us. All other reported defects will be repaired within five working days.

We will repair hazardous streetlights and illuminated signs within four hours of you contacting us. Non-emergency work will be done within five days.

Faulty traffic lights will be attended to within one hour of you telling us, and repaired within 24 hours.

We will make sure that all salting routes are treated within three hours of the predicted time for severe frost or snowfall issued by the Meteorological Office.

Residential roads will be swept every eight weeks or as necessary. This will not be before 8am or after 6pm.

Registering Births, Deaths & Marriages

All births, deaths and marriages must be registered. The Council operates this service through register offices in Dudley and Stourbridge. To enable us to offer a caring confidential service we run an appointments scheme.

Appointments are offered by telephone for the registering of births and deaths; they will be within two working days.

Legally you can only give notice of your wedding three months in advance. However, we try to assist by offering provisional bookings up to one year in advance.

Duplicate birth or marriage certificates are available from the office at which they were registered (a charge will be made for this service).

Council Housing

The Council provides nearly 31,000 homes in the Borough and around £38 million per year is spent on repairing and improving them. This money comes from rents and government grants.

Houses are allocated according to a points system. Generally the greater your housing need the more points you will have. Once you have been provided with a Council property, we have responsibility for its repair and maintenance, not including interior decoration, gardening or repairs due to neglect, wilful or accidental damage. Enquiries should be made at your local estate office.

We provide application forms for housing on request or within three working days of your written or telephone enquiry.

Within three weeks of receiving your completed application form, we will inform you if you qualify for inclusion on the waiting list, and if so how many points you have been given.

We will offer you properties when any that you fully qualify for become available in your preferred area; however, if you do not respond to reasonable offers, your application may be delayed.

You will be given two days from receiving the keys to decide if you want to accept a property.

We test all electrical and gas supplies in houses before we let them; we service all our gas appliances every year.

When you tell us that a repair is needed, we will advise you of its priority category and note any access arrangements. There are published response times for each category.

We will make safe emergency repairs on the day you report them; these include things like gas leaks and electricity failure. Outside office hours in an EMERGENCY, call (01384) 818182.

When your repair is completed, we will ask if you were satisfied, by leaving a questionnaire for you to return confidentially.

You can pay your rent and council tax, or get a balance of your rent account at any housing district or estate office in the Borough. Clients wishing to make payments in Dudley town centre are advised that the Finance Department's Banking Hall in Priory Street accepts payments on behalf of the Dudley District Housing Office.

Every eight weeks we produce a local report for tenants, giving information about your estate, this will be displayed at the estate office.

We offer a free, confidential service for any person, not just tenants, who require advice on any housing matter. The office is at 2 Birdcage Walk, in the centre of Dudley (no appointments necessary).
