Changes in 1999/2000

Council Tax to increase by 4.7%. For over four out of five Borough Council Tax payers, the increase will be less than 60 pence per week.

Maintaining the Council's existing policies in 1999/2000 would cost £251.8million. The Government sets the amounts councils may spend above which council tax benefit subsidy would be lost - for Dudley this is just under £246 million. This target will be met by making 'good housekeeping' savings of £1.1 million and using £6.1 million from Council balances (available because of careful financial management over the years). This leaves £1.3 million available for a package of increased funding for key priority services .

  • an extra £500,000 for Education to support Early Years Development, Behaviour Support, and improving teaching and learning through use of information Communication Technology.
  • an extra £300,000 for Social Services - more for Home Care, Social Work Assessment, Youth Offending Teams,and initiatives with Health and the voluntary sector.
  • an extra £300,000 to improve the quality of cleanliness through out the Borough.
  • an extra £50,000 for Lifelong Learning - to increase out reach work and independent advice to learners.
  • an extra £ 1 50,000 towards the development and regeneration of our town centres, and to encourage inward investment into the Borough.
In 1999/2000, the Council's net spending will be £252 million. This is how each £1 will be spent.

Published Audit Commission figures show Dudley to be the best performing metropolitan authority in the country in terms of collecting local taxes. This has been achieved through a combination of a high collection rate and low collection costs. Local tax payers can be assured that Dudley Council is determined to collect all taxes due from all those who are liable to pay.

Thanks to all Council tax payers who pay promptly. Your co-operation helps keep to a minimum the amount of Tax you pay.
