The Dudley Borough Agenda is the Council's key strategy statement for the next five years. It sets out the principles and values that give the Council its focus and direction, highlights the key pressures the Borough faces, and proposes key priorities for action. It was first produced and launched in 1996 and is intended to provide an overview of Dudley Council's assessment of the problems, challenges and opportunities facing the Borough over the next five years. More detailed plans and strategies are also produced that provide the background to the Agenda, such as the Unitary Development Plan, Community Care Plan and Housing Strategy.

Principles for Action

Within the Agenda, Dudley Council sets out its key principles for action. It states that the Council is:

  1. proud of the Borough, its people and its unique character
  2. determined to carry into the future the history, traditions and landscape that has shaped the Borough
  3. keen to sustain the best in the Borough - and striving to improve the less good
  4. supportive of all the communities in the Borough - whether communities of place, culture or interest
  5. committed to local democracy and its own leadership in the communities it serves as well as amongst other service providers
  6. pragmatic: willing - within its overall strategy - to seize opportunities as they arise

In providing services it is determined to

  • promote equality of access and opportunity for all service users
  • provide quality services and give good value for money

In responding to the changing world, the Council has identified four themes that describe the key issues which need to be addressed over the 5 year period, these are:

  • ECONOMIC VITALITY - Fostering a climate in which jobs can be created, new skills promoted and developed and a diverse local economy sustained.
  • PERSONAL WELL BEING - providing services and opportunities that improve the quality of life local people enjoy, including their health and social care, their safety, their ability to exercise self determination and choice, achieve personal development and equal access to opportunities and services.
  • LIFETIME LEARNING - Lifetime learning enables people in the Borough, through access to education, learning and, in the broadest sense, cultural opportunities, to make choices in their lives, become empowered and able to change and seize new opportunities as they arise.
  • ENVIRONMENT - managing land and environmental issues to balance all needs, ensuring that people can have decent homes, opportunities for investment are created, Local Agenda 21 targets are achieved and people have access to recreational opportunities.
