Community Choice
the Dudley Borough Citizens' Panel
Q. Why have a Community Choice Panel?
A. Community Choice will give the public a regular opportunity to say what they feel about the services they receive, and what they would like to see change, to improve life in the Borough. The Panel is a new way of ensuring that local people's choices and preferences can be heard and listened to.
Q. How will it work?
A. Some 2,500 local people will be invited to join the Panel, and receive regular questionnaires asking for their views on a range of local issues. The Panel itself will include people from different backgrounds and places in the Borough, to make it a fair and balanced group. There will be regular changes to panel membership, to enable new members to take part.
Q. What do we expect the Panel to achieve?
A. We hope to use the Panel in many different ways, but with two key aims.
  • By improving our understanding of what the public want we can make sure that our services achieve the best value possible - giving them the quality of services they seek at a cost they feel is reasonable.
  • To help us plan for the future. Whether it is for issues that affect the whole Borough, or more local areas, or communities, we can use the Panel as a way of improving the extent of public influence on future decisions in the Borough.
  • There will be regular reports produced to explain what the Panel feel, and also To show what changes have happened as a result.
Q. Will it be fair and independent?
A. It is most important that the Panel remains representative of the community and that the views of Panel members are reported fairly and objectively. To achieve this, we will be working with Wolverhampton University who will make sure that the Panel is managed correctly, and who will produce independent reports on the survey findings.
