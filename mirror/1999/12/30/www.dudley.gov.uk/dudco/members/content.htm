Dudley's Councillors 1999/2000

Dudley Metropolitan Borough Council has 72 Councillors, three for each of the 24 wards in the Borough. Each Councillor is elected for a four year term, with elections being held three years out of every four. Civic ceremonies are led by the Mayor, who is elected for a term of office lasting a year.

Councillors perform two major roles:

  • To represent the interests of ward residents.
  • To help decide how the Borough is run, making important decisions such as how available resources are to be allocated between services.

Councillors hold surgeries to allow residents to raise problems regarding Council services. To find out when your Councillors are holding their surgeries see local press or ring the Councillor(s) concerned (see Councillors details on these pages).

The political constitution of the Council, as of May 6th 1999, is:

  • Labour - 54 seats
  • Conservative - 8 seats
  • Liberal Democrats - 10 seats
