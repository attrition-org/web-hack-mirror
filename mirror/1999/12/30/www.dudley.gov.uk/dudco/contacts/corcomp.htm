Corporate Complaints Procedure

A Corporate Complaints Procedure was introduced in October 1992 in order that people wishing to comment or complain about a Council service should know how to go about it, and so that staff should be clear about how they should respond. Members may be asked by members of the public for assistance on this.

The Council tries to provide good services to the people of the Dudley Borough. But there may be times when things go wrong. When this happens customers are left unhappy or dissatisfied with the service that has been provided. The Council wants to hear from such customers and has produced a leaflet which explains to members of the public how to make a complaint and what will happen to it. A copy of this leaflet is available from reception areas in all departments. Basically, complaints should be referred to the Department concerned, where they will be investigated. If complainants are dissatisfied with the Department's response they can refer the matter to the Chief Executive, who will arrange for the issues to be investigated further. In difficult and complex cases the Chief Executive is able to take advice from a small number of leading Members who may be asked to give a final adjudication. Should the complainants still be dissatisfied with the outcome they can refer the matter to the Local Ombudsman.

The Council is required by law to establish a procedure for considering representations (including complaints) in relation to its social services functions. A leaflet outlining how this particular complaints procedure works is available from the Social Services Department.
