The Environment, Engineering & Transportation Department is headed by the Chief Environment, Engineering and Transportation Officer, Dr. Martin Courtis. The Department carries out a variety of widely differing functions, with the overall aim of protecting the health and well being of the community, promoting a fair and equitable trading environment, providing a comprehensive range of quality, cost effective services, and heads the Authority’s work on Local Agenda 21 issues.

The Department provide a range of functions as follows:-

1. Bereavement Services

Providing a prompt and caring service for the bereaved, offering burial, cremation, memorialisation and assistance whilst maintaining cemetery and crematoria buildings and grounds to a high standard.

2. Commercial

Officers of the division protect the public when they visit commercial premises to ensure they are safe, and when they buy food it is fit for human consumption. The investigation of communicable disease is an integral part of public health protection provided by the division.

3. Drainage

Managing and improving both Council and Severn Trent Water infrastructure to enhance and maintain the operation of highway drainage, watercourses and foul and surface water sewerage assets.

4. Environmental Protection

Controlling emissions to atmosphere and noise from industrial premises, carrying out reviews and assessments of air quality and commenting on development proposals from the Planning Department, control and remediation of contaminated land including the installation of landfill gas control measures at former landfill sites.

5. Civil Engineering

Highway Design

Providing a highway design and construction supervision service for the Authority to upgrade the Borough’s road infrastructure, providing a safer environment for the movement of goods and services.

Highway Structures

Maintaining Council owned bridges, retaining walls and safety fences within the Borough and administering the structural safety provision of the Building Act, 1984, together with the provision of structural advice within the Council.

Geotechnical

Providing geotechnical expertise to advise on all matters associated with good stability and contamination, designing and management of mineshafts and limestone infilling works.

6. Fleet Engineering & Road Safety

Managing the engineering functions of the Council’s vehicle fleet, ensuring compliance with statutory requirements. Providing a Road Safety and Driver and Operator Training Service.

7. Highway Maintenance

Repairing and maintaining highways and public rights of way, including winter maintenance, monitoring and inspection of Public Utilities street works and Ordnance Survey Mapping.

8. Highway Operations

A relatively diverse strategic service division which manages all the Borough’s Street Lighting and Illuminated Signs, Car Parks and Parking Control Services throughout the Borough, the School Crossing Patrol Service and Highway Adoptions.

9. Traffic

Identifying, developing and implementing local safety and other minor schemes, undertaking transportation studies and safety auditing functions together with advice in the routing of abnormal loads.

Forward Planning

Enhancement of transportation in the Borough through the procurement of funding in support of the Council’s Capital Highway programme; encouraging the rise of sustainable modes of transport through the protection of public right of way and the provision of cycle control together with engineering input to local land charges.

Traffic Management and Development

Safeguarding the interests of all users of the highway and assisting public transport through the management and improvement of the existing highway network and transportation input into land use planning control together with the call time of statistical information and the monitoring of H.G.V. licenses.

10. Trading Standards

Enforcing consumer protection legislation, including offering advice and guidance on the civil law to help consumers and traders, resolve their valid grievances about goods and services purchased or supplied in the Borough.

11. Waste Management

Providing waste management functions borough wide including waste collection, disposal, waste to energy, recycling, recovery, cleansing services plus related public health activities.
