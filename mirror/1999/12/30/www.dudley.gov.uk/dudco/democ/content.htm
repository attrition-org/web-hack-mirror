Register of Electors

Qualifying Date

The qualifying date for inclusion in the Register of Electors is 10th October each year and the Register based on this qualifying date is published on 15th February of the following year and remains in force for twelve months

How the Register is compiled

Registration forms are hand delivered to every household in the Borough in August. If no reply is received a reminder form is hand delivered in September. If there is still no response the canvasser goes out and knocks doors and attempts to get a form signed on the doorstep. If there is still nothing received from a household then a further form and letter pointing out the penalties is sent out in November.

How people can check if they are included in the Register of Electors

A draft Register is published on 28th November and is displayed at libraries, council offices and post offices until 16th December. If people check and find they are not included then they can make a claim to be added.

What happens when the Register is published

People can make a claim at any time providing that they are claiming for the address where they were living on 10th October of the previous year. However the claim procedure is not an instant process - and may take some time. Anyone not registered on election day CANNOT BE ALLOWED TO VOTE.

Election of Councillors

At present, one third of the Council is elected each year. 24 Councillors are elected on each occasion. Every fourth year there is no election.

Public Access to Council Meetings

Meetings of the full Council, Committees and Sub-Committees are open to the public although, by law, certain categories of report may be dealt with in private.

Public notice of the time and place of meetings is given on the notice board outside the Council House, Dudley. This is done three clear days at least before meetings, unless the meeting is convened at shorter notice. Meetings are normally held at the Council House, Dudley in the evening.

Copies of the agenda and reports for meetings are available for inspection three clear days before the meeting, as follows:

  • the agenda and reports for full Council Meetings (including the minutes of the last cycle of committee and sub-committee meetings) can be seen at most public libraries in the Borough
  • the agenda and reports for Committees and sub-committees can be seen at the Housing District Offices in Brierley Hill, Sedgley and Stourbridge, the Social Services District Offices in Brierley Hill, Halesowen and Sedgley, and at the Information Centre in Dudley Town Centre.
  • Documents for any of these meetings can also be seen at the Council House, Dudley and at the Environment, Engineering and Transportation Department, Council House, Mary Steven's Park, Stourbridge.
