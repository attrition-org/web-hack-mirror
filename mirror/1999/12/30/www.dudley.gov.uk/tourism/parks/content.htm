Putting People Back Into Parks

Introduction
Dudley Metropolitan Borough Council is located at the heart of the Black Country but if you think this describes our urban landscape, think again!

For the past 20 years we have been "turning the Black Country GREEN", our industrial landscape has been transformed into a network of interlinking green spaces. There is now a total of 543 Ha of parks and open spaces within the 30 square miles of the Borough sustaining a population of 312,235 people.

These range from the two historic parks of Himley and The Leasowes, through the many formal parks, play areas and recreation grounds to the network of open spaces nature reserves and countryside which create the unique character of Dudley.

If you want to play sport we offer a range of facilities : tennis, basketball, croquet, bowls, pitch & put and golf. If you are keen to take part in team activities why not hire one of our many football pitches? If you are a keen gardener or simply want to take a stroll in a beautiful landscape then visit the National plant collections and Arboretum at Stevens Park Wollescote.

For the younger park user there are equipped play areas in most of the major parks all of which have safety surfacing and contain an exciting range of equipment. In addition there are over 100 play areas throughout the borough. Over the past 10 years we have been carrying out a continuous programme of improvement and replacement of these facilities.

Whatever the weather or the season, what ever your age or interests Dudley parks offer something for everyone. We are always striving to improve our facilities to suit your needs and our aim is to Put People Back Into Parks
