The Government has recognised the need to keep the theme high on the agenda and to this end appointed a UK Anti-Drugs Co-ordinator to implement the New National Strategy, Tackling Drugs To Build A Better Britain.

The Dudley Borough Strategy will reflect the National Strategy and make it relevant to local needs. The new strategy has four elements:

  • Young people – to help young people resist drug misuse in order to achieve their full potential in society;
  • Communities – to protect our communities from drug-related anti-social and criminal behaviour;
  • Treatment – to enable people with drug problems to overcome them and live healthy and crime-free lives;
  • Availability – to stifle the availability of illegal drugs on our streets.

All organisations within the Borough, whether providing public services or employing members of the community, benefit from a focussed strategy.
Drug awareness raising can be done by consultation with the Dudley Borough Drug Action Team, Drug Reference Group and Training Team as well as the local service provider, The Warehouse (Dudley Drug Project). ‘Focus specific’ group-work with managers and front line staff working together is an effective tool for enabling a working strategy to be created. Multi-agency co-ordination and co-operation, both at national and local levels will be required in order to make systematic progress towards the above elements.
NATIONAL STATISTICS
  • In any one year, some 3 million people (6% of the population) take an illegal drug.
  • Population Surveys suggest that among school children, 3% of 12-13 year olds and 14% of 14-15 year olds admit to taking an illegal drug.
  • British Crime Survey suggested that 28% of 16-29 year olds had taken an illegal drug.
    (‘Tackling Drugs Together’ May 1995 HMSO)
  • More than a quarter of those who apply for help at clinics and drug centres are in jobs; some have held full-time jobs despite being on ‘hard’ drugs for 20 years or more.
    (Home Office Notice 178/1990)
LOCAL ACTION
  • Recognition of the need to address the issue of drug use/misuse, and its effects on the community is a high priority. To action change within the Borough a long-standing and effective system of collaboration between providers and purchasers has been used. Dudley now has a range of high quality services in place, enhanced by development work during 1996-1997.
  • Growth in this specific service area has been through collaboration between Dudley Health Authority, Dudley MBC and The Warehouse (Dudley Drug Project). A plan of action was mapped out using the Drug Advisory Service Report, and ‘Purchasing Effective Treatment and Care For Drug Users’ as well as locally commissioned research. Most of the recommendations laid out in these documents are being undertaken and further expansion of the services is planned for 1998-1999
  • Collaboration & Resources are the keys to effective working. This means that we need to be more open & creative with the resources available, formulate better planing ideas to include new partners who have resources yet untapped by community investment.
