National Non-Domestic Rates (Business Rates)

 

The non-domestic rates collected by billing authorities are paid into a central pool and redistributed to billing authorities and major precepting authorities.

Dudley's share of redistributed rate income, together with income from its council taxpayers, revenue support grant provided by the government and certain other sums, is used to pay for the services provided.

Rateable Value

The rateable value of non-domestic property is fixed in most cases by an independent valuation officer of the Valuation Office Agency.

All non-domestic property is revalued every five years. The rateable value of a property is based on its open market rental value. The 1995 revaluation is based on rental values as at 1st April 1993.

The values of all property in respect of which rates are payable to Dudley are shown in the local rating list, a copy of which may be inspected at:
The Valuation Office Agency Crown House Bull Ring Kidderminster Worcestershire DY10 2AA Telephone 01562 620100 and Council Offices, Priory Street, Dudley.

The valuation officer may alter the value if he believes that the circumstances of the property have changed. The ratepayer (and certain others who have an interest in the property) may also in certain circumstances propose a change in value.
If in any case the ratepayer and the valuation officer do not agree, the matter will be referred as an appeal to a valuation tribunal.
Information about the circumstances in which a change in a rateable value may be proposed and how such a proposal may be made is available from the valuation office shown above.

National Non-Domestic Rating Multiplier

The national non-domestic rating multiplier is the rate in the pound by which the rateable value is multiplied to produce the annual rate bill for a property. It is set annually by the government and except in a revaluation year cannot by law rise by more than the amount of the increase in the retail prices index.

Transitional Arrangements

Transitional arrangements will phase in the effect of significant changes in liability which arise from the 1995 revaluation of non-domestic property. Where appropriate, these arrangements will operate until March 2000 (when all non-domestic property will be revalued again).
There are limits on the percentage by which bills may increase or decrease each year.

Special rules deal with changes in rateable values and the merger or splitting of existing properties.
Further information about transitional arrangements may be obtained from the Business Rates Section, Council Offices, Priory Street, Dudley, telephone 01384 814957 or by E-mail to: revenue.finance@mbc.dudley.gov.uk

Relief for Small Non-Domestic Properties

Where the transitional arrangements do not apply and subject to any changes in the circumstances of the property, properties with a rateable value of less than £10,000 have had their 1997/98 bills frozen at the same level as for 1996/97.
In 1998/99 and 1999/2000 their rate bills will be lower by approximately 2%, than they would have been if calculated by reference to the National Non-Domestic Rating Multiplier for each year.

Unoccupied Property Rating

Non-domestic properties which are unoccupied may be liable to empty property rates.
Rates are charged at 50% of the full rate bill or of the transitional bill where transitional arrangements apply.
Liability begins after the property has been empty for three months.
Certain types of property, e.g. factories and warehouses, are exempt from empty property rates.

Charitable and Discretionary Relief

Charities are entitled to relief from rates on any non-domestic property which is wholly or mainly used for charitable purposes.
Relief is given at 80% of the full rate bill or of the transitional bill where transitional arrangements apply.
The Council has discretion to remit all or part of the remaining 20% of a charity's bill on such property and also has discretion to remit all or part of any rate bill in respect of property occupied by certain bodies not established or conducted for profit.

Direct Debits

Compared to other payment methods, monthly direct debiting offers you the following advantages:

IT SAVES YOU TIME - no more queuing to pay, no more cheques to write!

IT SAVES YOU MONEY - No more stamps to buy or envelopes to find!

LOWER BANK CHARGES FOR YOU - If applicable, bank charges for direct debit payments may be lower than the charges for other payment methods.

FULL GUARANTEE AGAINST ERRORS - Your bank/building society will make a full refund if a mistake is ever made. See the Direct Debit Guarantee below.

In order to pay non-domestic rates in monthly instalments by direct debit, please complete and return the instruction on the bottom of your bill or contact the Council as soon as possible, asking for a direct debit instruction.

You will be notified of the amounts to be debited at least 14 days before the first payment date, normally on or about the 1st of each month.

If you have any queries about the direct debit scheme, please telephone 01384 814882.

The Direct Debit Guarantee

This guarantee is offered by all the banks and building societies that take part in the direct debit scheme. The efficiency and security of the scheme is monitored and protected by your own bank/building society.

If the amounts to be paid or the payment dates change, you will be told of this in advance by at least 14 days as agreed.

If an error is made by Dudley MBC or your bank or building society, you are guaranteed a full and immediate refund from your branch.

You can cancel a direct debit at any time by writing to your bank/building society. Please also send a copy of your letter to us.

This guarantee is offered by all the banks and building societies that take part in the direct debit scheme.
