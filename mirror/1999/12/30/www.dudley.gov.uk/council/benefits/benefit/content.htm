Who are we?

We are a Division of the Finance Department of Dudley MBC and as such are responsible to the Senior Assistant Chief Finance Officer and the Chief Finance Officer. The Head of Benefit Services, Ken Skelding is in charge of the day to day management of the Division.

Our Benefit Service has been shortlisted along with eight other Councils for Beacon status. We have received a visit from DETR, DSS and IDEA officials which gave us the opportunity to demonstrate our best practice and how we plan to share it with other Councils. A further presentation was made to an advisory panel in London on 29th October 1999.

Ministers will make a final decision on which Councils are to be awarded Beacon status based on the advice of the advisory panel. The announcement for first year Beacon Councils is expected towards the end of November.

What Services do we deliver?

Our main function is to administer claims for Housing Benefit and Council Tax Benefit. These are means tested benefits (dependant on someone’s income and circumstances) regulated by the Department of Social Security. This gives assistance with domestic rent and council tax payments to customers living in the Dudley Borough who need it. We calculate all claims at a central office based in the council house in Dudley.

We have a total of 95 staff (88.2 Full time equivalents) who deliver the following services:-

  • Housing Benefit administration for both Private Sector and Council Tenants on low income.
  • Council Tax Benefit administration for customers on low income.
  • Benefit Fraud Investigations
  • Home Visits where needed to assist/advise customers on Benefit issues.
  • A Benefits Shop giving free and confidential advice on all aspects of Social Security Benefits.
  • Free School Meals administration for children attending school in the Dudley Borough whose parents receive Income Support or Job Seekers Allowance ( Income Based).

How much does this cost?

Housing and Council Tax Benefit paid out by us for the financial year 1997/98 was in excess of 57 million pounds in respect of 32,000 claimants. A percentage of this money paid out by the Council is reimbursed by Central Government.

In 1997/98 we delivered this service at a total cost per case of £51.47, compared to an average of £67.12 for other similar West Midland Authorities and £58.36 for similar Metropolitan Authorities.

Who can claim Housing and/or Council Tax Benefit?

You can make a claim if you or your partner:-

  • Are working or do not work.
  • Pay rent to Dudley Council or a private landlord.
  • Pay Council Tax to Dudley Council.
  • Are sick or disabled.
  • Have less than £16,000 (Combined ) savings.
  • Have non-dependants on low incomes (Council Tax Benefit only)

If you are considering making a claim for Housing Benefit and /or Council Tax Benefit then this should be done as soon as possible. If you are entitled to benefit it would normally start from the Monday following the date your completed form is received by us, but can be backdated for up to 52 weeks in exceptional circumstances.
