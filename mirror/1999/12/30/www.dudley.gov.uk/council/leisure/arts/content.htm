THE ARTS & HALLS SECTION

The Arts & Halls Section promotes and organises professional performing arts events, manages the Borough's civic halls and carries out arts development work in conjunction with other Departments and agencies. We welcome comments and suggestions from the general public on the services we offer, particularly in relation to concerts and events you have especially enjoyed, performers you would like to see in Dudley and any improvements we could make in the facilities we offer.

ARTS AND ENTERTAINMENT EVENTS

The Section promotes a programme of professional performing arts events, known as Dudley Winter Season, which runs from October to March each year. Our current Season features performances by Joe Pasquale, Helen Shapiro, Jethro, CBSO, Voulez Vous, and many more. Further details of the 1999/2000 Season, including how to book, can be viewed on the Dudley Winter Entertainment Season Live! 1999-2000 web site.

Arts Officers assist with the organisation of Dudley Arts Council, the organisation which represents the amateur arts societies in the Borough. Many of these groups stage shows, concerts, exhibitions, pantomimes and other events. Dudley Arts Council produces a quarterly Arts Listings brochure giving details of these. This information can be viewed on the Dudley Winter Entertainment Season Live! 1999-2000 web site.

HALLS

The Arts Section operates the five civic halls in the Borough; Dudley Town Hall, Stourbridge Town Hall, Netherton Arts Centre, Brierley Hill Civic Hall and Cornbow Hall.

The Civic Halls are hired out to a huge number of organisations for various events, including concerts, tea dances, ballroom dances, exhibitions, meetings, tutorials, prayer groups, slimming clubs, dance classes and more. You can find out what's going on at the Borough's Halls in the Dudley Winter Entertainment Season Live! 1999-2000.

If you are interested in booking accommodation in one of our Halls for your event, please contact the appropriate Manager for details of availability and cost. Halls Managers' names and phone numbers can be found above in the list of contacts.

ARTS DEVELOPMENT

Arts Officers are involved in a wide range of arts development projects, providing arts activities for members of the community.  Dudley MBC subscribes to West Midlands Arts, and is in regular contact with WMA Officers.

The Inter-Departmental Arts Team meets regularly, with members from Education, Social Services, Community and Lifelong Learning and the Museums/Art Gallery. This group jointly produced the Borough Arts Strategy, setting goals for artistic activity within Dudley.

The Arts Forum is a group made up of Councillors and representatives from Council Departments and various arts & community organisations. It meets to discuss various issues concerning the arts. Past meetings have focussed on the arts and healthcare, the amateur arts and public art.

Arts Officers have been involved in successful drama and dance projects. Black Country Touring is an organisation initiated by Arts Officers from the Black Country boroughs. It offers small scale touring drama productions for all kinds of venues in the Black Country. The Dudley Dance Consortium is a collaboration between Arts Officers from Planning and Leisure and the Community and Lifelong Learning Unit. A successful lottery bid means that a Dance Artist is due to be appointed, to initiate dance work with communities in Dudley. Open Stage is a drama group for young people with learning disabilities. Arts Officers were involved in devising this successful group, together with colleagues from CLLU, Priority Health, Social Services and Education.

Contact the Arts Officers for more information about Arts Development work.
