Internet Access

Internet access is currently provided FREE to the public at the following Libraries;

Brierley Hill		 4 access points
Dudley 6 access points
(including one in the Childrens Library)
Halesowen 5 access points
Stourbridge 5 access points

and has been expanded to single access points at Coseley, Kingswinford, Lye, Long Lane, Sedgley and Wordsley Libraries.

Each access point allows web browsing only, with the option to print pages (first sheet free, all others after at 10p a sheet). There is at present no facility to download information onto disc or have access to PC based email.


Use of the access points is subject to the following policy;

Libraries Internet Access Policy

  1. Internet access at libraries in Dudley is free of charge during normal library opening hours.
  2. Access is available to all library members and to 'casual' users, primarily visitors to the Borough.
  3. Adult library members (aged 14 and over) are issued with 'Internet Sessions' using their library ticket, and this must be presented at the time of issue.
  4. Junior library members (aged under 14) must have written permission from a parent or guardian. The necessary forms must be signed in the library at the time of first use. Subsequently sessions are issued as in 3 above.
  5. Casual users/visitors must produce and surrender adequate ID (passport. driving licence, ID card, etc) and then they can be issued with a session using a temporary user card.
  6. Sessions are issued in hourly blocks and may be booked for a maximum of 2 consecutive sessions.
  7. Sessions can be booked in advance but no further than 1 week ahead.
  8. Library members who are defaulted in respect of other library services will be prevented from using the Internet, and any outstanding fines (etc) should be cleared before access is allowed.
  9. Users interfering with either the software, hardware or general operation of the PCs will be prevented from using them and may also be prevented from using all other library services.
  10. Access to illegal sites and any illegal activity carried out in conjunction with such access are strictly forbidden and will be reported to the appropriate authorities. Users should be aware that Internet access from the libraries is filtered and that random checks may be made on sites visited.
  11. No more than 2 people may use a PC at any one time.
