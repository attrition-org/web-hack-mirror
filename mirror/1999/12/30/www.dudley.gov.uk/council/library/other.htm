Library Services

Children’s services

As well as having children’s books available for loan, all libraries have a range of children’s reference books and other material to help with homework.

There are regular storytimes for under 5s at some libraries and other activities such as quizzes, competitions and author visits are organised throughout the year. Look out for special events and activities during the school holidays.

In the larger libraries, a specialist children’s librarian is available to offer help and advice on children’s reading.

 

  Reference

The main reference collections are held at Dudley, Stourbridge, Halesowen and Brierley Hill. At these libraries specialist staff are available to cater for your information needs.

All other libraries have at least a basic ‘quick reference’ section to answer straightforward enquiries and to help with homework.

Most libraries have a collection of material relating to their local community but the most comprehensive collection of local history material is at Dudley Archives and Local History Service in Coseley.

 

Other Languages

There are in some of Dudley Libraries books in other languages. Dudley, Halesowen, Long Lane, Lye and Stourbridge have collections of Asian language books in Punjabi, Urdu, Hindi and Gudjerati. While there are French, German, Italian and Spanish novels in Brierley Hill, Dudley, Halesowen, and Stourbridge libraries.

 

Open Learning

 

Publications

Libraries sell a range of publications, postcards and souvenirs relating to Dudley and the Black Country.

The largest selection can be found at Dudley Information Centre and at Stourbridge Library.

For examples of these publications see Dudley Archives and Local History Publications.

 

Books on wheels

People who are housebound may qualify for this book delivery service. Operated from Brierley Hill Library using 3 vans the service visits some 400 housebound readers throughout the Dudley Borough, delivering books and other library materials.

There is currently a waiting list for the service, further details can be obtained on 01384 812870

 

Ticket sales

Stourbridge and Halesowen Libraries, together with the Information Centre in Dudley sell tickets for concerts and other events in the Dudley Borough, ranging from classical concerts to ghost walks. Tickets can also be purchased by phone on 01384 812812.

 

Room hire

Meeting rooms of varying capacities are available for hire at a number of libraries. Hire rates are very reasonable and the rooms are often available in the evenings as well as during the day.

 

Internet Access

 

Online Public Access Catalogues (OPAC)

Seven libraries have public access terminals, allowing users to check their own loans, use the library catalogue and enquire on the Community Information Database.

 

Visitor information

Stourbridge Library and the Information Centre in Dudley are both Tourist Information Centres and have a wide selection of material of interest to visitors. Also, all libraries have some leaflets on local attractions as well as Borough Guides and street plans.

 

  Photocopying

Photocopying is charged at 10p per A4 sheet and 15p per A3 sheet. The copying of documents is subject to copyright rules and regulations;

The copy must be required by the user for research or private study
Only one copy of a periodical or part of a book can be made
Copies of complete periodicals, or complete books, or the whole of a copyright document cannot be made
Only one article from an individual issue of a periodical can  be copied
Ordnance Survey maps can only be copied in accordance with the Ordnance Survey guidelines

 

Fax Service

Fax messages can be sent for the charge of £2.00 for the first sheet and £1.00 for each following sheet.
