Lye Business Centre

Lye Business Centre situated off the Hayes Lane in Lye is a purpose built site designed to offer access to business information, advice and accommodation to new and existing businesses.

Information and Advice

The managed site offers a central administrative service where information and advice can be provided on:

  • starting a new business
  • small business development
  • grant and loan availability

The centre also offers:

  • confidential typing and word processing facilities
  • fax and photocopying services
  • conference facilities for up to 50 people
  • private office space for short meetings and interviews
  • access to information on other industrial and commercial workspace available in Dudley Borough.

Accommodation

Lye Business Centre comprises 31 self-contained business units, with planning permission for light industrial use ranging in size from 51.09 sq. metres to 92.90 sq. metres (550 sq. ft to 1000 sq. ft.). The units are connected to all mains services with metered water, gas and electricity. They are available on a monthly licence and provide workshop space for new business and project expansion.

Youth Business Advisory Service

Dudley MBC’s Youth Business advisory Service provides business advice, support and counselling for young people aged between 18-30 who wish to start or develop their own businesses and who are residents of the borough.

The service also hosts the local office of the Prince’s Youth Business Trust (PYBT) a national charity, patron H.R.H. The Prince of Wales which provides finance, professional advice, training and marketing opportunities for 18-30 year olds who are setting up and running their own businesses.

What Assistance is available?

i) Business Counselling - assistance with the compilation of a business plan.

ii) Financial Assistance:

  1. Dudley MBC - interest free loans over 2 years up to a maximum of £1000.
  2. The Prince’s Youth Business Trust
    • test marketing grants of up to £250 to investigate market potential.
    • low interest loans of up to £5000.
    • grants of up to £1500 (depending on personal circumstances).

iii) Training - basic induction training in book-keeping, tax, national insurance and day-to-day management.

iv) Aftercare - access to a personal volunteer business adviser. Legal advice is also available.

v) Exhibitions - local events such as trade fairs at the Merry Hill Centre and subsidised stands at national events such as the International Autumn Fair at the NEC, Birmingham.

Who is Eligible?

i) All applicants must be 30 years or under and have a business idea which has a reasonable chance of success. If help is required to expand the business, applicants must not have been trading for longer than 12 months.

ii) The Council and PYB are not providers of first resort funding and will not, normally, support projects which can obtain all their finances from commercial or other sources.

iii) Applicants will be expected to submit a business plan in respect of a proposed new business.

iv) Applicants will normally be unemployed, underemployed or of limited means.
