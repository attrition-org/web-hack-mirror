Aims of the Department

Whilst the Department concerns itself with all aspects of social care, the main aim is to arrange and provide support and care services for those people and their carers who, for various reasons, are vulnerable or at risk. In doing this, the objective is to ensure that people in Dudley receive the best services which can be provided within available resources. This includes services for:

  • Older people
  • Children in need, including children with disabilities, children in trouble with the law, or children at risk of abuse or neglect
  • People with mental health problems
  • People with a physical or sensory impairment
  • People with a learning disablility

Working Collaboratively


The Department has a diverse range of responsibilities and services, but there is a general commitment to ensuring that all aspects of the service are provided in a coordinated way, and in partnership with users of services and their carers, other council departments and other agencies.

How the Service is Delivered

Broadly, the Department is divided into four interdependent working Divisions. These are:

  1. Adult Care Division, which is responsible for assessing the help adults and their carers may need, by deciding if they are eligible for a service, making arrangements for the appropriate services to be purchased from the public or private sectors, and reviewing how successfully the services have been in meeting individual's needs.
    The Division is also responsible for planning and developing services for adults who are older, have mental health problems, have physical or sensory impairment or learning disabilities, by producing planning documents in collaboration with other agencies, and commissioning new services to meet changing needs.

  2. Children and Families Division, which is responsible for assessing the help needed for children and their families, including Child Protection and Youth Justice, by deciding if a child "is in need", making arrangements to provide services appropriate to meet those needs, and finally, to review over time how successfully the services have been in meeting needs.
    The Division is also responsible for planning and developing children's and families services, by producing planning documents in collaboration with other agencies, and commissioning new services to meet changing needs.

  3. Direct Care Services, which is responsible for the managment and delivery of the Department's in-house service provision for both adults and children and families. This covers 46 teams and establishments including residential homes, day centres, SECs, home care, transport, delivery of Meals on Wheels, respite care, family placements, specialist services for children with disabilities, young people leaving care, family support and education support.
    The Division aims to meet the needs of individual service users reliably and promptly and to be flexible and responsive to changing needs and the requirements of the Adult Care and Children and Families Divisions. Services are developed in collaboration with these Divisions and other agencies through a three year business plan and service level agreements for particular services.

  4. Business Services, which is responsible for providing support to practitioner and managerial staff throughout the Department to ensure high quality services are delivered. The Division provides the following: personnel services; training and staff counselling facilities; management of the capital programme, health and safety practices and accommodation issues; financial services, managerial information and public information service; interpreting and translation service: and the resources for developing information systems and strategies.
    As well as the above, the Division carries departmental lead for corporate policy development, coordinating the response to national regulatory and statutory requirements, taking forward departmental initiatives such as equal opportunities and departmental standards of service, and coordinating cross-divisional initiatives.

In addition, the Inspection and Quality Audit Unit works at arms length from the main body of the Department. It takes responsibility for the registration and inspection of private and voluntary residential care homes and inspects Local Authority residential homes.

The Unit also registers and inspects independent day care and services for children including childminders, play groups and day nurseries.

Additionally, the Unit has responsibility for managing the Department's independent comments, compliments and complaints service.
