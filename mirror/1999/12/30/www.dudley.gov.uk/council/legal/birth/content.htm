Registering a Birth

IMPORTANT

ALL REGISTRATIONS are by PRIOR APPOINTMENT
Please telephone the appropriate office listed on the back of this leaflet to make an appointment

When can I register baby’s birth?

Your baby’s birth must be registered within 42 days of the date of birth. You should allow about 30 minutes for the registration.

Where can I register the birth?

The birth will be registered in the Registration District in which the birth occurred (if your baby was born at Wordsley hospital this will be Stourbridge Register Office). If it is not convenient for you to visit the Register Office for the district you can go to any other Register Office in England or Wales to make a declaration of the particulars required for the registration. In that case, any birth certificates you require and the Form FP58 issued to enable you to register your baby with a doctor will be sent to you by post.

The addresses and telephone numbers of the Dudley M.B.C. Register Offices are listed on the back of this leaflet. To prevent unnecessary waiting, these offices use an appointment system. Please telephone if you would like to make one.

Who can register the birth

It is usual for the baby’s mother to register the birth. If, however, the baby’s mother is married to the baby’s father at the time of the birth it is equally possible for him to register.

If the baby’s mother and father are not married, the father’s details can be entered into the register if both parents attend together to register the birth. If that is not possible, please telephone a Register Office for advice.

What information will the Registrar need?
Baby

The date and place of baby’s birth. If the baby is a twin, triplet etc., the time of each baby’s birth will be needed.
Whether the baby is a boy or a girl.
The forename(s) and surname in which the baby will be brought up.
Father

(If details are to be entered in the register)
Forenames and surname.
Date and place of birth.
Occupation at the time of baby’s birth or, if not employed, the occupation last followed.
Mother

Forename(s) and any surnames which have been used before/since marriage.
Date and place of birth.
Address.
Occupation. If the mother has been working at any time prior to the birth her occupation can be entered into the register if required.
Date of marriage if applicable.
The number of previous children by the present and any former husband.

It is important that the information recorded in the register is correct. If any mistake is made it will put you to some trouble to have it corrected. You should, therefore, check the entry in the register very carefully before you sign.

Certificates

After registration you will be given a short birth certificate free of charge. Further copies of short and full certificates can be obtained for a fee at the time of registration. Please note that if you require further certificates at a later date the price may vary, your local office will be able to advise you of the appropriate fee. You will also receive a Form FP58 for your doctor.

Facilities

Both offices have wheelchair access and accessible toilets. Stourbridge office has baby changing facilities.

Where to get further advice

If you are still uncertain of how to proceed with registering the baby’s birth, please telephone or call in at your local Register Office. The addresses and telephone numbers are listed on the back of this leaflet.

Please remember you have a legal duty to register the birth within 42 days.
