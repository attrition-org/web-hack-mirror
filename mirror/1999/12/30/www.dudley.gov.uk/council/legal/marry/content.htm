Getting Married

Introduction

This acts as a guide to the facilities and choices available to you, and to the formalities, which are necessary.

Within the Borough we can offer you the services of two very different register offices, one located in the 19th century Priory Hall and the other a modern, bright, purpose built marriage suite. The staff can offer you various enhancements to your ceremony. Descriptions of their facilities and the suggested enhancements are described here.

Dudley Council is committed to a high standard of service and customer care. We pride ourselves on excellent facilities in our register offices and a high degree of expertise from our staff, to ensure that your wedding will be a joyful and memorable occasion for you, your family and friends.

Marriage Ceremonies are performed at Dudley and Stourbridge Register Offices on Mondays to Fridays from 10am to 12 noon and 2pm to 4pm. On Saturdays, marriages take place from 10am to 12.30pm.

Out of respect for all marriage parties who use our facilities, guests are asked not to smoke in the register office, and to wait until they are outside the building before throwing confetti or rice.

Dudley District Register Office

The Register Office in Dudley is situated in Priory Hall in Priory Park, Dudley. The Park is a public park owned by the Council.

The Priory Room has dark oak panelling and a large original fireplace, it will seat up to 75 guests, it also has a hearing induction loop. The alternative, Castle Room, is smaller and seats 25 guests.

There is a side entrance for wheelchair access and there are toilet facilities for people with disabilities. Priory Park is an ideal photographic backcloth for formal wedding photographs.

We are able to offer enhancements to your marriage ceremony. A booklet has been produced which has a range of selected verse that may be read by one of your guests during the ceremony. Please ask the Superintendent for a copy.

Please feel free to discuss with the Superintendent your special requirements regarding flowers, video recording, photographs and additional words used during the ceremony.

Stourbridge District Register Office

The Register Office in Stourbridge is in a new, modern purpose built office in Crown Lane, Stourbridge. The marriage suite is on the ground floor with a main entrance suitable for wheelchair and/or pushchair access. There are also toilet facilities for people with disabilities, and a hearing induction loop in the marriage room for people with hearing difficulties.

Background music is played before and after the marriage ceremonies in the marriage waiting area and marriage room. The Crown Suite, which is used only by marriage parties is furnished and decorated to a very high standard. There is a planted area outside suitable for photographs. There is also an attractive park located nearby. A parking space for the wedding car is located outside the entrance to the Marriage Suite. Public car parking for guests is available in the Crown Centre Car Park.

We have a choice of two ceremonies. You may choose between these. When you come to the office we can show you the choice of words. We work on the principle that "it's your wedding, not ours". Whatever you choose you can be sure that our staff will help make a success of your wedding as we have confidence and pride in our service. We want to make a celebration of your marriage, not just solemnise your civil marriage contract.

If you have a reading that you would like one of your guests to read, this is welcomed and encouraged, providing it has no religious content or connection. Alternatively, staff would be willing to read for you if you wish. We can also show you a selection of readings if you do not have a favourite of your own.

The Marriage Ceremony

You will no doubt want to know what happens when you attend for your marriage. Regardless of whether you choose to have any enhancements the marriage ceremony will have the following content at either office:

When your party is assembled the Registrar who is registering your marriage will see the two of your privately, to check the particulars which are to be entered in the Marriage Register. At this time the Registrar will also obtain the fee from you. Meanwhile the guests are asked to wait in the marriage reception area and, when requested, enter the Marriage Suite.

The Superintendent Registrar will begin by reminding you of the solemn and binding nature of the Marriage Ceremony.

Each of you, in turn, will be asked to declare that you do not know of any lawful reason to prevent your marriage, repeating the following words after the Superintendent Registrar:

"I do solemnly declare that I know not of any lawful impediment why I . . . (your full name) may not be joined in matrimony to . . ." (full name of your fiancé/ee).

If a ring is to be given, or rings exchanged, this will then take place.

Then each of you in turn, will be asked to speak the words by which the contract of marriage is made, repeating the following after the Superintendent Registrar:

"I call upon these persons here present to witness that I . . . do take thee . . . to be my lawful wedded wife/husband".

Who will then say:

You have both made the declarations prescribed by law and have made a solemn and binding contract. You are now husband and wife together.

After the ceremony the Registrar will make the entry in the register and ask you both to check its correctness. You and your witnesses then sign the register, the bride signing in the surname she has been known by immediately before the marriage. The witnesses must be able to understand the marriage ceremony.

The Superintendent Registrar and Registrar will then sign the register and the marriage certificate will be handed to the bride.

Legal Requirements to Marry in a Register Office

Before you can marry in a register office in England or Wales, you must give notice of your intention to marry. You can give notice in two ways, depending on how quickly you want to be married and how long you have lived at your home address. You cannot give more than three months notice to get married. The notice is a legal statement that must be signed by you or the person you are marrying. You cannot ask a relative or friend to give the notice for you.

You may choose to marry in any register office in England or Wales even though you may not be resident in your chosen district. Most register offices will now accept a provisional booking up to 12 months in advance of your wedding date.

To arrange a provisional booking, you should contact the office of your choice, but you MUST still give notice of marrying in the district(s) where you live. This must be given no more than three months in advance of your wedding.

There are two different options for giving your notice. By 'Certificate without Licence' or 'Certificate and Licence'.

Certificate without Licence

The following conditions apply if you give notice by 'Certificate without Licence'.

You must both have lived in any district in England or Wales for the seven days before the date you give notice of your marriage. It does not matter if either of you moves to a different address after the notice has been given.

If you live in a different district to the person you are marrying, you can marry in the register office for either district. Or you can marry in any other register office in England or Wales. If you choose to marry in another register office, you must give notice of your marriage in the districts where you both live. But first, you must contact the district where you want the marriage to take place.

Certificate and Licence

This is a more expensive option, but couples often choose it as there is less travelling around between register offices. The following conditions apply if you give notice by 'Certificate and Licence'.

You and the person you are marrying must have lived in England or Wales for the 15 days before the date you give the notice. The other person must be in England or Wales on the day the notice is given.

The marriage can either take place in the office where you give notice or in any other register office of your choice. It does not matter if either of you moves to another address after the notice has been given.

With this type of notice, you only have to wait one working day before the marriage can take place. If you are not getting married in the district where you gave notice, you can collect the authority for the marriage (the licence) from there after one working day and take it to the other district.

You only have to give one notice if you get married by 'Certificate and Licence'. So it doesn't matter if you live in a different district from the person you are marrying. Also, this notice is not put up for display to the public.

A fee will be charged for giving notice and for the issue of your licence. Please telephone for details.

Getting Married in a Church or Other Religious Building

If you are getting married in any religious building, apart from a church belonging to the Church of England, you must still give notice as if you were getting married in the register office for the district where you live.

You can get married by 'Certificate without Licence' or by 'Certificate and Licence'. You can only get married in a church in a different district to where you both live if either of you usually go to worship in that building. You may also get married in another district if there is no building of your religion in the district where you or the person you are marrying lives. Our staff will be glad to give you help and advice on this matter.

A registrar from the district covering the building must be present at the wedding unless the governing body of the religious building has someone approved to register the marriage instead of the registrar.

Civil Marriages in Other Approved Premises

Civil marriages are also allowed to take place in other buildings, apart from a register office, which have been approved by local authorities as a suitable venue for civil marriages. Marriages are not allowed to take place outdoors or in temporary or moveable structures such as marquees or boats.

To arrange a wedding at an approved venue, you must first contact the venue to make provisional arrangements. Then you must contact the register office of the district in which the venue is located to ensure that Registration Officers can attend your wedding on the date and time of your choice.

You will still have to give notice in the district or districts where you live and you will have to give your Marriage Authority or Authorities to the register office in the district where you are getting married.

The fees for getting married in licensed venues are set by each local authority and will not be the same throughout the country.

If you want further advice or wish to view the lists of buildings approved for civil marriages, please contact Stourbridge Register Office (Tel: 01384 815385)

Documents We Need to See

If either of you has been married before, either in this country or abroad, you will need to show proof of your divorce.

If you were divorced in England or Wales, we will need to see a court stamped copy of the decree absolute (the final divorce paper). If the divorce took place in a foreign country, we will need to see the original document issued by that country.

If either of you is a widow or widower we will need to see of certified copy of your partner's death certificate from the registration authorities of the country where your partner died.

If you have married each other in a foreign country you only need to get married here if you think that the marriage was not legal. If you decide to get married here we will need to see any documents issued by that country when you give us notice of your marriage.

If either of you is under 18 we will need to see proof that your parents or guardian agree to the marriage. If you parents are divorced we may also need to see the court order regarding custody. The Superintendent Registrar will give you advice on this matter.
