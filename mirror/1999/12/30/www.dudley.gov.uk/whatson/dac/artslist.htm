DUDLEY
ARTS COUNCIL


A diary of concerts and arts events in Dudley, Halesowen and
Stourbridge during September to December 1999.
The events listed here are promoted by societies and organisations which are members of
Dudley Arts Council and give an insight into wealth of enjoyment
on the amateur arts scene.

For more information about these events, please contact the name given under each listing.

Thursday 2nd September - 7.30pm
The Dudley Recorded Music Society meets on a fortnightly basis for programmes of RECORDED CLASSICAL MUSIC.  The sessions on alternate Thursdays until December 9th, take place in the Parish Centre, St Francis Church, Laurel Road, Dudley.  Current programmes are available from Dudley Information Centre or Dudley Library.  There is a fee of £1.50 per session.  Please note there is no session on September 16th.
Details from Barbara Petty 01384 252634
Wednesday 15th  - Saturday 18th September - 7.30pm
The Community Players perform THRILLER OF THE YEAR by Glyn Jones, a tense and intriguing thriller with an all female cast.   The play which has and unexpected and surprising conclusion is at Hawbush Community Centre, Brettell Lane, Brierley Hill. 
Tickets £3.00, children £2.00.
Details from Mike Harris 01384 295689
Saturday 18th September - 7.30pm
The Friends of Music at St Thomas's present a SONG RECITAL with Michael Downing (Bass) at St Thomas's Church, Stourbridge.  Michael is a well known singing teacher and performer and this recital includes works by Bach, Handel, Head and Poulenc.  Tickets £5.00 not available in advance.
Details from John Savage 01384 372419
Monday 27th September - 7.30pm
Kingswinford Ladies Singers are bringing to a close the four day Flower Festival to celebrate the 150th Anniversary of St Mark's Church, Pensnett in a MUSICAL EVENING which also features Viddlers Three.
Tickets £4.00 from John Lodge 01384 78681.
Tuesday 28th September - Saturday 2nd October - 7.30pm
Quarry Bank Amateur Operatic Society perform the ever popular Rodgers and Hammerstein musical OKLAHOMA!  at Netherton Arts Centre.  Songs include 'Oh, what a beautiful morning' and 'The Surrey with the Fringe on Top'.  Tickets Tue-Fri £6.00, Sat £7.00, concessions £5.50 (Tue/Wed only).
Tickets from Claire Hayward 0121 561 5098
Friday 1st October - 1.00pm
LUNCHTIME ORGAN RECITAL presented by the Friends of Music at St Thomas's at St Thomas's Church, Stourbridge.  Bring a packed lunch and spend your lunch hour at this organ recital given by Andrew Fletcher - organist at St Thomas's and regular recitalist in the UK and USA.  This is the first concert in the 1999 series entitled 'Fletchers Favourites'.  Free with a retiring collection.
Details from John Savage 01384 372419
Friday 1st October - 7.30pm
The Greenfield Singers and the Ad Hoc Jazz Group perform in a two part concert entitled GREENFIELD AND ALL THAT JAZZ! The venue is The Music Suite, RC High School, Brake Lane, Hagley and all the proceeds are for Mary Stevens Hospice.  Tickets £3.50 to include refreshments.
Tickets from Dorothy Clarke 01562 886642
Saturday 2nd October - 10.30am
You are invited to come and participate in a CHORAL WORKSHOP with Halesowen Choral Society at Graingers Lane Methodist Church, Cradley Heath, where the pieces worked on will be John Rutter's 'Requiem' and five traditional songs.  Refreshments will be available and there will be an organ recital during the lunch period.  Participation fees £8.00 plus £1 to hire the music.
Details from Mavis Morris 0121 501 1803
Tuesday 5th October - Saturday 9th October - 7.30pm
If you are a fan of Big Band music then Brierley Hill Civic Hall is the place to be.  Brierley Hill Amateur Operatic Society is performing SOME LIKE IT HOT, the famous film of which starred Jack Lemmon, Tony Curtis and Marilyn Monroe.  Tickets £7.00, £6.00 (Tue), £8.00, £7.00 (Wed to Sat).
Tickets available from Hazel Millward 01384 273942
Wednesday 6th October - Saturday 9th October - 7.30pm
Wordsley Amateur Dramatic Society has chosen RAIN by Somerset Maugham as its 75th Anniversary production.  The famous story of Sadie Thompson and the missionary who tries to save her soul with shocking consequences takes place at Stream Road Methodist Church, Kingswinford.  Tickets £3.50, concessions £3.00.
Tickets from Jean Cotton 01384 273026
Saturday 16th October - 7.30pm
'OPERA FOR YOU' is Viva Musica's latest offering.  The concert at Dudley Town Hall includes a selection of the most famous arias and choruses from some of the world's greatest operas.  Tickets from £5.00 to £8.00.
Tickets from Dudley Information Centre 01384 812812 or Peter Crofts 0121 550 5581
Sunday 17th - Saturday 23rd October - Times vary
Stourbridge Town Hall plays host to Stourbridge Amateur Operatic Society's Midland amateur premiere of FAME - THE MUSICAL.   The exciting adaptation of the television show about the high school of performing arts is straight from the West End.  Tickets from £4.50 to £9.00.
Tickets from Lorraine Lovett 01384 277398
Monday 18th October - 7.30pm
The Greenfield Singers present their AUTUMN CONCERT at Enville Street Methodist Church, Stourbridge.  The programme provides an evening of light classical, modern and show songs with guest soloists.  All proceeds are for Dudley Cancer Support Group.  Tickets £2.50 to include refreshments.
Further information from June Hartley 01562 883016
Thursday 28th - Saturday 30th October - 7.30pm
Sandwell Musical Theatre Company perform a compilation of songs from the West End and Broadway musicals in their show THANK YOU FOR THE MUSICALS at Netherton Arts Centre.  Tickets £4.00, concessions £2.50.
Tickets from Anne Harrison 0121 421 2946

Go along to a LUNCHTIME ORGAN RECITAL presented by the Friends of Music at St Thomas's at St Thomas's Church, Stourbridge.  Bring a packed lunch and spend your lunch hour at this organ recital given by Andrew Fletcher - organist at St Thomas's and regular recitalist in the UK and USA who will be playing some organ 'Fireworks' in the second recital in the 'Fletchers Favourites' series.  Free with a retiring collection.
Details from John Savage 01384 372419

Want some more?  Come along to Cradley Heath Amateur Operatic Society's OLIVER! and consider yourself at home with the Black Country's oldest established amateur operatic society's lively show at Brierley Hill Civic Hall.  Tickets £5.25 to £7.00.
Tickets from Marjorie Boycott 01384 394398
Friday 12th November - 7.00pm
YOUTH ENTERTAINS includes many of the outstanding items from Dudley Festival of Music, Drama and Dance's 1999 Festival last March.  The concert at Dudley College is presented by Radio WM's Ed Doolan and is sponsored by The Rotary Club of Dudley.  Tickets £5.00, concessions £4.00.
Tickets from Dudley Information Centre 01384 812812

Tickets from Dudley Information Centre 01384 812812
Thursday 18th & Friday 19th November - 7.30pm

Tickets from Gill Bennett 01384 376342

Details from Alan Hackett 0121 550 1288

Tickets from Carey Dalloway 01384 372930

The Friends of the Music at St Thomas's present the annual CHORAL CONCERT of seasonal music by their own Church choir of boy's and men's voices under the direction of Andrew Fletcher and accompanied by Kevin Gill on the organ at St Thomas's Church, Stourbridge.  The choir recently sang in Wells, Truro and Birmingham Cathedrals and Symphony Hall, Birmingham.  Tickets £5.00 not available in advance.
