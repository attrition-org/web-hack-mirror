Benefits of Dudley Arts Council Membership

Networking with colleagues within our own artform. DAC provides a forum for   discussion with other organisations and individuals with similar interests under the guidance of an artform representative, with the potential for joint problem solving, cooperation on planning, sharing of resources etc.

Feedback to Leisure Services, Dudley MBC on facilities and resources provided by local government, to minimise frustration, optimise utilisation and  assist planning.

Lobby on amateur arts issues both within the Borough and outside, to ensure that the social and economic importance of arts participation is properly recognised at all levels.

Cooperative ventures by offering know-how, experience and in some cases direct involvement in joint events which may be beyond the  scope of individual organisations.

Training in the form of day workshops on singing, writing etc. Meetings organised to bring in experts on particular related subjects, for example marketing, grant application and repertoire.

Enable Initiatives by providing advice, experience and, in many cases, finance in the form of grants, guarantee against loss or interest free load.

Publicity assistance with an Arts Listing leaflet of amateur arts events published and distributed to information centres, libraries and member organisations three times each year.

Safety Net for members in difficulty providing assistance with planning from an advisor within your artform and depending on circumstances, temporary support.
