Whether it's managing nature reserves, arranging walks and events for all the family, helping school children understand their environment or improving access to Dudley's countryside, the Green Umbrella team is committed to improving Dudley's green environment. We are here to help you discover and learn about the wild side of Dudley - why not give us a ring for more information and leaflets?

The Green Umbrella is the collective name of the Dudley Countryside Management Project, Environmental Awareness Team and the Warden Service, all based within the Planning and Leisure Department of Dudley MBC. The purpose of the Green Umbrella is to integrate and promote the activities of the three teams, for example Wildlife Watch, whilst allowing them to retain their separate identities.

ENVIRONMENTAL
AWARENESS TEAM
Claughton House,
Blowers Green Road, Dudley.

The Team's objective is to increase the environmental awareness of the people of Dudley Borough, and to help schoolchildren understand and appreciate the natural and built environment.

The Team works with the Advisory Teachers and other Council departments and voluntary organisations with a responsibility for education and awareness raising.

We provide information and practical help to schools in creating nature areas within their grounds from design through implementation to planning for management, and are also available for classroom activities and to lead walks and talks. We can help you to set up projects specifically linked to the National Curriculum. We also participate in INSET Days in conjunction with the Advisory Teachers, and organise activities for events such as Environment Week, National Tree Week, Wildflower Week and Dudley Bulb Week.

WILDLIFE WATCH

The Green Umbrella team also co-ordinate the "Wildlife Watch", "Water Watch", "Swan Watch" and "Badgers in Dudley' campaigns. Please contact us, the Police or the RSPCA if you see anyone or anything harming wildlife, especially Swans or Badgers, and contact the National Rivers Authority if you see anything polluting a watercourse.

DUDLEY COUNTRYSIDE
MANAGEMENT PROJECT
Claughton House,
Blowers Green Road, Dudley.

Established in September 1988, the DCMP is jointly funded by the Council and the Countryside Commission. The Project aims to improve access to the Borough's countryside, to enhance its nature conservation and landscape values, and to raise awareness of opportunities for informal recreation. This work is undertaken in liaison with landowners and local community groups.

Initiatives have included work on the 13kin Stour Valley Walkway, improvements to 3Okm of public footpaths around Halesowen, and, in liaison with other agencies, the publication of countryside walk leaflets.

The production of the Dudley Countryside Strategy was co-ordinated by the DCMP.  Published in March 1994, this important document directs action to sustain the agricultural, natural and historic resources of Dudley's countryside and to provide opportunities for its appreciation.

The DCMP operates a grant-aid programme for small- scale improvements in Dudley's countryside. Services offered include advice, scheme design, assistance with labour and materials, and grants of up to 50% of costs. Eligible schemes include habitat management and creation and access improvement.

The Green Umbrella team produces seasonal newsletters, an annual programme of guided walks and events and a series of leaflets called 'Countryside Walks in Dudley', all available free on request.  Free maps and summaries of the Dudley Countryside Strategy and the 'Action Plan for Nature Conservation in Dudley' are also available.
