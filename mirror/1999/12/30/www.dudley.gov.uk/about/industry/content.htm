The Local Economy

Dudley Borough's manufacturing base shrank by 19% between 1981 and 1991, while service activities grew by 27% over the same period. In the last 10 to 15 years the economy of Dudley has continued to diversify. Apart from the Merry Hill shopping centre, there is now a significant office campus development known as the Waterfront. Forty-four service sector companies have moved to the Waterfront employing around 3,000 people. Also, about 70 overseas-owned companies have been identified in Dudley Borough. Over the 3 years: 1994, 1995 and 1996, twelve of the 24 new investment projects in Dudley were overseas in origin. Dudley's largest employers (in terms of numbers of employees in Dudley) include the Local Authority, the Health Authority, Wolverhampton & Dudley Breweries, Dudley Police and British Steel.

(Further information: Jane Morgan 01384 - 814173)


Many of Dudley's characteristics, eg: unemployment, socio-economic profile, deprivation etc. closely match the national average - in fact the Borough appears to be less deprived than the national average according to the DoE Index of Local Conditions. However this is a misleading picture which masks great variation within the Borough. While the south and west fringes are relatively affluent, localised deprivation exists, and some of Dudley's enumeration districts are among the most deprived in the country.
