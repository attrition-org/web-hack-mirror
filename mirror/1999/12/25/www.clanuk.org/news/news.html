The Blue Review

(The views within this column are merely those of the author and do not necessarily reflect the views of ClanUK as a whole.)

Welcome to my slightly off-the-wall take on this weeks events within the lovely, cuddly & padded walled institution we call ClanUK. So, where to start? The middle seems somewhat apt but, for the sake of my sanity, I'll start where I would have finished were this not the first of my columns of verbal chunder. For the hard of reasoning, we'll call that "The beginning".

The New Kids on the Block

Unless you've been under a rock for the past couple of weeks you should be aware that the ClanUK Ops elections have taken place. My reference to the band that spawned a thousand bands, disbanded & then let loose the ghastly Jordan Knight on the world as a solo artist will, hopefully, not be an apt one as time passes. Indeed, our six slaves seem to have hit the ground running, a couple looking to break the sound barrier, impressive stuff indeed! The times they are a'changin', to coin even more abysmal cliches, and our furry ball of fury has already started adding to the smooth black hunk that is the ClanUK website, making it smoother, slightly redder and vaguely more approchable about emotional problems or teenage angst. The Ops are welcoming suggestions (of the concise nonsense-free variety) on the forums and via e-mail. So crack those whips & get busy! As for Hammy, well, just complain on the forums - he always sorts things out and seems quite used to getting hassled fr! om all sides. They say pain lies on the riverside (well, Live do) but our furry friend seems to have a level of tolerance to abuse similar to the levels shown to alcohol by that fat, bearded "I'd make satan wet himself" bloke in your local. Oh, and the former smiles a bit more too.

No Lights, Thankfully no Camera, Just Action a-plenty

The ugly beast of progressive thinking that is the Action Zone admin team is doing us proud once more. It seems the organisation is becoming lethal once more following the rather dramatic exit from a league no-one knew we were in & equally dramatic return to the league, supported by frantic ActionUK mailings & fast-paced tactical debates on the forums. Looking to capitalise on the increased enthusiasm for action games within ClanUK regular practices are being scheduled & no doubt the world will fall before us, doubled up in laughter? Maybe, but not according to the recent screenshot-frenzy proving our boys have what it takes to, um, kill stuff. John Romero himself has been heard to utter "There's room for both games" re: The great UT/Q3A debate. But will this be the case? Is there room for both in ClanUK? One would like to think so although both games play very differently (ok, ok, there are only so many ways to frag a lamer but it's all about weapon handling, ask any trained assassin) and seem to be attracting different crowds, I'm an unchanging Quake veteran myself, but I seem to be in the minority here so no doubt I'll have both games in my collection. Although one cannot deny the appeal of playing as ClanUK in Quake Arena and mixing the letters to form QUACK as our clan tag. You know you want it.

Surviving the Wait for D2

Having finally got my copy of Baldur's Gate working on my beast of a new PC I'm set for a while, but how are people coping with the long wait before the release of what could be the biggest anti-climax since sliced bread (which, you must admit, was hardly revolutionary)? Of course, D2 could be _the_ game of 2000/2001/2002/whenever, just make sure you sign up for the Beta test. Anyway, here's my guide to surviving the bitter days/weeks/months:

1. Write a long-winded & dull news column no-one is going to read. Give it up after one addition after claiming it's gunna be weekly.
2. Spend every waking moment checking www.battle.net & www.blizzard.com for new information, collect your drool in plastic containers & sell it to insecure people as a slimming drink.
3. Repeatedly e-mail Blizzard complaining, putting more of their staff on PR & slowing release down further.
4. Buy Live "The Distance to Here" and rejoice.
5. Hang around your local HMV/Electronics Boutique & ask repeatedly about release dates for Diablo2.. maybe throw in the occasional Team Fortess 2 comment. Camp on their doorstep, don't shave.
6. Start playing UO or EQ, drop greys in the former & kite in the latter, become so absorbed in macroing, cooking 5000 fish & waiting for server updates that when Diablo2 comes out you don't notice.
7. Read all the jokes posted on ClanChat. When you've finished, buy Diablo 2, 3 & get a copy of Warcraft 3 while you're at it.
8. Buy system shock 2, neglect sleep for a couple of weeks, lose all track of time & become amazed when Diablo2 comes out "just a few days later"
9. Write a list of things to do while waiting for something, pretend to be original and witty by adding "write a list of things to do while waiting for x" as one of the listed options, realise it has all been done before & drown in self pity. Diablo 2 becomes meaningless.

Forum Configuration Changes

Two configuration changes will be carried out the the ClanUK Forums this evening (13/09/99).

The first is straighforward: -
An 'Acceptable Use Policy' has been approved by the Arbiter and the Ops. In using the ClanUK Forums, you agree to be bound by this policy.

The second item has also been approved by the Arbiter and the Ops, and is as follows: -

A Forum account will be 'required' before any person can post there, although non-account holders would still be able to read the public areas. Anyone can apply for an account online, by clicking on the 'Edit Profile' option on the menu and following the on-screen instructions. Accounts are no longer granted automatically in real time, they are queued pending review by a moderator. If the account was then granted by a Moderator, a ClanUK member would be given a ClanUK account whilst a non-member would be given a non-ClanUK account. Permission on the 'secure' areas would remain as they are now.

The advantages to this system are as follows: -
We would know who everyone is - no anonymous posts, no pseudonyms etc ;
Should anyone contravene the 'Acceptable Use Policy', their account can be disabled immediately. They would be unable to post anything, pending further investigation and/or re-instatement of their account
