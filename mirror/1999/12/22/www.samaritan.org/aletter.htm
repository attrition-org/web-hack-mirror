Samaritan's Purse is a nondenominational evangelical Christian organization
providing spiritual and physical aid to hurting people around the world.
Since 1970, Samaritan's Purse has helped meet needs of people who are
victims of war, poverty, natural disasters, disease, and famine with the purpose of
sharing God's love through His son, Jesus Christ.  The organization serves
the church worldwide to promote the Gospel of the Lord Jesus Christ.

                                                                                                                                                                                        

 

  

Dear Friend,

Growing up as the son of Billy and Ruth Graham, I was not a stranger to the work of Christ. My own experience serving Him, though, began when I decided at the age of 22 to trust Christ personally as my Lord and Savior. Since then it has been my privilege to spend my life helping others understand His love and the Good News of salvation through faith in Him.

For me—and for thousands of other people today—this means walking the Samaritan Road. In other words, like the Good Samaritan our Lord described in Luke chapter 10, coming to the aid of hurting people and helping them get back on their feet.

Today the Samaritan Road winds through places such as Bosnia, Romania, Sudan, Lebanon, and India, where men, women, and children are victims of war, famine, disease, and natural disaster. In these and other troubled areas, Samaritan's Purse ministers to those who are suffering by providing them with food, water, clothing, shelter, medical care, and other critically needed assistance, all in Christ's Name. This, in turn, earns us a hearing for the Gospel, which offers forgiveness and hope to all who believe.

Samaritan's Purse can lift up Christ in these situations as concerned individuals such as yourself join with us—personally, prayerfully, and financially. Together God can use us mightily. Welcome to the Samaritan Road!

                         Sincerely,
                     
                         Franklin Graham
                         President,
                         Samaritan's Purse
