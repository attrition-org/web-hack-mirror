This is Rodney from Tin City ... he has quite a story to tell!!
He has been living in Tin City for the past 30 years in a shack made out of Corrugated Iron, Rodney is amazed that it took someone from the other side of the world to reveal the unique lifestyle and his ability to fight the adversity of the elements. Sand is a constant threat to Rodney, at least 4 to 5 times a year his unusual dwelling is covered by sand .... which makes living in the dunes a very exciting but dangerous lifestyle.
Without a doubt the most unique part about Roodneys lifestyle is the way he gets his water...above you can see Rodney using a pump ...which is connected to the underground water table ... the water he gets from the table is extremely clean ... this water is so clean because it is filtered through the sand ...and believe me there is plenty of sand !!
So come along and see for yourself how Rodney lives!!

HOW TO GET TO PORT STEPHENS

BY CAR

  2½ hours drive north from the Sydney Harbour Bridge via the Newcastle Freeway(F3). Take the Freeway to it's end and follow the Pacific Highway signs to Hexham.Cross the Bridge and take the first turn to the right for Port Stephens coastal towns.

BY TRAIN

Take a train to Newcastle and there are buses direct from Newcastle Station.
Train times call: 131500.

BY COACH

Port Stephens buses depart Eddy Avenue, Central Station in sydney Daily at 2.00pm.
Phone: (02) 49 822940

BY AIR

Direct Flights are available on a daily basis from Sydney, Brisbane, Melbourne and Canberra as well as various regional centres.
Phone Newcastle Airport: (02) 49 651925.
