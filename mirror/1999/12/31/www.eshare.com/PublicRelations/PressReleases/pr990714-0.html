eShare and The Digital Club Festival Team to Give Concert-Goers and Music Fans Around the World a Place to Chat Online

Commack, NY — July 14, 1999 — eShare Technologies Inc., the expert in human interaction on the Internet, today announced that it will team with the Digital Club Festival (www.digitalclubfest.com) to give concert-goers and music lovers an online destination where they can interact in real-time 24 hours a day.

The Digital Club Festival, which takes place July 20-23 and features 300 bands performing in 20 Manhattan venues, has chosen eShare's Expressions® 4.0 software to add chat capabilities to its highly trafficked site. Using Expressions, the Digital Club Festival will connect music fans from around the world so that they can simultaneously participate in Internet dialogs with fellow enthusiasts. The Festival will use Expressions to deliver the critical component of human interaction to its unique online music community, enabling the fans to log in and discuss their favorite bands with other fans in real-time, without the delays caused by plug-ins or downloads.

"eShare's Expressions product was the most appropriate choice among chat software and we are very happy to make them a part of this year's event," said Michael Dorf, co-executive producer of the Digital Club Festival. "Expressions stability and scalability were two factors that influenced our decision and made it the best solution for our purposes."

eShare Expressions powers the Web-based chat features found on four of the Internet's top 20 sites, as measured by Media Metrix, including GeoCities, Lycos, Tripod, and Snap.com. The product line features an open architecture with multiple client interfaces, Active X, Java, Java Light and HTML; easy access without time-consuming downloads or plug-ins; powerful communications log and online tracking. Expressions also offers full HTTP support for seamless operation through firewalls, alleviating the need for administrators to install additional software.

eShare Expressions can be utilized for virtual meetings, live training and conferencing, distance learning, moderated events and social chat. The product line features four key components: Chat, a server-based software solution for adding live real time interaction; Forums, a conferencing tool; Web Tours, which provides the ability to lead a group on a tour from page to page and site to site; Moderator, for hosting and managing live discussion and Integrator Pro, which integrates the software with existing databases.

About the Digital Club Festival
Now in its fifth year, The Digital Club Festival, formerly known as the Intel New York Music Festival, is considered the largest online music event in the world. The four-day event combines the excitement of live music with cutting edge interactive technology. Producers Andrew Rasiej, founder of Irving Plaza, and Michael Dorf, creator and owner of The Knitting Factory, have assembled a broad range of talent performing throughout 20 of Manhattan's hottest nightspots. Presenting sponsors include MTV, Intel, RealNetworks and Yahoo! Music.

The Festival website, www.digitalclubfest.com, is the virtual location for visitors to participate in the event and experience how music will be performed and purchased by the wired music fan in years to come. This year, all participating nightclubs will use streaming media technology to webcast live concerts from their stages, reaching thousands of music fans worldwide. While the 1994 Festival was only able to make digital photos and text available to a worldwide audience, in 1998 more than 400 continuous hours of live and archived performances were available on multiple "24 hour channels" along with interviews and online shopping links. Many of the performances will be archived for later viewing on the forthcoming Digital Club Network
