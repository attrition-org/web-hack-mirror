Next Generation of AT&T Chat 'n Talk Debuts on Lycos, Enabling Multiple Parties to Turn Chat into Voice

BASKING RIDGE, N.J. — (BUSINESS WIRE) — Aug. 25, 1998 — The next generation of AT&T Chat `N Talk (www.chatntalk.att.com) today makes its debut on Lycos (NASDAQ:LCOS - news), "Your Personal Guide to the Internet,'' enabling up to seven people to turn an online chat session into a confidential, real-time voice conversation.

AT&T Chat `N Talk is the first of a family of AT&T Inter@ctive Communications, developed by AT&T Labs, to be featured on the Lycos Web site (www.lycos.com). In addition to expanding the number of people who can participate on AT&T Chat `N Talk from two to seven, participants also benefit from a streamlined set-up process, and the ability to add known parties from outside the chat room.

The enhancements to AT&T Chat `N Talk on Lycos have been made possible through a collaboration between AT&T and eShare Technologies, a provider of enterprise-class interaction software and services.

"Working closely with industry leaders Lycos and e-Share, we are rapidly enhancing the AT&T Chat 'N Talk experience,'' said Dan Schulman, president of AT&T WorldNet(R) Service and AT&T Inter@ctive Communications. "The evolution of this service from a `one-on-one' model to a multi-party communications tool reinforces AT&T's commitment to driving interactive communications on the Web.''

"This next generation of AT&T Chat 'N Talk helps define the word ''community" when speaking about how the Net is bringing people together,'' noted Robert J. Davis, president & CEO of Lycos Inc. "Through a combination of AT&T's advanced communications and e-Share's hosting technology, Lycos chat group participants can come together in an anonymous vocal forum.''

"eShare is proud to include the technology of the Expressions product line in the expanded services of pioneering companies such as AT&T and Lycos,'' said James Tito, President and CEO of eShare Technologies, Inc. eShare's Expressions is a powerful server-based software solution for adding live, real-time interaction to Web sites.

How it Works:
An Internet user can initiate an AT&T Chat `N Talk session in Lycos Chat by highlighting and clicking on a name. The participant is then asked if he or she wants to accept a voice-chat. If the inquiry is accepted, the participant will be asked to enter in their phone number. All participants' phone numbers are kept confidential, in order to retain complete confidentiality.

After entering the number, the participant's phone will ring (this service requires one telephone line separate from the Internet connection). The initiator of the AT&T Chat `N Talk session - who has gone through a similar process - will be on the line.

The host can initiate this process with up to six chat-room participants or outside parties in the continental U.S., including Alaska and Hawaii. AT&T Chat `N Talk has a set-up fee of $0.50 per participant and a charge of $0.25 cents per minute per participant anywhere in the U.S.(a) All charges are payable by the conference host.

To expedite the evolution of AT&T Inter@ctive Communications, AT&T will open up the service's APIs (Application Programming Interfaces) for licensing, and provide software kits to create new communications applications. These applications will eventually benefit online gamers, shoppers, educators and others seeking to enhance their communications experiences on the Web.

System requirements: 486 or higher PC, running Windows 95 with at least 16 megabytes of RAM; Internet Explorer 3.02 or above, Netscape Navigator 3.0 or above; one phone line separate from an Internet connection.
