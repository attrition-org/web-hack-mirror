eShare Technologies Announces Strategic Partnership with Lotus Development Corp. to Bring Chat to Virtual Lotusphere Web Site

First Move in Broad Two-Year Strategic Partnership

Hauppauge, NY — January 22, 1997 — eShare Technologies, Inc. (www.eshare.com), a leading developer of community-building tools for the World Wide Web, today announced a strategic two-year partnership with Lotus Development Corporation to provide real time chat to the highly-anticipated 'Virtual Lotusphere' site as well as to other forthcoming Lotus Web sites. 'Virtual Lotusphere' is the online component of the Lotusphere '97 showcase for new Internet technologies from Lotus, IBM and Lotus business partners. Beginning January 25, the 'Virtual Lotusphere' site will employ eShare Expressions Chat Communications Server, a new Java-based communications tool, to host live chats and ongoing discussions with senior Lotus developers and business partners continuing over the site's three month lifespan. Under the agreement, eShare will grant Lotus two 1,000 user licenses for eShare Expressions, the company's new full-featured, 32-bit multi-threaded turnkey application that requires no downloadable client plug-in. eShare Expressions comes complete with a threaded Bulletin Board System and a chat server, which transforms Web sites into virtual environments, complete with entertaining and informative discussion groups and chat forums.

We are delighted that Lotus chose eShare Expressions to make their `Virtual Lotusphere' site truly interactive, said James Tito, CEO of eShare Technologies, Inc. eShare Expressions is the next in the company's growing family of easy-to-use, easy-to-install Web communications tools designed to add interactivity to any Web site in a matter of minutes — with no client plug-ins or downloads.

Our partnership with eShare is truly in the spirit of working the Web, said Matthew Malloy, manager of Internet Technologies for Lotus. Lotusphere allows those unable to attend the show or that want information on Lotus Technologies to keep abreast of the latest developments at the show and beyond. eShare's chat software, seamlessly integrated into our application design, offers a robust solution that truly meets the spirit of the web, which like Lotus products, empowers the end user.

Lotus Development Corporation, founded in 1982, is a subsidiary of IBM Corporation (NYSE: IBM). Lotus offers high quality software products and services that reflect the company's unique understanding of the new ways in which individuals and businesses must work together to achieve success. Lotus' innovative approach is evident in a new class of applications that allow users to access and communicate information in ways never before possible, both within and beyond organizational boundaries. Lotus now markets its products in more than 80 countries worldwide and provides numerous professional consulting, support and education services through the Lotus Services Group.

eShare Technologies, Inc., a leading developer of chat and community-building tools for the World Wide Web, is the first company to introduce easy-to-install, easy-to-use, live chat software for World Wide Web sites and corporate intranets. eShare is dedicated to offering inexpensive, user-friendly software that enhances the ability of Web sites to attract users and maintain traffic. Along with its current products, ChatWare 1.1, Net.Thread 96 and eShare Expressions, the company will soon introduce several new communications tools which aim to elevate the interactivity level on the Internet. Among the Web site which already use eShare Technologies' products are Universal Underwriters, FOX, Ernst Young, The Boston Globe, Wang, FastBall, American Express, America Online, The World Bank and IBM. Formerly Interactive Marketing Technologies, eShare Technologies recently received $2.5 million in venture capital from SOFTBANK Ventures Inc., Chase Capital Partners and Flatiron Partners.
