News Release

January 26, 1999

 

Funding will allow Hotel Construction to Begin in July.

The Coquille Economic Development Corporation has secured funding to begin construction on a hotel addition at The Mill Casino. The 112-room facility is expected to cost 5.7 million dollars, with additional money being spent to finish ancillary projects at the site. CEDCO CEO, Brady Scott says, "Overall the project is projected to pump an additional 8 million dollars into the local economy over the next year." The bay-front expansion will bring a finished look to the property, which is located on scenic Highway 101 in North Bend, Oregon.

Construction on the three-story, 55,000 square foot structure is scheduled to begin in July. Waterfront meeting rooms will also be added during the expansion. Coos Bay/North Bend Promotion and Convention Pureau Director, Beve Saukko says, "The additional meeting space will be a welcome addition to the areas current convention facilities. It will allow us to more aggressively market to groups of 500 people." The Mill has agreed to pay a seven-percent room tax, which could generate up to $150,000 annually for the city of North Bend.

Currently, The Mill employs 335 people and spends millions of dollars annually in the local economy. The hotel expansion is expected to create 30 to 50 new jobs.
