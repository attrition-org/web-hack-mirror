Income Tax Rates are High
The higher your tax rate, the more your charitable deduction is worth in tax savings.

If you own Long-Term Appreciated Stock
It usually makes more sense to make a charitable gift of stock than to write a check. (As a general rule, consider giving stock with the lowest cost basis.)

Save those Acknowledgement Letters
For all charitable gifts of $250 or more, the IRS requires you to have one to prove you made a charitable gift; your cancelled check is no longer sufficient.

Consider a Lasting and Meaningful Tribute
Many of our donors honor their loved ones with a tribute donation to the Foundation. These gifts may be made in memory, or in honor of individuals you wish to recognize in a most meaningful and lasting way.
