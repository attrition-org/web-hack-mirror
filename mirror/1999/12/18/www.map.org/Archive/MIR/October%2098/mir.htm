MAP Continues Relief Efforts for Bombing Victims
    NAIROBI, Kenya - The day after a terrorist car bomb's blast ripped through the U.S. embassy in Nairobi, MAP responded with critical medicines for survivors. Today, this effort continues with the delivery of $20,000 worth of medicines and medical supplies to the Kenyatta National Hospital, where many victims are being treated.
     Upon receiving the donated medicines, gauze, sutures, and surgical materials, Minister for Health Jackson Kalweo commented. "The medicines donated will be distributed to all hospitals that are catering to the bomb blast survivors. Please continue the good work."
     May God bless you for enabling MAP to deliver health and hope in Christ's name to those who need it most.
