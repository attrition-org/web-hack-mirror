Provision of Essential Medicines

MAP Hurries Aids for Growing Mitch Health Disaster

   LA CEIBA, Honduras - The devastating storm was only the beginning. Now MAP is rushing more medicines to keep thousands of Hondurans alive in the health crisis left by Hurricane Mitch.

    Despite initial relief efforts since last November, Hondurans continue to stagger under the weight of rampant malaria and dengue fever carried by exploding populations of mosquitoes spawned in hundreds of acres of stagnant water. Darkening the outlook is the lack of clean drinking water, causing outbreaks of cholera and hepatitis. Children, who comprise at least half the refugees, fall prey the easiest.

    In response, MAP continues to ship lifesaving antibiotics, antiseptics, antiparasitics, water purification tablets, and more. Although Honduras has enough doctors and hospitals, the lack of medicines threatens to undercut these otherwise lifesaving facilities.

    "I thank Jesus for you!" says Hilda Clarke, of La Ceiba, Honduras, to the donors and staff of MAP International. "I wish to express my appreciation to you for your sincere kindness and help to the people of Honduras during this most critical time in our history."

    God bless you for helping MAP continue to provide lifesaving medicines in what has become a long-term health disaster.
