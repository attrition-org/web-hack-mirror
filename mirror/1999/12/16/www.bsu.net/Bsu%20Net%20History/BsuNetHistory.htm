Brief History of the Project

At the beginning of the 1996 Spring semester we had a team of over 20 students with a loose objective to start an Internet service provider (ISP). It was put to the class by Dr. Robert Minch as a entrepreneurial business proposal. The class was to determine and acquire the appropriate hardware and software and then configure it all to be able to have clients successfully use the ISP services.

We were able to acquire two UNIX machines, two Intel based machines to run NT on, a couple of 386 pc’s to run as test machines and some CSU/DSU’s and a Ethernet hub to hold it all together. The class make-up was an interesting mix. In some opinions, it couldn’t have been better. The range of knowledge the class possessed was a perfect fit for what we had to do. We had people with skills in UNIX, Windows NT, Web administration and management of personnel. Anything we didn’t know didn’t take long to find out with this group.
