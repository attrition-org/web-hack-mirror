The Australian Broadcasting Authority is an

independent federal statutory authority responsible

for the regulation of the broadcasting industry. As

well as planning the availability of segments of the

broadcasting services bands (AM and FM radio and VHF

and UHF television), the ABA has responsibility for

the licensing, programming, and ownership and

control of broadcasting services.
