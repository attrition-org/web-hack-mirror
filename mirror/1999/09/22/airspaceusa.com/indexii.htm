IMAGINARY HIGHWAYS - The National Airspace System (NAS) includes 350,000 miles of controlled airways that the Federal Aviation Administration has divided into over 900 numbered routes. The airways are multi-layered and three-dimensional, stacked one on top of the other to altitudes up to 45,000 feet. Invisible imaginary highways (surfaces) lead to and from airports that are also surrounded by imaginary obstacle free surfaces. Construction of a building, tower, or any other type of structure near these imaginary surfaces or an air navigation facility may seriously affect or prevent the start of your construction project. Airspace® software helps you determine, well in advance, what problems you may encounter at a particular height and set of geographical coordinates where you plan construction. Airspace® and Terps® Professional software can determine if proposed new construction is in compliance with Federal Aviation Regulation Part 77.
