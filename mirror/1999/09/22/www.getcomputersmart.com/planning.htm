Your first internet site!  Maybe a revision of your existing site.  Either way you need to plan your venture carefully.   Before you expend hours and hours of your valuable time, getting your aunt's second cousin twice-removed to 'construct' a site for you, you need to answer one extremely important question :

Why?

Simple question.  Tough answer.  Why do you want an internet site?  Just because everyone else has one?  Nope - not good enough.  Putting together a viable, effective internet site is more than just doing some graphics and putting some words on a page.

In order for your web site to be successful, you need to place equal emphasis on the eight key elements listed below.  I invite you to explore each one, and download the various worksheets and guides located in the different areas.  They are designed to help make your web site the best it can be.

  • Planning
  • Content
  • Design
  • Involvement
  • Production
  • Follow-up
  • Promotion
  • Maintenance

Viewing the Worksheets and Guides

In order to provide you the highest quality product, our worksheets and guides have been published in Adobe Acrobat® format (PDF).   In order to view these PDF files, you will need the free Adobe Acrobat Reader.   The product is FREE and available for virtually every computer system on the market.   If you do not already have this fine product, please click on the "Get Acrobat Reader" button.

One you have the reader installed for your particular browser and computer, simply click on the worksheet or guide link.  Adobe Acrobat Reader will launch and you will see the document in your browser.

Downloading the Worksheets and Guides

To download the worksheets and guides you find throughout this section, place your mouse over the link.  With a PC, right-click and choose "Save Link As..."   If you have a Macintosh, click and hold the mouse button, then on the pop-up menu select "Save Link As..."  For specific instructions, please refer to your particular browsers HELP files.
