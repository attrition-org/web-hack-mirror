How the Archive Is Set Up

The "J" World Dates Archive is set up as a table for each day of the year divided into twelve months. You may either browse through the archive by month or look up a specific date. To select a particular month, click on the correct month listed below. To look up a specific date, first click on the month of the date you want. That will take you to that month's page. Then click on the date you want. In addition, there is information about each month at the beginning of each month's listing as well as information about movable dates, which are holidays which are celebrated on a different day each year such as Easter or Mother's Day.

Abbreviations used throughout the "J" World Calendar

@
This symbol is used for a date which is not always celebrated on the date listed but which is usually celebrated on or near the date listed.
debuted
Unless otherwise stated in the entry, the 1st airing of a television show.
Fairy, Goblins, Gremlins, Imps, Leprechauns, Pixies, Sprites
Any entry with these terms listed paranthetically has its orgin in Lady Cottington's Pressed Fairy Book and companion Calendar for 1997 published by Andrews and McMeel
Hobbit
Refers not only to events in J.R.R. Tolkein's The Hobbit, but also any of his other related works such as the Lord of the Rings trilogy.
Pb
Playboy (the date following this abbreviation is the month and year of the playmate's centerfold).
premiered
The 1st performance of particular work, whether film, classical music, or otherwise.
released
Refers to the release of a musical format, such as an album of compact disk.
Saint's Days

  1. Coptic
  2. Eastern
  3. Greek
  4. Western

United Kingdom of Great Britain, including Scotland, Wales, and England.
