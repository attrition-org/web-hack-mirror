Airlines

Below is a list of countries and the airlines that fly to them. Clicking on the country will take you to the respective Fun Site and clicking on the airline will take you to the respective company's website.

Antigua & Barbuda
American Airlines flies from Baltimore, Washington D.C., Miami, Puerto Rico, and New Orleans.
Air Canada flies from Toronto.
British West Indian Airlines (BWIA) offers daily flights from Miami and New York.
Continental Airlines now operates six times a week from Newark, New Jersey.
Leeward Islands Air Transport (LIAT).

Aruba
Air Aruba (Baltimore, Newark, Tampa, Miami).
ALM Antillean Airlines also flies to Aruba six days a week from Miami.
American Airlines flies daily nonstop to Aruba from New York and Miami.
VIASA (from Venezuela) flies three times a week from Houston.

Barbados
American Airlines, Air Canada, and British West Indies Airlines (BWIA) flying from New York, Toronto, Miami, and San Juan. Air France, Air Martinique, British Airways, and British West Indies Airlines (BWIA) can also be used. Air Caribbean comes in from Grenada, Trinidad and Tobago.

British Virgin Islands
Due to the small size of airports on these islands, visitors have to fly to either San Juan, St. Thomas, or Saint Maarten and then take an island hopper to one of the islands.
American Eagle makes seven flights a day from San Juan to Beef Island on Tortola.
Leeward Islands Air Transport (LIAT) flies from Antigua, St. Kitts, Saint Maarten, St. Thomas, and San Juan.

Cayman
Cayman Airways services Grand Cayman from Miami, Tampa, Atlanta, and Houston.
American Airlines from Miami and Raleigh-Durham.
USAir from Baltimore-Washington.
Northwest Airlines from Detroit, Minneapolis, and Memphis via Miami.

Dominica
Travelers cannot fly directly to the island and should island hop from Antigua, Puerto Rico, Saint Maarten/Saint-Martin, Guadeloupe, Martinique, St. Lucia, or Barbados.
Small plane companies like American Eagle, Delta, Air Guadeloupe, Leeward Island Air Transport (LIAT), or Windward Islands Airways (Winair) can be taken to fly into Dominica.

Grenada
American Airlines provides daily service to Grenada from its San Juan hub.
British West Indian Airlines (BWIA) offers two direct flights a week from New York and connections from Miami and Toronto via Trinidadand other Caribbean islands.
Leeward Islands Air Transport (LIAT) serves Grenada several times a day from Barbados, Trinidad, and Venezuela.
Air Caribbean comes in from Barbados, Trinidad and Tobago.

Guadeloupe
Air France or AOM from Paris, France.
Air Canada, Canadian Airlines and United from Canada.
American Airlines make year round flights from the US and Canada with either a connection in an US city or a stop over in San Juan, PR.
Air Guadeloupe flies daily from Saint Barthélemy, Saint Maarten/Saint Martin, Marie-Galante, La Désirade, and Les Saintes.
Leeward Islands Air Transport (LIAT) flies from Antigua, Saint Croix, and Saint Maarten.

Martinique
Air France from Miami on Sundays.
Air Martinique from St. Martin, Antigua, Dominica, St. Lucia, Barbados, St. Vincent, Mustique, and Union Island.
American Airlines from New York (direct flights on Saturdays) and regular flights from San Juan, PR through many connecting gateways in the US.
Leeward Islands Air Transport (LIAT) to and from neighbouring islands.
Réseau Aérien Français des Caraibes, a French Caribbean airline consortium (which includes Air Martinique, Air Guadeloupe, and Air France), flies from Guadeloupe.

St. Kitts
American Eagle offers daily connections to St. Kitts from American's hub in San Juan.
Leeward Islands Air Transport (LIAT) serves St. Kitts from either San Juan, Puerto Rico, or the islands of Antigua and Saint Maarten/Saint Martin.
Short-hop flights on local airlines—like Air St. Kitts-Nevis, Nevis Express, and St. Croix-based Coastal Air Transport—provide regular on-demand service to Nevis from St. Kitts and other islands.

St. Lucia
American Airlines from New York (direct flights on Saturdays) and regular flights from San Juan, PR through many connecting gateways in the US.
British West Indian Airlines (BWIA) nonstop twice a week from New York through 14 September and three times a week from Miami

St Maarten/St. Martin
American Airlines flies from New York, Miami, Dallas, Raleigh/Durham, and San Juan, PR. Continental flies out of Newark.
British West Indian Airlines (BWIA) flies from Miami. Air St. Barthélemy flies daily to and from St. Barts, as does the local airline, Windward Islands Airways (Winair). Inter-island service is provided by Antilles Airlines, Air Martinique, Leeward Islands Air Transport (LIAT), and Winair. Air Guadeloupe offers daily flights to and from Guadeloupe.

St. Vincent
Due to the small airport of St. Vincent, travellers will have to come through Barbados, St. Lucia, Martinique, Trinidad, or Grenada. The connection to St. Vincent will more than likely be on Leeward Islands Air Transport (LIAT), Air Martinique or Mustique Airways.

Trinidad & Tobago
British West Indian Airlines (BWIA) offers daily nonstop flights from New York, Miami, and Toronto. It also offers flights three times a week from London and serves Boston and Baltimore once a week and offers inter-island flights as well as Leeward Islands Air Transport (LIAT).
American Airlines and Air Canada also have regular nonstop flights.
