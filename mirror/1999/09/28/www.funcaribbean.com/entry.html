Entry Requirements

Anguilla For US, UK and Commonwealth nationals need not have a VISA and a passport is not needed by US citizens, but may enter with either a birth certificate with a photo, driver's license or another form of official identification with a photo.
Antigua & Barbuda Citizens of Canada and the US require either a valid passport or an original or certified copy of the birth certificate, along with an official picture of identification. Non-nationals require a return or onward ticket. Australian, British and Japanese citizens require valid passports. Visas may be required from nationals of all other countries.
Curaçao US and Canadian citizens require either a passport, birth cetificate or proof of citizenship and a return ticket is required. Other nationalities require a valid passport.
Dominica Valid passport and or official piece of identification as well as return or onward ticket.
Grenada US and Canadian citizens do not need passports but must have two(2) sets of documents proving citizenship, one with a photograph (voter's registration card, birth certificate or expired passport and an onward or return ticket). Other nationalities require a valid passport with an onward/return ticket.
St. Lucia Permits are issued on arrival, provided the visitor holds the correct documentation, valid passports must be carried by all persons entering the Island except British, Canadian & US subjects and OECS citizens who possess valid return tickets and I.D. who are not on a visit exceeding 6 months. Visas are not required by citizens of the US or Commonwealth country.
