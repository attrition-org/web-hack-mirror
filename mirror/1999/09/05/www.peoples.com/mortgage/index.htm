    ADJUSTABLE RATE MORTGAGE (ARM)

    We offer 1 year, 3/1-year, and 5/1-year adjustable rate mortgages. All have rate caps which protect you over the life of the loan.

    A 1-Year Adjustable Rate Mortgage is a mortgage with the following features:

  • Interest rate is subject to change, either
    - every year with caps of 2% per adjustment and 6% for the life of the loan
  • A variety of terms are available

    A 3/1 Adjustable Rate Mortgage is a mortgage with the following features:

    • Interest rate and payment amount is fixed for the first 3 years
    • After 3 years, interest rate is subject to change every year with caps of 2% per adjustment and 6% for the life of the loan
    • A variety of terms are available

    A 5/1 Adjustable Rate Mortgage is a mortgage with the following features:

    • Interest rate and payment amount is fixed for the first 5 years
    • After 5 years, interest rate is subject to change every year with caps of 2% per adjustment and 5% for the life of the loan
    • Owner-occupied properties only

    CHFA (CONNECTICUT HOUSING FINANCE AUTHORITY)

    The Connecticut Housing Finance Authority provides low down payment and below market interest rate financing for first-time home buyers. CHFA defines first-time home buyers as borrowers who have not had an ownership interest in a principal residence for a period of three years prior to application, or those who have owned in the past three years and are purchasing in a federally-designated targeted area.

    The CHFA down payment Assistance Program (DAP) provides financial assistance in the form of second mortgage loans to eligible first-time home buyers (or borrowers who have not owned a home in the past three years) based on their financial needs to assist in the purchase of a 1-4 family home, condominium or PUD (Planned Unit Development).

    FHA - FEDERAL HOUSING ADMINISTRATION Mortgage

    To qualify for the FHA Program, the Borrower:

    1. must occupy the property as a principal residence
    2. may not have another FHA-insured mortgage unless:
      - re establishing residency to another location not within reasonable commuting distance of principal residence
      - increase in family size where present house no longer meets the family's needs
      - vacating a jointly-owned property
      - non-occupying co-borrower purchasing a new residence

    3. or Borrower's employer must not appear on HUD's Limited Denial of Participation List
