1999 National GeoData Forum

Washington, D. C. June 7-9, 1999

Over 460 people participated in"Making Livable Communities a Reality," the National GeoData Forum. Highlights included addresses by Secretary of the Interior Bruce Babbitt and other important political and business leaders, group discussions on decision support systems, financial and commercial aspects of the National Spatial Data Infrastructure, technology, research and education, accessing data, geospatial data organizational issues, and community product development. GIS applications addressing community issues were demonstrated on Capitol Hill and the forum concluded with a hearing before the House Subcommittee on Government Management, Information and Technology.
