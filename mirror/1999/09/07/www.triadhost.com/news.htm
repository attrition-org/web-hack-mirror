The Fed tightens its fist…a little
May 19th, 1999

The Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC) adopted a tightening bias towards interest rates today. Although many analysts expected the move, the worlds markets moved sideways or down in reaction to the news.

So what are the implications for the everyday TriadHost citizen? Not many, really, the Fed had a tightening bias last year as well. You can expect small moves in the lending rate, which may affect some homebuyers. This correspondent feels you won’t see money this cheap again in the near (or far) future. That by no means indicates recessive pressure on the economy; only a small throttling back by Mr. Greenspan and his team.

So what pushed the Fed.’s button? A couple of key indicators should be noted. First on the inflationary monitor is a hike in crude oil prices. You may have noticed the cost of dinosaur fuel has risen at your pumps recently. The second is a drop in the unemployment rate, which adds wage inflation pressure – which isn’t a problem around my office! There is a third indicator not mentioned by many analysts.

Let me draw your attention to my debut article on the gold shenanigans being played out behind the closed doors of the worlds largest banking concerns. As you might recall the article spelled out allegations of price fixing in the gold markets by some of the worlds most powerful banks. To whit, the Bank of England announced a few days ago that it would sell 60 percent of it’s gold reserves! Why you might ask? Gold has historically been used as a prime indicator of inflationary pressure. The global governments and economist have been touting for well over a year that inflation is dead, because gold has not moved in so long – hardly a wiggle in price! With "gold" the last hold out under control, the world can go on breathing easily and the economy can barrel along merrily.

To countries like England, who are just now starting to recover from a decade of recession, any indication of inflation; could derail the only chance for their nation’s recovery. So they will do nearly anything to keep gold prices down – even auctioning off they’re own gold reserves. Sounds good to me, except some economist believe you can only fool the system so long before you have to deal with reality. One thing for sure, if the U.S. government announced tomorrow, that it was emptying Fort Knox of 60 percent of it’s gold deposits; wouldn’t you, or at least your neighbor, be wondering why?…

Gold Scam...The Golden Thread?
May 16th, 1999

A quick follow up to the sleepy investors on this remote subject.

Gold is under scrutiny from the Gold Antitrust Action committee (GATA). They have retained the famous investment law firm Berger & Montaque to head up the investigation.

Apparently the allegation is that the large Wall Street hedgefund group Long Term Capital Management, has been manipulating the Gold market on a global basis. This has caused the price of gold to stay below $ 300 Oz. for many years. In order to keep a bad mistake from rocking the worlds markets. The Fed and central banks have been teaming up to keep the price stable to control gold from moving up in price. Alan Greenspan and other high Treasury officials may be involved -- but are refusing to comment.

U.S. Rep. Jim Saxton (R., NJ) has been looking into the matter. His views are available at www.house.gov/jec.

More on the story is located at Thestreet.com.

This could be big -- but we'll see!
