ResGram (Spatial Modeling and Analysis)

ResGram is used to compute and model spatial variations for use in creating geostastitical models. These are then available for use in ResMod and ResSeis. ResGram 2.0 was released in May, 1999. If you have a fast connection, click here to see a gallery of ResGram capabilities (ResGram Gallery). Support or questions can be addressed through email resgram@rc2.com or through our Denver office.

Features:

  • Computes and models experimental variograms
  • Isotropic and Anisotropic
  • Data Types: Well Log, Model Data, Seismic, Marker (Picks), Surfaces (Grids)
  • Transitional, power, and periodic variogram functions fBm, fGn, Spherical, Exponential, Gaussian, Linear, Sine and Sinc
  • Nested structures - three nested structures - models can be additive or multiplicative
  • Full interactive modeling and lag visualization
  • Netscape Linked Help System Fully Integrated
  • 3D Linked Data Analysis and Quality Control
  • Gaussian, Uniform Score, Facies and Indicator transforms of data
  • Facies-based variograms of continuous properties
  • Cross-variograms of facies
  • Univariate statistics of field data and ResMod output models
  • Cut and Paste Functionality to Editor
  • Links to Stratamodel and IRAP-RMS
