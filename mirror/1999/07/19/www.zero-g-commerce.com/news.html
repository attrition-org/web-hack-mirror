Zero-G Commerce is On The Air

Zero-G Commerce was featured on the March 10, 1999 broadcast of CNNfn's "Entrepreneurs Only."

Caesar's Palate was featured on The Food Network's "In Food Today" show on May 30, 1999.

What they've been saying about Zero-G Commerce

"The [Zero-G] plan aims to take advantage of qualities that fueled early business interest in the Internet -- it scales, it democratizes, and it eliminates the middleman."

- @NY, "Building an E-Commerce Empire:
Zero-G Commerce Lays the Foundation," June 11, 1999

"Et tu, foie gras? For Epicureans with an appetite and wallet for Balducci's but an address in fast food chain hell, Caesar's Palate reigns supreme. The site fills a largely empty niche with over 1,500 high-quality foods. Ranging from $1 (Squid Ink) to $1,900 (a four pound tin of Beluga Caviar), any item in the inventory is shipped either overnight or within two to three days depending on perishability and the customer's choice of delivery."

- Silicon Alley Reporter, Special e-Commerce Issue, June 1998

"Zero-G Commerce, headed by former News Corp. and iVillage executive Mark Kahn, is setting up a series of online stores that meet a very specific set of criteria like a product line with what Kahn called "locked-in" gross margins --currently around 28%. This repetition has its appeal. "What I like in the Internet now," says Martin Bloom, managing director of venture capital group Emblem Technology Partners, are companies with a particular business model "that can replicate that model in new niches, using the existing infrastructure and ways of approach.""

- George Mannes, TheStreet.com, "Internet Hopfuls
Aim to Fill Niche Markets," May 12, 1999

"Zero-G-Commerce has uniquely positioned themselves as a strategic partner with their client suppliers. Rather than serving as merely an internet provider, Zero-G gives up the relatively low "front-end" development and monthly maintenance service fees in favor of the potentially greater return that will come from a percentage of the revenue from sales generated by the world-wide-web sites they maintain and control. By carefully selecting successful client-partners Zero-G has taken a great deal of the guess work out of determining product demand, pricing and quality. Zero-G is positioned for handsome returns should internet commerce become the direct selling vehicle everyone imagines.

- Ed Bremer, President, Nature's Aide® Vitamins

"Mark Kahn and Jay Hirschson are as aggressive and intelligent as anyone with whom I've worked with in this business during the last 3 years. I'll be shocked if Zero-G doesn't become a real e-commerce success story. The range of Zero-G's sites and products for sale makes media planning easy, efficient and very cost effective."

- Michael Cohen, President, iBalls

"Zero G is one of a tiny handful of companies that truly 'gets it' -- they understand the delicate, but potentially hugely profitable, balance between the needs of consumers, the realities of the supply side, the opportunity of the Internet, and the goals of investors. A first-class business plan from a first -rate group of experienced professionals."

- Chris Holden, CEO, Kesmai Corporation, a News Corp. Company

"Zero-G's management brings with it experience, talent, credibility and know-how in an environment which craves direction and order. Mark and Jay have hit the ground running, and Zero-G will benefit from their motivation and hands-on management style."

- Jan Constantine, SVP, The News Corporation Limited
