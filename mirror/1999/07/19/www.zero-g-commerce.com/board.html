Advisory Board Members

David J. Londoner
Managing Director, Schroders and Co., Inc.

Mr. Londoner has been with the securities firm, Schroders and Co., Inc. for the past twenty five (25) years. He has held the position, Managing Director, for the past seven (7). He has been recognized as a leading security analyst in Media and Entertainment for over twenty years (20) and is active in the firm's Corporate Finance efforts. Mr. Londoner has been in the investment business for his entire professional career following the completion of his MBA at Columbia Business School in 1959 and his BA, in Economics, from Columbia University in 1958. Mr. Londoner is also a member of the Board of Trustees with the American Museum of the Moving Image in New York City.

Christopher H. Holden
Chief Executive Officer, Kesmai Corporation

Mr. Holden is the Chief Executive Officer of Kesmai Corporation, based in Charlottesville, VA. Kesmai Corp. is the parent company for ARIES Online Games, a leading technology company and publisher of online multiplayer games; Kesmai Studios, a developer of large-scale online games; and GameStorm, a retail multiplayer games service on the World Wide Web. Kesmai is a News Corporation company. Mr. Holden has been with News Corporation since 1990. Prior to joining Kesmai, Mr. Holden was Vice President for Advanced Media at HarperCollins Publishers in New York. He has served on a variety of technology task forces and committees for News Corporation. Prior to joining News Corporation, Mr. Holden managed an advertising agency and a television production company. He has written, produced and directed over 300 news and feature segments for TV, video news releases and corporate videos.

Beth J. Polish
President, ChangeYourLife.com

Beth Polish was until recently the Managing Director of the New Media Group at WIT Capital. In her role as strategic advisor to Zero-G Commerce, Ms. Polish brings her experience to both established and emerging companies and provides strategic and financial advisory services including strategic planning, assistance with fundraising and general business advice. Before moving to WIT Capital, Ms. Polish was Managing Director at KPMG, and prior to that, Ms. Polish served as the CFO and SVP of Corporate Development for iVillage, Inc., where she raised $11 million in its first round of financing from America Online, Kleiner Perkins Caufield & Byers, TCI and the Tribune Company. Beth was also a VP at Bannon & Co., a media and communications investment bank. Ms. Polish received an MBA from Harvard Business School and a BA degree in anthropology from Franklin & Marshall College.

Jan F. Constantine
Senior Vice President and General Counsel, News America Publishing, Inc., a News Corporation Company

Responsible for legal affairs for U.S. operations in areas of litigation, intellectual property, mergers and acquisitions, corporate financings, bankruptcy, antitrust and trade regulation, labor and employment. Participate in all aspects of the business as legal advisor to client companies in the United States such as TV Guide (magazine), New York Post (newspaper), The Weekly Standard (weekly magazine), HarperCollins (book publishers), News America Digital Publishing (a website and online hosting service), News America Marketing Group (in-store and coupon promotion business) and various other technology companies. Experience with regulatory agencies (e.g. FTC, FCC, Department of Justice, State Tax Authorities, State Attorneys General). Lecture on topics relating to intellectual property, employment law and the internet.

Formerly Deputy General Counsel, Macmillan, Inc.; Assistant U.S. Attorney, E.D.N.Y.; Deputy Chief of Civil Division.

Member, The Association of the Bar of the City of New York: Served on Special Committee on Electronic Fund Transfers, Consumer Affairs Committee, Corporate Law Departments Committee, and current Chair of Committee on Communications and Media Law; Member of the Board of Directors, The Feminist Press at CUNY

Michael S. Cohen
President, iballs Internet Media Planning and Buying

Mr. Cohen is the President and Co-Founder of iballs Internet Media Planning and Buying. The company was founded in May 1997 to service the rapidly growing but under serviced segment of the advertising market. Iballs philosophy that the Internet is a direct marketing medium has led to their working for companies such as Alloy Online, BigStar Entertainment, Bose, CMP Media, DLJ Direct, Earthweb, J.Crew, Kesmai's Gamestorm, N2K/Music Boulevard, Netscape Comunciations, Polycom, The Princeton Review, Uproar, and others. Mr. Cohen is responsible for iballs' daily operations, overseeing business development, marketing & publicity, media planning, ad buying, strategic development, and creative campaign management. Prior to iballs, Mr. Cohen managed the Internet media planning and buying efforts for The Station @Sony.com at Kirshenbaum, Bond & Partners and served as a new media business consultant working with Random House New Media, dotcom gallery, Sixth Gear Interactive Business Solutions, and SiteSpecific. Michael earned an MBA from Columbia Business School in Marketing and Media Management and a B.A. in English from Tufts University.

Jonathan E. Kahn
Attorney, Cahill Gordon & Reindel

Mr. Kahn is an attorney with Cahill Gordon & Reindel, New York, New York, specializing in corporate and securities law. Prior to joining Cahill, Mr. Kahn was a full time associate at the law firm of Fried, Frank, Harris, Shriver & Jacobson from August 1990 to June 1994. Mr. Kahn received a JD from Georgetown University Law Center and a BA in history from Emory University.

Sean D. Ryan
Vice President, Business Development, Listen.com

Mr. Ryan is currently Vice President of Business Development at Listen.com. He was formerly Vice President and General Manager of SegaSoft Network's Virtual Makeover business unit where he expanded its strategic relationships and added Internet commerce functionality to the product line before managing its sale to The Learning Company. Prior to that, Mr. Ryan was Vice President of Business Development for SegaSoft where his team was responsible for electronic commerce, international sales, licensing and overall strategy, particularly SegaSoft's move into broadband relationships, such as its deal with @Home. Prior to joining SegaSoft, Mr. Ryan consulted for Sony Retail on its San Francisco Metreon project and worked as a product manager for General Magic, both during business school. In addition, Mr. Ryan spent two years in commercial banking at Chemical Bank and later, 2 years in finance and strategy at the GAP, helping to launch the successful Old Navy Clothing format. Mr. Ryan holds an MBA from UCLA's Anderson Graduate School of Management and a Bachelor of Arts from Columbia University.
