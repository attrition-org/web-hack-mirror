A Long Time Ago a Long Time in Coming
Thu, Apr 01, 99 02:00:10 AM CST

It was over a year ago when TFN first told you about the Marvel reprint series from Dark Horse called Classic Star Wars: A Long Time Ago. Since that time, the project was downgraded from color to black and white, delayed many times and finally made to serve as bait for retailers to order other DH titles.

After this long wait, I finally got my A Long Time Ago #1 today. For me, it was "free" with the purchase of three issues of DH's "Bonelli Comics" comics. You'll have to negotiate with your comic shop owner for your copy.

I must admit, it looks good in the black and white and smaller page size format. However, I was very disappointed not to see the work of two friends of mine who had been originally contracted to have work appear in this series. Comic guru and SW Galaxy Collector columnist Richard Handley was slated to write introductions to these stories. The intro was not there, though Rich is thanked on the inside cover. Also, TFN's own Amy Pronovost (an upcoming SW Kids artist) had contributed art for the cancelled introduction.

In any case, it's great to see these 'classic' stories see new light and if you can pick up this title, I'd encourage it.

Truman on Prequel-Era Comics
Wed, Mar 31, 99 12:54:10 PM CST

Comicbook Resources has a short blurb on Tim Truman's involvement with the SW Ongoing and Anakin one-shot...

TRUMAN RETURNS TO 'PHANTOM MENACE' ERA IN 'STAR WARS' ONGOING

Sure, you may be smug because you've convinced one of your buddies to start camping out for tickets for "Star Wars: The Phantom Menace" already. But even if you get in on May 19, Tim Truman's already seen the world of the first "Star Wars" prequel. Twice.

In addition to writing the "Anakin Skywalker" Dark Horse Comics one-shot that comes out before the movie reaches theaters, this summer Truman will also be taking over the ongoing "Star Wars" series set in the days before "The Phantom Menace."

Truman, as you might expect, is suffering manfully through it all.

"It feels great," he told the Comic Wire Sunday. "What a load of fun I'm having! I never thought I'd have so much fun writing this book, but it's become an incredible joy."

The script for the Anakin one-shot holds a unique place in the works of this comics veteran.

"I'm quite proud of the way the script turned out. It was the hardest story I think I'd ever done," he said. "I was picked because someone at Lucasfilm really liked the spirit and writing style of my 'Tarzan Meets the Moon Men' 6-issue series I did with Tom Yeates. It was a rather pleasant change of pace to be recognized for my writing abilities, rather than my drawing, which is usually the case."

Insider Co-Sponsors March Previews Contest
Wed, Mar 31, 99 12:02:29 AM CST

From this week's Diamond News

Contest entries for the Star Wars Triva Challenge on page 10 of the March Previews must be received by April 5, so there is still time to mail in your answers for a chance at some out-of-this-world prizes! Star Wars Insider magazine, published by the Official Star Wars Fan Club, contributed some especially stellar prizes.

The Grand Prize Winner will receive a piece of Star Wars original art by Chris Gossett and a stunning Boba Fett Bust from Legends in 3-D, both courtesy of Star Wars Insider. He or she will also receive (courtesy of Dark Horse Comics) a cameo appearance in an upcoming Star Wars comic book, a copy of SWPM #1 signed by the entire creative team, and a collection of Star Wars TPs. The Grand Prize Winner’s favorite retailer also wins a signed #1 and TPs.

The Second Prize winner will receive an Emperor Palpatine Bust from Legends in 3-D, courtesy of Star Wars Insider, plus a signed copy of SWPM #1 and a selection of Star Wars TPs from Dark Horse. The winner’s favorite retailer also wins the signed comic and TPs.

The Third Prize winner will receive a Greedo Bust from Legends in 3-D courtesy of Star Wars Insider, and a signed copy of SWPM #1 from Dark Horse. The Third Prize winner’s retailer wins a signed comic.

Enter the Star Wars Triva Challenge for a chance at these great prizes, courtesy of Dark Horse and Star Wars Insider!
