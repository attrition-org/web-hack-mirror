On April 30th, May 1st, and May 2nd, 1999 at the Wings Over the Rockies Air and Space Museum in Denver, Colorado. The Fans will get what we feel is something well over due! From the creators of the the Star Wars Insider and Lucasfilm present three days of interactive Star Wars activities in preparation for the May release of Star Wars: Episode I The Phantom Menace.

Some of the attractions planed are an exhibition from the Lucasfilm Archives of Star Wars Classic and Episode I models; a main presentation area with Episode I celebrities, a film presentation with behind-the-scenes footage from Episode I; an official Star Wars Celebration store that features exclusive merchandise; life-size props from Episode I and the Classic trilogy; a Star Wars timeline display; computer games; customizable card games; trivia matches; video presentations; a collectibles tent, and much, much more!

This will be the best place on the net to receive all the information you will need to attended the event of the century. And if you can't make it to the Celebration, members of The force.net staff will be there to report on what's going on with up to the min. updates, pictures, and interviews! So keep checking back and if you have any questions please drop us a line!

The Wings Over The Rockies Air and Space Museum opened its doors on December 1, 1994. The museum's mission is to educate the public about civil and military aviation history and space technology with and emphasis on Colorado's contribution to these fields. The museum is housed in a World War II vintage hangar (200,000 square feet) and has over 20 aircraft on display along with various aviation related items including aircraft armament. Also on display is a full-size mockup of a manned space station crew module. Click hear for more information on items on display. The museum is located in Hangar No.1, building 401, 7750 E. Irvington Place, on what was formerly Lowry AFB. The museum houses a photo reproduction laboratory, photo archive and research library.
