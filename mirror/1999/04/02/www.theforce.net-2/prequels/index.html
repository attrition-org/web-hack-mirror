Lucas Shows TPM To Friends and Reveals Timeline
Thu, Apr 01, 99 02:37:57 PM CST

A Daily Variety report by Army Archerd has some fun quotes from Lucas, as well as revealing that TPM is on time, he'll start writing Episode 2 in December for shooting in June 2000 for release (presumably) in 2002 and that 2005 will be the end of the Star Wars film run.

HOLLYWOOD (Variety) - ""That's what friends are for,'' said George Lucas, who has shown his ""Star Wars: Episode 1 - The Phantom Menace'' to friends Steven Spielberg, Ron Howard, Francis Ford Coppola, Martin Scorsese, Brian De Palma, Frank Darabont and Larry Kasdan, ""whom I trust,'' Lucas added.

Their reaction/opinion? ""Quite frankly,'' Lucas sheepishly admitted, ""they were blown away.'' Also quite frankly, Lucas admits he didn't expect a ""Return of the Jedi'' jubilant reaction. He says he'll be happy with ""Empire Strikes Back'' audience approval.

""But you never know,'' he admitted. ""However, this one is the funniest as well as being very exciting and very emotional. And it's been a fun experience. I'm very happy with it.''

He'd gone back to make editing changes after showing it to his friends, saying, ""I know what they were thinking. But, then I put it all back,'' he laughed. (His friends' reaction to the first ""Star Wars,'' you recall, was very negative.)

He is now a week and a half away from completing the final 100 shots and does about 40 of 'em a week. He'll be more than ready for the May 19 launching.

As for the next prequel, he'll start writing the script in January to shoot in June 2000. ""I have an advantage, I don't have to go through development hell. And I don't have to get the director's approval.''

He'll need only three years, instead of the usual four, to complete the next ""Star Wars.'' He has the preliminary designs as well as (some of) the established characters, costumes, planets and sets which were created for/with ""Episode I.'' He says he'll be ""stuck'' with his ""Star Wars'' until 2005.

After that? ""Anything different from this!'' But he reminds, ""This has been a fun experience'' -- for us, too, George.

TPM Tie-ins: 7-11 / Better Homes and Gardens ?
Thu, Apr 01, 99 11:48:10 AM CST

Two readers have checked in with reports of Episode 1 tie-ins that we haven't been able to confirm or deny. One was told by a 7-11 Convenience Store representative that they would have "special plastic drink/slurpee cups with characters from TPM on them". Another reader wrote us a few weeks ago to tell us he was unloading 300 boxes which turned out to labelled "Star Wars display for Better Homes and Gardens".

UPDATE: A reader writes, "Ohhh would that it were true. I've worked for Meredtih Corp, the parent company of Better Homes and Gardens, for years as an art director. I can tell you that no one here knows anything about a promotional tie in with the Phantom Meanace. Whatever this person unloaded is probably unauthorized."

UPDATE 2: The Replacement Killer writes, "I work in the advertising department for The Southland Corporation (7-Eleven), and I can confirm with you that there are indeed Fountain Soft-drink collectors cups going to 7-Eleven in mid May. There are several different cups to the collection. All but one cup can be found at other restaurant chains. There will be one collector's cup exclusive to 7-Eleven, so get out there and get one!! In addition to the collectors cups, there will be a 7-foot tall standee of Jar-Jar going into all 7-Eleven stores most likely starting in June. Let me tell you that this thing is HUGE! And the detail on Jar-Jar is absolutely amazing! 7-Eleven is expected to receive many other "goodies" through Pepsi which should roll out the day the movie opens."

If you happen to have any connection at all with either of these companies and can shed some light on these (or any other) promotions, please send us a note.

McGregor's Thoughts on Star Wars Dialog
Wed, Mar 31, 99 05:34:10 PM CST

In today's Hollywood Wire - Wire Datebook, they have a small writeup about Ewan McGregor (as it's his birthday). His comments on TPM dialog are interesting...

Ewan MacGregor, 28 - The Scottish-born actor plays the young Obi-Wan Kenobi in this summer's Star Wars: Episode One, The Phantom Menace. McGregor says his biggest challenge in portraying the character -- who was portrayed by Alec Guinness in the first Star Wars trilogy -- was getting his English accent just right. The film is filled with plenty of special effects, but McGregor admits the dialogue is just as bad in the new film as it was in the first Star Wars. In fact, he calls Alec Guinness' performance in the first flick "legendary" considering "all the bad dialogue." MacGregor is also the nephew of actor Dennis Lawson, who portrayed Wedge Antilles in the first Star Wars trilogy. McGregor flicks: Star Wars: Episode One, The Phantom Menace (1999), Eye of the Beholder (1999), South From Hell's Kitchen (1999), A Life Less Ordinary (1997), Trainspotting (1996) Shallow Grave (1994).

Internet Media Decision Irks Roughcut
Wed, Mar 31, 99 04:07:31 PM CST

Today's Hot Button column by David Poland complains about an LFL decision to exclude "internet outlets" from the press junket for the film. (You should swing by today if interested as the full article will likely disappear soon.) Here are some clips...

I found out this week that Lucasfilm has decided to disallow participation in the junket for the film by any internet outlets. Why? The stated goal is to "funnel all of the internet traffic to starwars.com." Let me be the first to say: "Screw you, guys!" After months of making promotional decisions on the basis of making the film "for the people," Lucasfilm is shutting the door on the very people who make up their core audience. You...

And they don't need the traffic at their site. It's not a money issue. (Even though their Website is now loaded with e-commerce upsales of toys and posters and movie-related events.) It's a control issue. Control of reviews. (Major 'net publications respect embargo dates.) Control of the secrets. (Major 'net publications respect the need to avoid spoilers, far more so than the New York or Los Angeles Times, thank you.) Control of the story. Sorry, guys. You can't control the truth. And as infuriating as any of this is the fact that all the news is likely to be good. What is this obsession? And why use this muscle on the medium that most honors you?

It's an interesting issue indeed. While fan sites like TheForce.Net wouldn't be considered "press" to LFL anyhow, we're curious about your thoughts on this issue. Please speak out in our Jedi Council non-spoiler prequel forum.

Celebration Ticket Information. . .
Wed, Mar 31, 99 08:21:01 AM CST

Hydrospanner is reporting on some intesting news regarding the Official SW Celebration coming up in Denver in about a month. Be sure to check out their site for the full report. Here's part of their story:

Due to counterfeiting problems, if you ordered the 3-Day Event Pass from the Star Wars Fan Club/MosEspa.com for the Star Wars Celebration (April 30th-May 2nd, Denver, CO) you will NOT recieve them!

Rod from our Rebel Friends section also called the fan club and here's the reply they gave him yesterday:

The operator said I would then receive a confirmation of the order first (I suppose like the e-mail confirmation the rest of our staff has been receiving), and that they don't send the tickets anyway: the only send a VOUCHER and you have to go to the ticket booth in Denver to get your tickets. The guy said that even when the voucher did arrive too late for me, they'd still have my name in the computer, and upon showing a legitimate ID I would be able to get my ticket at the ticket booth in Denver.

Then Amy contributes with this little tidbit about Cons in general:

This is the way alot of cons work nowadays.. actually, almost all of them. Even if you have paid in advance you still have to stand in line for your real entrance ticket, your con badge, your bag of goodies and things like that.
