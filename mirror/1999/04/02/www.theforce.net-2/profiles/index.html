Who is TheForce.net?

Scott Chitwood co-founded this site back in 1996 with Darin Smith when it was the Star Wars Page at Texas A&M. Scott works on the prequels section, books, trivia, interviews, and anything else that needs working on. His goal for the site is to make a site HE would be interested in visiting. All reports that he tried to Rogaine himself into a Wookiee are completely and totally false.
Darin Smith (D!) helped Scott found this site, back when they were roommates at Texas A&M. He's TheForce.Net's primary tech-head--writing scripts and otherwise keeping things running smoothly. He also does some PR work, helping with conventions and such. Some of his other contributions are book reviews, editorials, and just generally anything he has time to cover. He is currently employed by Raytheon Systems Company in Lewisville, TX (in the Dallas/Fort Worth Metroplex).

"My aim for TheForce.Net: to provide a fun, clean, entertaining site for Star Warriors to play in. Star Wars has always meant a lot to me, and it deserves a fan page that brings out the best in it for everyone, from little kids to our "closet fan" parents and grandparents."

Brian Linder is one of the newshounds for TheForce.net, verifying informants, sifting through information, and making contacts.  He contributed to many sites in the early days of 'net geekdom, primarily TFN when it was the Texas A&M Star Wars Page.  Ain't It Cool News readers might know him as Geek #3 due to his unofficial association there.  Brian was officially brought on board when the TAMU page was reborn as TheForce.net in August of 1997.
Paul Davidson (Tinman) was previously the maintainer of a Prequel web site. He also joined TheForce.net in August of 1997.

"I want TheForce.net to be a site with something for every Star Wars fan, and to be a site that is worth coming back to each day."

Nick Brandt is a master of 3D rendering and graphics, and TheForce.net is pleased to host his Skystation site. His prequel trailers, in fact, has made its rounds among the Lucasfilm people themselves!
Brian Zoromski started his first VRML Star Wars page back in 1995. He brings many years of experience with him to TheForce.net.

"I grew up playing with Star Wars toys. Now I play with Star Wars VRML models and Web pages. I just want to provide the best interface for me and everyone else to play with these new toys."

Paul Ens (Ghent) brings a great comic review section to TheForce.Net!

"The Star Wars universe is meant to be experienced visually. That's why Star Wars comics have such potential for greatness."

Joe W. Wiles is a big-time Star Wars memorabilia collector and a man at arms when it comes to Star Wars collecting Information. Joe was brought on to add his talent to our collecting section!

"It is my honor to be able to work with TheForce.net. Star Wars was always there for me when I need it It's only right that I pass on the knowledge and inspiration that it has taught me."
Terrence Daniels is the youngest member of our group. A college freshman majoring in Graphic Design, Terrence brings a wealth of knowledge about the Star Wars universe and web-page creation. You've seen some of his work in The Museum.
Amara (ArtyEwok) is a cartoonist who also has strong loves of Roleplaying, both online and tabletop. She is the only woman aboard the force.net team and thinks it's rather spiffy. She has this strange thing for ewoks, Wedge Antilles and that grotesque looking beany Jabba (it's so irresistably ugly!).
Roderick Vonhogen is the creator of the Virtual Edition and does freelance work in the prequels section and Rebel Friends.
Steve Head has been a Star Wars fan since June 3, 1977. The Holonet and the Prequels sections are his main areas of focus. Steve has professional filmmaking experience as an assistant cameraman and set production assistant for various films and commercials. He currently creates and manages information databases and marketing materials for a commercial real estate company.

"I still can't believe that when Star Wars was released, I wanted to go see Rollercoaster over Star Wars."

Joshua Griffin is one of the newest members of TheForce.net team, doing code since January, 1999. He has been a long-time news contributor to fansites, and now has joined our team. He concentrates on the prequel section, as well as working on archiving for the new site. In his spare time, Joshua is the youth pastor of a large Baptist church in the Midwest.
