  Welcome to DENCOM!


The U.S. Army Dental Command, or DENCOM, provides centralized command and control of Army dental-care facilities, maintaining soldiers' dental readiness and serving as the proponent for the dental health needs of the Total Army Family. It oversees the world's largest comprehensive dental system, providing care to over 450,000 active-duty soldiers and 95,000 family members in the United States and in Europe.

DENCOM is a major subordinate command of U.S. Army Medical Command, and its headquarters is located in the Medical Command headquarters building at Fort Sam Houston, Texas.

Paralleling the Medical Command’s six regional medical commands, DENCOM has six regional dental commands. Each regional dental command is responsible for direct, day-to-day oversight of the front-line dental facilities that treat patients.

This frees the DENCOM to concentrate on strategic leadership, planning and budgeting for Army dentistry worldwide. It also puts day-to-day management authority closer to the working level, where leaders better understand local conditions, resources and needs.

Regional dental commands are responsible for cross-leveling personnel and fiscal resources among the subordinate dental facilities in their respective regions. They also provide staff assistance visits, consultation services, mobilization planning, etc., for those facilities.

Each regional dental command serves the same geographical area as the associated regional medical command. Each is commanded by a colonel, who also serves as senior dental staff officer for the regional medical command and its associated Army medical center.
