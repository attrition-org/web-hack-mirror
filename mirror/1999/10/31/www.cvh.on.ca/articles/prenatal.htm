Pre-natal Education Program

Having a baby at The Credit Valley Hospital is a special event, and we treat it that way. Our prental education program offers several programmes to meet the expectant families needs. Our family-centred maternity care enables family members to share the experience of birth from the very beginning.

1. The Basic Program

a) Early Pregnancy: consists of two classes beginning in the third to fourth month of pregnancy and includes; the unborn baby's development; physical and emotional changes of pregnancy; nutrition, fitness and healthy lifestyles in pregnancy; relaxation; hospital tour.

b) Post-partum and parenting preparation: consists of two classes beginning in the fifth to sixth month of pregnancy and includes: physical changes and care of mother's post-partum; father's role; layette; childhood illnesses/immunization; relaxation; normal newborn characteristics and procedures; adjustment to new roles as parents.

c) Childbirth preparation: consists of four classes beginning in the seventh to eighth month of pregnancy and includes: labour and birth; relaxation and breathing techniques to enhance comfort in labour; initiating breastfeeding; medical procedures and analgesia/anaesthesia; Caesarean birth; role of father or support person in labour; immediate post-partum mother/baby.

2. Breast feeding

Breast feeding preparation: recommended for all parents who plan to breast feed their newborn. This educational session is two hours and will include: benefits of breast feeding; how breast feeding works; initiating breast feeding; practical tips; technique; video presentation.

3. Refresher

Refresher childbirth: this series of three classes is designed for experienced parents expecting a second or later baby and includes: review of labour and birth; comfort measures in labour including a review of relaxation and breathing techniques. (The sibling class is recommended for families taking the refresher childbirth program).

4. Sibling

Sibling Preparation: this ninety minute class is designed to help prepare for the arrival of a new brother or sister and includes: story-time and sibling video; family tour of the maternity unit; parents discussion group; play-time with child's favourite doll or bear; newborn visit and discussion of healthy newborn.

5. Pre/post-natal fitness

Keeping fit: this class is recommended for mothers during pregnancy and in the months following birth: keeping in shape through exercises led by a fitness expert.

6. Early pregnancy tour

Refer to (a) early pregnancy. Meet in the lobby, bring a snack and pillows.
