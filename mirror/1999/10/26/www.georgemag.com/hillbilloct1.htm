Going, Going Gore!
 By Paul Begala

What's the real reason behind the veep's stumbling campaign? A bunch of high-paid advisers who don't know the meaning of commitment.

Vice President Al Gore once told me he wanted people involved in his campaign who aren't merely participants but rather are truly committed. "Do you know the difference?" he asked with a sly smile. "In a ham-and-egg breakfast, the hen participates -- but the hog is committed."

The problem with the Gore campaign is that it has too many hens. Gore's flat-footed run thus far can be traced to the fact that many of his top aides are continuing to work as lobbyists, advisers, and public relations flacks for a long list of corporate clients.

Gore supporters (like me) might be tempted to overlook his many missteps as typical opening-night jitters, to take solace in the notion that there's plenty of time to make corrections, or to ignore them altogether. But what's really troubling is that there's something fundamentally wrong with the Gore campaign. It lacks passion, it lacks focus, it lacks direction, it lacks energy. And that's because too few of the senior people in the campaign are truly committed.

I find this situation extremely frustrating because I know Al Gore to be passionate, focused, highly directed, and very energetic. So it's time for supporters to tell the vice president what he needs to know, not what he wants to hear: He needs more hogs.

If I were running the campaign, I'd gather the entire high command and deliver the following message: "Ladies and gentlemen, let's play a little game. Close your eyes and pretend that this campaign is something important, like, say, a fight to determine who will occupy the most powerful office in the most powerful nation in the history of humanity. Let's pretend also that opposing us is a band of reactionary fanatics and special- interest sleazeballs who have banded together to try to reverse every bit of social, economic, and environmental progress this country has made in the last six and a half years.
