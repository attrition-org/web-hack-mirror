The Director's Corner




         We're excited about this inaugural issue of NSEP NEWS. There is a great deal of activity at NSEP and we hope that this newsletter provides some important insights into our events, plans, achievements and the accomplishments of NSEP grantees.

          NSEP is pushing the margins in promoting global skills for the 21st century. Since 1994, we have funded almost 1,200 undergraduate and 598 graduate students to study all around the world, excluding Western Europe. NSEP has awarded 37 institutional grants that are making a difference, generating a new paradigm for international education at a time when American national interests increasingly depend on knowledge of languages, cultures and socioeconomic or political conditions in little known world regions.

Making a Difference

How is NSEP playing such a role?

  • NSEP has not only increased financial support for study abroad in and about areas of the world critical to US national security; it has also focused attention on specific areas of the world that need emphasis today in order to prepare for future challenges.

  • NSEP has advanced the debate about what constitutes US national security in the 21st century. Through its institutional grants and projects of graduate fellows and undergraduate scholars, NSEP has generated recognition that globalization means that Americans are affected by events beyond our borders to a far greater degree than at any other time in our history. Ethnic conflict, weapons of mass destruction, terrorism and drug trafficking, resource depletion, population growth, environmental damage, new infectious diseases and refugee migration are but a few of these perils.

  • NSEP has, through its annual assessment of national security needs via a survey within the federal government, highlighted the importance of combining international skills with many different disciplines and fields of study. These disciplines include history, international affairs and political science as well as business, economics, applied sciences, health sciences, agricultural sciences and engineering.

  • NSEP, through all three components of its program, effectively promotes increasing opportunities for minorities and other students who have not frequently included international study in their curriculum.

  • Through the innovative application of NSEP's service requirement, we are providing federal agencies and offices with heretofore unavailable expertise and international competency in critical fields of study. Indeed, the anxiety over the NSEP service obligation evident in our program's early days has been translated into an opportunity to seek an experience or a career in public, federal service.

  • As NSEP NEWS reports on the work and achievements of our scholars, fellows and institutional grantees, you will see why we point proudly to NSEP's impressive results, attained only a few years after we began to make awards. We've begun a vital task to create a reservoir of global skills and knowledge for American national security needs. For us, no goal could be more important or rewarding.

Robert O. Slater, Ph.D., Director
