Most often Asked Questions

What are the student eligibility criteria?

The National Defense University does not have an open admissions policy. Students are sent to NDU by the military service or federal government agency they work for. The NDU experience is part of their professional development and will prepare them for strategic-level assignments. To attend NDU, the student must be at least a Lieutenant Colonel or Commander if they are military officers. If they are government employees, they must be at least a GS-14. Although the great majority of the students have earned advanced degrees prior to attending, at a minimum, the must hold a baccalaureate degree.

How do I apply for admission?

Unfortunately the National Defense University programs are only open to Department of Defense employees, and other federal agencies that support national security. Each of the military services nominate active duty and reserve members in the grade of O-5 (LtCol/CDR) or O-6 (Col/CAPT) to attend the different colleges. The federal government nominations are limited to personnel in the grade of GS-14 and above. In essence, the university does not have an open admissions policy.

Do you accept foreign students?

The university does have an International Fellows program. However, these are senior-level military officers, who attend under special invitation from the Chairman of the Joint Chief of Staff. Again, we do not have an open admissions policy for international students; their country must be officially invited to participate in our programs.

Can I take your courses by correspondence?

We no longer offer a correspondence course. Unfortunately the nonresident National Security Management Correspondence Program was discontinued in September of 1992. Please disregard any reference to it in training publications made available through the Services. Military officers interested in correspondence courses should contact their personnel officer for information on other programs offered through the Services.

Is the university accredited?

The National Defense University is accredited by the Middle States Association of Colleges and Schools.

RE: Accreditation Questions

Which classes are affected by the accreditation?

The NDU granted the first master degrees to the ICAF, NWC and SIWS students from the class of 1994. On 15 November, 1994, the Middle States Association of Colleges and Schools (MSA) conferred on NDU the status of "Candidate for Accreditation." The MSA policy firmly states that they will only accredit degrees from that point forward. Therefore, students who graduated in 1995 are the first to hold accredited degrees.

Will degrees be grandfathered back to earlier years?

No. Unfortunately, there has been a great deal of confusion and misinformation regarding this issue. According to the President’s files, the initial request made by the university was for degree-granting authority for the period of 1990 forward. However, when the legislation was signed it specifically stated that the university was not allowed to grant degrees until they had begun the process for accreditation with the Middle States Association of Colleges and Schools.

How does an officer get their service to reflect the degree?

The university forwarded transcripts to the services for the military officers who graduated with the class of 1995, 1996, and 1997. It is not necessary for the officers to make a request.

What does this mean to IRMC’s AMP students?

Although the AMP students are not in one of the university's degree granting programs, they will certainly benefit indirectly from the accreditation. The university is accredited as a whole, therefore, it has been awarded the "blessing" of passing a very thorough peer review which sanctions it's programs in the eyes of the academic community. The IRMC has cooperative agreements with both Syracuse University and University of Maryland. The university's initial accreditation augments those agreements, and makes a stronger case for AMP courses to receive transfer credit to other programs.

How does a student request a transcript?

The university's policy for transcript release is in compliance with the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) of 1974. Students must make a written request through the registrar's office. For the military student's convenience, following commencement, the registrar will forward transcripts to the appropriate service and request that the degree be entered into the officer's formal record. With this exception, under no circumstances will a transcript be released to a third party without the student's written permission. A transcript request must include the following information:

Full name

College attended

Year graduated

Social security number

Address where they would like it sent

Phone number where they can be contacted

Signature

Mail the request to:
