SUMMARY OF DUTIES: Develops and teaches courses in information management. Serves as a faculty expert in information management (includes information management planning, information assurance, information technology architectures, acquisition of information technology (e.g., computers, telecommunication networks, and software systems), and information technology implementation. Conducts research in the area of information management in support of national security and defense preparedness. Supports the IRM College’s Advanced Management Program, Chief Information Officer’s Certificate Program, and information operations, assurance and security courses by developing and teaching the core curriculum. Develops and teaches electives for the Information Resources Management (IRM) College, Industrial College of the Armed Forces (ICAF), and the National War College (NWC). Serves as research advisor to individual students and student groups performing research in information management. Serves as expert/consultant and advisor to senior DoD officials on the implications of information resources management on national security policies and strategies. Helps organize and direct special symposia that analyze issues and problems in DoD information technology management. Deals directly with senior policy-making officials and recommends appropriate changes to national defense policies and processes.

QUALIFICATIONS: Applicants should have progressive professional experience in a relevant area; evidence of maturity as a scholar, including a substantial record of continuing research and service contributions within a given field of academic study; and publications of books, monographs, and pieces in recognized professional journals. Full-time professional teaching experience at the university level at the rank of associate professor or professor is preferred. Applicants will be rated on the basis of the criteria listed below and the potential to successfully accomplish the duties by subject matter experts appointed with the purpose of identifying the best-qualified candidates.

HIGHLY QUALIFYING CRITERIA:

1. Demonstrated experience in research and consulting in the area of information management in industry, university, and/or government circles, as demonstrated by presentations and publications.

2. Graduate or doctoral degree in information systems management, related, or support disciplines.

3. Ability to deal effectively with high-level government and industry officials, senior military officers, and distinguished academicians.

4. Broad, deep and current knowledge of information management, includes information planning, information assurance, information technology architectures, acquisition of information technology (e.g., computers, telecommunication networks, and software systems), and information technology implementation as demonstrated by academic background and working experience.

5. Demonstrated experience in developing and teaching information management to senior level government officials or in colleges or universities, particularly at the graduate-level.
