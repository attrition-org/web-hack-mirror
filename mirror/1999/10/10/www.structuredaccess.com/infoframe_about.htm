This is why.   This is why not to use structured wiring.
  1. Creating a logical infrastructure of conduit within a building allows you to install "any low voltage wire in any wall of any room, any time!"™.
  2. Add additional technologies at any time without opening up walls.
  3. Replace wiring if technology changes.
  4. Conduit is less expensive then structured wiring, approximately thirty cents per foot.
  5. Move technologies to a different location at any time.
  6. Take the guesswork out of what your technology needs may or may not be.
  7. Your technology needs today may not be the same tomorrow.
  8. Conduit becomes and asset to the person buying your building when you decide to move on. Their needs will certainly be different from yours.
  9. With Add-A-Gang brackets, you will have less wall acne and better appearing outlets.

 

If you install structured wiring will it even be enough to meet your needs?

 

 

  
  1. No one can or will guarantee you the structured wiring is "future proof".
  2. Structured wiring, 2 RG6’s, 2 Cat5’s and 1 Fiber optic cable cost about $1.50 per foot.
  3. Five or six years ago TV or cable used RG59 not RG6. After RG6 came quad shielded RG6.
  4. What will be required for TV or cable five or ten years from now?
  5. Five or six years ago we used Cat3 wiring, Cat5 did not exist.
  6. Today we use Cat5 and there is talk about enhanced Cat5.
  7. Five or ten years from now will you need Cat8?
  8. Different media require different types of fiber optic cable. Glass fiber or plastic fiber have different characteristics and are technology specific. If you install fiber today, what type do you need?
  9. Why install structured wiring at $1.50 per foot, at a location where all you need is a telephone? An analog telephone only requires one pair of wires at about three cents per foot.
  10. Why install structured wiring at $1.50 per foot, at a location where all you need is a television set? A television set only requires one RG6 cable at about ten cents per foot.
  11. Most often you would have structured wiring in more than on wall in a room to allow you the option of having technologies in two or three different locations. This increases your cost.
