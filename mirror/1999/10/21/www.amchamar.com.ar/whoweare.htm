 WHO WE ARE

..
In 1918, the U.S. Chamber of Commerce in Argentina was founded to represent the U.S. business community in Argentina. Ever since that day, the Chamber has supported its members efforts to build their companies and generate wealth for the country.
The 1990s have been a remarkable decade which has laid the foundation for Argentina’s future prosperity. Dramatic reforms have freed private initiative, opened the formerly closed economy, and generally reduced state intervention in the marketplace. As a consequence, Argentina has seen rapid economic growth and strong foreign investment flows.
As it has since its founding, AmCham has been at center stage in this process. U.S. companies have been the leading investors throughout the 1990s. Together, Chamber members produce more than $33 billion dollars of Argentina’s annual GDP every year. Inside the AmCham Directory 1999-2000, you will see our member companies and recognize for yourself the combined strength they represent.
Six-hundred members strong, AmCham proudly represents these companies and salutes their contribution to the prosperity, opportunity, and welfare of Argentina.  

Sincerely, Carlos Paz Soldán President

The Chamber´s Goals Are the Following: 

To promote bilateral trade, direct investment, technological transfer and other special items of mutual interest between Argentina and the United States of America.
.
To promote free enterprise, market economies and the practice of ethics rules among its members and among the Argentine community.
.
To support through its activities economic development in Argentina and the welfare of citizens’.
.
To develop and strengthen relations among its members and other public or private institutions, and among those who operate in areas of interest to the Chamber.
.
