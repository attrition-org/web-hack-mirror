The Basic Rules of Estate Tax

The goal of estate taxes is to try to pay the minimum taxes by the use of deductions, exemptions, and credits. The first rule is that an unlimited amount of assets can be passed to a surviving spouse without one dime of death tax being paid. This is known as the unlimited marital deduction.  The second rule is that one can pass assets to one's heirs, tax free, in an amount equal to what is called the Federal Estate Tax.  An excellent benefit of a Living Trust is that married couples can take advantage of a loophole in the law and double their federal estate tax equivalent exemption.  This term refers to the total net assets that can be transferred to named heirs whereby no estate taxes will be due.  The equivalent exemption  is scheduled to go up in stages between year 1998 and year 2006 until it reaches $1,000,000.
