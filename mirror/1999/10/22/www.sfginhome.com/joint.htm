Many advisors in the past have recommended that you titled your assets joint tenancy with right of survivor...This advice can be a bomb just waiting to go off!!..When your assets are joint titled and one spouse dies you will not get a full step up basis step...This could cost the surviving spouse a great deal of money and reduce the assets they will have to live on for their lifetime...Another big problem we see out there is that many people are putting their children on their property with them, this is a big mistake...If that child is sued, the courts could attach all assets that includes the assets you titled with your children!..A much better way to make sure your children get your assets when you pass away is to have a living trust and make your heirs beneficiary at your demise...This will ensure that your assets will be there for your benefit while your living and will avoid probate!!
