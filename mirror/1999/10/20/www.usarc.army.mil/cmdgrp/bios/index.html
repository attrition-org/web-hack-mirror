Biographies of Senior Leadership

Major General Thomas J. Plewes, USA, currently serves as Commander, United States Army Reserve Command (USARC); Chief, Army Reserve; and Deputy Commanding General for Reserve Affairs, Forces Command.  Prior to assuming his present assignments in May, 1998, he was the USARC Deputy Commanding General.  MG Plewes was born in Zeeland, Michigan.  He graduated from Hope College, Holland, Michigan, with a bachelors Degree in Economics,  and received a Masters Degree in Economics from the George Washington University.

Major General Craig Bambrough, Deputy Commanding General of the U.S. Army Reserve Command (USARC), is a native of Ogden, Utah. Bambrough has more than 31 years of service and was commissioned in 1967. He is a graduate of the University of California with a bachelor's degree in business administration and of St. Mary's College where he earned a master of business administration degree.

Major General George W. Goldsmith Jr. assumed his present position as Deputy Commanding General (IMA) of the United States Army Reserve Command (USARC) and Commander, United States Army Reserve Readiness Command in June 1999. A native of Greenville, South Carolina, Goldsmith has more than 35 years of service and was commissioned in 1961 after graduating from The Citadel in Charleston, South Carolina.

Brigadier General Mitchell M. Zais, Chief of Staff of the U.S. Army Reserve Command (USARC), is a native of Fayetteville, N.C. Zais has more than 29 years of service and was commissioned in 1969 after graduating from the U.S. Military Academy, West Point.
