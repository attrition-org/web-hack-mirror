88th RSC, Fort Snelling, MN -- Minnesota, Wisconsin, Illinois, Indiana, Michigan & Ohio (612) 713-3011.  The mission of the 88th RSC is to provide ready units for active duty deployment at a moments notice. The bulk of the Total Army's CS and CSS (medical, transportation, quartermaster, signal, engineer, maintenance, military police, public affairs and port operations) units reside in the Army Reserve. In peacetime, unit members train for mobilization. They also participate in community projects and disaster relief operations in the communities where they live and work.

90th RSC, N Little Rock, AR -- Texas, New Mexico, Oklahoma, Arkansas & Louisiana (501) 771-8974/7900/8752 -- is one of 10 RSC organizations established in July 1995 to provide command and control of U.S. Army Reserve units in the continental United States. Officially activated in April, 1996, the 90th RSC was established with headquarters at Camp Pike Armed Forces Reserve Complex, located in North Little Rock, Arkansas. The RSC's are each assigned a geographic region which coincides with the Standard Federal Regions. The 90th RSC is the result of a major reorganization of the 90th and 122d Army Reserve Commands (ARCOMs) and the 420th Engineer and 807th Medical Brigades.

99th RSC, Oakdale, PA -- Pennsylvania, West Virginia, Maryland, Delaware & Virginia  (724) 693-2167 -- The 99th Regional Support Command is one of 11 RSC Major Subordinate Commands of the U.S. Army Reserve Command. It provides command and control of all assigned Army Reserve units in its area of operations. Further it provides administrative and logistical support to all designated but not assigned Army Reserve units. It is concerned with the mobilization and the reconstitution of reserve forces. It has major subordinate commands and direct reporting units throughout the region.
