The year 2000 (Y2K) is fast approaching and many people are becoming concerned about the possible effects of the "Millennium Bug". Simply stated, many computer programs and computer chips only use 2 digits to represent the year and will not work correctly when the year becomes "00". No one really knows how serious the impact of this event will be. There is a potential for interruptions to power, which could affect a number of things. For example, water pressure, traffic signals, and access to computers.

The City of Fresno is taking Y2K very seriously and is committed to guarding the public safety and bringing all of our technology into compliance. Our efforts began in January 1998 with a citywide survey to identify non-compliant hardware and software applications. Since that time we have been working diligently to upgrade or replace these systems as necessary. Our citywide Y2K project team is currently meeting every two weeks to centralize information, conduct contingency planning, and promote public awareness.

Thank You for your interest in this very important issue. Please continue to visit this site for monthly status reports, notification of special events, personal preparedness information, and more as we count down to the year 2000!!
