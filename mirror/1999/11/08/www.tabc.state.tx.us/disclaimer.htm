Privacy Policy

Our privacy policy is clear: We will collect no personal information about you when you visit our web site unless you choose to provide that information to us. Here is how we handle information about your visit to our website:

Information Collected and Stored Automatically

If you do nothing during your visit but browse through the web site, read pages, or download information, we will gather and store certain information about your visit automatically. This information does not identify you personally. No information about TABC users/visitors will be sold, shared, or utilized in any commercial way. Our web server generates a file that documents all activity on our website, including, but not limited to the following information about your visit:

    1. The IP address or domain name from which you access our website;
    2. The type of browser and platform (operating system) used to access our site;
    3. The date and time you visited our site;
    4. The page(s) you visited;
    5. Any file(s) you downloaded;
    6. Any errors that you encountered; and
    7. The URL of the site that referred you to our website.

We use this information to help us make our site more useful to visitors -- to learn about the number of visitors to our site, what part of the site they are interested in, how long they are on our website, and the types of technology our visitors use. WE DO NOT TRACK OR RECORD INFORMATION ABOUT THE IDENTITY OF INDIVIDUALS AND THEIR VISITS.

If You Send Us Personal Information

If you send us e-mail, your identity and the contents of your message is covered by The Privacy Act. Be assured that:

    1. The information will not be shared with anyone not on the staff of the Texas Alcoholic Beverage Commission.
    2. Your e-mail address will not be provided to any third party or used for any electronic mailing lists - Government or private.
    3. We do not create individual profiles with the e-mail information you provide or to give it to any other organizations.
    4. The e-mail material, including your e-mail address and/or name will in no way correlated or linked to the material that is automatically collected as described above.
    5. TABC does not collect information for commercial marketing.

The only exceptions to the above would be pursuant to a bona fide Court Order.

The use we will make of your information is to:

    1. Consider your suggestions.
    2. Possibly respond directly to you for clarification.
    3. Process your request for materials if you make one.
    4. Try and answer your questions if you ask them.
    5. Distribute TABC material with our list server (Requires you to join our Internet mailing list).

Use of Information Provided

Information provided through TABC’s web site is not intended as a replacement for the detailed information found in the Texas Alcoholic Beverage Code, Texas Alcoholic Beverage Commission Rules or any pending legislation, regulation or other statute, nor is it intended to replace the advice of legal counsel. The web site simply offers a selection of brochures, bulletins, manuals and news releases, as well as selections from and summaries of statutes and regulations, to help consumers and the industry with some of the more common questions about alcoholic beverages and the regulation of alcoholic beverages in Texas.

This web site includes some language from the Texas Alcoholic Beverage Code, as adopted by the Texas Legislature, and the Texas Alcoholic Beverage Commission Rules, which includes regulations adopted by the Commissioners of the TABC.
