The Advisory Committee on Participatory Democracy is created by State law with the expressed purpose of identifying and proposing solutions to any problem concerning the level of participatory democracy, increasing or facilitating the interaction of citizens with governing bodies, and improving the operation of government. The Committee maintains a repository of records related to its subject matter at the Nevada State Library and Archives.

Appointed by the Director of the Department of Museums, Library and Arts, the Committee is comprised of Nevadans from all walks of life, representing political and geographic balance. The Committee advises the director on special projects such as election training and get-out-the-vote programs, awards for citizen participation, and humanities-based outreach projects.

For information about the records repository or special projects under consideration, contact the Director's Office at 687-8393.
