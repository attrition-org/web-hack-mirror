Career Planning Tools & Resources

Newfoundland and Labrador:

  • Employment Counselling Services offered at each HRCC office
  • Career Information Resource Centres (CIRC's)
  • Skills Assessment and Training Center (S.A.T. Centre)

Canada:

  • Canada Prospects - includes a wide range of articles and quizzes about the world of work.
  • Career Considerations - describes 90 occupational groups and provides examples of occupations within each.
  • Career Directions - provides detailed information on 160 occupations which do not require a university education.
  • Career Moves - helps high school students explore occupational and career possibilities.
  • Career News - this University of Manitoba publication contains a lot of very interesting and informative articles that are still timely.
  • Career Planning from Canada WorkInfoNet.
  • The Edge - is a magazine for youth that discusses career development and understanding oneself.
  • Job Futures - Canada - this publication is the national complement to the provincial guide.
  • Job Search Techniques - the Youth Resource Network provides information on job preparation for youth.
  • News & Events - the National Association of Colleges & Employers provides their own perspective for job seekers, employers, and career services professionals.
  • Sectoral Activities - contains information about a wide variety of industries and our partnerships with them.
  • Work Search - is a guided tour of the work search process. Plan to spend some time here!

International:

  • Career Magazine - NCS Inc. keeps us in touch with the latest trends
  • Career Web Bookstore - Landmark Communications provides us with a library full of good information for workers and employers.
  • The Catapult - the springboard to career- and job-related sites that career services professionals from around the world have found helpful in working with college students and alumni, courtesy of William & Mary College.
  • What Color is Your Parachute? - The Net Guide version of this popular manual contains pages of employment- and career-related links.
