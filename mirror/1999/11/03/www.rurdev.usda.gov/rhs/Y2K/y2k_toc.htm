Are you ready for the year 2000?


For the last several decades, computer programmers have designated years by the last two digits. For example, 1975 was designated by the number 75. This was done to save electronic storage space and reduce operating costs. In such a format, however, the year 2000 is indistinguishable from the year 1900. Software and systems experts nationwide are concerned that this could cause systems to malfunction in unforeseen ways, or to fail completely. This problem has been dubbed the "millennium bug," or the "Y2K bug."
