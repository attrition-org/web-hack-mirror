Overview


SBA's ONE STOP CAPITAL SHOP (OSCS )

WHAT?

the U.S. Small Business Administration's contribution to President Clinton's Empowerment Zone/Enterprise Community Initiative.

WHY?

to provide the tools for small businesses to get started, stay in business, and expand. Strong small businesses will provide sustainable economic growth which is one of major objectives of the initiative.

HOW?

by bringing the SBA's business development and capital resources together with other federal, state, local and private sector resources in one convenient location. The collocation is a redeployment of existing resources which will not only provide convenience to the customer but it will also help leverage resources.

PROCESS

for determining what the OSCS will contain and how it will be run will come from the community with consultation from the SBA and other participants. The communities will be "stake holders" and will be responsible for housing, managing and maintaining the OSCS.

SBA

one of many participants. The SBA will make available its resource partners such as Service Corps of Retired Executives (SCORE), Small Business Development Centers (SBDCs) as well as those delivering its financial programs such as 7(a) lenders, Small Business Investment Companies (SBICs), microlenders, and Certified Development Companies (CDCs).

The OSCS should redeploy, not duplicate, existing technical and financial assistance to maximize effectiveness and supplement assistance where there is a need.

  6. What is the source of funds and how can they be obtained?
  7. How will the organization be structured and governed?
  8. What organizations will participate?
  9. Who will be responsible for the day to day management?
  10. How will clients be handled?
  11. How will results be measured?
