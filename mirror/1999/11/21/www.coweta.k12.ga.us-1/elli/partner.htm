Partnerships

  1. State University of West Georgia - Carrollton
    • Research and evaluation
    • Training development and instruction
    • Media production
    • Personel
      • Dr. Barbara McKenzie Chair, Department of Research, Media, and Technology
      • Project coordination Dr. Elizabeth Kirby Assistant Professor, Department of Research, Media, and Technology Media resources and Distance Learning
      • Dr. Elaine Roberts Assistant Professor, Department of Early Childhood Education and Reading
      • Reading specialist Dr. Tom Davison (retired) Brain research and Reading specialist
      • Dr. Sharon Newbill Assistant Professor, Department of Research, Media, and Technology Evaluation
  2. State University of West Georgia - Newnan (Shenandoah) Center
    • Provide GSAMS access
    • Local, customizable, training facilities and staffing "24-hour" access center concept
  3. Coweta Association for the Advancement of Technology (CAAT) - a community partnership group that works to promote technology use and community networking efforts for the good of all citizens in the area.
    • Help provide coordination and communications of community technology project goals
    • Provide mentors for Community Access Centers
    • Help promote awareness of the project
  4. Newnan Water and Light services
    • Continue partnership with school system in building and subsidizing wide-area fiber optic network
    • Continue "public service" aspect of business plan by helping setup and sponsor Community Access Centers
    • Provide schools free access to cable TV and a dedicated channel for school system use.
    • Continue to be strong CAAT supporter of building a community-wide network
  5. Newnan-Coweta Public Library
    • Act as a Community Access Center
    • Promote adult literacy training efforts
  6. Newnan Times-Herald newspaper and cable TV channel
    • Promote project efforts and awareness, such as adult literacy, via print, cable and web site promotion
  7. Alliance for Children's Enrichment - a partnership group of various social services, governmental and educational agencies which work together to coordinate services and resources.
    • Help coordinate parental involvement and social issues associated with the project
  8. United We Stand - a community action and mentoring group strongly supported by project area churches.
    • Coordinate survey among members to provide Community Access Center sites
    • Mentor at Community Access Centers
    • Promote project goals through access to its membership
  9. Coweta County Certified Literate Community initiative
    • Help coordinate and promote on-line literacy training for adults
  10. Coweta County Prekindergarten Advisory Council
    • Coordinate the private/public pre-kindergarten training/resource sharing project component
  11. Newnan Housing Authority
    • Provide access to office space to setup Community Access Centers in housing projects
  12. Local civic groups
    • Promote project awareness and provide literacy mentors

In addition to these partnership groups, we envision these methods such as these being used to involve parents in the learning process:

"How to reinforce reading skills" cable TV literacy instruction

Community Access Centers being used for joint student/parent training

Home-based on-line access for parents and students to project resources
