REFERRAL PROCEDURES FOR THE

GIFTED EDUCATION PROGRAM

1999 – 2000

To ensure that all students have an opportunity to be referred for the REACH Program for Gifted Students, the Coweta County School System offers two referral periods during which time students may be referred for consideration.

October 1 – 31: Grades 3 – 12

January 1 – 31: Grades K – 2

Students may be referred who:

  1. Attended Coweta County schools last year but were not referred or evaluated,
  2. Entered Coweta County Schools this school year from another state or from a private or home school.

A teacher, counselor, administrator, parent/guardian, or the student him/herself can make a referral. Parents may request a Student Referral for Consideration form in the office at each school during the month of October. The Student Referral for Consideration forms must be returned to the teacher of gifted at the school by October 31 for students in grades 3 – 12 and January 31 for grades K - 2.

To be eligible for gifted program services, a student must meet specific levels of superior performance in the areas of mental ability, achievement, creativity, and motivation as measured by standardized, school system-administered evaluation instruments.

Many students were referred, considered and found not eligible during the1998 – 99 school year. System staff will begin automatically reconsidering these students’ achievement data in September 1999 for eligibility. If any other testing is needed for these reconsidered students, their parents will be notified.

If you have any questions regarding the referral or eligibility procedures for the gifted education program in Coweta County Schools, please contact the designated Teacher of Gifted in your student’s school.
