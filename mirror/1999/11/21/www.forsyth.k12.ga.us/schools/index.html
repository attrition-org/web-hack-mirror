The Forsyth County Board of Education Believes...

The Forsyth County school district serves approximately 15,560 students system-wide.  All 20 schools are accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools and are Standard Schools as defined and measured by the Georgia Department of Education.

  • The Forsyth County School System is embraced by a progressive community that supports a quality education for all students.
  • Our schools are experiencing ever-increasing growth rates (10% for the 1998-99 school year, and 9% for the 1999-00 school year) as more and more families are attracted to our smaller, more caring schools where the type of personalized education they are seeking for their children is available.
  • The cohesiveness of the rapidly changing community is evidenced by the overwhelming support (78.6%) of a massive $55 million bond referendum to build new schools, upgrade and expand existing schools and implement state-of-the-art technology throughout the system. Again, in 1997 the community supported a 1% tax to build new schools and expand technology.
  • North Middle and South Middle Schools have been replaced with new facilities.   Otwell has been named a National Blue Ribbon School, a Georgia School of Excellence and a Georgia School of the Future.  Vickery Creek Middle opened in a new facility for the 1998-99 school year.  All four schools offer a program rich in opportunities for the middle school learner to grow socially, emotionally and intellectually.
  • Our three high schools offer a wide range of college prep and advanced placement courses along with an expansive tech prep program that includes an opportunity for students to participate in structured apprenticeship programs in a growing number of career fields.   Numerous opportunities in extra-curricular, athletic and academic activities are available.
  • Forsyth County Schools have a proven record of excellence.  A growing commitment to providing the best facilities, the best instruction and the latest technology in every classroom creates a learning environment that makes parents, teachers and students proud to call Forsyth County home.
