A message about New York State agriculture from
Governor George E. Pataki:

Governor's Press Releases

Country Folks Article 8-24-98

The heritage of farming runs rich through the culture of New York State. Agriculture is a vital segment of our economy and our communities. We want to make sure it remains that way. That is why we're working so hard to give farmers in New York a level playing field as they compete in the burgeoning worldwide marketplace.

In just five years we have:

  • cut farm property taxes up to 100 percent,
  • cut workers' compensation rates for farmers more than 25 percent,
  • strengthened New York's agricultural district laws,
  • significantly reduced energy costs,
  • positioned NYS to be the next member of the North East Interstate Dairy Compact,
  • preserved thousands of acres of farmland for future generations,
  • introduced innovative marketing programs promoting our homegrown products.

As we progress toward the new millennium, you can be sure we will vigorously maintain our commitment to agriculture and New York's farm families.

New York farmers are leaders in supplying the State, the nation and the world with safe, wholesome food. Through our continued efforts we will ensure our children and grandchildren also enjoy New York's wonderful agricultural bounty.
Thank you.

  Very Truly Yours,

George E. Pataki
