SUBJECT:       STATUS REPORT -- ISSUES RELATED TO NEW SCHOOL START TIMES AT BASCOMB ES, BOSTON ES, OAK GROVE ES, E. T. BOOTH MS AND ETOWAH HS

Each year, the Superintendent must make recommendations to the School Board for school start times. That decision is influenced by many factors, including:

·        Elementary students who have to wait in the dark for a bus.

·        Transportation costs (current costs are over $7,000,000 annually; and the State reimburses the School District only $2,300,000).

·        Recommendations of Boundary Focus Groups (e.g., it was requested by last year’s Boundary Focus Group that, if possible, E. T. Booth Middle School students ride separate buses from Etowah High School students).

·        Transportation route changes to accommodate new grade configurations (e.g., the Chapman 5th and 6th Grade Center).

·        Budget considerations/limitations when also considering the funding of textbooks, safety and security measures, employee compensation, transportation and other critical needs (recognizing that, in all of these areas, needs far exceed available revenue).

Several strategies were initially implemented to address the impact of this year’s time changes:

·        A Before School Program was established to accommodate students and parents. Activities in this program include Spanish, art, music, story time, board games, reading activities and computer studies.

·        Additional buses were added to separate middle and high school students.

Additional accommodations have been made since the start of the school year:

·        Several bus routes were modified/refined to address extraordinary traffic congestion and other issues.

·        Student release patterns at the Etowah/Booth complex were reconfigured to improve ingress and egress.

·        After the first few weeks of school, the initial length of the school day at Bascomb, Boston, and Oak Grove was able to be shortened by 15-30 minutes.

·        Plans are underway to expand the after school intramural program at Booth MS to include additional activities for interested youngsters.

A staff review of the literature and research on sleep patterns, as they relate to school start times, has provided some data and information that can be useful to schools and parents; even though the research is inconclusive as far as establishing a “best school start time” for students at a particular grade level(s). As a result, we are considering the advisability of incorporating sleep-related education into health and drivers’ education courses so all of our students can learn about the consequences of lack of sleep. Some parents may need this information, as well.

Most of the sleep literature focuses on students in grades 7-12.  The researchers contacted stated that there has been very little data generated involving elementary school start times.  Other implications of sleep research are summarized in the attached literature review. Sleep research experts were contacted at the National Sleep Foundation and other research centers.  In addition, we arranged for an external review of our research summaries, which was conducted by Dr. John H. Neel, Department of Education Policy Studies at Georgia State University.  His comments are attached for your information, as well. 

We are attempting to do the best we can with limited funds, overcrowded schools, limited access to school campuses and unprecedented traffic congestion… none of which were caused by the School District.  We have heard from fewer than 50 parents of the 5,210 students at Bascomb ES, Boston ES, Oak Grove ES, Booth MS and Etowah HS on this issue.  The vast majority support the new hours.  However, to gain even more information in this regard, I have instructed the Office of Accountability, Technology and Strategic Planning to conduct surveys in the spring to solicit input from affected students, parents, and staff regarding the new start times.  We will share the results of those surveys with the Board and public and will use that data to work toward minimizing concerns/problems and to develop next year’s recommendations for school start times.
