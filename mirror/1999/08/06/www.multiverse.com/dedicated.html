    • DDS-56 [56KB] Leased Line

    The perfect starting point for most businesses, a 56KB Leased Line circuit will provide your corporation with a 24 hour-a-day, 7 days-per-week digital connection to the Internet. Unlike modems, which are susceptible to such telephone company conditions as line noise, dropouts, surges and dips, 56KB circuits are "conditioned" to provide a seamless, clean connection. An added advantage of the 56KB circuit over modems is that it is digital as opposed to analog, which gives you synchronous data with sometimes as low as 10% of the "lag" you would experience over Analog Modems.

    ISDN [112/128KB] Connection

    A great alternative to a 56KB connection (especially for residential areas) is ISDN. An acronym for Integrated Services Digital Network, ISDN uses a two-wire, "three channel" method of transmitting data. While this may not be the perfect solution for all businesses because of Ameritech's per-minute tariffs, it proves again and again to be the best option for residentially-operated areas. ISDN Centrex®, a service of Ameritech, "bypasses" minutely rates if you are terminating a circuit within the same "C.O." - Contact a Multiverse sales or technical representative for more information.

    Fractional T1/T1
    [384Kbps/1.54Mbps]

    To date, the fastest and best way to transmit large amounts of data is to go with a Fractional or Full T1 circuit. A switch/relay/repeater system, T1 service provides you with unparalleled speed, efficiency and bandwidth. At about 10ms (0.01 second) "lag" time and bandwidth up to 1.5 Megabits per second, T1 service is the number one choice of corporations and Internet providers/resellers today.
