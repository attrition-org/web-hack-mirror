Accessibility

All markup language here has been designed to make these pages accessible to people with disABILITES and functional with assistant technologies in conformance with W3C standards, i.e., screen readers, screen magnifiers, speech synthesizers, etc. I've used text links in lieu of image links to provide site navigation with non-graphical browsers (Lynx ET AL) and all pages are equally readable with and without style sheets as per level A standards. Fonts are also generally much larger, to improve readability for those with visual disABILITIES. I've generally used softer colors for better contrast and I do not use flash and other multimedia tricks which would require special plugins and sometimes cause software conflicts. Every effort has been made to insure compliance with W3C net standards for accessibility. And, all pages have been validated for correct XHTML markup language and CSS2 type stylesheets.
