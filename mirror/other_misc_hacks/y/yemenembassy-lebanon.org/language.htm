The ancient South Arabian language belonged to the southern group of Semitic languages and has disappeared except for some evidence remaining in the dialects spoken on Socotra Island and in Al-Mahrah Governorate . The beautiful archaic Sabaean script has fallen into oblivion as well; only the Amharic script of Ethiopia, a Sabaean colony in antiquity, still bears some resemblance to it. Arabic, another language of the Semitic family, was spoken in Mecca at the time of the Prophet Muhammad (P) . It became the language of Islam and was adopted by the south Arabians, together with the new religion .
