Islam permeates every aspect of Yemeni life . Islam forms the basis of the constitution and the judiciary system. While Christianity ceased to exist in Yemen probably before the 10th century. There is a small population of Yemeni Jews still living in the northern areas of Yemen. There are two major Islamic denominations in Yemen: the Zaydis in the north and the Shafa’is in the south. No definite border line divides them. In religious practices, the Shiite Zaydi and the Sunni Shafa’i denominations vary only slightly, and both groups use the same mosques and marry each other in normal life.
