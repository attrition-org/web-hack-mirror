I. Leagues

Leagues will consist of 10 teams. Each league will have two divisions (East and West).

II. Cost

This league is open by invitation only! There is no cost for a team to those who were invited!

III. Scoring Method

You will fight "head to head" against your foe for a week. The week begins on Monday and finishes on Sunday. Scoring will be as follows:

Batters
1B + 2B * 2 + 3B * 3 + HR * 4 + RBI + RUN + SB * 2 +BB - E - SO

Pitchers
IP - ER + SO + W * 5 + SV * 3 - L * 3

For pitchers who start one game during the week, their fantasy points per game (FPPG) count toward your total score for the week. For the rest of the team (batters and relievers) the total fantasy points (TFP) for your starters for the week will count towards your total score. Only your starters will count toward your total points for the week. That is, your 8 position batters and one DH (of any position) plus your 6 pitchers. Your starting pitchers can be relievers or starters, there is no difference.

In the case that one of your starters in certain positions score 0 TFP, they will be replaced by an alternate

IV. Draft

The draft will be held at TBD at http://fsn.thetop.net/draft.

a. Live Draft


The live draft will occur at the time specified. It will be conducted via a special draft page via Netscape. You must be present at the URL page at the time of the draft and for the duration of the draft. There are no exceptions!! If you do not show up for the draft your team will be forfeited. You will be given 3 minutes to make each selection. Drafting will be done in the format 1-10,10-1 for each two rounds. More information on players that must be drafted, will be announced. rosters for more information on what your roster may be like after the draft.

V. Roster

Rosters will be set to 20 players. You can pick up free agents and make trades. You can place a player on DL if they are listed on the injury page (on your team page) as being on the 15 or 60 day DL. As soon as the palyer comes off the DL you must drop any one player from your roster to make it legal.

a. Roster Size

Your team will never exceed 20 players, not including those on the DL. These players can be of any position, but you must have at least one of every position. (min 6 pitchers, max 10 pitchers)

b. Starters

You must start a 1B, 2B, 3B, SS, C, 3 OF, and 6 pitchers (starting pitchers or relief pitchers)

c. Alternates

Each week you must choose at least 3 alternates for your starters. Alternates will be a Corners Alternate who can replace your 1B or 3B positions (alternate must be a 3B or 1B), a Middle Alternate who can replace your 2B and SS positions (alternate must be a 2B or SS) and an OF Alternate. The batting alternates are required. You may also have a Starting Pitcher and Relief Pitcher alternate if you wish to have one.

d. Free Agent

Free agents will be made available immediately following the draft. All free agent moves are first come first serve. You can pick up free agents using the rankings list for your league. Each league will have their own rankings list. Go to your league page and click on "Rankings and Free Agents". A player with a "checkbox" next to his name is a free agent. You can request to pick up this player using the automated form. Just enter your password at the bottom of the page and check those free agents you wish to take. This will write out to a transaction file, and your moves will be evaluated prior to making them official. You must drop a player from your roster before you pick up another player if your roster is full with 20 players.

VI. Trades

Trades can be made at anytime by anyone with any other team. A trade must be sent via the email form listed on the team pages by BOTH teams. A trade is not official until the commish recieves the form from both teams.

a. Trade Complaints

A trade complaint occurs when a team feels that a trade that has been completed is not good for the league and is extremely unfair. Realize, that this does not mean that the trade was good or bad for one team or the other, but that the trade is so unfair that it makes the league less competitive. (This is mainly to keep two friends from stacking one team by trading Shaq for Charlie Ward, etc.)

The process for a trade complaint is as follows:

1) Usually, as the commish, I will decide, when the complaint comes in, if it is worthy of a vote to the league. I may feel that the trade is fair and will not put it to a vote, or I might move to step 2. A complaint must be registered within 48 hours of the completion of the trade. If my team is involved in the complaint we will move directly to step 2.

2) A preliminary vote will occur here. If 4/8 of the teams that weren't involved in the trade feel the trade hurts the league, we will move to step 3, otherwise the complaint is void. Once the issue is put to vote, the league has until Midnight that night to vote on the issue. Only a negative (or vote that the trade is unfair) is needed, otherwise just don't vote.

3) If 4/8 teams see the trade as unfair there will be a defense for each team. First the team that challenged the bid will write a reason for the complaint and send it to the league. Then, one or both of the teams involved in the trade will make their defense.

4) Final vote - The last step is a final vote. 5/8 teams must vote for the trade to be nullified for it to occur.

The higher wildcard will play the division champion with the worse overall record of the two (East and West). Seeding for the playoffs will be as follows:

  1. Overall Record
