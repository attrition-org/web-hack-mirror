Alien Voices
A Leonard Nimoy / John de Lancie Enterprise

Alien Voices is a multi-media production company created by Leonard Nimoy and John de Lancie that is dedicated to quality dramatizations of classic science fiction. In 1996, these former "Star Trek" actors began their Alien Voices enterprise by writing, producing, and fully dramatizing state-of-the-art audio plays. By the spring of 1997, the first three Alien Voices audio productions of H.G. Wells': "The Time Machine", Jules Verne's: "Journey to the Center of the Earth," and Sir Arthur Conan Doyle's "The Lost World" were published by industry leader Simon & Schuster Audio. Since then, additional titles, "First Men in the Moon" , "The Invisible Man" and "Spock vs. Q" have been added to the Alien Voices audio collection.

In addition to Simon & Schuster Audio, Alien Voices work has attracted such media groups as the Sci-Fi Channel, National Public Radio (NPR), New Line Television, Dramatic Publishing, Rhino Home Video, and most recently the popular Internet group, Real Networks.

For television, The Alien Voices company has written and produced "First Men in the Moon"; "The Lost World", and "The Halloween Trilogy" (which included Kipling's "Mark of the Beast"; Wilde's "Canterville Ghost"; Poe's "Cask of Amontillado"). The productions aired on The Sci-Fi Channel and were highly praised as "Inspired Television". Video productions are now available through the Alien Voices store.

In 1998, National Public Radio (NPR) fed 10 hours of Alien Voices' audio to their affiliates nationwide. The programming ran heavily during the Halloween and Thanksgiving holidays, and received a great deal of recognition.

Alien Voices' scripts are published by Dramatic Publishing for performance by theatre groups and schools.

The casts of Alien Voices have included actors who have appeared in various "Star Trek" television and feature films series, among them (alphabetically): Susan Bay, Roxann Dawson, Richard Doyle, Robert Ellenstein, Marnie Mosiman, Ethan Phillips, Andrew Robinson, Dwight Schultz, William Shatner, Jean Simmons and Armin Shimerman. Incidentally, this marks the first time that actors and actresses drawn from all "Star Trek" generations have performed together in single productions. Nimoy and de Lancie headline each drama.
