Susan Bay

Susan Bay is not only an actress, she's an accomplished producer and director, as well. In the theater circuit, she has performed in over 40 stage productions, among them "Picnic," "Third City," starring Paul Mazursky, "Calm Down Mother," "Lion in Winter," "The Shadow Box," and "Love Letters," in which she co-starred with husband, Leonard Nimoy.

For television, she has appeared in numerous pilots and more than 40 national commercials. Most recently, Prudential Health Care, Mitsubishi, Hanes, Tropicana Orange Juice, and Fidelity Investments.

As a producer, she worked for Paisley Productions and Dove Films, both television commercial companies, at Presson Productions where she produced the film, "Survival," and at Ten Four Productions/Paramount, and New World Television where she developed series and two hour movies.

Her directing credits include the TV series "All that Glitters," educational films and many stage plays, among them "Black Comedy," "The Harmfulness of Tobacco," and "Letters from Earth".

Her favorite production to date is her 14-year-old son, Aaron, who inspired the Lifetime Cable Network series Susan co-created, co-produced, and starred in, "Mommy, Daddy and Me."
