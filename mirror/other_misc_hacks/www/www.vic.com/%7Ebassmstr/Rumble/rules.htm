1999 Rumble at the River Rules
1 Tournament is open to B.A.S.S. National Federation Members or minor dependents less than 16 years old (minors under 16 are not eligible to win boat) and Federation Clubs Only - Winning clubs and individuals will be required to verify federation affiliation with a current member card or a B.A.S.S. Times with mailing label affixed with members name legible.
2 Eligible fish shall be largemouth, smallmouth, kentucky, or spotted bass of a minimum legal length of 14" measured with mouth closed and jaw intact.
3 Each bass submitted for weighing at the weigh-in site that does not meet the minimum legal length shall be assessed a penalty at the rate of one pound for each illegal fish submitted.
4 A late penalty for any late boat shall be assessed at the rate of one pound per minute for the first 15 minutes after which the late boat shall be disqualified.
5 A penalty of one pound will be assessed for all dead fish.
6 Limit of 5 fish per boat of eligible fish in rule number 2.  No boat limit of bass shall exceed 5 bass at any time.  NO CULLING AT THE RAMP.
7 The sponsors of this tournament assume no liability for any participant, bystander, participant's property or bystander's property at any time.
8 No participant shall be under the influence of alcoholic drink or under the influence of drugs preceding or during the tournament.  EXCEPTION:   Prescribed medication taken in the prescribed manner, and does not affect the safety of the person taking medication and/or other.
9 All eligible/legal bass shall be caught on the designated tournament lake during the designated tournament hours in strict accordance with these tournament rules.
10 Only artificial lures may be used.  The possession of or the usage of live bait is illegal and strictly prohibited.
11 Trolling is illegal and strictly prohibited.
12 Participants shall stay in their respective boats while in the act of fishing.
13 Use of electronic communications to share information to team members during tournament hours prohibited.
14 Boat safety standards shall comply with U.S. Coast Guard regulations.
15 Each person shall have the respective state fishing license or permit where this tournament is held.
16 Tournament officials have the authority to determine legal/illegal fishing equipment and, at their discretion, inspect live wells, storage lockers, and/or other suspected areas where illegal items may be found, at any time before, during or preceding payback time of tournament.  Failure to comply with tournament officials requests pertaining to this tournament and its participants shall result in automatic disqualification form tournament with loss of entry fees.
17 The possession or usage of trammel nets, seines, and other nets used primarily for commercial fishing purposes are illegal and strictly prohibited in this tournament.
18 Respective state regulations pertaining to sport fishing that are not covered in these tournament rules shall apply also.
19 Participants are to keep a 50 yard courtesy distance from other boats whose occupants are in the act of fishing.
20 In the event of a tie, the bag with largest total number of fish weighed in will be declared the winner, in the event there is still tie then the winnings for the place in question and the next place will be split between the tied participants.
21 Taxes due for any winnings or draw prizes are the sole responsibility of the winning individual and/or club.
22 Must be present to be eligible for any draw prized during the reverse drawing for the 18½ foot Hawk boat.
23 Tournament officials may change or alter these rules prior to tournament time.
