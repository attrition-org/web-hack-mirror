"I'VE NEVER NOTICED A PROBLEM WITH MY EQUIPMENT"

Actually, you probably have, but odds are you've attributed it to sources other than power problems. Those times when your PC seizes up, or files are corrupted inexplicably, may not be software problems. There are three types of surge damage: glitches, hard failures, and latent failures. Glitches usually result in temporary damage or lost data and do not cause permanent damage. Hard failures cause problems significant enough that you must repair or replace electrical components. Latent failures usually happen after continuous exposure to smaller surges, which leads to deterioration of equipment and performance. In most cases, the end result is hard failure and you may never know how it happened.

As I mentioned earlier, an over-voltage situation -- like lightning -- seems to be what people fear the most concerning their equipment.

We encourage them to be more paranoid: "noise" is another power problem. It can come from any simple electrical device: office machines, transformer, dimmer switch. This invisible force may not bother you much when it comes in the form of semi-annoying lines across your TV screen, but it can cause damage to your office equipment and corrupt data transmissions. Thankfully, most surge suppressors also feature filters for electrical-line noise and radio frequency interference.
