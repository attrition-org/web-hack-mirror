StarMedia Chief Says Content Is Not King
Mo Krochmal
TechWeb

CONTENT IS NOT KING, said the chairman of the Latin American Internet portal StarMedia Network (New York) at a conference on Latin American Internet commerce Wednesday.

"The experience is king," said Fernando Espuelas of StarMedia, which serves Latin America with online information in Portuguese and Spanish. Espuelas was keynoting the Jupiter Communications Internet Commerce Forum in Miami.

StarMedia is the early leader in providing Internet services to Latin America, a sector that is growing rapidly. The online population in Latin America is expected to grow to 66 million out of a total population of 500 million by 2005 from 10.6 million in 1999, according to Jupiter Communications (New York), a market researcher.

"A great user experience is the driver," Espuelas said. "Content is a commodity, it is a small part of the picture. The idea that content is a driver of this is flawed. There are many content generators. Audience aggregators are harder to find. New media is driven by interactivity, and users are much more sophisticated than we give them credit for."

Espuelas, who founded StarMedia in 1996 and took it public in 1999, reignited an issue that many companies in the United States might have seen as settled--that content, the information that the Internet makes so easy to find, is the critical reason driving people to use the Internet and the reason that America Online Inc. (AOL, Dulles, Va.) acquired Time Warner Inc. (New York).

Others disagreed with Espuelas.

"If you don't have meat on the bones, you eventually will have a brand that stands for nothing," said Jay Scharer, the chief operating officer at Cisneros Television Group, a Venezuela-based broadcaster serving Latin America. Cisneros is partnering with AOL to provide a Latin American Internet service.
