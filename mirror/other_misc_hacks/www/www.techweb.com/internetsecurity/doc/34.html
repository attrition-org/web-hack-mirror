Healthy Forecast For Firewall Market
Marcia Savage
Computer Reseller News

Frost & Sullivan, based in Mountain View, Calif., said the U.S. firewall market grew 59 percent last year and is expected to continue to grow at a compound annual rate of 38.7 percent over the next several years.

"The network-security industry is set to explode with a bright future ahead as e-business evolves into the newest of e-trends," said Jason Wright, Frost & Sullivan market analyst, in a prepared statement.

While large businesses have been the primary targets for firewall vendors, vendors now are targeting small and midsize businesses, which often cannot afford to invest in a firewall, the researcher said. Vendors are releasing scaled-down products for cost-conscious small offices that are beginning to realize the importance of protecting their networks.

Ramp Networks, in Santa Clara, Calif., which relies on integrators to sell and maintain the bulk of its products, recently launched a new program called Small Office Security (SOS). Participants in SOS can get access to hacking prevention tips and a $25 rebate on Ramp's new Webramp 700s, a device that includes firewall, virtual private networking, and content-filtering features.

The Webramp 700s costs $479 with a five-user license. The rebate is available with the purchase of a 25-user license.

Also targeting small business is NetGuard's new Guardian SOHO NT, which provides a firewall for small businesses with 10 or fewer users connected to a Windows NT 4.0 server for file and print serving.

The product is available via download from www.guardian-soh.com. The cost is $199 for a five-user license and $299 for a 10-user license.
