Key security policy tips

In an effort to avoid some of the bottlenecks, I'm providing some basic guidelines that you can apply when establishing information security policies. These guidelines can be useful to every company, even the smallest, because they all need some type of policy regarding the use of IT equipment and data.

No matter how sophisticated our systems become or how rapidly they change, security policies are not going to disappear. To think they will be gone or lose importance in the future is wishful thinking. These policies don't have to be fancy, but they do need to be complete. Follow the six guidelines listed here, and you'll be amazed at how much simpler establishing and maintaining a security area will become.

Key Security Policy Tips

1. Write policies in a language that people understand. Whether it's English or some other native language, use basic terminology. When using technical terms, define them clearly.

2. Be clear about what's acceptable and what's not regarding the use of data, systems and related equipment. If you don't want your employees using company e-mail for "unacceptable" reasons, such as surfing the Internet for personal reasons, or bringing visitors to secured company facilities, be sure to tell them those rules. Not only should you publish the policies, you should also discuss them with employees.

3. Define standards for dealing with systems operations, system errors, outages, emergencies, upgrades, violations, change management and so on. Document those standards and make them readily available to staff.
