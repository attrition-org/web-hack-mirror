The Department of Music

There are three options for majors in music at Cumberland College:

Music Education--This option focuses on the training and certification of music educators. With a bachelor's degree in music education, you'll be qualified to teach music in grades K-12. Besides being prepared to enter the educational marketplace, you'll be prepared to enter graduate school to pursue a master's degree.

Church Music--The church music program offers training in and exploration of music as it relates to the worship service. You will be trained not only to work with adults but also to help children and young people further their enjoyment of music and understanding of God's plan. Students majoring in church music will receive the Bachelor of Music degree.

Bachelor of Arts in Music--This program will provide you with sufficient training in applied music to pursue continued study on the graduate level.

No matter which option you choose, you'll have access to fine facilities, including three lecture rooms, nine studios, a band rehearsal hall, a choral rehearsal hall, 16 practice rooms, and a music education classroom complete with a variety of teaching series and multiple Orff instruments. There is a 150-seat recital hall, an electronic piano lab, a professional 16-track recording studio, and a computer-assisted instruction laboratory, plus a music sequencing/synthesizer laboratory.
