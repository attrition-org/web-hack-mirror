The Department of Education

Here's a quick overview of your options in our Department of Education:

Early elementary education (K-4)_designed for those students who are primarily interested in teaching young children.

Middle school education (5-8)_designed for those students interested in teaching middle school or junior high school students. With this program, you'll focus on two subject areas.

Education of exceptional children (Learning and Behavioral Disorders which include learning disabled, emotionally disturbed, mildly handicapped, and mildly orthopedically handicapped)_designed for those students desiring certification to teach exceptional children in grades K-12. Because of the strong supporting curriculum in elementary education, those persons choosing this major will be endorsed to teach in the regular classroom, grades K-4 or 5-8.

Education of exceptional children (Learning and Behavioral Disorders Grades 7-12)_designed for those students desiring certification to teach exceptional children (L&BD) in grades 7-12. It shall be accompanied by at least one other specialization, either a teaching major or a teaching minor.

Secondary education_The Department of Education in cooperation with other college departments offers courses leading to certification to teach in grades 9-12.

All of our Education students benefit from professors who have experience both inside and outside the classroom and from access to our Instructional Media Library with its variety of teaching materials and media.

You'll also have an opportunity for field study, choosing from field assignments in several schools within the district. Your student teaching practicum is an involved 12-week internship where you'll really experience what life in the classroom is like.
