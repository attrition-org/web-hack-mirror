Staff Directory
       Floyd Stroud enters his seventh year as the Athletic Director at Cumberland College.  Stroud first came to Cumberland as a student in 1970.  While a student at CC, Stroud ran cross country and track and was named All Conference in 1972, 1973 and 1974.  He was also named Outstanding College Athlete in American Colleges and Universities in 1973 and 1974.
     Stroud was graduated from CC in 1974 with an area in business administration.  He continued his education with an M.A. in education from Union College. Stroud was named Assistant Dean of Men at Cumberland in 1974.  While holding this position, Stroud was a major force behind the chartering of the Fellowship of Christian Athletes at CC.  In 1980, Stroud was appointed Dean of Men and held that position until being named Athletic Director in 1993. 
     In 1992, Stroud restarted cross country and track programs that had been dropped in 1982.  He has returned the programs to national prominence.   His cross country teams have had top 20 finishes in the NAIA National Meet each of the past seven years while the track teams have slowly developed into one of the NAIA's best.  Coach Stroud has been named the Mid-South Conference Coach-of-the-Year every year since the Conference's formation.  He has also been named the Mid-South Region Coach-of-the-Year three of the last seven years. 
     Stroud is married to the former Geraldine Park of Rockholds, KY.   He and his wife have two daughter, Amy and Holly, and reside in Williamsburg.


     Julie Hill enters her sixth year as the Assistant Athletic Director at Cumberland College.  Julie first came to Cumberland in the fall of 1987 on a softball scholarship and was graduated in 1992 with a B.S. in health and physical education.  She continued her education at Eastern Kentucky University earning a Master's of Science in sports administration in 1993. 
      Julie became the Assistant Athletic Director in July 1993.  During her softball career at CC, she helped lead her team to the 1989 KIAC and KWIC Championship and a 32-4 record.  Julie was also named to the All-KIAC team in both 1989 and 1990 and the All-KWIC team in 1989 and 1991.  Julie served as an assistant softball coach in 1992 and 1994.  She was hired as the head coach in the spring of 1994.  As head coach, Julie accumulated a career record of 96-64 and coached her team to a 1994 KIAC Championship, and runner-up at the Mid-South Regional.  Julie retired from coaching after the birth of her second child to pursue administration full time.  She was named NAIA Mid-South Region Administrator of the year in 1999.
      Julie is married to former Indian basketball player, Tim Hill, and they have two children, Jalyn, 5, and Trevor, 3.
