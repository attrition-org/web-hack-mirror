Welcome To Indian Country

     Cumberland College offers a wide range of sports for both men and women.  Intercollegiate programs for men include football, basketball, tennis, golf, baseball, soccer, swimming, track, wrestling, judo, cross country and cheerleading.  Lady Indians compete in basketball, tennis, volleyball, soccer, cross country, softball, swimming, golf, judo, track and cheerleading.

     We have some of the finest athletic facilities for a small private college in the nation.  A new basketball arena was completed in 1990.  In 1995, a new football field, all-weather track, and soccer field were completed.  Baseball completed construction on a new field in the spring of 1998.

     Playing with great facilities, Cumberland athletes take their sports and academics seriously.  In the 1998-1999 school year, Cumberland had eighteen athletes to earn NAIA All American Honors and eleven athletes earned NAIA Scholar-Athlete All American Honors.  Cumberland was also represented in NAIA National Tournaments by the following teams:
     Men's Cross Country - 3rd
     Men's Indoor Track - 9th
     Men's Swimming - 4th
     Women's Swimming - 9th
     Men's Track - 14th
     Men's Tennis - 14th
     Women's Golf - 12th
As a whole, the Cumberland athletic program finished tied for 12th in the Sears Directors' Cup among NAIA Schools.

     Cumberland competes as a member of the National Association of Intercollegiate Athletics and the Mid-South Conference.  There is no lack of stiff competition for Indian teams.
