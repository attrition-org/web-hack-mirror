 1999-2000 LADY INDIANS VOLLEYBALL

      Cumberland College Women's Volleyball heads into the 1999-2000 season with high expectations. Coming off their best season in school history, the Lady Indians will be returning four All Mid-South Conference players and a contingency of young but experienced players. 

      The team will be led by returning Indians, Laura Walker and Amanda Walton, both team captains and first team All Conference members.  The Indians also have returning players Kara Clemenz, All Conference 2nd Team, and Nancy Addison, a middle hitter and an honorable mention selection. 

      With the loss of only two seniors from last year's team, and actually having eight of their top nine players returning, the team expects to improve on its 3rd place conference finish of last year.  With another outstanding recruiting class led by Jamie Selgo, a 5'11" All State selection from Ohio, and Heather Ramey, a 6' All State selection from Tennessee, the Lady Indians have been able to strengthen areas where they have been a little weak. 

      Coach Dale Walker entered his 3rd season feeling that the rebuilding process he started in the spring of 1997 is nearly complete.  The team started the 1999 season with excellent size and tremendous athleticism in every position. 

Key returning players:
 

Laura Walker 5'10' OH 3 Times All Conference - 1999 All Mid-South Region pick
Amanda Walton 5'11" OH/MH 1999 All Conference selection
Kara Clemenz 5'9" S 1999 2nd Team All Conference selection
Nancy Addison 5'11" MH/OH 1999 All Conference honorable mention
Julie Davis 6' MH Most Improved player past two seasons - has potential to make impact
Jessica Davis 6'2" MH/OH Injured all of last year - should be ready
