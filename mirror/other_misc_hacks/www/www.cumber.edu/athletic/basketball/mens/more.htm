1999-2000 Season Outlook

Cumberland College finished with a 23-11 record last season and reached the championship game of the Mid-South Tournament.  Most would call it a successful season, but for the Indians it fell just a little short of expectations.  The 23 wins fell two short of a team goal of winning 25 games, which Cumberland College has done ten times under Coach Randy Vernon.  The 63-58 loss to Georgetown in the finals of the conference tournament blocked another goal.  "The bottom line for us is we want to get to the National Tournament," Vernon said.

There will be a lot of new faces on the Cumberland College roster this season, but the goals will remain much the same.  Gone are starting guards, Tony Pietrowski and Dewayne Saulsberry.  Pietrowski holds the school's assist record.  Starting forwards Dan Emmons and Shawn Rhymer must also be replaced.  Damien Troupe departs after missing most of last season with a knee injury.  Those five seniors comprised most of the team's minutes, points, assists and rebounds.  The hardest things to replace could be the experience and basketball knowledge they brought to the team.

Ivan Johnson and Frank Brillhart have the most experience of the six returning players.  Brillhart (6'7", 225) is the team's only returning starter.  He led the team in rebounding last year at over seven per game.  Brillhart pulled down 20 rebounds in one game and reached double figures in rebounds several other times.  "The thing Frank can do best for us is rebound," Vernon said.  "Brillhart added some size and strength in the off season, which should help him battle with some of the bigger post players in the conference."

Johnson (6'0", 205) played the role of sixth man last year.  Johnson is both strong and athletic, which gives him a variety of ways to score offensively.  Johnson is best in the open court.  "As the season went on he earned more and more minutes," Vernon said.

The other returning players from last season include guards Calvin Damper and Mark Vernon.  Forwards John Mahal and Chris McGill return for their senior season.  Each of the four saw limited playing time last season.  Mark Vernon (6'1", 165) and Damper (6'2", 190) will add depth on the perimeter.  Mahal (6'6", 210) and McGill (6'7", 200) are expected to battle for playing time with a talented group of newcomers.

The majority of the Indians' roster will come from a group of eight recruits.  The class brings a lot of athletic ability and adds size to the roster.  There is also a question mark when you add so many new players to the mix.  "I just know from past experience when we try to break in that many it takes a while," Vernon said.

One interesting battle to watch will be between freshmen J.P. Storm and Shannon Miller at the point guard.  "I like both of them.  I think they're going to have a heck of a battle," Vernon said.  Miller (6'0", 165) comes to Cumberland from Lawrence Central High School in Indianapolis, Indiana.  He is a heady player who understands how to play the game.  His athletic ability impressed both his coach and teammates during the pre-season.  Storm (6'0", 175) scored over 1,500 points in his high school career at South Laurel, which ranks second in school history.

The only other freshman in the recruiting class is Marco Renz (6'8", 190) from Stuttgart, Germany.  Renz has a lot of potential once he adjusts to changes he faces on and off the court in making the move from Germany.

From the junior college ranks comes Jerry Williams (6'5", 210).  Williams is originally from Orlando, Florida.  He comes to the Indians from junior college power San Jacinto (TX).  The junior swingman could be the most athletic player the Indians have had in a number of years.  He averaged 13 points and 7 rebounds per game at San Jacinto playing both inside and on the perimeter.  "His strength is in transition and getting to the basket," Vernon said.  Williams will give the Indians a very athletic perimeter attack when combined with Johnson and one of the freshmen at point guard.  That may lead the Indians to turn up the tempo on offense.  "I think this team is going to have to push it up the floor a little more than some of my teams," Vernon said.

Another junior college transfer is Anthony Simmons from Frank Phillips Junior College (TX).  Simmons (6'6", 200) averaged 14 points and 7 rebounds as a two-year starter at Frank Phillips.  Simmons is also strong and athletic and capable of running the floor.  Jim Handlos (6'8", 195) played high school basketball at Wayne County (KY) but comes to the Indians from Allegheny Community College in Maryland.  Handlos has good fundamentals and is capable of stepping out to shoot the perimeter.  He has also developed a baby hook shot that is hard to defend.

Mario Hall comes to the Indians after red shirting last year at Hazard Community College.  Hall (6'7", 215) played his high school basketball at Owensboro Apollo where he was one of the top-rated players in the Third Region as a senior.  Peter Arigu (6'5", 200) from Nassarawa, Nigeria played last year at Bismark State Junior College (ND).  The hard-working Arigu could play either forward position.

The Indians' schedule doesn't feature many changes this year.  Along with the conference teams, there are the usual opponents like Pikeville, Transylvania and Brescia.  Cumberland will be playing in the American Host Tournament in Ashland, Ohio December 29-30.  They will open against Mount Vernon Nazarene and could face Ashland, probably a top-20 team in NCAA Division II.

Mitch Howard
Corbin News Journal
