We know that many families need help in paying college expenses. That's where financial aid comes in. Financial aid can close the gap between the cost of a college education and what you are expected to pay. College costs, which include tuition, room and board, books, transportation, and personal expenses, will vary from college to college. What you are expected to pay is determined by a number of factors, including your family's income and assets, taxes, medical and current living expenses, the number of dependents, and the number of children in college. Most schools use a standardized form to compute this amount.

No matter who you are, part of the cost of your education at Cumberland College has already been paid for you. Gifts from foundations, alumni, and friends, support from the Kentucky Baptist Convention, endowment income, and sound fiscal policies allow us to keep our costs low, much lower than the "market value" of the education we offer. In fact, Cumberland is one of the most affordable private, four-year colleges in America. Around 85% of our students receive some type of financial aid.

For the 1999-2000 school year, basic charges for a full-time student will be as follows:

Tuition and Fees: $9,480
Room and Board: $4,076
Total: $13,556

Cumberland College does not charge an additional tuition fee for students who are not residents of Kentucky.

Financial aid is designed to make up the difference between the cost of your education and what you are expected to pay. Normally, financial aid comes in a package that combines different types of assistance: scholarships, grants, low-interest loans, or opportunities for campus employment.

SCHOLARSHIPS

Students who meet special qualifications may receive financial aid in the form of a scholarship.

Presidential Scholarships are available for students with outstanding college entrance exam scores. An interview is required before the award is made.
Program Title: Presidential Scholarship.
Amount: Up to $7,600 per year, no repayment required.
Eligibility: Based on student's ACT or SAT and high school GPA.
Application Procedure: Contact the Director of Financial Planning.

Academic Scholarship and Leadership Grants occupy a special place in the financial aid picture because they are given to deserving students without regard to issues of financial need.
Program Title: Academic Scholarship and Leadership Grant
Amount: Up to $4,000 per year, no repayment required.
Eligibility: Based on student's ACT or SAT and high school GPA.
Application Procedure: Contact the Director of Financial Planning.

Bert T. Combs Leadership Grant is available for qualifying students who are residents of Bell, Breathitt,Clay, Floyd, Knott, Knox, Harlan, Jackson, Laurel, Leslie, Letcher, Lincoln, Magoffin, Martin, McCreary, Owsley, Perry, Pike, Pulaski, Rockcastle, Wayne, or Whitley counties in Kentucky.
Program Title: Bert T. Combs Leadership Grant
Amount: Complements other available aid programs and will not exceed the cost of full tuition
Eligibility: Must be a full-time student; must be in the top one-half of graduating class; must have a minimum of 19 ACT (900 SAT); must apply for and be eligible to receive a Pell Grant and have an estimated EFC of $1,500 or less.
Application Procedure: Contact the Cumberland College Admissions Office

Church-Related Scholarships: Cumberland offers Pastor's Recommendation Scholarships ranging from $1,000 to the cost of full-tuition and are renewable for up to four years. In addition, Cumberland will match gifts from churches throughout the nation which contribute up to $200 towards a student's tuition. Children of ministers may also be eligible for special scholarship assistance.
Program Title: Church-Related Scholarship.
Amount: Varies; no repayment required.
Eligibility: Varies; see description elsewhere.
Application Procedure: Contact Cumberland's Admission Office or the Director of Financial Planning.

Community Service Scholarships: Cumberland offers five $1,000  Community Service Scholarships to students who have shown outstanding service in their community. Applicants must submit three letters of recommendation that document their community service and be currently involved in service to others. To renew this scholarship, applicants must complete and document 75 hours of service each academic year and maintain a grade-point average of at least 2.25 each semester.
Program Title: Community Service Scholarship.
Amount: $1,000. No repayment required.
Eligibility: Students must be currently involved in service to others.
Application Procedure: Contact Cumberland's Admissions Office or the Director of Financial Planning.

Athletic Scholarships: Some scholarship aid may be available to students who compete on Cumberland's intercollegiate athletic teams. Interested students and their coaches should contact the Cumberland College coach of the sport in which participation is desired.
Program Title: Athletic Scholarship.
Amount: Varies; no repayment required.
Eligibility: Students with demonstrated ability in athletics, cheerleading or athletic training.
Application Procedure: Contact Cumberland College coach or sponsor of appropriate sport.

NEED-BASED FINANCIAL AID

When the cost of college exceeds the ability of your family to pay, Cumberland offers various types of financial aid to make up the difference. They include grants, loans, and work study.

GRANTS Money from various federal and state sources can be awarded to eligible students and does not need to be repaid.

Program Title: Pell Grant.
Amount: Up to $3,125; no repayment required.
Eligibility: Students with financial need.
Application Procedure: U.S. Government, file FAFSA.

Program Title: Supplemental Educational Opportunity Grant.
Amount: Up to $4,000; no repayment required.
Eligibility: Students with financial need.
Application Procedure: U.S. Government, file FAFSA.

Program Title: Kentucky Grant.
Amount: Up to $2,560; no repayment required.
Eligibility: Students with financial need who reside in Kentucky.
Application Procedure: State of Kentucky, file FAFSA.

LOANS Students may borrow money from the college at special long-term, low-interest rates. In most cases, repayment of such loans does not begin until six months after the student leaves college.

Program Title: Perkins Loan.
Amount: Up to $3,000 per year with a maximum of $15,000 for undergraduate study; repayment at 5% interest begins nine months after leaving school; up to ten years to repay.
Eligibility: Students with financial need.
Application Procedure: U.S. Government, file FAFSA.

Program Title: Stafford Loan Program.
Amount: Up to $2,625 first year of study, $3,500 for second year of study, and $5,500 for each year thereafter, with a maximum of $23,000; repayment begins six months after leaving school; interest rate will be variable but not higher than 9%.
Eligibility: Students with financial need.
Application Procedure: Local lending institutions; file FAFSA.

Program Title: Parent Loans for Undergraduate Students:
Amount: Up to total cost of education less other financial aid; repayment at a variable rate (but not more than 10%) begins 60 days after loan is received.
Eligibility: Parents of students.
Application Procedure: Contact the Director of Financial Planning.

WORK STUDY Eligible students may earn money to meet part of their college expenses by working part-time on campus. At Cumberland, work study jobs are available in nearly every office and department on campus. Our Financial Planning Office will gladly help you determine your eligibility for these kinds of need-based aid and will design a financial aid package to suit your particular situation.
Program Title: College Work Study.
Amount: Varies; no repayment required; average earnings $1,700 per year.
Eligibility: Students with financial need.
Application Procedure: U.S. Government and Cumberland College, file FAFSA.

DEFERRED PAYMENT PLAN

After your financial aid package has been approved, the portion of your college expenses that you are expected to pay would normally be due at the beginning of each semester. However, at Cumberland College, you may choose to participate in our deferred payment plan. Under this plan, the balance due for each semester is divided into monthly payments, to be paid as the semester progresses. There is no carrying charge, and participation in the plan is your option.
