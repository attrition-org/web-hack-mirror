Cumberland College article for Western Recorder, December 14, 1999

JOY - Hebrews 12:2

It is possible to assemble a jigsaw puzzle face down - well, at least hypothetically. Someone, somewhere is probably doing that very thing right now.

Most Christians are aware that their lives can be as complicated as a jigsaw puzzle. There is a lot to think about. There are many things that must get done, especially if you are to have a successful life. At least that is what we are told.

We plan our days, weeks, months, and even years. We foresee problems and try to come up with workable answers. It is part of our day-to-day life. And we become machine-like bodies carrying busy brains through a complicated existence, putting the pieces together.

Then someone reminds us that we are to give attention to our spiritual lives, and we envision the solution in another list of requirements that will demand more of our limited resources.

Thankfully, especially around Christmas, we have moments of insight and revelation and see how readily we have turned our perspective upside down. We realize that we haven't looked at the cover of the puzzle box or even seen the "picture side" of the pieces in quite a while.

I like simple solutions. Jesus came to bring a simple solution . . . a way to eternal life. And while He was here, provided some helpful suggestions: things like "God is spirit, so those who worship Him must worship Him in spirit and truth" (John 4:24). A reminder that we are to see ourselves first as spiritual beings - the "not upside-down" view.

Picture side up. Picture side up. Picture side up. . .

- Bret Kuss

Dr. Bret Kuss is Professor of Biology at Cumberland College. Kuss joined the Cumberland College faculty in 1989 and became Head of the Biology Department in 1999. He is actively involved with students on campus and with his local church in ministering for Christ. He is an excellent role model for our students.
