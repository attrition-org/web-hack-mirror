Cumberland College article for Western Recorder, December 21, 1999

LOVE - John 15:12-17

Childhood memories are priceless. Attending the candlelight Christmas Eve services at my grandparents' church is among my most precious memories. As if it were yesterday I remember entering the vestibule, filled with laughter and conversation. My grandfather's strong, gentle hands guided me along, and I was filled with love as he reacquainted me with everyone we encountered.

As the service began we listened while the minister spoke. He read from the Bible important words, but to me, words none the same. And then the music began. As we sang, the room became alive. Old voices, young voices, good voices, and bad voices wondrously intertwined together into a beautiful choir singing praises about the birth of the Christ child.

As we took our candles and lit them one by one, the now darkened sanctuary took on a new dimension. It was magnificent and magical with flickering candles and dancing shadows. I sang loudly and proudly. As if with the Magi, I was there following the star to the manger where the baby Jesus lay.

"Silent Night, Holy Night, wondrous star lend thy light. ...

Christ the Savior is born, Christ the Savior is born."

There was a hush to the room as we left. Couples holding hands, singles in groups, families arm in arm, strangers now become friends. I had come filled with love of my family, but in the span of an hour I was leaving brimming with the love of mankind in my heart, for together we had shared in the spirit of Christmas, which is the birth of Jesus Christ.

- Lindsay Vernon

Lindsay Vernon serves as Secretary in the Basketball Office at Cumberland College. She has worked at Cumberland College for twelve years and is actively involved in service on campus and in her local church.

We hope you have enjoyed these devotionals for the Advent season. Cumberland College students, staff, faculty, and administration wish you a blessed Christmas season as we all celebrate the birth of Christ our Savior.
