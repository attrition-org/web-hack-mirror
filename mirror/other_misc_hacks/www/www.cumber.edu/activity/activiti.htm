           Clubs and Organizations
      Get Involved!    Involvement in a club or organization can be an exciting part of your college experience. College is a great place to explore new ideas and challenge yourself to set and  reach goals.
      Each student has a particular
contribution  to make, and we want to help you find your place to fit in and use your abilities not only for self-fulfillment, but also for the benefit of others.
     At Cumberland, we offer a wide range of activities and organizations because we believe that a well-rounded education does not consist solely of books, papers, and labs. Activities can be rewarding whether they complement your academic major or support an interest in an unrelated area. By interacting with a diverse group of students (and an advisor) who share a common interest, you will gain valuable information and interesting perspectives about campus life and friendship. Participation in campus organizations can enhance your academic career by providing opportunities to develop leadership, organizational, time management, and decision-making skills--expertise that will benefit you well after you graduate.

   Cumberland offers a major theatrical production each semester; more than 40 clubs, academic honor societies, and organizations, including one of the largest Baptist Student Unions in Kentucky; a wide variety of on-campus programs and special events sponsored by the Campus Activity Board; and much more.

      Participation and involvement in extra-curricular activities should not come, however, at the expense of your academic career. It is important that you find the right personal balance between academics and outside activities. Over involvement can result in sagging grades as well as waning enthusiasm. Before joining an organization, learn as much as you can about the group. Ask yourself, "What am I interested in?", "What abilities or skills do I have to share or want to learn?", "What does this organization require for membership?", and "How much time can I commit to this organization?"

      Club Search, held during Welcome Week, provides students the opportunity to meet organizational representatives and to get information about each organization recognized by the College. Once you do decide to join a group, make a personal commitment to attend meetings, be dependable, and be an active participant. If you know when to say, "Yes!"--and when to say, "No!"--your extra-curricular involvement can be a richly rewarding experience.
