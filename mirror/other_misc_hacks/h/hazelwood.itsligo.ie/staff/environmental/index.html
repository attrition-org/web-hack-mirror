 The Institute of Technology Sligo, being the first third level institution in Ireland to offer degree courses in Environmental Science, has continued to maintain it’s leading position  in the field by following a wide range of research programmes. A number of research groups are currently active within the School of Science, in areas as diverse as microbiological bioremediation, toxicology, radiochemistry or the development of chemical and biological sensors for environmental contaminants. The School of Science maintains close contact with industrial partners and state bodies as well as having strong links with third level institutions in countries such as Italy (University of Florence), France (Universitee de Limoges, Institut Universitaire de Technologie de Poitiers), Germany (Giessen Fachhochschule) and the U.K. (Coventry University).

The Institute of Technology Sligo hosted the 1st and 8th Irish Environmental Researchers Colloquia in 1990 and in 1998, as well as the 2nd Institute of Technology Science and Computing Research
Colloquium in 1997.
