SCHEDULE OF EVENTS:

  • All Week: Visits to primary and secondary schools by The Science Works – interactive experiments and demonstrations for students.

Friday 10th November:

Venue: Institute of Technology Sligo.

Open day for second level students.

Exhibitions and experiments by the Science Dept., Institute of Technology Sligo.

Monday 13th November:

Venue: Institute of Technology Sligo –

Theatre A004

7.30 PM: Lecture by Dr. Declan Shelly, Institute of Technology Sligo

"Flatulence and the Common Man ! ";

8.00 PM Lecture Dr. Mark Rogers, University College Dublin

"BSE: Incidence in Ireland and epidemiology ";

This lecture will review the science of BSE, the current status in Ireland and future prospects.

Tuesday 14th November:

Venue: Institute of Technology Sligo –

Theatre A004

7.30 PM: Lecture and demonstrations by Dr. Billy Fitzgerald, Institute of Technology Sligo.

"Issues relating to the recycling of municipal waste".

This lecture will cover waste statistics, collection and segregation of recyclables, recycling of various waste commodities, glass, paper, metal, plastic and organics.

8.00 PM: Lecture by Dr. Richard Collins, (RTE, Birdwatch Ireland)

"Swans – Births, Marriages and Deaths""

This lecture will present an overview of the fascinating world of the Swan.

 

Wednesday 15th November:

Venue: Institute of Technology Sligo –

Theatre A004

7.30 PM: Lecture & Slide Show by Frances Lucy, Institute of Technology Sligo.

"Zebra mussels in the uppers Shannon – the spread of an exotic species" What are Zebra Mussels?

8.00 PM: Lecture by Professor Moira O’Brien, Professor of Anatomy, Trinity College Dublin.

"Monitoring Fitness in the prevention of overtraining and sports injuries."

Professor O’Brien is the Medical Director of the Osteoporosis Unit and Human Performance Laboratory, President of Irish Sports Medicine Association, Medical Officer, Irish Olympic Council 1979 – 1987, Team Doctor for Moscow, Los Angeles & Seoul Olympic Games.

Thursday 16th November:

Venue: Institute of Technology Sligo –

Theatre A004

7.30 PM: Lecture by Dr. John Bartlett and Ms. Margaret Cullagh, Institute of Technology Sligo.

"Are we sustainable ?";

This will involve a short presentation intended to raise issues and awareness of environmental sustainability.

 

8.00 PM: Lecture by Dr. Tony Kavanagh, Genetics Department, Trinity College, Dublin.

"Genetically Modified Organisms and the future of Agriculture"

This lecture will present an overview of the theory and practice of GMO’s in Agriculture.

 

Friday 17th Nobember:

Venue: Institute of Technology, Sligo.

Open Day for primary and secondary school students.

Lectures, Demonstrations, Interactive Science Experiments.

Participants:

The Science Works.

  • Lectures and Interactive Science Experiments aimed at Primary and Secondary School Students.

Dr. Richard Ellam (Science Historian)

  • Lecture on "A Brief Social History of Time"
  • Have you ever felt that there isn’t enough time to go around?, have you ever wondered who is to blame?. In this illustrated lecture which explores the history of Western Time Keeping over the last Millennium, the truth will be laid bare – it is all the fault of Monks, Astronomers and the Railway Companies !
  • The A4 Show – Science with Paper.

If you want to know how a Scientist thinks or how an Airplane flies, why a suspension bridge is so slender or how to make one side of a piece of paper vanish, this is the show for you.

  • The Illuminating Gherkin.

We all take the electric light bulb for granted, but there are other ways of making light from electricity such as arc lamps, discharge tubes and pickled gherkins.

Dr. Ed Barnett, Armagh Planetarium & Dr. Mario di Maggio, Armagh Planetarium

Lecture title "Whats Hot on Mars ? – recent results from the robotic explorers, Mars Global Surveyor Images and Future Missions.

Lecture and Demonstration by John Fitzsimons.

  • Robotic telescope demonstration. Computer based slide show of astronomical and environmental images from the Sliabh Daeane Observatory.
  • Interactive demonstration of Sky Window Education resource.
  • At Sliabh Daeane Observatory throughout the week – group visits, viewing of lunar features, planet Jupiter, rings of Saturn, star clusters and galaxies.

 

 

Friday 17th (evening):

Venue: Institute of Technology, Theatre A004.

7.30 PM: Lecture and Slide Show by Ms. Monica Sullivan, Institute of Technology Sligo.

Title: "Exploring the Animal Kingdom of the Indo-Pacific Ocean"

This will involve underwater slides of the Animal Kingdom of Indonesia.

This describes the mapping of The Wakatobi Marine National Park in Indonesia. In fact Dr. Monica Sullivan worked as the senior marine biologist on several expeditions.

8.00PM: Lecture by Leo Enright (RTE / BBC)

"Will the Sky fall tonight? – A preview of tonight’s dramatic meteor shower"

Leo Enright is Ireland’s leading broadcaster on Space Exploration. Leo will give a talk on Space exploration and will focus on tonight’s meteor shower, and how meteorites have influenced Ireland over the millennia.

Venue: Tesco Shopping Arcade, Sligo Town.

  • Irish Heart Foundation.
Which is the dodgy heart? Who has the dodgy heart?

Health promotion, carbon monoxide testing; community CPR demonstrations.

  • Frances Lucy, Institute of Technology Sligo.

Exhibition on Zebra mussels.
