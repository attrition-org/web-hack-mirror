SYSTEM DESIGN/SOC

Introduction

IME offers system level design capabilities through its System on Chip (SoC) Department. Efforts over last several years were focused on areas of digital multimedia and wireless communications, where DSP was the core technology. Today, we have built up the capability to provide our partners a seamless development flow from standard investigation, algorithm development, system architecture design to fast prototyping through DSP and/or FPGA, and eventually to actual IC chips. Some valuable designs can be cited as videophone, voice-fax modem, Viterbi decoder, Turbo codec and advanced Rake receiver.

The System-On-A-Chip (SoC) group is working closely with the VLSI department on the backend design technology and APDS department for the packaging support to provide a total solution of electronics systems. Our vision is to strive to become a recognized center of expertise in the area of SoC designs.

In addition, SoC and Economy Development Board (EDB) have jointly established the Infocomm Signal Processing Programme to train engineers in the fundamentals of DSP for audio, video, speech, wired and wireless communications. This is in response to growing demand for skilled manpower to support Info-Communication activities. For upcoming courses, please check the Training Calendar.

A Digital Signal Processing Network Forum is hosted by IME via DSPList. This moderated mailing list is set up to inform subscribers on up-to-date DSP activities and important announcements. It also provides an open forum for subscribers to discuss, or ask questions about, issues they are concerned with.

IME became a member of Virtual Socket Interface (VSI) Alliance in January 1999. The VSI Alliance's mission is to facilitate us to mix and match Virtual Components from different sources so as to speed up the growth of the system-chip industry while reducing the design cycle time, and also to reduce the cost of supporting and using the Virtual Components.
