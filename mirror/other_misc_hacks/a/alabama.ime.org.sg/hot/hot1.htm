Press Release - January 18 2001
Tachyon and IME to build 3D chips for greater speed and performance

Tachyon Semiconductor (Singapore) Pte Ltd and the Institute of Microelectronics (IME) have unveiled plans to develop faster and more powerful chips by stacking and bonding multiple wafers into one. The result is a truly integrated chip where up to 4 layers function as if they were a single layer. It can store up to 1 Gb of memory within the same space as a 256Mb DRAM chip. The greatly improved capacity and higher speed will enable memory-intensive applications to migrate to smaller devices within a shorter span of time.

This 3D approach using wafer bonding technology to stack the wafers is the first truly integrated solution in the world.

"By diffusing the copper patterns on the wafers, we are enabling a direct interconnection between the layers," said Dr Subhash Gupta, Managing Director, Tachyon Semiconductor (Singapore). "This direct interconnection enables faster speeds that approach FLASH and SRAM speeds and much higher capacity chips," he added. "Current stacking methods offer limited interconnection - they typically use only edge connectors which save space but do not yield any improvement in speed or cost."

Bonding two or more wafers, however, can be very difficult, especially for eight-inch wafers which will be used for the 3D chips. This is where Tachyon's partnership with IME is crucial. Said Dr Tan Khen Sang, Director, IME: "The 3D chips require a near-perfect bonding across the entire surface of the eight-inch wafer at each layer. Air gaps caused by surface contamination or the unevenness of the wafers could result in incomplete bonding. In addition, it is important to ensure that the high temperature and pressure at which the wafers are subject to do not damage the circuitry," he explained.

"Bonding is one of the key technologies to producing viable 3D memory," added Mr James T. Ayers, Jr., Tachyon's Chairman and Chief Executive Officer. "We are excited about the opportunity to jointly develop this part of the stacking technology with IME, which enables us to benefit from IME's world-class facilities and technical expertise," he said.

Currently, IME is one of the few research institutes in the world that has the expertise and facilities to work on wafer bonding for integrated circuits.

Another unique feature of the 3D chips is a built-in self-test and error correction capability for which Tachyon has already obtained a patent. In addition to significantly improving the yield, this feature ensures that the chip will continue to function even if one or more of the layers becomes faulty.

The applications of the 3D chips are wide-ranging. They can be widely used with memory applications to help narrow the huge gap between processor speed (1 nanosecond) and the slow speed of today's affordable memory (60 nanoseconds).

"Tachyon is committed to Singapore. Our long-term plans include building or jointly venturing with a Singaporean company to create a stacking facility capable of producing 50,000 stacked wafers per month at full production," Mr Ayers said.

Some major market segments that Tachyon is targetting are the computer market (from PCs to mainframes, especially high-end workstations), communications sector (both telecommunications and data communication), routers and switches, as well as network servers. Other potential markets include the transportation and industrial sectors.

In all, Tachyon is targetting a US$540 million slice of the total projected US$5.9 billion market when its initial products are released in the year 2002.

About IME
The Institute of Microelectronics (IME) was formed in 1991 as Singapore's national research institute for microelectronics. IME contributes to R&D sustainability throughout the entire value chain of microelectronics - VLSI circuit design, wafer fabrication, failure analysis and reliability, and electronic packaging.

In circuit design, IME is among the few leading companies in the world that have developed RFICs in CMOS technology for wireless communication applications such as Bluetooth, CDMA and WCDMA. Our deep submicron IC fabrication achievements in low-k dielectrics and copper interconnection built on 0.18 micron geometry (and subsequently 0.13 micron technology) are well positioned to make major contributions to the increasing demands for high-performance electronics. In addition, we have developed advanced products such as high-power IC devices and silicon-based sensors for better control in automotives, higher-quality and lower-cost manufacturing of microphones and for the advancement of DNA research.

Our capabilities in advanced packaging technologies include flip-chip and three-dimensional packaging. Central to our R&D activities are our competencies in failure mode analysis and reliability.

About Tachyon
Tachyon Semiconductor is a fabless semiconductor company specialising in high-performance, high-speed affordable memory. Tachyon's FaStack® memory uses a combination of its own patented intellectual property (IP), stacking technology it has licensed, and the wafer bonding technology being co-developed with IME.

Tachyon is the successor company to ASIC Designs, a 14-year-old company specialising in custom system and IC designs. Tachyon is commercialising the large body of IP created by ASIC designs. The company is headquartered in San Jose, California, USA. Its primary engineering facility is in Naperville, Illinois, a suburb of Chicago. Its process deisgn and process engineering activities are based in Singapore where the company expects to locate its wafer stacking facility capable of handling up to 50,000 wafers per month by mid-2002.
