IME Quality Policy

"IME is dedicated to add value to our partners, and to ensure that our R&D processes and procedures are compatible with their manufacturing practices and with the needs of their customers."

Quality Objectives

  • Continue to improve in both tangible and non-tangible benefits to business partners through IME value added services.
  • Promote awareness of quality procedures to all employees through training and education.
  • Continue to inject new technologies, new ideas and new methods into IME business focuses.
  • Excellence in R&D practices, manufacturing, documentation and support functions.
  • Consistently improve project management to ensure productivity, yield and on-time delivery.
  • Consistently improve performance to meet or exceed internal demand, customer / partner (hereafter referred to as customer) and funding agency expectations.
  • Ensure that quality objectives for all IME practices are consistent with safety, health and environmental concerns.

ISO9000 certification

BVQI (Bureau Veritas Quality International) awarded the ISO9001 and ISO9002 certification to IME in 1996.

Scope of ISO9001 certification

Project Management, Consultation, Design & Development Activities in the following Technical Groups :

  • Advanced Packaging Development Support
  • Deep Submicron Integrated Circuit Technology
  • Failure Analysis & Reliability
  • Foundry for Innovative Devices
  • Micro-Electro-Mechanical Systems
  • System on Chip
  • VLSI Circuit Design & Test

Scope of ISO9002 certification

Provision of Training Courses and Workshops

Quality Documentation

IME has a 3-tier documentation structure, namely :

  • Quality Manual ( Outlines how IME complies with the ISO9000 requirements. )
  • Quality Procedure ( Defines the process for various function. )
  • Work Instruction ( Procedures at department level. )

IME Staff can access to these documents via the Intranet.

The Quality System is managed by the Management Representative (MR). The Internal Audit Team (IAT) assists to maintain the system at the department level.
