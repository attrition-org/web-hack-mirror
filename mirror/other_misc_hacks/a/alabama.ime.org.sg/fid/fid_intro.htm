  FOUNDRY FOR INNOVATIVE DEVICES

Introduction

The FID department, formerly known as MPT, specializes in development, design and fabrication of innovative silicon based LDMOS, VDMOS and MEMS devices of industrial relevance, which are capable of low volume mass production at reasonable cost. FID has setup a complete 6" wafer CMOS process Line that can process upto 0.5um device geometry. We have also established a 0.8 um double poly double metal CMOS process. To take care of the processing needs of MEMS devices, a Sensor Lab has also been setup, which offers surface and bulk micromachining capabilities. As the processes in Sensor Lab are not CMOS compatible, they can only be tailored as a back-end process to any CMOS compatible processes. This enables us to offer a comprehensive range of products and services from fabrication of discrete MEMS devices such as accelerometers to Integrated MEMS devices such as integrated pressure sensors.

During the last few years we have embarked on RFIC activities to address the emerging portable microelectronics market. We have successfully developed state of the art LDMOS, VDMOS and RF inductor process technology, with products being introduced in wireless communication market.

One unique feature of our process line is the capability to develop and port custom processes in a short time required for different technologies. Some of the significant milestones are:

  • Partial SOI LDMOSFET. With partial SOI structure, LDMOS shows a low parasitic capacitance so as to improve RF performance
  • Polycide Gate LDMOSFET. This is an improvement over normal polysilicon gate LDMOS process for high power RF applications.
  • Super Junction VDMOSFET with high aspect ratio silicon trench process. This is novel VDMOS structure which can optimize the on-state resistance as well as breakdown voltage.
  • Silicon LDMOS RF Power Amplifer modules
  • Silicon Microphones
  • Accelerometers using LISA process (Lateral Isolated Surface Accelerometer)
  • Silicon Microrelay
  • Integrated piezo-resistive pressure sensors

Other areas in which FID is working on are "Wafer fabrication Training" and "General fabrication services". To support the growing needs of an expanding semiconductor industry in Singapore, a large number of qualified wafer process engineers would need to be trained. Wafer Fabrication Training Course offers to help in the development of a pool of such engineers.

The activity of General Fabrication Services was initiated to make full use of our equipment capacities. Our customers range from Universities (NUS/NTU), Polytechnic Institutes, Research organisations (DSI, IMRE etc) and Companies (Like HP, Sumisho, Syspac etc). The activities include the following:

  • Develop novel processes to fabricate MEMS devices/structures
  • Do only standard processes as per customers specifications
