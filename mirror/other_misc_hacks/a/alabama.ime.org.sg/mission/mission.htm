DIRECTORS REPORT 2000

It has been another fruitful and exciting year for IME in which we saw a significant increase in major project collaborations with our partners. Overall we have more than thirty major projects across the various capabilities of IME. We have also provided almost one thousand advanced services and solutions to a broad spectrum of companies in Singapore. Our collaborative R&D efforts with the industry have also generated many joint technical publications and patents. We are very grateful to our partners and supporters who have given us the opportunities to fulfil our mission to help increase value added from electronics industry in Singapore.

Sharpening our Focus
Our close collaboration with partners has made us more attuned to the fast moving and dynamic market place. We are more convinced than ever that our strategy in R&D must continue to be application driven. We continue to sharpen our focus on various new and emerging applications with the goal of creating opportunities for better services, potential business spin-offs and joint ventures.

Our relentless effort in pursuing excellence in RFIC design and applications has allowed us to successfully migrate to fine line CMOS process - the technology of choice for high volume consumer products. With the support of our partners, we have championed the design and development of CMOS RF ICs aimed at Bluetooth and WCDMA wireless communication applications. The results have been so encouraging that we are now mapping out new business strategies and opportunities based on our RFIC design capabilities.

MEMS R&D and prototyping for various applications are being realised at IME through vigorous efforts in MEMS device design, signal conditioning circuits, process technology development, as well as the development of unique IC packages for MEMS. Our collaboration projects with industrial partners in silicon accelerometers, silicon microphones and silicon micro-relays have reached various stages of development ranging from initial prototypes to pre-production samples. For these MEMS devices to be mass-produced locally, we shall continue to explore the establishment of a MEMS foundry in Singapore with the technologies developed at IME.

IME’s core competencies in advanced packaging have also made tremendous progress over the past year. We are now equipped with some of the most advanced tools and methodologies to support the industry. Our capabilities have been well recognised and supported by our partners. We have already delivered complete end-to-end packaging designs meeting unique applications and requirements of our customers.

The explosive growth in silicon technology has led to a huge demand in system-on- chip implementations. In response to the new challenge, we have channelled our system-level expertise in real-time DSP and audio/video sub-systems into providing System-on-Chip IC solutions for multimedia and mobile communication applications. Due to the shrinking product design cycles and life spans of these ICs, we are being challenged to achieve faster design turn around through design automation and design reuse.

For IME to stay in the forefront of research and provide technology solutions to our partners, IME’s capabilities and facilities must be upgraded continuously. With the support of the National Science and Technology Board of Singapore (NSTB), we have recently embarked on the Deep Sub-micron Integrated Circuit (DSIC) initiative to develop next generation high-speed interconnect technologies with 0.18 and 0.13 micron features for future generations of wafer fabrication and RFIC applications. The DSIC program will also serve as a platform for industry collaboration, manpower development and up-stream research with other research institutes and universities.

A Salute to our Director Emeritus
After eight years of distinguished service, Dr. Bill Chen, the founding director of IME, stepped down from the role of IME Director on 1 March 2000. IME owes its many successes and achievements to the leadership and invaluable contributions of Dr Bill Chen. His vision and enthusiasm continuously nurtured IME to its current admirable stature. His relentless effort to pursue quality in R&D has established IME as one of the few Research Institutes in the world which is certified with ISO9001 credentials.

Conclusion
As we grow in strength and capabilities, we are determined to stay relevant to the industry we serve, while striving to achieve excellence in research and development. With these twin goals in mind, we look forward to your continued support.


Dr. Tan Khen-Sang
Acting Director,
Institute of Microelectronics
