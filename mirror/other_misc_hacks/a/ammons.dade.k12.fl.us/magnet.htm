Introduction:

The International Education (IE) Magnet is a rigorous, academic program that requires the study of a second language, either French or Spanish.   The program opened in 1998 - 99 with a class of 220 sixth graders who came from over 30 different elementary schools including the International Education students from Frank C. Martin Elementary and Air Base Elementary.  Ammons implements the curriculum model of the International Baccalaureate Middle Years program which stresses theme - based learning through five Areas of Interaction.  These Areas of Interaction focus on study skills, the environment, community service, health and social education, and the inventions of man.  Ammons is one of five M - DCPS schools that will be sharing funds secured through a federal, Magnet Schools Assistance Program (MSAP) grant.  The grant will run for three years, through 2001 and represents a total of over $8 million.  Students completing the IE Program at Ammons will be well prepared to apply for any high school magnet program.

WHAT IS THE INTERNATIONAL EDUCATION CENTER AT AMMONS?

The International Education Program at Ammons is a three year, middle school program designed to provide students with a rich, varied, and challenging educational experience.   This program emphasizes academic rigor and an integrated approach within and across the subject areas.

Students study a second language either French or Spanish, and can pursue elective courses in the areas of computers, technology, physical education, music, drama, creative writing, and the arts.  Also, students will have the opportunity to pursue school and community service activities.

 

WHO CAN APPLY FOR THE INTERNATIONAL EDUCATION PROGRAM?

Students currently in grade five may apply for admission.  Applications are available at the Ammons office, or at any magnet middle school, and will be accepted from October 1st through the last day of February.

 

HOW ARE STUDENTS SELECTED FOR THIS MAGNET PROGRAM?

Students must submit a completed application, including the recommendation form, to be considered for admission.  Eligibility will then be determined by a standard review of the submitted application.  If the number of eligible applicants exceeds the number of available spaces in the program, a random selection process will be utilized.    School Board rule 6Gx13-6A.46 established guidelines which provide bus transportation only for magnet students who reside in the area of designated target schools. 

 

WHEN WILL STUDENTS KNOW IF THEY HAVE BEEN ACCEPTED?

The random selection process takes place after the application period ends at the end of February.  Acceptance notification will be mailed out during the first week of May.  Responses must be returned by May 12.

WHY SHOULD A STUDENT APPLY?

High expectations and a balance of academics, activities, and athletics make the magnet at Herbert A. Ammons Middle School an excellent preparation for high school.   Students who attend Ammons will benefit from foreign language study along with the opportunity to earn high school credits in grades seven and eight.

 

WHAT MAKES THE AMMONS PROGRAM SPECIAL?

Through the required language course and the interdisciplinary approach to learning, students develop an awareness and sensitivity to other cultures, and an appreciation of the common themes that link all facets of learning.  Our magnet curriculum stresses the development of critical thinking, problem solving, and effective study skills.
