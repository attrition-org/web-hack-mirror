AMS French I

 

SYLLABUS MJ FRENCH I

 

PURPOSE
To introduce students to the French language and its culture
to develop interactive communication skills and cross-cultural understanding

 

CONTENT EMPHASIS
Listening skills (comprehension)
Speaking (pronunciation)
Learning "everyday" vocabulary
Basic grammatical structures
Reading comprehension (aloud and silently)

 

 

TEXTS
Bienvenue French Fun (and anything else that can be used effectively)
French reading books relative to beginning level
ACTIVITIES
Projects (collage, and or picture/word album - a type of scrapbook)

 

ORAL LANGUAGE DEVELOPMENT
Greetings, social pleasantries
Classroom objects
Personal data
Family relationships
Daily activities
Weather, date, time, days of week
Transportation
Ordering in a restaurant
Colors, numbers
Geography
Idioms, proverbs

 

WRITING
Present tense "er" verbs (sentences, questions, negatives, and commands)
Irregular verbs: etre, avoir, faire, and aller

 

COMPETITION
French Mini concours where students compete in speaking, poetry recitation, and skit recitation

 

TEXT
Bienvenue and French for Fun (also, anything else available and effective)
Bienvenue is the principal text and will be used 3-4 days a week. French for Fun will be used 1-2 times a week.

 

DAILY ACTIVITIES
Warm-ups (review of previously learned material) daily: 5 minutes
Textbooks check homework, new material: 30 minutes daily
Memorization of poetry or dialog: 2-3 per grading period
Homework: study, copy or do comprehension activity: 15 -20 minutes nightly
Present songs 2-3 times per week (10 minutes of class time)
Wrap up: tie in all material presented in a class period: 10 minutes (games, etc.)

 

 

FIELD TRIPS
French restaurant and or French bread factory
French play --Epcot (countries: "France")
France (Europe) at end of school year (teacher coordinated and parent chaperoned

 

TESTING
Written chapter tests, quizzes, oral drills, short dictations

 

LITERACY
Students practice spelling orally on a daily basis (5-10 minutes) using accents to constantly reinforce the skill
Recognize and apply sound/letter correspondence
Demonstrate reading comprehension

 

CULTURE
Identify regions of the world that are 'Francophone"
Identify holidays (special activities), historical events
Identify typical foods
Learn some of the influences of French culture on the development of North America
Influence on the English language
Know non-verbal French responses

 

 

CULTURE
Prepare oral presentations on culture, history, and famous persons
See French movies and French documentaries
See a French play
