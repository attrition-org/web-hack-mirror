Industrial Opportunities at the Microfabrication Applications Laboratory and MicroFluidics Center

We have five basic methods of collaboration with industry. These can be tailored toward companies of all sizes and include:

  1. Joint Research - Companies can participate in research at the MAL in areas of specific interest. Research programs may be developed to support specific company goals especially in the area of new product technologies. A key element in this research is the transfer of technology back to the industry which includes a limited amount of prototyping, and assistance in setting up a miniature clean room at the company's facility or transferring any processes to a commercial foundry;
  2. Industrial Service - The MAL's state-of-the-art processing facilities are accessible on a contractual basis. This includes: thin film deposition of a broad range of metals, and dielectrics; electrical and optical characterization, and custom lithography;
  3. Training Center - Joint university and industry projects are possible whereby scientists from industry work at the MAL in conjunction with a professor to develop and investigate industrial and manufacturing applications of microfabrication. In addition, training workshops in microfabrication methodology are held at the MAL to aid companies in continuing education of their engineers and technicians in this significant technology. This training and research can promote the successful transfer of state-of-the-art techniques to industry;
  4. MicroFluidics Center - An Industry/University Cooperative Research Center
  5. Topical Workshops - on research are regularly held at the MAL and are open to the industrial community.
For more information about the Microfabrication Applications Laboratory or the MicroFluidics Center, please contact any of us!
