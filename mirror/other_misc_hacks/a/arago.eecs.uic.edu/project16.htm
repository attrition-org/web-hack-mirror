Advanced Tomography for Phase Object Measurements
$120,000 - National Aeronautics and Space Administration
Dr. Soyoung Cha (PI), Feb 1994 - Feb 1997

This project explores the area of multidirectional interferometry coupled with computational tomographic reconstruction. Interferometric tomography, can be developed into an important diagnostic tool in flow measurements. It can provide various advantages over conventional optical techniques including instantaneous gross-field capture, nonintrusiveness, high spatial resolution, and good detection accuracy. To make interferometric tomography practical and to realize its full potential in flow measurements, some intrinsic problems that appear in practice need to be further investigated; that is, instantaneous multidirectional projection recording and subsequent accurate data reduction in addition to reliable field reconstruction from a partial data set involving incomplete projection and restricted angular scanning. In the past, Dr. Cha has conducted an investigation of the various subprocesses in interferometric tomography.
The main purpose of this proposal is to test and combine all of these subprocesses which consist of experimental, theoretical and computational techniques. This will allow us to determine the practical application feasibility of interferometric tomography.
