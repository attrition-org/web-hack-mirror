A Micropump for Microdialysis
$28,300 - KTEC Biotechnology Research Consortium
Dr. Peter Hesketh (PI); Dr. James Boyd, Dr. George Wilson, and Dr. Susan Lunte (Co-PI’s)
Aug 1994 - Jan. 1996

The objective of this project is to develop a miniature pump suitable for microdialysis sampling which is small, light weight, and low power. The desired micropump must have the following attributes. The dimensions must be small enough so that it fits snugly on the back of a rat (or a slightly larger animal). It must also be able to deliver liquid to the probe reproducibly even when the animal is moving. Chemical resistance to saline solutions, which are commonly employed as microdialysate perfusates is also important.
The pump is a layered structure consisting of two silicon wafers bonded to a glass plate. The pump diaphragm is deflected to allow filling of the pump chamber. This pump also includes two passive check valves. The diaphragm is driven by an external source of compressed air.
The ultimate goal of this project is to couple more sophisticated analysis systems to the miniaturized microdialysis sampling system. By coupling the sampling system to a capillary electrophoresis system with either electrochemical or laser-induced fluorescence detection, it will be possible to build a customized biosensor for any application.
Potential analytes of interest include drugs and their metabolites, neurotransmitters, amino acids and catecholamines. In order to be able to apply this instrumentation to a large number of analytes, it will be necessary to develop the associated chemistry which permits separation of components and/or selective derivatization to permit subsequent high sensitivity detection.
