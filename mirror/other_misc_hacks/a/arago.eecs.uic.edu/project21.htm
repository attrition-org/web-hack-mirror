Weld Repair of High Pressure/ Intermediate Pressure Steam Turbine Rotors
$189,657 - Electric Power Research Institute
Dr. J. Ernesto Indocochea (PI)
July 1994 - Dec 1996

This project examines localized repair welding as an alternative way to prolong the life of these steam rotors rather than complete replacement of the unit. This project looks at process optimization of the weld process as well as service stability of the complete weldment when exposed to elevated temperatures. A new alternative filler metal was obtained that outperforms the rotor material. In addition modeling work is presently underway to predict the remaining life of the repaired unit and define parameters to restrict the service variables if needed.

Development of Heat-Resistant Active Brazed Silicon-nitride Joint
$ 15,410 - National Science Foundation
Dr. J. Ernesto Indocochea(PI)
April 1996 - March 1998

Engineering ceramics are finding increasing applications in the development of highly efficient, light and compact units in the manufacturing of motors and turbines. Improved efficiencies imply high service temperatures, which ceramic materials are most likely to withstand. There is a need to develop ceramic/ceramic and ceramic/metal joints to perform at temperatures of about 800 oC and in corrosive environments. This investigation is methodically testing a number of filler metals in terms on interfacial reactions, bonding capability, mechanical properties and oxidation resistance. Non-oxide and oxide ceramics are being tested.
