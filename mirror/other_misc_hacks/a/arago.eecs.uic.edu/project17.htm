An Immunobiosensor for the Detection of Microbial Food Toxins
$115,500 - The National Center for Food Safety and Technology
Dr. Peter Hesketh (PI), Sept 1992 - Aug 1997

The food industry needs a rapid, sensitive, selective and reliable method for the detection of toxins to ensure food safety. The detection of toxins by direct sensing of the specific binding reaction of the toxin (antigen) with its specific partner (antibody) is a rapid and low cost method. Our approach to develop a biosensor is unique. It not only meets the requirements of the food industry. but can be used in other applications where low-cost methods for fast, selective and specific detection of toxins are needed.
