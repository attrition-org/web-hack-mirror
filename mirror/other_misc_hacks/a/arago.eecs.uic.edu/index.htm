Research Areas

The Microfabrication Applications Laboratory (MAL) at the University of Illinois at Chicago is a research and development laboratory dedicated to the application of integrated circuit and fiber optic technology to improve manufacturing methods for microfluidic, electromechanical, mechanical, chemical, optical and multi-functional devices, some of which have previously been built by more traditional techniques.  

The Laboratory has very versatile equipment for photolithography, and several methods for thin film deposition of (metals, semiconductors, and dielectrics) and etching, sample characterization (electrical, optical, and surface), dicing and lead attachment, and CAD workstations. There are fifteen experimental and theoretical faculty members associated with the laboratory in UIC's Bioengineering (Bioe), Chemical Engineering (ChE), Chemistry (Chem), Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (EECS), Mechanical Engineering (ME), Physics (Phys), and the Civil and Materials Engineering (CME) departments.

The MicroFluidics Center (MFC) involves several faculty throughout the College of Engineering in close collaboration with researchers in many of the natural and life sciences. This Industry/University Cooperative Research Center is being established to apply the benefits of micromachining to components and systems used for monitoring and manipulating fluids.
