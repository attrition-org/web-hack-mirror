Creating Healthier Communities Fellowship Program Chooses Wolfson

San Francisco -- Daniel B. Wolfson, President and CEO of the Alliance of Community Health Plans, has been selected to join fifty health care, business and community leaders leHHto participate in the Health Forum's award-winning Creating Healthier Communities Fellowship program.

Selected through a competitive application process, the new class of Fellows represents 19 states plus Canada, and come from a cross-section of community life including health care executives, business and educational professionals, government leaders and public health officials. Additionally, the new class includes three community teams, representing unique partnerships between health systems and local organizations committed to improving the health and well-being of their communities.

"The Fellowship has given me a rich opportunity to learn from some of the brightest people in the country engaged in efforts to improve the health of their communities," said 1999 -2000 Fellow Douglas J. Leonard, president and CEO, Columbus Regional Hospital in Columbus, Ind. "I've forged relationships that will last a long time after the fellowship."

The Creating Healthier Communities Fellowship, now in its eighth year, provides leadership development opportunities through a powerful combination of leading-edge faculty, specially designed curriculum and field-based projects. The Fellowship program combines face-to-face leadership retreats, self-study educational curriculum, on-line computer conferencing and site visits.

The heart of the Fellowship program is an individualized Action Learning Project that is implemented by each Fellow in his or her own community. These projects are collaborative, problem-solving efforts focused on improving health and quality of life in a community.

Among the planned Action Learning Projects planned are: a project to develop community wellness programs based on traditional Navajo philosophy for the Navajo Nation Division of Health in Shiprock, N.M.; a school-based, multi-focused project incorporating an interactive CD-ROM assessment survey that will create programs to promote healthy teens in New Jersey; a project focusing on the utilization of health care partnerships to advance the leadership competencies necessary to create healthy communities in British Columbia, Canada; a program where senior medical school students provide free physical exams and health education to indigent women and children at their homes in Houston; and, the creation of a center where programs for older adults will support them in reviewing and affirming the successes of their lives, and passing those legacies on to future generations.

"Improving the health and quality of their communities comes first with this year's Fellows," said Kathryn E. Johnson, president and CEO of Health Forum. "We applaud their commitment to making their vision of a healthy community become a reality."

Health Forum was created through the union of The Healthcare Forum and the American Hospital Association's publishing and data and information subsidiaries. Health Forum offers health care leaders access to new information and fresh ideas which they can use to strengthen their organization's clinical and business performance. Its Web site is www.healthforum.com .
