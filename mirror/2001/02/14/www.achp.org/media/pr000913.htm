HealthPartners Earns First National Merit Award

Measurable Heart Disease Improvement Found in Minnesota Plan's Patients

Washington, D.C. (September 13, 2000) ­ Minnesota-based HealthPartners health plan has been awarded the inaugural top performer designation for heart disease in the Benchmarks in Quality & Safety (BIQS) outcomes measurement program.

According to BIQS reviewers, the plan met and surpassed the difficult heart disease merit designation scoring criteria by improving the processes of medical delivery, empowering patients to take a more active role in their care, enhancing patient safety, and developing related programs for the entire community. Key to HealthPartners' success was the close working relationship between the plan and practitioners in developing initiatives that improved care delivery to patients with coronary artery disease.

"HealthPartners' systematic improvement efforts in heart disease reduction serve as a guide for all plans committed to improving the health status of their members and communities," noted Alliance of Community Health Plans' (ACHP) president and CEO Daniel Wolfson. ACHP spearheaded the development of both HEDIS (Health Plan Employer Data and Information Set) and BIQS. "We are honored that this designation recognizes our efforts to carry the banner for setting and achieving measurable outcome improvements in heart disease through sharing best practices and enhancing clinical processes," said HealthPartners Chief Medical Officer George J. Isham, MD.

HealthPartners is a nonprofit, member-governed family of Minnesota health care organizations and plans focused on improving the health of its members and the community. HealthPartners provides health care services, insurance and HMO coverage to nearly 660,000 members. The HealthPartners family includes the HealthPartners and Ramsey clinics, HealthPartners Medical Group, HealthPartners Dental Group, Regions Hospital, Regions Hospital Foundation, HealthPartners Research Foundation, HealthPartners Institute for Medical Education, HealthPartners Central Minnesota Clinics, Group Health, Inc., HealthPartners Administrators and Midwest Assurance Company.

Established in 1984, ACHP supports its member plans' ongoing efforts to improve the health of their members and communities. ACHP includes 22 leading non-investor owned and provider-based health plans serving more than 8 million Americans in 23 states and the District of Columbia.
