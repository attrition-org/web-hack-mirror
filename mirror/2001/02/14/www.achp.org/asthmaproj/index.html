FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

ASTHMA INTERVENTION FOR INNER-CITY CHILDREN

Background Problem Statement

Asthma is a highly prevalent disease that affects the quality of life of many persons in the United States. Roughly one of 18 Americans has asthma. It is a major cause of emergency room visits, hospitalizations, and missed school and work days, many of which are preventable. Although the burden of asthma is especially great in those urban areas that have high levels of poverty and large minority populations, few interventions have been designed or evaluated specifically for these settings.

Asthma Intervention for Inner-city Children

The Alliance of Community Health Plans (ACHP) will work with Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) to identify healthcare organizations to implement and monitor the implementation of the National Cooperative Inner-city Asthma Study (NCICAS). The project will fund intervention programs that use an asthma counselor (masters-level social worker) who will tailor the intervention to the needs of individual clients byworking closely with families over the period of a year, helping them address a wide variety of problems.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

1. Is this a study?
This project is NOT a study. Those who are funded will be expected to implement the protocol as outlined and to perform outcome measures. No funds can be supplanted, but will consider supplementing funds to serve inner-city children not already receiving the care.

2. What if the population speaks Spanish?
This intervention is available for English- and Spanish-speaking patients.

3. What is the age limitation for the study?
5-11 years old

4. Will IRB approval be required?
This intervention is not human subjects research as defined by the regulations for protection of human subjects, 45 CFR 46. Therefore, CDC, as the funding agency, will not require certification of approval by an Institutional Review Board (IRB) for an institution to be funded to participate in the implementation. Local institutions must, naturally, follow their own requirements regarding possible IRB approval for the implementation.

5. Does award total include indirect costs?
Yes, but these are limited funds. It will help if institutions can contribute. The $100,000 estimate includes both direct and indirect funds.

6. Do applicants need to submit the PHS application form?
No. Please follow the format in the RFA.

7. What will be the criteria for continued funding in years 2-4?
This is based on how well clients are enrolled and maintained. While targets will be established, it is expected that the first year will be lower.

8. What kind of data access will there be to individual site data and who will own it? The data belongs to the site. Only summary/aggregate data will be requested and no aggregation of data across all sites will be conducted. We do not need outcome data as we assume that if the protocol is followed, the outcome would be the same as NCICAS.

9. We serve only the uninsured. Are we still eligible?
Yes, as long as you can meet the criteria and provide the services outlined in the RFA.

10. Is there supposed to be some legal setup to the consortia?
Any combination of sites will be accepted. Just demonstrate how it is held together.

11. How do we show that we meet the patient population criteria?
Provide some breakdown of the clinic population and its insurance sources, or something that demonstrates your population.

12. Is there a cap on in-kind contributions?
No cap, but part of the criteria will evaluate how much institutions are contributing.

13. Does the physician consultant have to be a pediatric pulmonologist?
No. If the physician is qualified to do the work, they can be used. Be sure to provide CV.

14. What is the Project Manager's day-to-day role? If it's a multi-site project, do they have to have one for each site?
The Project Manager is the one who pulls it off and supervises staff. It depends on what you need and your setup. Please provide an appropriate justification.

15. Can a RN or NP substitute for the MSW position?
No. This intervention must be provided by a MSW. They are uniquely qualified to do this work and will be expected in this position.

16. What is the total funding available?
Expectations include funding 20-25 units at around $100,000 per unit.

17. What would be the ideal applicant?
Preference would be give to organized clinical systems located throughout the United States that are listed in the RFA.

18. What is a large urban area?
No definition is provided. Apply if a sufficient number of SES exists within a city.

19. Will you consider areas with high asthma rates in surrounding rural areas that make up the population?
No. This hasn't been tested in rural areas.

20. Are the training costs in the budget for the asthma counselor or the client?
The training costs are for the asthma counselor.

21. How will we assess other interventions that might impact outcomes, like the school air quality programs, coalitions, etc?
F unds to support extra evaluation on outcomes are not available at this time. If you wish to do so, you might want to gather outcome data.

22. What is the suggested size for the classroom sessions?
It depends on the space available and how many people can be accommodated while still allowing for interaction.

23. What is the suggested staffing level for support staff?
1 person per unit is suggested. The person coordinates appointments and babysitting duties, among other responsibilities. Base it on your other support available.

24. If you have kids drop out, can you continue to recruit?
We expect some degree of dropouts. You need eighty children in at all times, but it need not be the same eighty children. It will take 6-8 months to get up to that level, then others will rotate off as they finish, move, etc.

25. What kind of data reporting will be required?
Quarterly reports on processes like number of children enrolled, retained and finished are expected.

26. Could you describe who will do the review?
The panel will have members from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), Alliance of Community Health Plans (ACHP), National Institutes of Health (NIH) and other independent organizations.
