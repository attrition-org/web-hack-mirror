Retail Video Watch Archives
 

Haiko
Mr. Susil S. Dungarwal - He is currently working for the Hiranandani group of Companies as Chief Manager - Development & Operations, Retail Division. The group intends to get into multi-segment retailing viz Supermarkets, department stores, speciality stores, & lifestyle stores. The group had soft launched the first supermarket under the brand name "HAIKO".

Pantaloon
Mr. Kishore Biyani. CEO Pantaloon Retail (India) Limited  - the force behind the Group Pantaloon, had a vision - to create the largest retailing conglomerate in the country. He is instrumental in building up a network of distributors; dealers and franchises through out the country.

Ebony
Mr. HS Narula is the Chairman of the DS Group of companies, which has set up the Ebony chain of retail stores. From his varied experiences abroad, he realized the relevance and importance of organised retail in India and decided to pool in his international expertise to take the initiative of redefining the concept of retailing for Indians. 
Shoppers Stop

Mr B V Nagesh, has been selected as one of the top 50 managers in India in a survey of Managers who can influence Indian business in the 21st Century.  Currently the Chief Executive Officer of Shoppers Stop, Mr Nagesh has been the driving force behind the success of this Rs 180 crore retail chain.
Globus

Mr. Ved Prakash Arya CEO, The Globus Chain of Stores was working with one of the Rajan Raheja Group companies, before taking up this challenging assignment. Under the leadership of Mr. Arya, the company has implemented the latest retail technology thus achieving the status of being among the largest garment and accessory stores.
Crossroad
Ms. Urvi Piramal, who is also jointly heading Piramal Enterprises, a Rs. 1,500 crore Group. Mrs. Piramal is a Bachelor in Science and has completed an AMP from Harvard Business School, and is the Vice Chairperson of Crossroads
Westside
Simone Tata, chairperson of Trent Ltd, who owns the Westside brand. She shares her insights on the retail business and the future of Westside stores.
