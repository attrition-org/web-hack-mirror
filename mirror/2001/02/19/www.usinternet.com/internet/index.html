Integrated Services Digital Network (ISDN) is used by the home-user and the corporate world because the price is reasonable and the performance is good. ISDN has two channels 1B and 2B, or 64K and 128K. If the through-put of data is less than 64K, then the line will travel at that speed. If the data is 112K, then the 1B and 2B channels will bond to increase the bandwidth to the full 128K bandwidth allowed.

Frame Relay allows for multiple logins and data transfer through a single line. When connecting Frame Relay into a network, there are two additional components to complete the connection. One is a DSU/CSU (similar to a modem), which talks TCP/IP (Internet Protocol). The second is a Router that routes all traffic throughout your network. Frame Relay is one of the most stable of any connection and is sold on low ratio to ensure your traffic through-put. That is why it is more expensive than ISDN or DSL. The other great thing about Frame Relay, unlike ISDN, is that it allows for growth. ISDN speeds peek at 128K; Frame Relay allows for T-1, T-3, OC12's, and so on.
