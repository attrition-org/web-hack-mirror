Introduction to Course

New millennium has in a dynamic way brought technical revolution in India. Needless to add that digital technology has now crept in to every sphere of human activity, be it a simple contract or a highly skilled criminal activity. Thus the laws, which govern the existing matters of Contract, Patent, Trademarks and crime, are unable to cope with the technological invented problems. It has therefore, been necessary in every country and in India too to think in terms of modifying, amending or enacting new laws to deal with these legal problems. It is with this background that Indian Government too in its good faith has brought out a uniform comprehensive Act (Information Technology Act, 2000) to deal with such problems. This Act has been drafted too technically, which requires interpretation of techno-legal terms for proper understanding. Even to understand other legal developments in cyber laws (i.e. Domain disputes, trademarks, copyright etc.) basic knowledge of techno-legal terms is must both from practical and academic point of view.

The present course intends to provide an insight to participants to know the existing legal regime dealing with these growing problems as to well as evolve by interaction with experts the possible futurology of laws and cyber related disputes both civil and criminal. Keeping in view the constraints of time and other related problems the indialegal.com intends to impart training course leading to Diploma in Cyber Laws. This course will equip the participants to deal with cyber related problems in there respective sphere of activity be it legal, technical or commercial.
