Ask the Torch!

Are you experiencing problems in your council?   Do you just need a new idea to get your council and school energized again?  Why don't you Ask the Torch?   Members of the WASC TORCH BEARERS are here to help you - after all, they've been there!

 

 
I WANT TO BE A STUDENT COUNCIL OFFICER
Q. I have always wanted to be a student council officer, but I'm not sure what they're supposed to do. 
A. I think it's great that you want to serve your school by being a student council officer!  In the Student Zone you can read officer profiles to help you get a better idea of responsibilities. Between now and your elections, why not volunteer to be a committee chairperson? Heading up a committee is a great way to get your feet wet, and you'll learn valuable skills in the process. You can read more information about committee chair responsibilities in the Student Zone.  Good luck!
 
SUMMER LEADERSHOP AND SCHOOL MEMBERSHIP
Q. I've gone to WASC Camp for two years, and next year I want to go to Level 3, but the high school I'm going to isn't a member of WASC -- can I still go?
A. Yes, you can still attend the Level 3 summer workshop, you'll just have to pay the non-member fee.  Your current student council advisor might be able to provide you with the camp registration forms.  Also, if your high school hasn't joined the WASC, maybe your middle school student council could offer a gift of WASC Membership to the high school.   
 
FUNDRAISING IDEAS
Q. We're planning a class trip in May. It's going to cost $10 a person, and there are 200 of us. How do we raise $2,000 in four months? That's a lot of money.
A. You're right, that is a lot of money, but I have confidence that you can do it! Here are some quick ideas to get you started:
  • Donut Sales
  • Sell Chocolate Roses during lunch hours the week before Valentine's Day, then have Student Council members deliver them in homerooms on the 14th.
  • Penny Drive
  • Shovel snow or do yard work for members of your community
  • Car Wash
  • Hold a Dance
  • Make and sell custom T-shirts
  • Craft Sale
  • Movie Night

I'm sure it won't take you long to come up with some great money-raising ideas for your class!
  
IDEAS FOR HOMECOMING WEEK
Q. We always do the same thing year after year for our Homecoming Week. Where can I find some new ideas for next year?
A. Well, you've definitely come to the right place. One of the greatest benefits of WASC membership is the opportunity to meet students from other schools -- and share ideas. I recommend that you speak to your advisor about attending an upcoming event. In the Fall, each WASC Region hosts a JAM Drive-In and a Fall Conference. In the Spring, two outstanding State Conferences are held to get students fired up. And from April to August, seven week-long leadership workshops are held, at five different levels. For more information on these programs, please visit our Major Programs section. And don't forget to check out the Student Zone to get great ideas from other state leaders.
 
HOMECOMING BONFIRES
Q. Our school board says we can no longer have homecoming bonfires, what kind of spirit-building activities could we do to substitute for this event?
A. I'm sure you understand why your school board voted this way, they just have the safety of all their students in mind.   But don't be discouraged, there are lots of things you can do to keep spirits high.   For example:
  • Powder Puff Football Game
  • Float Building Competition Between Classes
  • Spirit Days
  • Door or Locker Decorating Contest
  • Penny Drive
  • Class Skit or Talent Competitions
  • Junior Olympics

I'm sure you can come up with many more ideas for your school.   This is an opportunity to think "out of the box" and come up with something unique for your student body.   Good luck!
  
OUR COUNCIL CAN'T GET ORGANIZED!
Q. We can't seem to get organized.   People volunteer to help with events, but they don't follow through.   I'm a new President - what should I do?
A. You're not alone.   Leading a student council is an incredibly tough job, but I'm sure you're up to the challenge.  I think I would start with a retreat for your council.   This can be at a conference center, at the school, or in someone's backyard.   Maybe all your student council needs is some instruction on how to be a successful member or officer.   The WASC has many great resources for you, and people that are willing to help you plan something in your school.   Also, talk with your advisor about attending the Advisor/Officer Conference.   You'll learn many things you can use back home.   You might also want to check out the poem called, Are You an Active Member?   It might be just the perfect thing to read at the close of your next meeting.
