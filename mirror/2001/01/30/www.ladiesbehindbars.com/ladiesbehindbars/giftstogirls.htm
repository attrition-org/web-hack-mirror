Gifts to Ladies

Security is always a concern, from a prison administrator's point of view. Contraband, in its numerous forms, varies widely from institution to institution. It's always advisable to call/or write the respective institution where the lady is incarcerated, obtaining advise and assistance before you send the lady anything other than a card or letter. Ask for a list of noncontraband items. The institution will mail one to you. Do this before you begin, and it will save you time and expense.

If you submit something that is considered contraband to an institution, the lady may receive disciplinary action based upon the items you mailed her; she may be required to forfeit "good" or "gain" time (that is time off their sentence for good behavior) for your mistake. Take care not to allow this to happen.

Most institution allow the ladies to receive cards, letters, photographs (of a nonsexual or non suggestive nature), a small number of postage stamps, and U.S. Postal Money Orders are the only form of money inmates can obtain; never send cash. Other types of money orders just won't do. Therefore, be sure that if you do send a money order,that it's only in the form of a U.S. Postal Money Order.

The ladies will appreciate your simple act of kindness, friendship, emotional support and love.
