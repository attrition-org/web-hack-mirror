14 die in Kashmir explosion, ambush


SRINAGAR, Dec 26: Fourteen people, including four Indian soldiers, were killed and 31 others injured in a car bomb blast on Monday and in an ambush by Mujahideen in occupied Kashmir on Tuesday.

A police spokesman said a patrol of the 32nd Rashtriya Rifles unit was attacked in the Kupwara district. "The patrol was passing the village of Wavoora Lolab," about 100km from here, when they were ambushed, he said. The troops returned the gunfire but four soldiers died and eight others injured, he said.

On Monday, 10 people were killed when Mujahideen set off the powerful car bomb near an army zone here, police and witnesses said.

Two groups claimed responsibility for the explosion, with one of them asserting the attack was carried out by a suicide bomber who had traveled from Britain for the mission.

A police spokesman said six Indian soldiers, three civilians and a freedom fighter were among those killed.

At least 23 others were injured in the attack in Srinagar's fortified Badami Bagh army cantonment district.

The previously-unknown Jamiat-ul-Mujahideen was the first to claim responsibility for the blast, adding that the attackers had escaped unharmed.

The Jaish-i-Mohammad also said it was responsible. It identified the attacker as Abu Abdullah from Birmingham, Britain. They said he was on a suicide mission and died in the blast. Srinagar's police chief, however, rejected the claim and said the attack was conducted with the help of a remote-controlled device. He said the blast occurred outside the cantonment gates near a Srinagar shopping mall.

The army headquarters here said the attack was aimed at sabotaging an ongoing Indian process to restore peace in occupied Kashmir. "In efforts to disrupt and annul the ongoing peace negotiations, which are gaining momentum and strengthening with each passing day, militants (Mujahideen) from an as yet unidentified group detonated a car bomb," the army said in a statement.

The Christmas blast is the second car bomb attack in the cantonment area this year. In April, a Jaish-i-Mohammad suicide bomber detonated a car there, killing seven soldiers and injuring several civilians.

And militants of the pan-Islamic Lashkar-i-Taiba stormed the army headquarters last month, killing four soldiers and three army officials.
