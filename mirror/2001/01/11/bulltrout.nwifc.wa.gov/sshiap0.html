WILD SALMON STOCK RESTORATION INITIATIVE

SALMON AND STEELHEAD HABITAT
INVENTORY AND ASSESSMENT PROJECT

ISSUE:

Implementation of SSHIAP is a necessary component for the overall Wild Salmon Restoration Initiative. Identification and evaluation of the status of salmon and steelhead stocks (SASSI) in itself is inadequate without assessing the quality and quantity of their habitat. The purposes and benefits of the SSHIAP are several fold. The habitat inventory and assessment project will provide:

  • the necessary counter part to evaluation of stock status.
  • an assessment of the degradation of the quality and quantity of salmon and steelhead habitat;
  • necessary information to support state, federal, and tribal efforts to conduct watershed analysis as part of state regulation of forest practices and implementation of the President's Forest Plan.
  • information necessary to evaluate impaired water bodies as required under the Clean Water Act;
  • basin specific protection and restoration strategies;
  • a means to prioritize restoration projects that are most cost effective at restoring basin production;
  • a mechanism to coordinate state, federal, tribal, local, and private restoration activities; and
  • the necessary information to integrate habitat strategies with those of harvest and enhancement management programs to allow for overall basin plans to recover salmon and steelhead resources and fisheries.

BACKGROUND:

During the past several years Indian Tribes and WDFW have cooperatively developed a joint assessment of the status of salmon and steelhead stocks in Washington State in response to concerns about declining populations. To address these issues the state and Tribal governments have joined in a Wild Stock Restoration Initiative (WSRI). As part of the WSRI, the state and tribal governments in 1995, conducted the first version of the Washington State Salmon and Steelhead Stock Inventory (SASSI). The SASSI process identified distinct spawning populations (stocks) and described their status based on information currently available. More detailed material on individual stocks has been compiled in regional SASSI appendices.

In the meantime, federal involvement in the issue of stock status has accelerated as a result of the submission of numerous petitions to list salmon and steelhead stocks under the Endangered Species Act and subsequent stock status review by the National Marine Fisheries Service (NMFS) to determine the merit of these petitions.

In order for the State and Tribes to take constructive action to reverse stock decline and achieve recovery, it is critical that the role of habitat loss and impairment in the decline of individual salmon and steelhead populations be well understood and documented. The SASSI Appendices did not assemble or draw conclusions on habitat data or related issues.

The counter part to SASSI is to conduct a joint tribal/state cooperative action to document current habitat conditions, assess the role of habitat degradation and loss in the status of salmon and steelhead stocks, develop stock or watershed/basin specific strategies for habitat protection and restoration, and prepare a cooperative strategy to obtain funding to implement the habitat restoration/protection strategies.

Defining habitat protection and restoration strategies are only part the solution to a comprehensive recovery of the salmon and steelhead stocks. To achieve stock recovery goals, habitat protection and restoration must be part of an integrated stock strategy that also addresses hatchery production and harvest management..

PURPOSE

The purpose of this project is to develop a blueprint for joint tribal/state cooperative action to document current habitat conditions, assess the role of habitat degradation and loss in the status of salmon and steelhead stocks, develop stock- or watershed/basin specific strategies for habitat protection and restoration, and prepare a cooperative strategy to obtain funding to implement the habitat restoration/protection strategies.

TIMELINE

The project began in August 1995 and will continue through at least August 1997.

PRODUCTS

Products will include:

  1. Expanded habitat sections for the SASSI Appendices that describe the location, amount and current condition of habitats utilized at various life history stages, historic habitat loss, and the natural and human factors contributing to habitat loss and impairment;
  2. Maps showing habitats used by each stock;
  3. A habitat protection and restoration strategy for each stock or watershed;
  4. A list that identifies future study needs to fill data gaps and improve the analysis;
  5. A funding strategy to obtain resources necessary to implement habitat protection/restoration strategies and collect necessary research.

WATERSHED REPORT OUTLINE

To facilitate use of habitat inventory information on a statewide, regional or evolutionary significant (ESU) scale, it is important that information be collected and compiled in a consistent manner. This outline is intended to provide a consistent format for information from each watershed, recognizing that the type, amount and quality of information will vary between watersheds. The following sections of a salmon and steelhead habitat inventory and assessment report are listed in order of priority of completion. It will be important to focus on the first four sections of the report to adequately address the remainder of the sections.

Section 1: Basin Description

This should be a brief section that describes the physical setting of the basin, the stream network, climate, geology, vegetation and the general land-use patterns. Give an overview of the basin that documents its unique characteristics . Sources of information include WARIS, the stream catalog, and watershed management plans.

Section 2: Description of Stocks Present in Basin

This section should describe the salmon and steelhead stocks present in the basin. Describe the life history stages and general habitat requirements for each stock.

Section 3: Current and Historic Habitat Utilization

This section should describe, and identify on maps, the habitats used during the fresh-water and estuarine life history stages of each stock (both current and historic). Sources of information include WARIS, the WDFW stream catalog, and basin specific information on habitat loss.

Section 4: Physical Habitat Description

This section should describe current habitat conditions and the extent and causes of habitat loss and impairment for stream reaches throughout the basin. For each stream reach, describe:

  • Current habitat conditions
  • Nature and extent of historical habitat loss or impairment
  • Factors causing habitat loss or impairment (adverse impacts)

Examples of habitat conditions to consider in the analysis include:

Migration barriers restricting access to historically available habitat such as culverts and road crossings, dams, temperature and pollution barriers, dry stream reaches and other miscellaneous barriers. Also consider adult holding habitat such as spring chinook and summer steelhead.

Spawning and incubation habitat conditions such as loss of spawning gravel, gravel degradation from fine sediments, gravel scour associated with peak flows, pollution, and other destruction, blockage or degradation of spawning habitat.

Summer and over-winter juvenile habitat conditions such as pool habitat (quantity, depth and volume and cover),stream temperature/shading, LWD, low flows, water quality degradation, destruction or blockage of side channel, slough and off-channel habitats, and the destruction, blockage or degradation of juvenile habitats.

Out-migration and estuarine habitat conditions such as availability of channel margin habitats, stranding, destruction of estuarine habitat, shoreline habitat alterations, water quality and other factors causing destruction, blockage or degradation of out-migration and estuarine habitats.

Identify natural constraints and human impacts that are now or have in the past contributed to habitat impairment or loss. Contributing factors should include both those that directly impact habitat and those that alter watershed processes contributing to disturbance or alteration of downstream habitat. In addition, identify any non-impaired habitats that are sensitive to future impairment due to changes in land-use or other factors.

Sources of information include WARIS for stream blockages, WDFW stream catalog, TFW Ambient Monitoring habitat surveys, Watershed Analysis reports; state, federal, tribal, or local water quality reports, watershed management plans and miscellaneous habitat surveys. To the maximum extent possible, quantify historic loss recognizing appropriate assumptions and caveats.

Section 5: Effects of Habitat Loss and Impairment on Stocks Present in the Basin

Discuss how habitat loss and impairment (discussed in Section 4) is affecting overall production of each stock present in the basin. Identify significant factors limiting production at each life history stage. Significant limiting factors will probably vary among stocks due to differences in the life history stages present, and differences in the locations of important production areas. Using the best information available, use quantitative methods (such as those developed by the Skagit System or the PNPTC Coho model) to estimate production lost due to habitat destruction and impairment for each species. Compare limiting factors to identify the most significant overall production bottlenecks for each species.

Section 6: Habitat Protection and Restoration Strategy

Use the information assembled in the previous steps to develop habitat protection and restoration strategy for each basin that addresses the needs of each stock present. The habitat protection/restoration strategy should identify:

  1. Habitat goals and performance standards (targets for habitat conditions).
  2. Measures (and information) needed to protect habitat (including action needed by state. tribal, federal, local and private entities).
  3. Measures (and information) necessary to restore habitat (these may need to be rather general given the time and information constraints).
Section 7: Research and monitoring information gaps

This section should identify information gaps, i.e. areas where further research or monitoring would improve the habitat inventory, or the habitat protection and restoration plan.

Section 8: Funding proposal

This section is an opportunity for parties in the watershed to identify and coordinate funding needs for implementation of the habitat protection and restoration plan or to address information gaps identified in section 7.
