PJ’S In Vietnam
The Story Of Air Rescue In Vietnam
As Seen Through The Eyes Of its Pararescuemen

Volume One – PJ’s in Vietnam, covering 1964 through Tet in 1968 is finally complete. It has been two years three months since I stated my intention to document the history of Air Rescue in Vietnam. Initially I had conceptualized a single 400-page book that would cover the entire war. After collecting thousands of pages of primary source material and outlining the draft copy, it became apparent that it would take more than 800 pages to do this historical work justice. One of the books goals is to cover all significant missions flown during the war. Rather than modify that goal and delete known material, I chose to publish in two volumes.
 
Now volume one is complete. It is over 400 pages of action packed stories about the missions many of you and your friends flew in Vietnam. It is not a story only about PJ’s. Each mission in the book also names the pilots and other enlisted crew members. Volume one has rescues flown by HU-16’s, HH-43’s, CH-3’s, HH-3’s and introduces the HH-53 into the war. It is also a story of the supporting aircraft that made air rescues happen in the war. Many of the missions have the names of the Sandy pilots. It is also about the survivors. Almost all of the missions in the book have the survivors names. I also cover the accomplishments of our maintenance support, tactics development and much, much more.
 
Appendixes in the back of the book cover all known missions (over 1700) the names of the rescue crew, date, location, type SAR aircraft, type downed aircraft and name of the survivor/s. This book is extremely detailed and factual. It is the only comprehensive work of its type. And now it is done !!!
