In 1961 Bulgarian intellectuals laid the foundations of a new higher school of medicine in Varna the idea of which they not only cherished but also developed trying to bring to perfection. It incarnated the nation's educational ideal and concentrated medical knowledge and research spirit. The future promises more and more bright young people - both Bulgarians and foreigners - to find their vocation here and thereafter serve the art of medicine. Is not this what a university's schooling is meant to be?

The beginning ... The Varna University of Medicine was founded in 1961 as Higher Medical Institute of Varna. The date October 1, 1961 when the first students from the third course crossed the threshold of the first Higher School of Medicine in North-Eastern Bulgaria is considered its birthday. The creation of the third higher medical school in Bulgaria meets the increasing public requirements of highly qualified medical staff and specialized physician's care for the population of this region of the country. In 1965 the first 78 physicians graduated. All main departments and clinics were established during the first three years. Initially, use was made of the existing clinical basis of the District Hospital and City Hospital of Varna. Along with the extention of the Institute, the building of new hospitals was carried out, additional beds were provided not only for educational purposes but also to meet the needs of health care in North-Eastern Bulgaria. Structurally, the departments, clinics, and laboratories were upgraded in order to correspond to the criteria and requirements of contemporary medical science and practice. The basis for fundamental sciences education and the clinical hospital basis has been constantly enlarged and enriched. Nowadays the process of education is conducted in the buildings of the Varna University Hospital, of the Surgical, Psychiatric, Dermato-Venereal, Obstetric-Gynaecologic, Ophthalmic, Infectious and some other hospitals as well. Many recognized specialists work with the latest equipment for diagnostic and therapeutic purposes there. In 1993/94 the specialty of management in public health and social care was introduced. In 1995 the Higher Medical Institute of Varna was approved as the Varna University of Medicine. Over 6400 students have graduated with medical diploma, including 620 foreigners from 45 countries in Europe, Africa, Asia, and South America. The number of foreign postgraduates already reaches up to 110. The Varna University of Medicine is an authoritative centre for teaching-educational, research and diagnostic-therapeutic activities with undoubted contribution to the progress of medical science and education.
