MEDICINE

EDUCATION MEDICINE Full-time medical students only can be admitted to the University. The course of academic studies covers 6 years for medicine. It consists of 10 semesters and 10 months of clinical practice (internship). The teaching process is conducted in 35 departments and 44 clinics staffed with 118 professors and associate professors as well as with 322 assistants and lecturers. Students' education is performed through lectures, seminars and laboratory practice. It is group-based as groups count between 8 and 10 students.

PRE-CLINICAL PERIOD During the pre-clinical period (I-IV semester) fundamental biomedical sciences such as biology, biophysics, biochemistry, anatomy and histology, physiology, etc. are studied. The students get acquainted with the structure and function of the human body, perform their first experiments on laboratory animals, and enrich their knowledge in medical chemistry and medical physics as a basis for their education in biochemistry and biophysics. Multimedia computer language laboratories for foreign language training in English, French, German and Bulgarian using the latest CALL software are used.

PROPAEDEUTIC PERIOD The propaedeutic period (V-VI semester) aims at making students familiar with the basic principles of origin and development of the pathological process and the diseases, with the methods of patient's examination as well as with the clinical features of the most common diseases. The main theoretical and applied biomedical disciplines such as pathological anatomy, pathological physiology, propaedeutics of internal medicine and surgery, pharmacology and toxicology, medical statistics, etc. are studied. The students already experiment confidently on laboratory animals and face the patient in a hospital environment for the first time. The teaching of the specific clinical thinking of future physicians and researchers starts at the patient's bed. The link between the propaedeutic and clinical periods is realized through the education of some disciplines such as roentgenology and radiology, medical psychology, social medicine, etc.

CLINICAL PERIOD The clinical teaching (VII-X semester) maintains an orientation towards an immediate involvement with patients. The theoretical course aims at creating skills for argumented diagnostic and therapeutic thinking and for analysis and synthesis of the large amount of scientific information collected during the investigation and treatment of the patient. At that cause-consequence relations of single diseases in the concrete case are looked for. Besides the students learn jow to handle the most-contemporary diagnostic and therapeutic equipment available in the departments and clinics. All main clinical disciplines are studied. The students are educated as carriers of a high professional morals, fellow-feeling, and humane attitude towards the suffering patient and his relatives.

INTERNSHIP The internship (10 months) provides students - trainee-doctors the opportunity to acquire a more complete idea of the whole diagnostic, therapeutic and preventive activities in the hospital and polyclinics. They participate together with the attending doctors in the various forms of patient's care such as doctor's visits, performance of different diagnostic and therapeutic procedures, consultations, etc. At that they have to follow-up the status of concrete patients for a period of several weeks - from admission to the clinic to discharge. Together with that they prepare abstracts devoted to actual topics of the corresponding diseases, participate in colloquia, seminars, presentations of new-drug manufacturer firms, etc. In the Varna University of Medicine, an internship in an optional subject is carried out, too, enabling a better preparation of trainee-doctors for their future work.

STUDETS who have successfully passed their state qualification examinations in the specialty of medicine obtain a diploma according to an established standard for completed medical university studies with an education-qualification of master's degree and are awarded the professional qualification of "physician". Students who have successfully completed their university studies in health management obtain a M.A. diploma with an education-qualification of master's degree. All the students who have completed their university studies obtain their diploma at an official ceremony in an solemn setting facing their lecturers, colleagues, parents and the public of the city of Varna. They swear by the Hippocratic oath and start their way at the service of the greatest welfare - the human health.


PUBLIC HEALTH MANAGEMENT

The training is full-time and extramural. This specialty faces the new needs of up-to-date health care management of institutions of different type. The curriculum is elaborated in agreement with the international experience of training university graduates in management in public health and the social sphere.

The course of academic studies in public health management includes 9 semesters, one-month specialised pre-graduation practice and defence of a M.A. thesis. Students' education is conducted through lectures, seminars, and practical exercises. The students independently prepare their course papers.

The purpose of the training consists in the acquisition of integrated knowledge and practical skills for independent posing and solving the specific administrative and business problems related to the reconstruction of the health-care and social system in our country as well as for formation and management of health-care and social funds, hospital banks, etc. The problems of the immediate management of the hospital and social institutions of different range are of special interest to the students, too.
