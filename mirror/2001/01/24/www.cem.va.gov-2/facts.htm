• As of October 1, 2000, NCA currently maintains more than 2.3 million gravesites at 119 national cemeteries in 39 states (and Puerto Rico) as well as 33 soldier’s lots and monument sites.

  • More than 340,000 full-casket gravesites, 58,000 in-ground gravesites for cremated remains, and 29,700 columbarium niches are available in already developed acreage in our 119 national cemeteries.

  • There are approximately 13,440 acres of land within established installations in NCA. Just under half are undeveloped and have the potential of providing more than 2.9 million gravesites.

  • Of our 119 national cemeteries, 61 are open to all interments; 31 can accommodate cremated remains and family members of those already interred; and 27 are closed to new interments, but may accommodate family members in already occupied gravesites.

  • Of the more than 82,700 interments conducted in FY 2000, 74 percent were in our 20 busiest national cemeteries: Riverside (Calif.), Calverton (N.Y.), Florida, Fort Snelling (Minn.), Jefferson Barracks (Mo.), Willamette (Ore.), Fort Sam Houston (Texas), Fort Logan (Colo.), Fort Rosecrans (Calif.), National Memorial Cemetery of Arizona, Houston (Texas), Massachusetts, San Joaquin Valley (Calif.), Long Island (N.Y.), Tahoma (Wash.), Indiantown Gap (Pa.), Puerto Rico, Abraham Lincoln (Ill.), Santa Fe (N.M.), and Fort Custer (Mich.) National Cemeteries.

  • Largest National Cemetery: Calverton National Cemetery, N.Y., 1045 acres
    Smallest: Hampton National Cemetery (VAMC), Va., .03 acres
    Oldest: 14 established in 1862 (12 are currently maintained by VA)
    Newest: Ohio Western Reserve National Cemetery, opened June 19, 2000

  • Major expansion projects at the National Memorial Cemetery of Arizona (NMCA), Florida, Jefferson Barracks (Mo.), Leavenworth (Kan.), Fort Rosecrans (Calif.), Fort Sam Houston (Texas), and Fort Logan (Colo.) National Cemeteries will add over 167,000 gravesites to the Administration.

  • In FY 2000, NCA provided well over 327,000 headstones and markers.

  • Approximately 322,000 Presidential Memorial Certificates were provided to the loved ones of deceased veterans in FY 2000.

  • Since 1980, our State Cemetery Grants Program (SCGP) has obligated over $82 million in grants for the establishment, expansion or improvement of more than 42 State veterans cemeteries. In FY 2000, SCGP-supported State cemeteries provided over 14,300 interments.

  • Volunteers donated more than 246,000 hours at our national cemeteries during FY 2000.

  • More than 8.8 million people visited our national cemeteries in FY 2000.


    Since 1973

  • Annual interments in our national cemeteries have more than doubled from 36,400 to more than 82,700.

  • The total NCA acreage in established installations has more than tripled from 4,260 to 13,440 in FY 2000.

  • Total gravesites maintained has increased from 1.2 million to more than 2.3 million in FY 2000.

  • NCA provided more than 327,000 headstones and markers in FY 2000 compared to 190,000 headstones and markers in 1973.

  • Over 7 million headstones and markers have been provided by NCA since 1973.

  • Since NCA responsibility for the Presidential Memorial Certificates program began in 1988, more than 3.6 million certificates have been provided to the loved ones of deceased veterans.


    Veterans Statistics

  • Veterans’ deaths are expected to peak at 620,000 in 2008. It is estimated that more than 574,000 veterans died in 2000, 66 percent of whom are estimated to be World War II era.

  • There are no known living veterans who participated in any war era prior to WWI.

  • The following is a list of the number of American participants and living veterans in all conflicts after the Spanish-American War:
