    On May 12, 2000, the Dallas-Fort Worth National Cemetery was dedicated as the 118th national cemetery within the Department of Veterans Affairs National Cemetery Administration. Burial operations began on May 15.

    The new 638-acre cemetery will serve more than 460,000 veterans and their families in north-central Texas.

    In 1987, VA identified the Dallas-Fort Worth area as one of the ten areas of the country most in need of burial space for veterans based on veteran populations.

    An Environmental Impact Statement was prepared in 1991 and the Secretary of Veterans Affairs formally selected a site near Mountain Creek Lake, between Dallas and Fort Worth, in 1992.

    A contract to develop the master plan for the new cemetery was awarded to Carter & Burgess, Inc. in 1994.

    A $12.7 million construction contract was awarded to Incore Incorporated of San Antonio, Texas, in October 1997. Construction has begun and the cemetery is expected to open in 2000.

    When the 638-acre cemetery site is fully developed it will provide 280,000 burial spaces. Initial construction will develop approximately 110 acres, including:

    • 12,000 gravesites, including 2,000 lawn crypts and 2,200 columbaria and garden niches for cremated remains;
    • Cemetery entrance area and public information center; memorial walkway;
    • Two committal service shelters;
    • Flag/assembly area with lake;
    • Administration/maintenance complex; and,
    • Roadways, utilities and landscaping.

    Jimmy Adamson was selected as Dallas-Fort Worth’s director in April 1998.
