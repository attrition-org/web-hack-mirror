    December 18, 2000


    CONGRESS SUPPORTS VA NATIONAL CEMETERY GROWTH

    Washington, D.C. - The Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) plans to accelerate its planning, land acquisition, design and construction of several new national veterans cemeteries, based upon recent Congressional appropriations.

    “We are pleased to be given the resources to carry out, expeditiously, our mission to honor the service of our nation’s heroes – those who served in our armed forces,” said Acting Secretary of Veterans Affairs Hershel Gober. “More of our veterans will be able to find a final resting place in our national shrines.”

    Congress recently approved:

    • $12 million to construct the future national cemetery at Fort Sill, Okla., serving the greater Oklahoma City area. The land has been acquired and master planning completed. Design has begun and may be completed by summer 2001, when a contract may be awarded for construction. Construction could take two to three years.

    • $15 million to purchase land to build a cemetery in South Florida. VA is considering two potential sites near Miami in Palm Beach County, where environmental assessments are underway.

    • $2.5 million to complete master planning and design for a new cemetery near Atlanta. The process to acquire a site offered for donation is underway. When completed, master planning and design will go forward.

    • $1 million to complete master planning for a site near Pittsburgh. • $2 million for advanced planning purposes for three national cemeteries. The funds will be used for master planning of cemeteries to be located near Sacramento, Calif., Detroit and Miami.

    VA’s goal is to have national cemeteries operational at all six of these locations within five years. VA identified these locations among areas of greatest need for national cemeteries, based on concentrations of veteran population, in a report to Congress earlier this year as required by the Veterans Millennium Health Care and Benefits Act.

    Veteran deaths are expected to peak at 620,000 annually by 2008, up from 572,000 estimated for 2000. Even after the peak year, it will be nearly a decade before death rates fall below current levels.

    Gober noted that VA opened five national cemeteries since 1997, representing the largest expansion of the National Cemetery Administration since its beginnings in the Civil War. He said VA’s goal is for 88 percent of veterans to live within 75 miles of a national cemetery or a VA-supported state veterans’ cemetery by 2006. Currently, nearly 75 percent of veterans live within that distance.

    Information on VA burial benefits can be obtained from national cemetery offices or by calling VA regional offices toll-free on 1-800-827-1000, or from VA web sites on the Internet at http://www.va.gov or http://www.cem.va.gov.
