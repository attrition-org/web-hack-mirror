Israeli troops kill Palestinian woman

 

The latest victim, Fatma Abu Jeifh, was driving home in the late afternoon after university studies when she was hit by Israeli gunfire

 

January 07, 2001, 08:37 PM
OCCUPIED JERUSALEM (Reuters)

 

- Israeli soldiers shot dead a 20-year-old Palestinian woman on Sunday at a crossroads near a Jewish settlement, east of the West Bank city of Nablus, Palestinian hospital sources and family members said.

It was the second young Palestinian woman reported shot and killed by Israeli troops in two days.

The latest victim, Fatma Abu Jeifh, was driving home in the late afternoon after university studies when she was hit by gunfire that came from a nearby hill manned by Israeli soldiers near the Alon Moreh settlement, her cousin, Nasser Abu Jeifh, said.

The woman died of chest wounds at a Nablus hospital, hospital sources said.

The Israeli army said it was checking the report.

The woman’s death brings the toll to 333 Palestinians have been killed in 14 weeks of violence that erupted at the end of September in the wake of deadlocked peace talks.

Israeli troops shot and killed a 19-year-old Palestinian woman in her home in the divided West Bank town of Hebron on Friday. An 18-year-old woman was wounded in the chest in the same incident.

Palestinian witnesses said the two women were shot in their home in Hebron. Children were playing with fireworks in the yard and Israeli soldiers opened fire on the house.
