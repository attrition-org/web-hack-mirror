Our school was opened on the 11th September 1959 with 113 students with the school colours of Royal blue and orange. A globe of the world was adopted as the school logo in 1962 to relate to our school name of  "a fair view of all things". A new logo was introduced during the 1980's.

During 1978 three classrooms and a new library were added to accomodate the increase in numbers.

In 1982 the four factions were given a name representing a locality of Collie.

Red - Cardiff - originally an important coal mining area that supported a big township. It became a virtual ghost town during the 1960's. It is situated about 13 km south east of Collie.

Blue - Ewington - situated about 5 km's north east of Collie, it was the site of open cut coal mining between 1952 - 60.

Green - Cheetara - a large housing estate at the northern end of town.

Gold - Stockton - a large mine about 10 km's east of the town. It was closed in the early 1960's and is now filled with water and used as a recreation area.

A new wing of classrooms with an activity area was opened in 1992 along with a purpose built library and undercover area.
