HISTORY:

" ARAB COMMUNICATION GROUP " ( ACG ) was the first to foray into the demanding field of specialized industrial exhibitions, it was established in 1986 to organise the first printing and packaging trade show in Egypt " PrintPack 88 ".

Organising such a difficult and challenging exhibition ensured that the company started very strong.

The company's growth led to organise high profile international exhibitions including: " Plastex 92 " (The Pan-Arab/African Plastic and Rubber Exhibition) and "ITCE 94 " (The Pan-Arab/African Exhibition for Textile, Embroidery & Sewing Machinery, Accessories & Finished Products ).

The company's early success was attributed to a professional and enlightened management that was based on introducing and exceeding the highest international standards, paying attention to details meticulously, thoroughly studying the industry's needs and a clear vision for the future.

In 1995, the company organised the biggest " Vehicles, Buses, Motorcar Worshops, Automobile Spare Parts & Accessories Exhibition " in Egypt and the Arab World, namely " Automech 96 ".

In 1996, having three international branches in Singapore, Cyprus and England as well as 15 trade representative offices throughout the world, ACG became the biggest Arab Group for organising international exhibitions and conferences in Egypt and the Middle East.

November 4th, 1999 a partnership agreement was signed between ACG and the UK based ITE Group Plc. one of the biggest exhibition organising companies worldwide (ITE Group organises around 150 trade shows per year ).

This association crowned the company's success and firmly placed ACG & ITE as the leading exhibition organiser in Egypt and the Middle East.
