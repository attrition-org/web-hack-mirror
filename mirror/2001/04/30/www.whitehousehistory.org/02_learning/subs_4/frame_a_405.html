"How can I make history come alive for students?" Putting historical events in a comprehensible context is one of the most important tasks that educators confront. Understanding the roles of key players or groups in the drama of American history is critical to building a complete picture of past events and their influence on the future.

Since George Washington essentially invented the office of the president in the 1790s, the nation’s chief executive has rarely stayed on the sidelines when major events unfold. To explore the markers of time, the White House Historical Association offers Making History at the White House. It provides teachers with a resource that explores major themes in American history as they evolved within the nation’s most recognizable residence.

In student text you will find images and historical background that will illustrate the role of the president and the White House in American history. Each theme includes an introduction for educators. The historical accounts are intended to be brief, adding an extra dimension to topics already explored in the classroom. Suggestions for themes that might be offered in the future may be sent to edu@whha.org.
