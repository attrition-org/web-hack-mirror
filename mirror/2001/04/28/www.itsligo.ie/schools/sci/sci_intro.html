A major proportion of Ireland's and Europe's wealth-creating activity is Science - based. For this reason, Governments and the industrial sectors invest heavily in the training and education of young scientists, who are essential to economic development. This is reflected very strongly in Ireland's ERDF projects - a high proportion of which are scientifically based. There is an exciting future in Ireland and in EU partner countries, for technicians and scientists, educated in specialist fields, but more importantly, with the hands-on experience of the sophisticated scientific equipment, used in modern laboratories. These laboratories form an essential part of most modern industries and Government Departments such as Environment, Marine and Agriculture, Food and Forestry. Science courses at Sligo Regional Technical College provide this hands-on training. In 1994, Sligo Regional Technical College invested heavily in providing new, state-of-the-art laboratories, for training of students, particularly in the areas of Applied Biology, Food Science, Analytical Chemistry and Environmental Science. The School of Science is a dynamic part of the College and provided the first Honours Degree in Environmental Science in Ireland and also graduated the first PH D in the whole Regional College system. In the Academic year 1993/94, the School of Science produced 26 graduates with Honours Degrees, 7 Post-Graduates Diplomas, 25 M Sc Degrees by course work, 2 M Sc Degrees by Research and one PH D. The School of Science has two departments Department of Applied Science Department of Environmental Science A wide range of courses exist within the School, at National Certificate, National Diploma, Honours Degree, and Post Graduate levels. Students are recruited mainly from Leaving Certificate into National Certificate courses in Applied Biology, Applied Chemistry and Food Science. Recruitment to any of the above three Certificate courses is through a common first year general science foundation course, with students indicating their preferences at the end of the first year. This provides students, who may not have taken all three science subjects to Leaving Certificate with an opportunity to gain knowledge in each one. Entry to a particular option in second year is subject to availability of places. 

There are two, long serving National Diploma courses:- National Diploma in Science (Pollution Assessment and Control) National Diploma in Science (Analytical Chemistry) 

Students may enter these Diploma courses by one of two modes, from Leaving Certificate or by progression from National Certificate courses. 

The School of Science has Honours Degree courses in Applied Chemistry and Applied Biology, run jointly with Coventry University. Students wishing to attend these courses are recruited from Leaving Certificate to the appropriate National Diploma in Science, spend second year in Coventry University, third year in Sligo RTC and fourth year in Coventry University. They obtain two qualifications, a National Diploma (NCEA) and an Honours Degree (Coventry University). 

The School of Science at Sligo Regional Technical College has a very active Post-Graduate Section, where suitably qualified students may register for Post-Graduate Diplomas and Masters in Science based on course work. In addition, there are approximately ten post graduate students involved in research at M Sc and Ph D level. A new Diploma will commence in the academic year 1995/96 in the area of Safety, Health and Industrial Hygiene subject to the agreement of the NCEA and Department of Education. This is a critical and fast-growing area with all facets of employment in Ireland. Students will be recruited, mainly from Leaving Certificate, but progression may also take place from appropriate Certificate courses. 

The School of Science has strong links with third level institution, within the European Union. There are several ERASMUS and COMETT programmes involving both students and lecturer exchanges with Coventry University (UK), Middlesex University (UK), Giessen Fachhochschule (Germany), Bremen Fachhochschule (Germany), University of Florence (Italy), University of Limoges (France) and others.
