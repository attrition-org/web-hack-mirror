The School of Engineering consists of two Departments, the Department of Civil Engineering and Construction Studies and the Department of Mechanical Engineering and Electronics. There are 650 wholetime equivalent students taking courses at Certificate, Diploma and Degree level all validated by the National Council for Educational Awards and there is also an involvement in the education of apprentices. Areas of particular specialisation in the School are Quality Assurance, Tool Design and Water Engineering. 

One of the strongest features of the course structure in the School is the facility for students to progress from an initial Certificate programme to Degree level programmes. Examples are as follows: 

1. National Diploma holders with Merit in either Science or Engineering are considered for admission to the Bsc programmes within the School of Engineering.

2. Students on the National Certificate in Electronic or Mechanical Engineering following selection on merit may avail of a joint study programme with the University of Coventry leading to a BEng (Combined Engineering).

3. Graduates from all Certificate and Diploma programmes have successfully transferred to Degree Programme in the past.
 

Advanced plans exist for the widening of this process to allow easy access to new degree programmes within the School. The School offers a particular welcome and encouragement to girls as it feels that traditional attitudes militate against girls even considering courses in Engineering. When girls have taken the programmes available, they have been most successful, regularly topping the class. 

Areas of research in the School coincide with the specialisations in Quality, Tool Design and Water Engineering. The most obvious example of this is the National Toolmaking Centre which is located on the Campus and which houses the Toolmaking Industry Research and Advisory Centre.
