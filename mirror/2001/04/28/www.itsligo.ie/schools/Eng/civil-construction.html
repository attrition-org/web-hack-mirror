The Department provides three undergraduate programmes of study as follows:

Civil Engineering

This programme comprises the two year National Certificate in Civil Engineering, the one year add-on Diploma in Engineering (Water) and the two-year add-on course leading to the award of a Bachelor of Engineering (Civil).
Students who achieve Merit level Civil Engineering Diploma awards in other Institutes of Technology can gain entry into the two-year add-on degree course.
All graduates of the degree course to date are currently employed as Civil Engineers or are engaged in postgraduate courses of study.

Construction Studies

This programme comprises the two-year National Certificate in Construction Studies and the one-year add-on Diploma in Construction Economics.
The Diploma in Construction Economics is recognized by the Royal Institute of Chartered Surveyors. Arrangements have been made so that graduates of the Diploma can gain full professional qualifications in Quantity Surveying by transferring to another University for two further years, one year of which consists of a supervised work placement in the construction industry.

National Diploma in Interior Design and Architecture

The aim of this three-year course is to produce Interior Design graduates qualified to diploma level who can assume positions of responsibility in the field of Architecture / Interior Design and who will be capable of designing and presenting architectural solutions to environmental problems. Graduates of the course will have a deep understanding of Interior Architecture in terms of design, environment, social considerations and physical context. Subjects include design, graphic communication, technology, management and a European language.

The Department also engages in significant research in waste-water treatment and other civil engineering areas. There are currently four students studying for Masters Degrees by research in the Civil and environmental research laboratory.
