The School of Business and Humanities has a long and successful tradition of high quality education and is dedicated to the provision of full-time and part-time courses of academic excellence and vocational relevance. It offers courses at degree, diploma, certificate and professional level to cover 1100 full-time students. The School's strong portfolio of courses which has a high national and international profile covers areas such as Business Studies, Accountancy, Computing, Office Information Systems, Marketing and Languages, European Business, Art and Design and Applied Social Studies. All of these disciplines contain the facility for students to transfer from Certificate to Diploma and progression from Diploma to Degree is also available to students of Accountancy, Languages and Marketing and European Business.

While the School is justly proud of its academic achievements over the years, it is also very conscious of the necessity of preparing students for the real world of work. This is in part accomplished by the inclusion of the curriculum of subjects such as Computing, Languages, Enterprise, Organisational Behaviour, Projects with field trips as well as mandatory periods of work placement on certain courses in particular should enable students to better cope with the rapidly changing work environment of the 1990's.

A significant feature of the School of Business and Humanities is its strong commitment to the importance of developing links with other institutions of learning in Europe. The School has links with partner Colleges in France, Germany, Greece, Spain, Portugal, Norway, Sweden, Finland, Belgium, the Netherlands and the United Kingdom. Under the ERASMUS Programme in excess of 60 students from partner Colleges in the EU will spend a period of study, usually extending over one academic year, attending courses in this School. The mingling of so many foreign students with the Irish student population serves as an enriching influence on the academic, cultural and social life of the College generally and the School of Business and Humanities in particular. Reciprocal arrangements under the ERASMUS Programme also facilitate our students to undertake periods of study in Colleges in the EU and during this academic year over 50 Diploma students are undertaking courses in International Marketing, Tourism, Computer Applications and Computing in France, Germany, Spain and Portugal.

The staff of the School of Business and Humanities are ever conscious of the rapid changes taking place in society and of the demands which these changes place upon them as educators. In response to these changes, staff of the School are continually updating their knowledge and skills thus ensuring that graduates are made familiar with the most up-to-date theory and practice in their subject areas. Equally, staff are also keenly aware of the necessity for a supportive learning student environment where the development of the individual student is closely monitored to ensure that each one receives that individual attention and personal support which in turn should enable them to achieve maximum potential now and in their future careers.
