Speech by the Taoiseach, Mr. Bertie Ahern, T.D.,

At the Official Opening of the New Library and Administration Complex

at the Institute of Technology, Sligo

On 4 February, 2000 at 12.30 p.m.

Acknowledgements

Chairman of the Sligo Institute of Technology, Dr James Devins;

Director, Dr Breandán MacConamhna;

Buildings Officer, Ed Miller;

Ladies and Gentlemen.

Introduction

It is good to be back in Sligo among old friends.  I am delighted to have been invited here to open the Sligo Institute of Technology’s administration complex.

Today is a great day for the Institute.  This ceremony marks another phase in the provision of educational facilities for nearly three and a half thousand students.

I thank all of the staff for their enthusiasm and hard work.  In particular, I would like to salute Breandán MacConamhna who has been the leading light for the Institute for very many years.  I am told, Breandán, that there is a certain smoking zone here in the Institute.  But, I promised I wouldn’t tell anyone where it was!

The Design and Construction Team

I also would like to say a big “thank you” to the design and construction team who made this occasion possible.  I understand that Kilcawleys built this extension.  The specialist glazing which is really very striking and beautifully done was manufactured by Sligo Aluminium.  I am delighted to see so many local businesses working together on the project.  Your contribution is a tribute to the skills of local industry.

I salute the design team including:

·                     Scott Tallon Walker, Architects,

·                     Homan O’Brien, Services Engineers,

·                     Jennings O’Donovan, Structural Engineers and

·                     Mulcahy McDonagh and Partners, Quantity Surveyors

The work done here is a great credit to you all.  This new, beautifully designed building and its bright, happy atmosphere is a statement of self-confidence which bodes very well for the future of this Institute.  

Changing Times for the Institute of Technology, Sligo

Important events like this really show how much progress has been made here since this Institute was founded 30 years ago as a Regional Technical College.  Over the decades there has been a dramatic increase in the demand for places in the RTC sector.  Here in Sligo these changes can be measured in many ways.

·                     I understand from the Institute that 25 years ago the enrolment here was 474.

·                     Today it is in the region of 3,400.

·                     There were between 80 and 90 staff in those days.

·                     Now, there are 350

The wide range of courses on offer today from certificates to masters degrees and doctorates is a far cry from the initial brief of 1970 of national certificates and apprenticeships. 

These figures show that the Institute has the courses and the facilities that the school leavers want.  It proves that students are voting with their feet.

With the bringing on stream of these new facilities particularly the magnificent library, I am confident that the numbers will continue to grow.

The development of the Institute is a great credit to the academic staff and management.  The Institute will continue to play a key role in the economic prosperity of the North West.  

 

A Fitting Place

It is really fitting, it seems to me, to open these fine facilities and modern library so close to Cúl Dreibhne where, I’m told the famous ‘Battle of the Books’ was fought in ancient times between St. Columba and St. Finian.  That ancient Irish literary quarrel resulted in the famous judgement of the High King, “to every cow its calf, every book its copy’.

Official Opening

Thankfully, these days books are not so rare.  A great number of books will pass through this library in the coming years and thousands of students will benefit from them.  I wish everyone who uses these marvellous new facilities the very best of success with their studies and long-term plans.  And so, I take great pleasure in officially opening these new facilities. 

Go n-éirí go geal libh go léir.

Go raibh míle maith agaibh.
