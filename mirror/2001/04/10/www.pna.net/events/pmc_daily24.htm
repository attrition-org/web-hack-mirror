PALESTINE MEDIA CENTER

Daily Information Bulletin

Saturday, March 24, 2001

 

Today’s Highlights:

1) Palestinian man beaten to death by Israeli settlers

2) Red Cross being hindered by Israeli authorities

3) Israeli Army responds brutally to peaceful demonstration

 

1) Palestinian man beaten to death by Israeli settlers

The body of Jabri Ahmad Hamdan, a 44 year-old Palestinian man, from Dora Al-Khalil, was found killed on the night of March 23rd, near the Jewish settlement of Halmeesh north of Ramallah. He was beaten to death by Israeli settlers. Eyewitnesses have stated having seen bloodstained rocks in the area. After examining his body, medical officials stated that Hamdan was probably killed one week ago.

2) Red Cross being hindered by Israeli authorities

Walter Stocker, head of a delegation of the International Committee of the Red Cross, complained that Israeli authorities have been hindering their work in the last few weeks, by subjecting the organization’s trucks coming from Jordan to new regulations. The goods now have to be unloaded at the border and then reloaded onto other trucks (with Israeli license plates), which raises the cost of the aid efforts significantly.

Mr. Stocker also criticized the Israeli siege imposed on Palestinian cities and villages, which causes long delays in getting sick and wounded people to hospitals.

3) Israeli Army responds brutally to peaceful demonstration

Hundreds of Palestinians participated in a peaceful march against the Israeli siege today. The march was organized by a coalition of Palestinian civil society institutions and went from the north of Ar-Ram to the checkpoint seperating Ar-Ram from Jerusalem.

At the Israeli military checkpoint, the protestors were met with tear gas and sound bombs fired by Israeli soldiers. Protestors did not throw stones. More than thirty potestors were injured.
