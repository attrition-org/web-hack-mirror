PALESTINE MEDIA CENTER

Daily Information Bulletin

Thursday, March 15, 2001

 

Today’s Highlights:

1) 17-year old shot in the head and killed by Israeli soldiers

2) Brothers of murder victim denied right to return to work

3) Israeli settlers unleash their dogs on Palestinian school girls

4) Palestinian girl hit by an Israeli settler in his car

 

 

1) 17-year old shot in the head and killed by Israeli soldiers

Murtaja Abdel Latif, a 17-year-old Palestinian boy, was shot in the head by an Israeli Army sniper yesterday during confrontations with Israeli soldiers on the outskirts of Qalqilia in the north of the West Bank. His body was found this morning.

2) Brothers of murder victim denied right to return to work

The four brothers of Mohammed Soof, a Palestinian man killed by Israelis about six weeks ago in the village of Hares near Salfit, have been denied a renewal of their work permits for their jobs at a nearby Jewish settlement. The four have been working at the industrial zone of the settlement of Barqan for many years, one of them has been the shift supervisor for ten years. After the death of their brother a month and a half ago, the Israeli security forces (Shabak), refused to renew their permits.

27-year-old Mohammed Soof was shot on January 8th as he was walking from his house to that of his brother in the early evening. He was not involved in any demonstration or confrontation at the time of his death – he was organizing his little daughter’s birthday party.

That evening, village youth were throwing stones at settlers’ cars driving past the village and Israeli soldiers were shooting at them. Mohammed decided to make sure his teenage brother came to the party, and went out to get him. "Don't lock the door", he told his wife. "I'll be right back."

On the way to his brother’s house, Mohammed was shot in the chest. Israeli soldiers did not allow anyone near to assist him. He bled to death. After a military medic confirmed that he was dead, his family was allowed to take his dead body away.

Mohammed leaves behind his wife and two daughters, one of whom is paralyzed.

3) Israeli settlers unleash their dogs on Palestinian school girls

Students of the Girls’ High School in the village of Al Liban Al Sharki north of Ramallah were chased by a big dog which Jewish settlers released on them while they were on their way home yesterday. One of the girls broke her arm when she fell while running in fear for her life. This is not the first time that settlers have used this method of terrorizing the villagers of Al Liban.

4) Palestinian girl hit by an Israeli settler in his car

Fatme Suleiman, a seven-year old Palestinian girl, was run over by a Jewish settler’s car in the village of Jabba, southeast of Jerusalem. The settler drove away after hitting the little girl, who suffered fractures in many parts of her body.
