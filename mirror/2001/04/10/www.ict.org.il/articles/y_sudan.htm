Ossama bin Ladin's Links to Sudan

ICT Commentary

Quite apart from the clear operational reasons for attacking the "al-Shifa" facility in Khartoum--if as Western intelligence sources maintain, the factory was engaged in chemical weapons production--there are other factors linking bin Ladin to Sudan.

Osama bin Ladin has been involved in a number of commercial operations in Sudan.  His Sudanese projects include  agriculture, construction and infrastucture.  For many years, bin Ladin lived in Khartoum, in a residence guarded by the local security forces, while he was arranging for many of the "Afghan veterans" to move to Sudan.  Bin Ladin is said to be close to Sudanese  leader Omar Albashir, and to Hassan Turabi, the head of the National Islamic Front (NIF) in Sudan.

Bin Ladin worked in partnership with wealthy NIF members on joint infrastructure and development projects all over Sudan.  A good deal of his construction work there has been done for the Sudanese government. There are rumors too, that several companies are still bringing in money for him. Among these is "el-Hijrah for Construction and Development Ltd." This is the company that built the new airport at Port Sudan, as well as a 1200 km-long highway linking Khartoum to Port Sudan.

Another company reputed to be owned by bin Ladin is the "Wadi al-Aqiq" Company, an export-import firm.  He also runs the Taba Investment Company Ltd. and the "el-Shamal Islamic Bank" in Khartoum, a joint effort with the  NIF, in which bin Ladin is said to have  invested $50 million.
