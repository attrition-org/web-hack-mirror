Reactions of Islamic Groups Lauding the Embassy Bombings

The International Islamic Front 
The following is an excerpt from a notice by Al-Muhajiroun (The International Islamic Front) vowing to continue the struggle "against the Enemy of Islam."  The document was published in MSANews under the heading USA YOU MUST PAY. 

"We, Al-Muhajiroun as much as we regret the innocent deaths among the civilians who are nothing to do with the US embassies, declare that we endorse and applaud today's bombing of US embassies in Dar-as-Salaam, Tanzania and Nairobi, Kenya. This is because they are the embassies of the enemy of Islam and the Muslims, and the occupiers of Muslim Land e.g. Saudi Arabia. The Bombs were explosions of pain and grief of the masses that live under the domination of the colonialist oligarchic Americans. Al-Muhajiroun salutes the message that has been sent - American interests are not safe anywhere in the world. The Mujahideen will seek out and obliterate them into rubble as happened today. Let the cowards in the USA army know that they will not always slaughter women and children and go unaccounted." 
Source: MSANews
The Hamas Movement 
Sheikh Ahmed Yassin's responded to the bombing of the two US embassies in East Africa by saying, "America has placed itself by the side of Israel against the Arab world,...I consider what happened self defense by someone who was seeking revenge for the injustice of American policy and the destruction it has brought on their country."  
Source Jerusalem Post, August, 12, 1998
The Taliban, Afghanistan 
The Taliban on Saturday, one day after the bombings, insisted that Osama bin Ladin had nothing to do with the two attacks.  The exiled Saudi millionaire is viewed by many as the prime suspect in orchestrating the bombings.  Security analysts claim that he has both the capability and the motivation to have arranged the highly sophisticated attacks.  However, the Taliban claims that bin Ladin "does not have the capability or permission to conduct such deeds.  Any suggestion to the contrary is false propaganda of the enemies of Osama and Islamic Emirate of Afghanistan".  The Taliban spokesman went on to say that "Osama is a guest of the Islamic Emirate. We cannot extradite him to anybody." He revealed that the Taliban had recently signed an intelligence agreement with the Saudi intelligence, and that the Taliban had promised the Saudis that bin Ladin would not be allowed to carry out subversive activities against them from Taliban-controlled territory.  However, he added, no such assurances  had been given the Americans. 
Source: Agence France Press, August 8, 1998

Reactions of Islamic Nations & Groups Condemning the Embassy Bombings
 
Iran 
The Official Iranian News Agency IRNA published a statement on August 8 condemning the bombings.  "The Islamic Republic of Iran condemns the bomb explosions at U.S. Embassies in Nairobi, Kenya, and Dar es Salaam, Tanzania," said Foreign ministry Spokesman Mahmud Mohammadi. The statement added that "the Islamic Republic of Iran condemns any acts which may result in the death of innocent people," and that Mohammadi hoped that "through international cooperation and an all-out campaign against terrorism the sinister phenomena of terrorism will be uprooted." 

Iran, ironically, immediately took up the cudgels against bin Ladin by attempting to blame Bin Ladin as being responsible for the Dhahran bombing and the Taliban government as the sponsor of the attacks in Africa. It should be remembered that the Taliban recently took hostage 11 Iranian diplomats, and that Iran has been considered as a potential culprit in the Dhahran bombing. The following quotation appeared in the official Iranian News Agency, which based itself on "foreign sources": "Usama bin-Ladin, the real criminal of the recent explosion of American embassies in Kenya and tanzania is Taleban's main financial supporter of terrorist training." Bin Ladin, according to the source quoted by IRNA was also accused of "planning a bomb explosion on the American base in Dhahran (Saudi Arabia). . ." The source added that bin Ladin "was also responsible for terrorist activities and financial supporter (sic) against the Algerian Government and some of the para military group in Saudi Arabia". 
Source: IRNA, August 8, 1998
The Sudanese Ummah Party 
The Sudanese opposition Ummah Party denounced the US embassy bombings.  The Sudanese opposition was quoted as saying that the two "regrettable incidents" showed that the "international network" of violence and extremism is "still active in implementing their global agenda, benefiting from the facilities and services offered by some regional states and groups."  The Ummah Party hinted that the Sudanese Government could have been involved in the attacks, "given that since the 1990s the Sudanese regime has offered itself as a gateway for the Islamic movements to Africa and a springboard for the African continent under the guise of Islamic call and aid."  The Ummah pointed out that there were factors "pointing to the Sudanese regime's hidden role and the possibility of its involvement" in the terrorist attacks.  Such factors included  Sudan's connections to "terrorist networks, it propagation of violent and extremist thought, and the presence of fundamentalist security centers within the regime." 
Source: MENA, August 9, 1998
The Moslem Brotherhood in Cairo 
The Moslem Brotherhood group in Cairo condemned the bombings, saying that "they do not accept haphazard assassinations like what took place in the two embassies."  They claimed that every such attack was used as an excuse to "lay blame on Arab nationals, ethnic or Islamic groups before any evidence is established,"  such as happened in the case of the Oklahoma City bombing. ArabicNews.com quoted an official Moslem Brotherhood source as saying, "It is too bad that each time an aggression takes place no one is accused but the Islamic organizations." 
Source: ArabicNews.com, August 10, 1998
