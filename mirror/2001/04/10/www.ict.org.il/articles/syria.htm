Syrian-Sponsored Terrorism

PROVISIONAL SUMMARY OF THE MOST SERIOUS TERROR ACTS CARRIED OUT ABROAD IN WHICH SYRIA WAS EITHER DIRECTLY INVOLVED OR INVOLVED BY MEANS OF “TERROR CONTRACTORS”

This summary includes terror acts outside Syria’s borders, with the exception of operations against Israel which were carried out via Israel’s borders with the Arab states or inside Israel and the territories. This summary is based on the Army Spokesman’s publication  (Information Branch - March 1986) “The Terror Organizations, Eighteen Years of Terror Abroad” (June 1968 - March 1986): We also referred to the chronology of the most serious terror incidents,  carried out by the Palestinian organizations abroad, mentioned in Ariel Merari and Shlomi Elad’s book, The International Dimension of Palestinian Terrorism  pp.166-179.

Attacks Against Israeli and Jewish Targets

  • 21.2.70 Switzerland:  A Swissair aircraft which took off from Zurich to Lod, blew up en route a short time after take-off. All 38 passengers and 9 crew members were killed.  The attack was carried out by the “Popular Front - General Headquarters” (i.e. Jibril’s organization). The terrorists succeeded in escaping to the Middle East. The dead included: 13 from Israel; 10 from West Germany; 5 from the U.S.A; 2 from Switzerland; 2 from Kenya; 2 from Mexico; 1 from Thailand; 1 from Great Britain, 1 from Belgium; 1 from Senegal and 9 Swiss crew members.
  • 24.2.70 West Germany: Two packages of explosives were sent by Jibril’s organization from Frankfurt to addresses in Israel.

    •  
  • 28.7.71  Italy:  An attempt by Jibril’s organization to blow up  an El Al aircraft  en  route from Rome to Lod, failed. The attempt was carried out by a terrorist  who exploited an innocent Dutch girl in order to load a suitcase with explo- sives onto the aircraft.
  • 1.9.71  Great Britain. An attempt by a terrorist belonging to Jibril’s Front, to blow up  an El Al plane en route from London to Lod, failed.  He took advantage of  an  innocent Peruvian girl to load a suitcase of explosives onto the aircraft.
  • 28.12.71 Yugoslavia and Austria.  15 booby-trapped envelopes were dispatched by  Jibril’s organization from Yugoslavia and Austria to Israeli businessmen. An  Israeli policeman from the bomb disposal unit  was injured whilst  dismantling  one of the envelopes.
  • 29.4.72 West Germany: Packages with explosives were sent by Jibril’s organization  to the Israeli pavilion at the Hanover Fair in West Germany.
  • 16.8.72 Italy: An attempt by Jibril’s organization to blow up an El Al aircraft  en  route  from Rome to Lod, failed. The attempt was carried out by exploiting  two innocent English girls in order to load a booby-trapped gramophone into  the cargo hold. The two English girls were released by the Italian authorities.  The two terrorists were released on bail in February 1973 and disappeared in  Italy.
  • 7.12.72 Singapore: Jibril’s organization dispatched envelopes with explosives from  Singapore to public institutions  in Israel.
  • 30.8.73 Beirut: Two people from the “Sa’iqa” organization, were arrested at the  Beirut airport with explosives in their possession. Their intention was to retreat  to Bratislava in Czechoslovakia, in order to reach the Austrian-Czech border  and attack the train carrying Jews from the Soviet Union.  The terrorists were  released after a few days.
  • 28.9.73 Austria: Terrorists from the Sa’iqa organization took control of a train of  immigrants from the Soviet Union,  at the Austrian border. The terrorists took three Jewish hostages. In return for the  hostages’ release, the Austrians declared that they would close the transit camp for new immigrants. The Sa’iqa terrorists were given a plane to Libya.  The operation was carried out under the code name: “The Revolution’s Eagles”. Sa’iqa’s Commander at that time, Zuhir  Machsan, declared in 1975, that the operation was intended as a diversion from the “Yom Kippur War”.
  • 5.9.75 Holland. Four terrorists from Sa’iqa, who held Syrian passports, were arrested in Amsterdam. They intended taking hostage 12  train passengers in order to pressure Holland to stop assisting the Jewish immigrants. The terrorists  received a sentence of one year. It is possible that the operation was carried out against the background of the Interim Agreement between Israel and Egypt (1st September) which Syria was against.
  • 15.10.78 West Germany. Two packages  of explosives were found near two Jewish sites in West Berlin: The Jewish Community Center and a Jewish shop. The organization “The Eagles of the Revolution” claimed responsibility for  the attack.
  • 26.3.79 Turkey.  An explosive charge was thrown into the back yard of the Israel Consulate in Ankara. The attack was carried out by the Eagles of the Revolution (Sa’iqa) . The background - the signing of the Agreement with Egypt  and as a result  another wave of terror attacks was carried out.
  • 27.3.79 France. A bomb exploded in a Jewish restaurant in Paris. 26 people were injured. The attack was carried out by Sa’iqa.
  • 27.3.79 France. An explosion occurred in a Jewish cosmetics shop in Paris. The attack was carried out by Sa’iqa.
  • 1.4.79 Turkey.  An attempted attack in the El Al offices in  Istanbul.  Possibly by Sa’iqa.
  • 5.4.79 Cyprus. A bomb exploded in the Israel Consulate building in Nicosia. The attack was carried out by the Eagles of the Revolution. (Sa’iqa).
  • 7.4.79 France. The French government dismantled a time bomb which was hidden in a bag on a motor cycle parked outside a cinema in Paris where the Jewish Cultural Week was being held.  Sa’iqa. took responsibility for the attack.
  • 8.4.79 Turkey.  An explosive charge went off at the Israeli Consulate in Ankara. There were no casualties. It is possible that the attack was carried out by (Sa’iqa).
  • 22.4.79 Austria.  Explosions occurred at the Jordanian community’s building in Vienna and near the Central Synagogue in Vienna. There were no casualties. The attack was carried out by (Sa’iqa). under the name of  The Eagles of the Revolution.
  • 20.6.80 Denmark/Sweden:  A group of terrorists including 4 Swedish citizens and 2 Palestinians were arrested  at the Danish-Swedish border. Hand grenades and machine guns were found in their possession. They had been collecting information on El-Al personnel in Copenhagen in preparation of an attack. The group belonged to the Jibril organization.
  • 1.5.81 Austria: Heinz Nittel, Chairman of the Israel-Austria Friendship Association,  was murdered.  The murder was carried out by Abu Nidal’s organization, apparently  encouraged by the Syrians.
  • 29.8.81 Austria. Abu Nidal’s organization attacked a synagogue in Austria apparently at the instigation of the Syrians. Two Jews were killed and 19 people were wounded including 2 Jewish children and 2 Austrian policemen.
  • 30.9.85 Holland.  An explosive charge went off  near the El Al offices in Amsterdam.  Abu Nidal’s organization took credit for the attack.
  • 27.12.85 Italy.  Four terrorists from Abu Nidal’s organization slaughtered  13 people and wounded 74 during an attack on El Al’s offices at Leonardo di Vinci Airport in Rome. Amongst the dead were 5 American citizens, 4 Greeks, 1 Italian, 1 Algerian, 2 Mexicans. Amongst the  wounded were 7 Israeli citizens, 1 American boy aged 6, 4 Jordanian children, 1 Jordanian citizen, 1 American and many Italians. 2 terrorists were killed and a fourth was caught. During his interrogation, it became apparent that the terrorists had come from Damascus and that that the Head of Security of their airforce, Machmad Huli, gave ligistical assistance to the perpetrators (training in Lebanon under the supervision of Syrian army personnel). The Syrian regime knew in advance all the details of the planned attacks and approved them.
  • 27.12.85 Vienna. At the same time as the attack in Rome,  3 terrorists attacked the El- Al desk at the Vienna airport before the El Al aircraft took off  from Vienna to Lod. 3 people were killed: 1 Israeli  and  2 passers-by. 45 people were wounded (2 Israeli citizens including a baby of 9 months, 23 Austrians, 1 Italian, 3 Nigerians, 2 Americans, 1 Chinese, 1 Hungarian). Abu Nidal’s organization took credit for the attack. 1 terrorist was killed and 2 terrorists were wounded and subsequently caught.
  • 17.4.86 London. An attempt to blow up an El Al plane in the air by loading a suitcase of explosives  with the help of an innocent girl, was foiled.  The girl was sent by Niz’ar Handawi who was sent directly by the Head of the Syrian Airforce security, headed by Machmad Huli.
  • 26.6.86 Madrid:  An attempt failed in Madrid to load a suitcase of explosives with the help of an innocent courier, on to an El Al plane and blow it up in the air . The man who sent the girl is a terrorist belonging to Abu Mussa’s organization. He holds a Syrian passport and receives his instructions directly from the Syrian intelligence. Mahmad Juli’s name was connected to the attack.
 Attacks Against Western Targets
  • 21.2.70 West Germany - An explosion occurred in the cargo hold of an Austrian Airline during a flight from Frankfurt to Vienna.  The aircraft was damaged but the pilot managed to land safely. The attack was perpetrated by Jibril’s organization. The terrorists succeeded in escaping to the Middle East.
  • 29.3.79 Turkey - An explosion occurred  at the American Consulate in Istanbul. It was carried out by the “Eagles of the Revolution” (Sa’iqa). The Consul’s car was also attacked. There were no injuries.
  • 2.4.79 Lebanon - R.P.G. rockets were fired at the American Embassy in Beirut. The attack was perpetrated by the “Eagles of the Revolution (Sa’iqa).
  • 6.6.80 Lebanon -  There was an attempt to kill the Reuter correspondent in Beirut, who regularly criticized the Syrian regime.
  • August 81 Lebanon -  Three French banks were blown up in Beirut. The attacks were attributed to the Syrians.
  • 4.9.81 Lebanon - The murder of the French Ambassador, Louis Delmar, in Beirut. In the French press,  the blame was placed on Syria’s shoulders and it is probable that they indeed were behind this attack.
  • 8.12.81 -  Lebanon - A booby-trapped vehicle exploded outside the home of the French ambassador in Beirut. The attack was attributed to the Syrians.
  • 15.3.82 - Lebanon - An explosion at the French Cultural Center in West Beirut.  The attack was attributed to the Syrians.
  • 29.3.82 -  West Germany - Two members of Sa’iqa were arrested in West Berlin on suspicion that they intended to carry out attacks.
  • 29.3.86 - West Germany - An explosion in the building of the Arab-West German League in West Berlin.  One of the men arrested is the brother of Nazzar Handawi who was involved in the attempted attack on the El Al plane in London.  From their interrogation it was learned that the Syrians were directly involved in the planning of the attack and its execution.
  • 5.4.86 - West Germany - An explosion at the La Belle Discotheque in West Berlin where two people were killed (one of them an American soldier) and more than 150 people were injured.  In retaliation, the Americans bombed Libya. From the attack on the Arab-West German League building, Syria’s involvement becomes clear:  Handawi’s brother had in his possession a plan layout of the La Belle discotheque.  The explosive material used in both instances was identical.
  Attacks Against Arab Targets
  • 4.10.76 - Iraq -  A Syrian intelligence agent who was caught at the Iraqi border, admitted that he had been sent to carry out attacks against government institutions and to kill Syrian emigrants in Iraq.
  • 31.12.77 - London -  A vehicle owned by the Syrian embassy exploded in the center of London. Apparently, the men in the vehicle, who were employed by the Syrian embassy,  had planned to attack the Egyptian airline offices situated near the site of the explosion. The background - Saadat’s visit to Jerusalem.
  • 2.1.78 - West Germany - Bonn: An attempt was made to blow up the Egyptian Embassy in Bonn. The attempted explosion is attributed to Sa’iqa. The background - Saadat’s visit to Jerusalem.
  • 5.4.79 - Cyprus - An explosion occurred in a tourist office in Cyprus which represents the Egyptian airline. The Eagles of the Revolution (code name for Sa’iqa) was responsible for this attack. The background - Signing of the Peace Treaty between Egypt and Israel on 26 March 1979.
  • 16.4.79 Egypt - Two Syrians were arrested in Egypt. During their interrogation, it became evident that they intended to carry out attacks on the Egyptian Foreign Ministry building, the Sheraton Hotel, as well as other buildings. The background- Signing of the Peace Treaty between Egypt and Israel.
  • 19.4.79 Egypt - A booby-trapped envelope exploded in a Cairo post office. One person was killed and 3 were wounded.  The Eagles of the Revolution (code name for Sa’iqa) were responsible for the attack. The background - Signing of the Peace Treaty between Israel and Egypt.
  • 24.4.79 - Egypt - Two men from Sa’iqa, who intended perpetrating attacks in Egyptian towns, were arrested. The background - Signing of the Egyptian-Israeli Peace Treaty.
  • 13-15.5.79 Turkey - Four terrorists from The Eagles of the Revolution (code name for Sa’iqa) took control of the Egyptian Embassy in Ankara and took 20 hostages. Two Turkish policemen  and an employee of the Egyptian Embassy were killed. Two Egyptian security personnel were wounded. The terrorists were sentenced to death in December 1980 but were released after negotiations. The background: Signing of the Israeli-Egyptian Peace Treaty.
  •  31.1.81 Jordan - A squad of terrorists were sent to murder the Jordanian Prime Minister, Mudar Badran. They were captured before they carried out their mission. According to the Jordanian television, the order came from Rafat Assad, the Syrian President’s brother. The commander of the squad, Adnan Barachat, (a Syrian intelligence officer) appeared on Jordanian TV and gave a detailed description of the incident and said he was sent by the Syrians to carry out the attack.
  • 6.2.81 - Lebanon - About 20 men from The Eagles of the Revolution (code name for Sa’iqa) attacked the home of Hisham Machsan, the Jordanian representative in Beirut..  The Jordanian diplomat was kidnapped and dragged away in a car. Three men were killed (two Jordanians and one Lebanese) and two were injured. The conditions for his release -  extradition to Damascus of all the Syrian pilots who deserted to Iraq and Jordan. In April 1981 the kidnapped man was returned to Jordan.
  • March 81 - Jordan - 3 men from Sa’iqa were arrested in northern Jordan. They had explosives and weapons in their possession which they intended to use to attack Jordanian targets.
  • Oct. 81 - Ashaf - Two attempts were made on Arafat’s life. The perpetrators’ identity is unclear. The planning of the attempted attacks is attributed to Rifat Assad, the brother of the Syrian President.
  • 19.12.81 - France (Paris) : An attempt to blow up the offices of the (pro-Iraqi) Lebanese weekly “Alutan ElArabi” with an explosive charge.
  • 11.1.82 Jordan - An attack on a supermarket in Aman which was perpetrated by Syrian embassy personnel in Aman. TheThird Secretary at the Embassy was deported as a result.
  • 4.2.82 Turkey:  The Turkish security authorities stated that the terrorists who had blown up the Iraqi oil pipeline in ’79 and ’80 which goes through Turkish territory, were sent by Syria.
  • 22.4.82 France. A vehicle exploded in the center of Paris on Marbeuy Street, next  to the offices of the Pro-Iraqi Lebanese weekly “Alutin Elarabi”. A pregnant woman was killed and over 60 passers-by were injured. Consequently, two Syrian intelligence people from the Syrian Embassy in Paris, who were known to be Rafah Assad’s men, were deported. The two were Asst. Military Attache - Lt. Col. Hassin Ali and the Cultural Attache, Mishal Kassuha, who were working under diplomatic cover. The two were also involved in attacks on Syrian opposition members and in a previous attempt to attack Alutin Elarabi).
  • 10.4.83 Portugal. In the holiday village of Al Buferia, Azzam Sitawi, a member of Ashaf, considered a moderate who maintained that a dialogue with Israel was necessary,  was murdered. The murder was carried out by a gunman belonging to Abu Nidal’s organization, apparently according to direct instructions from Syria. The announcement of the attack was made public in Damascus and the murderers left on their mission from Damascus.
  • 13.10.83. Jordan. Two hand grenades were thrown at the police headquarters in Jordan. A Palestinian from Sa’iqa was arrested and confessed.  The Jordanian authorities suspected that Abu Nidal’s organization was also involved in the attack.
  • 1984-1985 Jordan.  Between the years  ‘84-‘85, a Jordanian opposition organization called “ The People’s Jordanian Revolutionary Party”, which received assistance from Syria and was connected to Palestinian and Pro-Syrian organizations, carried out about five attacks in Jordanian territory against Jordanian institutions and Western targets.
In the years 1983-1985, a series of terror attacks were perpetrated against Jordanian diplomats,  institutions and installations abroad. These stopped towards the end of 1985 when Jordan drew nearer to Syria. The attacks were carried out by Abu Nidal’s organization.  Circumstantial evidence points to the supportive hand of Syria in all these attacks.
The following are detailed examples of these attacks:
 
  • 25.10.83 The Jordanian Ambassador in India was shot and injured.
    • 26.10.83 The Jordanian Ambassador in Rome was shot and injured.
    • 7.11.83 Two bodyguards from the Jordanian embassy in Athens were shot. One was killed and the other was injured. The organization “The Revolutionary Brigades”, an alias for Abu Nidal’s organization, admitted responsibility for the attack.
    • 29.12.83 Two Jordanian embassy employees in Spain were shot. One was killed. “The Revolutionary Brigades”, an alias for Abu Nidal’s organization, admitted responsibility for the attack.
    • 24.3.84 An explosive charge was detonated in the parking lot of the “Intercontinental Hotel”  in Amman.  Two additional charges,  which were aimed at British targets in the City, were dismantled.
    • 3.8.84 An attack on and American Embassy installation in Aman.
    • 4.12.84 A Jordanian diplomat in Bucharest (Rumania)  was shot and killed. Two others were injured.
    • 9-13.3.85 A bomb was discovered on a Jordanian aircraft flying from Pakistan to Jordan, whilst  it was parked on  the airport tarmac. The Jordanian aircraft was damaged. Shots were also fired from a moving vehicle at the home of the local Jordanian Ambassador.
    • 21.3.85 Three offices of the Jordanian Airline El-Alia were attacked simultaneously  in Athens, Rome and Ankara.
    • 3.4.85 An attempt to fire a “Low” missile (or “bazooka” according to another version) at the Jordanian Embassy in Rome.
    • 4.4.85 Two bazookas were fired at a Jordanian aircraft about to take off  from Athens airport.
    • 1.7.85 A bomb was detonated in  Madrid in a building where T.W.A. and British Airways offices were situated. Shots were fired at the offices of the adjacent Jordanian Airline “El-Alia. The attacks were carried out by Abu Nidal.
    • 24.7.85 A Jordanian diplomat was shot and killed in Ankara. Abu Nidal, under the alias of “Black September”, admitted responsibility for the attack. The Turkish prosecutor warned that the Syrian Second Secretary in Turkey would be accused of being involved in the attack. In Nov. 86,  the Syrian diplomat was declared an undesirable alien in Turkey and he consequently left the country.
    • 18.9.85 Mishel Nimri,  a publisher of the Jordanian-Palestinian anti-Syrian paper “Al Nashra”, was murdered in Athens
