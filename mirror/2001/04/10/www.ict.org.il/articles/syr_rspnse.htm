24 August, 1998
The American Strikes in Afghanistan and Sudan
Syria and Lebanon Respond

Dr. Reuven Erlich
ICT Research Fellow

The Syrian Response
The Syrians have so far refrained from voicing an official reaction to the American offensive, however the Syrian state-controlled media quoted an “official source” as condemning the strikes. The same source, quoted by “Radio Damascus” (21 August) declared that “It is impossible to justify such actions because they violate the sovereignty of nations and the sanctity of their land, as well as gravely endangering the lives and property of innocent people.” The same “official source” argued that Syria condemns all forms of terrorism, including state terrorism, and that the use of force if it is not in the framework of the UN Security Council “sets a dangerous precedent that could endanger the basis of international law and the UN charter. It invites acts of indiscriminate revenge without offering any solution to the source of terrorism.”

This reaction is characteristic of the rejectionist and hypocritical tactics of Syria when dealing with terrorism. On the one hand she pays lip service to the United States and the Western countries by condemning terrorism in any form, and protesting the murder of innocent people in Nairobi and in Dar es-Sala’am. At the same time, Syria denies the legitimacy of the American response, arguing that such action violates international law and the sovereignty of the countries in whose territory terrorists have taken refuge. (This no doubt stems from the awareness of the connotations of such a response toward Syria itself which harbors and supports a whole list of terrorist organizations--Palestinian, Lebanese and others.)

Ahmed Jibril’s organization, a Palestinian terror group operating under Syrian patronage, allowed itself to take a sharper stand. The organization issued a statement sharply condemning the American strikes and accusing the United States of “state terrorism” and “hatred of everything Arab or Islamic.” American is accused of the “Racist Zionist conquest of Palestine,” the blockade against women, children and old people in Iraq and Libya, and of supporting the rebels in southern Sudan.

The Lebanese Reaction
A number of different sources and groups in Lebanon--inspired by Syria--have taken an anti-American propaganda line. The most prominent was Hizballah. In their announcement they sharply condemn the American offensive, and interject an implied threat against the U.S. The Lebanese government was somewhat more cautious, but refrained from supporting the U.S. attacks on terrorist targets in Afghanistan and in Sudan.

Sheikh Naim Kassem of Hizballah denounced the dangerous “American aggression” and called on the international community to combat it. He also argued that this “American aggression” constitutes interference in the affairs of sovereign nations. Omar Musawi, Hizballah’s parliamentary representatives in the Lebanese government and one of the group’s spokesmen declared (Reuters in Beirut 21 August) that the American strikes are liable to bring about more attacks on U.S. targets around. “The American logic justifying the attacks,” he said, “means that Arab fighters can rightfully attack American factories that produce weapons for the use of Israel in killing Arabs.” Sheikh Mohammed Hussein Fadlallah, spiritual leader of Hizballah and of Lebanon’s Shi’ites, warned that American violence would expose the United States to a wave of counter actions of increasing violence on the part of the terrorist groups targeted by the U.S.

Lebanese Foreign Minister Fares Buez declared (August 21, 1998) that there is a general impression that the evidence connecting the American strikes with the bombings in Africa was insufficient.  A “high-ranking Lebanese official” quoted by the French News Agency Beirut as (August 21) even argued that the American attacks in Afghanistan and Sudan were merely intended to distract public opinion from the Lewinsky affair. Moreover, it appears that the American strikes have the Lebanese government--which supports and endorses terrorist activity from Lebanese soil--worried about possible Israeli offensives in the same vein as the American one. Hence “Radio Lebanon” reported (August 23) that the Lebanese Foreign Minister had contacted the United States and France to warn that Israel might use the American actions as an excuse to attack Lebanon. He asked the two countries to intervene in order to prevent a further deterioration in the situation.
