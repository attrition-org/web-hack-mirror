London-Based Islamic Group Issues Fatwa against Israel

As violence flared between Yasser Arafat's Fatah activists and Israeli troops, the London based al-Muhajiroun group issued a fatwa (religious edict) calling for Jihad against Israel. The Fatwa was published on the group's website and sent out by email.

The fatwa calls on Muslims all over the world to attack Israeli interests. "this Fatwa is a call to fight against Israeli forces, their government, Israeli Embassies, military airports and jets etc...as they are legitimate targets for Muslims wherever they may be," reads the fatwa, adding "The Israeli cancer in Palestine must be uprooted. Islam obliges us to liberate all Muslim land from occupiers whoever they may be whether American, Jewish Israeli or other forces."

"We must act now and rise against the presence of Israelis in Muslim countries and close down their illegitimate embassies and companies." The document concludes, "The Jihad against the Jews occupying Palestine will continue until they withdraw completely from the whole of Palestine."

Al-Muhajiroun: background

Al-Muhajiroun seeks to establish a "world Islamic state." The group's leader, Sheikh Omar Bakri Muhammad, is a judge of the Shari'ah Court of the UK. He was the founding leader of Hizb-ut-Tahrir, the Islamic Liberation Party in Britain before splitting from the Party over differences with it's Middle Eastern-based leadership.

Muhammad, who sought asylum in Britain after leaving his native Syria advocates setting up an Islamic state in the U.K. "One day the black flag of Islam will be flying over Downing Street," says another al-Muhajiroun organizer, Anjem Choudray.

In 1998 al-Muhajiroun led protests in London against the British role in compelling Iraq to allow inspection of weapons sites. It has called on Muslims to use "all means" to oppose the Britain and its American allies. In December, 1999 the group issued a fatwa bearing the authority of  the Shari'ah Court of the UK, following a ruling by the Beirut-based International Islamic Front, calling for the assassination of Russian President Yeltsin. The group said the Russian embassy in London and similar establishments were also "legitimate targets."

Links to Osama bin Ladin
But al-Muhajiroun is best known as the only Western-based Islamic group that came out in open support of the bombings of the American Embassies in East Africa in August 1998. Muhammad claims to act as a spokesman of Osama bin Laden. Throughout 1998 the al-Muhajiroun website carried bin Laden's communiqués.

After the attacks against the American Embassies, the group published an endorsement of the bombings, saying "Al-Muhajiroun salutes the message that has been sent - American interests are not safe anywhere in the world."
