The objective of the Strategic Counter-Terrorism course is to provide a deeper understanding of the strategy and psychology of terrorist organizations, and to foster a greater awareness of the issues related to international terrorism. This enhanced understanding of terrorist objectives and methods is intended to promote more informed decision making in real life scenarios. Course participants will gain new analytical tools for coping with terrorism and assessing their own situational concerns. 
Among the topics addressed in the course are the following:
  • Definitions: terrorist or freedom fighter?
  • Terrorist strategies
  • Objectives and modus operandi of terrorist organizations
  • Policies for combating terrorism: military, political, legal and economic aspects
  • Analysis of various forms of terrorism: hostage-taking attacks, suicide bombing and the threat of non-conventional terrorism
  • The media-terrorism connection
  • Public opinion and dilemmas facing decision makers
The course includes thirteen two-hour lectures, spread over one week. An abridged course of twenty hours is also available.  The lectures will be delivered by ICT staff—leaders in their respective fields of expertise, who have conducted extensive academic and applied research in various disciplines.

Course Program

1 Defining Terrorism - The need for a technical definition of terms such as “terrorism,” “terrorist,” and “freedom fighter.” A proposal for a common and consistent definition of terrorism acceptable to the international community, as a basis for international cooperation
2 Terrorist Strategy - The evolution of terrorism from the 19th century to present. The connection between terrorism, the media and public opinion.Terror threats against Western democracies. Terrorism as a psychological tool
3 World Islamic Jihad - A shift from ideological, secular terrorism to religious fundamentalist terrorism. A look at the broad spectrum of modern terrorism worldwide, from secular to Sunni and Shiite terrorism
4 Countries that Support Terrorism - Analysis of the general characteristics of this phenomenon and its influence on the global arena. In-depth case studies: Iran, Libya, Syria, Iraq and Sudan
5 Hostage-Taking - Worldwide case study characterizing extortionary terrorism
6 Suicide Terror Attacks - A look at suicide terrorism as practised in India, Israel, Turkey and elsewhere. Analysis of the psychological profile of a suicide bomber. Tactics to counter the phenomenon
7 Non-Conventional Terrorism - Characteristics, threats and response
8 Terrorist Activity Worldwide - Asia, Latin America, Africa and others
9 Israel’s Strategy in Combating Terrorism - A case study
10 How Western Countries Have Coped with International Terrorism - Historical development
11 Role of Intelligence in Counter-Terrorism
12 Simulation Workshops - Hostage-taking tactics and psychological warfare
13 Panel Discussion - Countering terror threats in a democracy
