Banquet Speech by the Prime Minister in Honour of the 
Greek Prime Minister
H.E. Mr. Constantinos Simitis
February 4-9, 2001-New Delhi

Your Excellency Mr., Prime Minister, 
Ladies and Gentlemen: 

It is a privilege for us to welcome you and the distinguished members of your delegation to India.

You are here at a time when we are coping with the aftermath of one of the worst natural disasters that this country has seen. I would like to express the sincere gratitude of my Government to the voluntary and generous assistance, which your Government has extended towards our relief efforts.

Mr. Prime Minister, the civilizational linkages between our two countries spanned the last millennium and built important bridges between the east and the west. The intertwining of our ancient cultures is most graphically reflected in the Gandhara School of Art. 

Our relationship today as modem dynamic societies is based on this rich cultural heritage. We are both committed to the principles of democracy. To Greece goes the credit of having given the word "democracy" to the English language. 

We are proud of our cordial and problem free bilateral relations with Greece. We hope these relations will catalyze a growth in our economic cooperation. The business delegation accompanying you would have seen the opportunities for trade and investment offered by India's economic liberalization and industrial expansion. Indian companies also see a role for themselves in contributing to Greek projects in Information Technology and other areas.

Excellency, as we enter this new millennium, communications technology and economic intercourse have shrunk the globe. But the blurring of national frontiers has also been exploited by transnational networks of terrorism, drug trafficking, gun running and money laundering. These networks have become the tools of religious extremism, which threatens democratic and pluralistic societies. As heirs to a tradition of democracy freedom and tolerance, India and Greece must join hands to cooperate against this menace. We can cooperate in international fora on a number of other issues on which we have similar views. We share some ideas on making the United Nations and its Security Council more representative of current realities.
I 
Excellency, these are areas which provide the contemporary dimension to our ancient relations. I am sure your visit will provide a strong impetus in this direction.

Ladies and Gentlemen, may I now request you to join me in a toast:

  • to the health of Prime Minister Simitis 
  • to the progress and prosperity of the friendly people of the Hellenic Republic 
  • to the further strengthening of the cordial ties between our two countries 
Thank you.
