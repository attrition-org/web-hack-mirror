Statement By Dr. Najma Heptulla
President Inter-Parliamentary Union
at the reception hosted in honour of H. E. Li Peng, Chairman, Standing committee of NPC Republic of China

January 12,  2001- New Delhi


Your Excellency Mr. Li Peng,
Madam Zhu Lin
Members of the delegation 

I warmly welcome you to India. I am pleased that this is the third opportunity of our meeting in the past six months. I remember the warmth with which you received me during my visit to China in July last year. We had very useful exchange of views. Your visit to India shows the importance you attach to the relations with my country. I hope that during this visit we can identify new areas of cooperation between our two countries. On the important issues before the IPU, we have total agreement. 

You have made important contribution to the development of our relations. Your last visit to India in December 1991 was part of the resumption of our high level political exchanges initiated by late Prime Minister Rajiv Gandhi in December 1988. Our Government attaches importance to your visit. Our President paid a successful visit to China in May last year, and our Prime Minister will be meeting you. We desire to have friendly and good neighborly relations based on panchsheel and mutual sensitivity to each other's concerns. We also believe that all outstanding problems can and should be resolved through peaceful dialogue. 

You are aware that among the problems that we have been discussing at the IPU are the new challenges that we face as Governments. Many such challenges come from non-state actors, and we need to develop new means of global cooperation to handle these new issues. This is particularly important for countries like India and China with their multi-ethnic and multi-religious societies. The scourge of religious fundamentalism, terrorism and drug trafficking is threatening to disrupt the stability of our region. We have been a long-suffering victim of cross border and state sponsored terrorism. But this is not a challenge faced by India alone. 

I believe that China is also beginning to face this problem. Many of our Central Asian neighbours are also suffering from externally sponsored terrorist activity. Excellency, in your address at the Conference of the presiding Officers of national parliament at New York, you had expressed your concern on growing menace of terrorism. We must cooperate to tackle this menace before it overcomes the region. The phenomenon of terrorism, which has now taken on a religious dimension, transcends national and international borders. Countries in the region must understand that any form of support or inaction is neither in their interest nor in the interest of regional peace and security. 

Excellency, Political and economic cooperation go side by side. IPU is convening a joint Conference with WTO and European Parliament in June in Geneva to give a parliamentary dimension to trade. I am happy to announce that China would be a member of the Steering Committee to this Conference. Later in April, I proposed to take a delegation of parliamentarians with CII, the apex Industry association of the India to your country to study the path of development, you have adopted. This would foster a greater cooperation between the two great countries of the region. 

I hope that Your Excellency has a pleasant and successful visit. We also welcome Madame ZHU LIN. I hope that she is being well looked after.

I am convinced that this visit will deepen our mutual understanding and trust, in the days to come.
