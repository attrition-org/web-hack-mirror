The External Affairs Minister, Shri Jaswant Singh 
made following statement at 1945 hours today

The Government of  India is monitoring the situation in northern Sri Lanka closely. The Government of Sri Lanka has been in touch with the Government of India on the evolving situation.

2.    The Government of India will be guided in its response to developments by its continued commitment to a peaceful resolution of the conflict within the framework of a united Sri Lanka, where all communities can realise their aspirations. 

3.    India will work to mitigate the hardship inflicted upon civilians by the conflict, taking such humanitarian measures as become necessary, in consultation with Sri Lanka. India is already host to over 100,000 Sri Lankan refugees. 

4.   It is India's hope that the peace sought by all Sri Lankans will soon return to a country that is a close and friendly neighbour. India is ready to contribute to making that hope a reality.

New Delhi

Press release issued by the Army headquarters on the 
SITUATION IN SIERRA LEONE 
  
 According to reports received from Force Headquarters United Nations Mission in Sierra Leone (UNAMSIL), it is learnt that the Revolutionary United Front (RUF) has had a stand off with the UN peace keeping mission in Sierra Leone and has
detained 45 personnel of different nationalities operating as part of the UN mission.  Besides 11 Indian peacekeepers to include 7 officers and 4 Other Ranks, there are personnel of other contingents and military observers of Kenya, UK, Indonesia and the crew of a Russian helicopter. 

2. The Force Commander, Maj. Gen. V.K. Jetley, UYSM, is in the process of negotiating with the RUF leader, Foday Sankoh.  The UN Security Council met on 02 May and has also condemned the action of RUF.  The Security Council has
urged RUF to free the detainees immediately and abstain from violence. 

3. The President of the Security Council has expressed appreciation for the Force Commander and commended UNAMSIL troops for their courage and resolve in a press statement issued by him.  The Indian Government is in close touch with the UN
which is taking all possible action for early release of the detainees. 
  

New Delhi 
May 3, 2000 

Visit of Ms. Sadako Ogata, UN High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR) to India on May 4-5, 2000 

UN High Commissioner for Refugees, Ms. Sadako Ogata is paying goodwill visit to India on May 4-5, 2000 at the invitation of the Foreign Secretary. During the visit, she would be accompanied by the Regional Director for Asia and Pacific and other officials in her office. She is scheduled to meet the External Affairs Minister and the Minister of Law, Justice and Company Affairs. She would also be meeting the Chief Justice of India and the Chairperson of the National Human Rights Commission. Ms. Ogata is scheduled to participate in an event to be co- organized by the National Human Rights Commission and Indian Council for Social Science Research (ICSSR) in Vigyan Bhavan on 5th May at 1700 hrs. where she will address the gathering. She would also be meeting the Heads of UN agencies in India, academicians and the legal fraternity. 


 

May 3, 2000.
New Delhi
