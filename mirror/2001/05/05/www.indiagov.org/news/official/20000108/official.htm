General V.P. Malik, Chief of Army Staff of India and Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff Committee paid a friendly one-day visit to Mandalay in Myanmar on Thursday, January 6, 2000 at the invitation of General Maung Aye, Vice Chairman of the State Peace and Development Council, Deputy Commander-in-Chief of the Myanmar Defence Services and Commander-in-Chief of the MyanmarArmy.

2. A delegation of senior military officials including representatives of the Indian Navy and Air Force accompanied General Malik.

3. General Maung Aye, paid a friendly one-day return visit to Shillong on Friday, January 7, 2000, as a guest of General Malik. He was accompanied by a high level delegation, including Lt. Gen Tin Oo, Secretary-II of the SPDC and Chief of Staff, Myanmar Army, Lt. Gen. Win Myint, Secretary-III of the SPDC and Adjutant General of the Myanmar Defence Services and Lt. Gen. Tin Hia, Deputy Prime Minister, Minister of Military Affairs and Quarter Master General  of the Myammar Defence Services. The Commander-in-Chief of the Myanmar Navy, Vice-Admiral Nyunt Thein and Commander-in-Chief of the Myanmar Air Force, Maj.Gen. Kyaw Than also accompanied General Maung Aye.

4. During this exchange of visits, General V.P. Malik and General Maung Aye discussed enhanced cooperation and more regular exchanges between the armed  forces of the two countries, including in the field of training. The two sides also discussed measures to improve communications and exchanges between their forces deployed along the common border. General Malik also visited the Defence Services Academy and Defence Services Institute of Technology at Pyin Oo Lwin.

5. Upon arival in Shillong, General Maung Aye and his delegation paid a visit to the Assam Regiment Centre, H.E. Shri M.M Jacob, Governor of Meghalaya hosted a lunch for the visiting delegation at Raj Bhawan. The delegation also held wide-ranging bilateral talks aimed at expanding economic and technical cooperation between the two countries, with a Government of India delegation led by Shri Murasoli Maran, Minister of Commerce and Industry. The Indian delegation included Shri P R Kumaramangalam, Minister of Power as well as Secretaries in the Ministries of External Affairs, Home, Power and Commerce.

6. The two sides agreed to adopt concrete measures to enhance cooperation in the fields of agriculture, industry, infrastructure development and science and technology.

7. The two sides reviewed the growth of bilateral trade and various measures required to be taken to give it further impetus. Both sides also decided to take all measures necessary to ensure that border trade at the existing border point Moreh-Tamu is fully facilitated. The two sides agreed to consider operationalising, the second border trade point at Champhai-Rih in the Mizoram sector, including through improvement in infrastructural facilities in the area.

8. Both sides agreed to cooperate in the energy field by carrying out detailed investigations with a view to determining the feasibility of joint power projects.

9. In order to maintain the momentum of ongoing cooperation in various industrial sectors, it was agreed that Myanmar would receive, at an early date, an Indian multi-disciplinary delegation, drawn from the public and private sectors.

New Delhi,
January 8, 2000.
