The fifth meeting of the Indo-Seychelles Joint Commission was held in New Delhi from 16th to 17th October, 2000.  The Indian delegation was led by Mr. Pavan K. Varma, Joint Secretary (Africa) in the Ministry of External Affairs and the Seychelles delegation was led by Mr. Alain Butler Payette, Principal Secretary in the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

 The two sides emphasised the significance of the fifth session as the commencement of a new era of meaningful and expanding economic interaction between the two countries.  Both agreed to infuse the close political relationship with matching economic content.

 They also agreed on measures to expand cooperation in the areas of Solar energy, Pharmaceuticals, Information Technology, Fishery and Coconut Processing.  It was also agreed that Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs) of India would play a pivotal role in the implementation of joint venture projects in Seychelles.

 Discussions during the meeting also focussed on cooperation in international and regional fora.  The Government of Seychelles has confirmed its support for India’s candidature for a permanent seat in an expanded UN Security Council.

 During the meeting the Seychelles delegation called on Minister of State for Commerce and Industry and Minister of State for Small-Scale Industry and Agro and Rural Industry.  They also extended invitations to these Ministers to visit Seychelles.  The invitations have been accepted.
 

New Delhi
October 17, 2000
