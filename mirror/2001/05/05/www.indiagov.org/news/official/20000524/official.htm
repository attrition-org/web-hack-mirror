Mr. Eric Solheim, M.P. from Norway and Special Envoy visited New Delhi today after two days of talks in Colombo where he had held discussions with Sri Lankan leaders. Mr. Solheim had an exchange of views with the Foreign Secretary, Shri Lalit Mansingh, on developments relating to the ongoing conflict in Sri Lanka and the current position of Norway’s efforts at facilitating talks. The Foreign Secretary and Mr. Solheim agreed on the need for a political settlement to the conflict within the framework of the unity, sovereignty and territorial integrity of Sri Lanka, in a manner that would safeguard the aspirations of the Sri Lankan Tamils and all other communities. They also reviewed the position with reference to aspects concerning the humanitarian problems emanating from the conflict. India remains prepared to provide humanitarian assistance if requested by the Sri Lanka Government. No such request has been received by the Government of India.

New Delhi
May 24, 2000
