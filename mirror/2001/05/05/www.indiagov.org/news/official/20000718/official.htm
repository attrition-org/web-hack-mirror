Shri Ajit Kumar Panja, Hon’ble Minister of State for External Affairs, will formally inaugurate the new Passport Application Collection Centre (PACC) at 3.30 p.m. on July 19, 2000 at Pondicherry.  Dr. (Smt) Rajani Rai, Hon’ble Lt. Governor of Pondicherry, will be the Chief Guest at the function to be held at Raj Niwas and Shri P. Shanmugam, Hon’ble Chief Minister of Pondicherry, will be the Guest of Honour on the occasion.

 The PACC in Pondicherry would work under the Regional Passport Office, Chennai.  The PACC is located in the Animal Husbandry Complex, Maraimalai Adigal Salai, Pondicherry just a 150 meters away from the Pondicherry Bus Stand.  Applicants from Pondicherry, Karaikkal, Villupuram and Cuddalore districts can submit passport applications at the PACC.

 The PACC would facilitate about 30,000 passport applicants per year from the area.  Applications sent by post, however, have to be forwarded to the parent office – Regional Passport Office, Chennai only, as currently, there is no facility to handle postal applications in PACC. Applications for issue of fresh passports, re-issue of expired passports, renewal of passports after one year/five years of its issue,
minors' passports, additional booklets, maiden name to married name, change in name and features, Police Clearance Certificate, ‘Emigration Check Not Required’ endorsements and other miscellaneous services will alone be accepted.

 Applications for issue of passports under Tatkaal Scheme, duplicate passports in lieu of lost/damaged passports, official/diplomatic passports and passports for adopted children will be accepted only in parent office – Regional passport Office, Chennai.

 No cash will be accepted along with application and demand drafts drawn in favour of Regional Passport Officer, Chennai payable at Chennai alone will be accepted along with the application.  Authorised Travel Agents have to submit their applications only in RPO, Chennai and the PACC will not deal with them.  Passport prepared against the applications submitted at the PACC will be despatched to applicants directly from RPO, Chennai.

 On July 20, 2000, the website of RPO, Chennai containing procedures
relating all passport services including application status enquiry will be launched by Shri Ajit Kumar Panja, Hon’ble Minister of State for External Affairs, at Chennai.

 Responding enthusiastically to the call of Minister of State Shri Ajit Kumar Panja to enter the new millennium with zero pendencies, the Regional Passport Office, Chennai prepared about 45,000 passports during last year in November and December against normal output of 12,000 passports a month and brought down the pendency to zero at the dawn of new millennium.  Today, it takes just about 45 days to issue a fresh passport.  RPO, Chennai has streamlined the procedure for issue of passports and all the instructions are prominently displayed in the
office for the benefit of public.  Special counters for senior citizens, disabled persons and mothers with infants are under operation.  All Wednesdays are declared as “Grievances Day” and applicants can meet officers without prior appointment in the afternoon between 2.30 p.m. and 4.30 p.m. to redress their long-pending  grievances.  A special facility to get their applications filled in through ex-serviceman has been arranged in RPO, Chennai for the benefit of illiterate applicants which will also be extended to PACC, Pondicherry shortly.

New Delhi
July 18, 2000
