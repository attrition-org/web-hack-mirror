Visit of Hon'ble Ranil Wickremesinghe, M.P., Leader of the
opposition, Sri Lanka to Delhi

1. Mr. Ranil Wickremesinghe, M.P. Leader of the United National Party and Leader of the Opposition in Sri Lanka, visited Delhi from April 18-21, 2000. He was accompanied by Mrs.  Wickremesinghe and two advisers. 

2. During his stay in Delhi, Mr. Wickremesinghe called on the Prime Minister, the Home Minister, the Minister of External Affairs, the Minister for Human Resource Development, the Minister for Commerce & Industry and the Minister of State for External Affairs. Mr. Wickremesinghe's visit to India was in continuation  of the practice of maintenance of contact between the Government of India and political leaders in Sri Lanka. He last visited India in April 1998. 

3. In his discussions, Mr. Wickremesinghe reiterated his keen interest in and support for the strengthening of relations between India and Sri Lanka, particularly in the economic sector. Mr. Wickremesinghe apprised the Indian leadership of progress in the resolution of the ethnic conflict in Sri Lanka, including the UNP's talks with the Sri Lankan Government on the devolution package and role being played by Norway as a facilitator. 

4. India's relations with  Sri Lanka are close, cooperative and friendly. These have been marked by regular high level contacts in increasing spirit of mutual understanding. India's position on the ethnic conflict in Sri Lanka has been consistent and well-known. India supports a peaceful, political settlement within the framework of Sri Lanka's unity, sovereignty and territorial integrity. India believes that this is an internal matter for Sri Lanka, which would be best resolved by the Sri Lankans themselves.
 

New Delhi
April 21, 2000
