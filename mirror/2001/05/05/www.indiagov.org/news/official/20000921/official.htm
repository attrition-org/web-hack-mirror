India has always had an abiding commitment to the cause of international peace and stability. In this context, India has contributed generously to U.N. peace-keeping operations in different parts of the world. Indian armed forces personnel have addressed themselves to all situations with a high degree of professionalism and
military capabilities.

India has participated in UN peace-keeping operations in Sierra Leone for the past two years, first as a part of UN observer mission in Sierra Leone (UNOMSIL) and then as a part of UN mission to Sierra Leone (UNAMSIL). This was a reflection of India’s unflagging involvement with U.N. peace-keeping operations as well as its long standing ties of friendship and cooperation with Africa.

The Government of India has decided to withdraw India’s contingent from UNAMSIL in a phased manner.  This has been conveyed to the United Nations through appropriate diplomatic channels.  It is our view that an opportunity should be given to other countries to take part in the peace-keeping mission in Sierra Leone.

India remains resolutely committed to UN peace-keeping efforts and is willing to consider joining peace-keeping missions in other parts of the world, if requested.

With respect to the continuation of General Jetley as Force Commander of the UNAMSIL, a decision would be taken at the appropriate time.  In consonance with the decision to withdraw the Indian contingent from UNAMSIL, the appointment of a new Force Commander from India is not anticipated.
