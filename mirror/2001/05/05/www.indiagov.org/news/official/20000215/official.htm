India-Japan Foreign Office consultations

The India-Japan Foreign office consultations took place in New Delhi on 14th February, 2000. The Indian side was led by Foreign Secretary, Shri Lalit Mansingh; the Japanese side was led by Deputy Foreign Minister, Mr. Royuzo Kato. The previous foreign secretary level talks were held in Tokyo between then Indian Foreign Secretary, Shri K. Raghunath and Mr. Tamba, Deputy Minister for Foreign Affairs, in January, 1999.

2. During his visit to India, Mr. Kato called on External Affairs Minister, Shri Jaswant Singh and Principal Secretary to the Prime Minister & National Security Adviser, Shri Brajesh Mishra. He held talks with Foreign Secretary and met Secretary (ER), Shri S.T. Devare. The talks were held in a friendly and constructive atmosphere. Both sides adopted a forward-looking approach. During the discussions on bilateral relations it was agreed that Indo-Japanese relations should not be hostage to any single issue.

3. The two sides had an in-depth exchange of views regarding bilateral, regional and global issues of mutual interest. They briefed each other about their relations with major partners. They exchanged views on UN reforms and expansion of UNSC and agreed to cooperate in this regard.

4. The two sides expressed understanding of respective security concerns. On CTBT, the Indian side reiterated its position and stated that the effort to build the possible consensus was continuing.

5. The two sides also discussed possible mutual visits of dignitaries towards the year 2002, which will be the golden jubilee of Indo-Japan diplomatic relations.

6. The two sides agreed on various exchanges in the coming months to further our multi-faceted dialogue covering economic, commercial, political and military issues. They agreed to stay in close touch in regard to the issues likely to come up at the forthcoming G-8 summit to be hosted by Japan in July.

7. The two sides agreed to consult each other and cooperate on issues relating to international terrorism including trans-border terrorism.

8. Both sides agreed that India-Japan relations were important in the context of peace and stability in Asia. It was common desire of both sides to work towards a cooperative partnership.

New Delhi
February 15, 2000

Passport Adalat

The Regional Passport Office, Delhi would hold a PASSPORT ADALAT on March 11, 2000 from 10.00 AM to 4.00 PM at Regional Passport Office, Hudco Trikoot-3, Bhikaji Cama Place, R.K. Puram, New Delhi-110066. 

2. The Adalat would endeavour to expeditiously clear applications made prior to October 31, 1999 which are pending for reasons such as discrepancies in application, additional documents/information required, unclear police report etc. 

3. All the passport applicants who have applied for a passport at RPO, Delhi prior to 31 October, 1999 but have not yet received their passport, are invited to the Adalat. To facilitate speedy disposal the following details may be sent to RPO Delhi latest by February 29, 2000. 

(i) Name of the Applicant

(ii) Address & Telephone No.

(iii) File No. or Key No. & Date of Application

(iv) Brief description of grievance and copies of representations made to the Passport Office, if any 

(The envelope should be superscribed "Application for participation in Passport Adalat - March 2000") 

4. Thereafter, the applicants should come to RPO on March 11, 2000 with 4 photographs, original and attested documents proving date of birth and present residential address, school leaving certificate; graduation certificate/documents for ECNR entitlement; NOC from employer, if applicable and verification certificate as per Annexure 'G' of the application form, if Passport requirement is urgent. 

5. Officials of the Ministry of External Affairs, including the Chief Passport Officer, would be present to give on-the-spot decisions. Senior police officials have also been requested to attend to take care of the grievances resulting from unclear police verification. 

New Delhi
February 15, 2000
