We have learnt with great regret of the brutal killing of the Sri Lankan Minister for Industrial Development, Mr. C.V. Goonaratne, and of several other innocent persons today near Colombo.  This is an act of terrorism, an attack on the forces of democracy and a coercive attempt to achieve political objectives through violence.  There is no place for such acts in a democratic society.  The Government of India strongly condemns this cruel act of terrorism and extends its sympathies to all the bereaved families as also to the injured.

New Delhi,
June 7, 2000.

The Government of India notes the Statement released at the end of Special Meeting of the Commonwealth Ministerial Action Group on Fiji suspending Fiji from the councils of the Commonwealth and welcomes the decision to despatch to Fiji immediately, a mission under CMAG’s Chairman, the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Botswana, to press for a clear time-table for the restoration of democracy in that country.

The Government of India hopes that the CMAG’s call will be heeded and Prime Minister Mahendra Pal Chaudhry and other members of his Cabinet would be released immediately and unconditionally.

New Delhi,
June 7, 2000.
