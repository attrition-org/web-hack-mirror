The President of India Shri K.R. Narayanan will pay a State Visit to the People’s Republic of China from May 28-June 3, 2000 at the invitation of President Jiang Zemin.

It will be recalled that President Jiang Zemin visited India in November-December 1996. The last visit of a President of India to China was that of Shri R. Venkataraman in May 1992. President K.R. Narayanan last visited China in October 1994 as the then Vice President of India.

President Narayanan’s visit assumes special significance as it takes place as India and China commemorate the 50th anniversary of the establishment of diplomatic relations (April 1, 2000).

President Narayanan is scheduled to hold talks with President Jiang Zemin and other leaders over two days in Beijing. He will present a bust of Rabindranath Tagore to Peking University, followed by an address to a distinguished gathering of scholars, academicians, students and officials. The President will also visit the cities of Dalian (Liaoning Province) and Kunming (Yunnan Province).

President Narayanan will be accompanied by Smt. Usha Narayanan, Shri Manohar Joshi, Minister for Heavy Industries & Public enterprises and Members of Parliament – Smt. Sushma Swaraj, Shri S. Ramachandran Pillai, Shri Somnath Chatterjee and Shri Sushil Kumar Shinde besides senior officials.

New Delhi

May 22, 2000
