Joint Press Statement

At the invitation of Mr. Abul Hasan Chowdhury, State Minister of Foreign Affairs of Bangladesh, the Minister of State for External Affairs of India, Mr. Ajit Kumar Panja, visited Bangladesh from January 20-22, 2000. This was the first visit from India to Bangladesh at the political level after the recent elections in India and the formation of a new government.

On arrival, Mr. Ajit Kumar Panja called on Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina and conveyed warm greetings and good wishes from Prime Minister Atal Bihari Vajpayee. During the call, various issues of mutual interest were discussed. They stressed the commitment of both governments to further strengthen political and economic relations between the two neighbours.

During his stay in Dhaka, Mr. Ajit Kumar Panja called on the Foreign Minister, the Finance Minister and the AgriculturalMinister.

Mr. Ajit Kumar Panja held wide-ranging discussions with Mr. Abul Hasan Chowdhury on both bilateral and multilateral issues of mutual interest. Mr. Ajit Kumar Panja thanked the Government and people of Bangladesh for their message of support during the recent incident of hijacking of an Indian Airlines aircraft to Kandahar and for Bangladesh's strong condemnation of this terrorist act. Both Ministers noted with satisfaction the excellent state of bilateral relations between the two countries and underscored the necessity of holding such meetings at regular intervals at the political level.

All discussions were held in the most friendly and cordial atmosphere and covered a broad range of issues pertaining to bilateral relations. The discussions covered the need to expand multi-modal transportation and communication links, intensify cultural and educational exchanges, the creation of opportunities for young artistes, greater collaboration on improving border managment, water resources, measures to enhance trade and commerce, curbing of smuggling and consular matters. The discussions also covered regional issues in the context of the commitment of both countries to promote regional and sub-regional cooperation. Both sides reiterated the need to cooperate in combating trafficking in women and children and narcotics. They noted that an agreement on combating trafficking in narcotics was expected to be concluded soon. They recognised the need to conclude a bilateral investment promotion and protection agreement expeditiously and the utilisation of the Rs. 200 crore line of credit from India. It was noted that the Joint Economic Commission was expected to meet at an early date in Dhaka. Both sides noted with satisfaction that discussions on these and other related subjects are scheduled to take place at the level of officials in the immediate future. It was agreed that a bus service between Agartala and Dhaka would be started, the modalities for which be worked out by the two countries. The two countries signed a Memorandum of Understanding on Co-operation in the Field of Agriculture.

During the visit Mr. Ajit Kumar Panja, visited the Bangladesh National Memorial at Savar, Bangabandhu Memorial Museum, Shaheed Minar and the Liberation War Museum.

Mr. Ajit Kumar Panja invited Mr. Abul Hasan Chowdhury to visit India at a mutually convenient date. The State Minister of Foreign Affairs of Bangladesh accepted the invitation.

January 23, 2000
