8th Session of Indo-Tunisian Joint Commission Meeting concluded in Tunis.  The Indian delegation was led by Minister of State for External Affairs Shri Ajit Kumar Panja.  During his stay in Tunisia, Shri Panja called on Tunisian Prime Minister, Foreign Minister and other dignitaries and exchanged views with them on important global and regional issues and on ways and means to enhance and expand our
bilateral co-operation.  Both sides agreed to have an institutionalized exchange of views and to encourage private sector to set up industrial joint ventures.  In addition, the following were signed during the Joint Commission Meeting:

i) Extradition Treaty – to have more effective co-operation for suppression of crime.

ii) Memorandum of Understanding for Cooperation in the field of Agriculture – to promote cooperation in areas like Agriculture, Fish Genetics and Fish Processing, Soil Conservation, Vegetable and Animal Production, Exchange of Training Courses, Exchange of Experts and also Exchange of Genetic Material and Scientific Information.

iii) Programme of Cooperation in the fields of Science and Technology, that focuses on cooperation in the areas of biotechnology, information science, medical sciences and renewable energy sources.

iv) Cultural Exchange Programme for 2000-2002 – to promote exchange programmes in the fields of education, culture and arts, information, mass media, youth and sports.
 

New Delhi
April 6, 2000
