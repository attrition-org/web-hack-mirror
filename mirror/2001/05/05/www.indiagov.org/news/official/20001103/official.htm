The Second Round of the Indo-German Strategic Dialogue was held in Berlin on November 3, 2000. The Indian delegation was led by Foreign Secretary, Mr. Lalit Mansingh while the German delegation  was led by State Secretary in the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Federal Republic of German, Mr. Wolfgang Ischinger. It may be recalled that India and Germany had agreed to institutionalize this strategic dialogue earlier this year and the First Round of the Dialogue was conducted by State Secretary, Mr. Ischinger and Foreign Secretary, Mr. Lalit Mansingh in Delhi in April 2000.

The issue discussed during the Second Round of the Strategic Dialogue included matters relating to regional security and stability, disarmament and non-proliferation, measures to combat international terrorism and other global issues of common concern. The talks were marked by extreme cordiality and warmth and with mutual understanding and appreciation. The two sides also noted the rapid improvement in bilateral relationship between India and Germany, reflected inter alia
, in the increasing frequency of high level visits between the two countries . High level visits scheduled to take place in the coming year were also reviewed.

Mr. Ischinger accepted Foreign Secretary's invitation to visit India in March 2001 for the Third Round of the Strategic Dialogue.

Berlin
November 3, 2000
