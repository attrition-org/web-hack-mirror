Visit of Prime Minister of Morocco to India

Prime Minister Abderrahmane Youssoufi will pay a state visit to India from 21 to 24 February at the invitation of Prime Minister of India , Mr Atal Bihari Vajpayee. 

Prime Minister Abderrahmane Youssoufi will be accompanied by a number of ministers and deputies from the House of Parliament, businessmen and journalists. 

The visit of Prime Minister Abderrahmane Youssoufi to India is within the framework of strengthening ties of friendship, consulation and cooperation between the two countries. Several Agreements are likely to be signed during the visit. 

A Passport Application Collection Centre at Tirupati will be inaugurated by Hon'ble Union Minister for External Affairs, Shri Ajit Kumar Panja on 11th February 2000 at 1130 hours. This Centre would cater to the needs of passport applicants from four districts of Andhra Pradesh namely Chittoor, Ananthpur , Cuddapah and Nellore. The passport seekers of these Districts can collect applications and submit completed application forms either at this Centre or at Regional Passport Office, Hyderabad. They can also send their applications to Regional Passport Office, Hyderabad through post. This is the second Collection Centre under Regional Passport Office, Hyderabad, the first being at Vijaywada.

A Passport Application Collection Centre at Tirupati will be inaugurated by Minister of State for External Affairs, Shri Ajit Kumar Panja on 11th February 2000 at 1130 hours. This Centre would cater to the needs of passport applicants from four districts of Andhra Pradesh namely Chittoor, Ananthpur , Cuddapah and Nellore.  The passport seekers of these Districts can collect applications and submit completed
application forms either at this Centre or at Regional Passport Office, Hyderabad. They can also send their applications to Regional Passport Office, Hyderabad through post.  This is the second Collection Centre under Regional Passport Office, Hyderabad, the first being at Vijaywada.

The Regional Passport Office, Hyderabad had brought the pendency of passports to " Zero" as it entered the new millenium . All the passport applications submitted upto November 15, 1999 were processed and passports were issued before 31 December 1999 if the documents were complete and the police report was clear. This was accomplished as a concerted drive was launched in all the 28 Passport Offices to clear the backlog and as a result 25.8 lakh passports were issued in 1999 throughout the country marking an increase of 16% over the output in 1998.
