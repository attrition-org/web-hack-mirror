Interim Civilian Cabinet announced in Fiji

The Government of India notes that an interim Civilian Cabinet was announced in Fiji yesterday by the Commander of the Military Forces and Executive head of State, Commodore V. Bainimarama.

The Government of India wishes to express its continuing anxiety about the welfare of Mr. Mahendra Chaudhry and his Cabinet colleagues who have been held hostage since May 19 and hopes that they will be released without further delay. We are encouraged by Commander Bainimarama’s Statement yesterday which indicates that the safety and release of the 27 hostages will remain a top priority.

The Government of India hopes that along with the release of the hostages, Fiji will return to normal political activity with a democratic, non-racial and representative Government within the framework of the 1997 Constitution.

Foundation stone of a new Passport Office Complex laid in Bangalore

The Minister of State for External Affairs, Shri Ajit Kumar Panja laid the ‘Foundation Stone’ of a new Passport Office Complex at Bangalore on 3rd July 2000. The Chief Minister of Karnataka, Shri S.M. Krishna, the Union Minister for Tourism, Shri Ananth Kumar and the Minister of State for Finance, Shri Dhananjay Kumar were also present at the function.

The Government of Karnataka has provided the 2 acre plot free of cost for the building which would also house the Regional Office of the Indian Council of Cultural Relations. The total project cost would be about Rs. 10 crores.

Speaking on the occasion MOS indicated that the building will be constructed in about two years and would be a most modern and prestigious building.

On the same day Shri Ajit Kumar Panja inaugurated the Passport Officers’ Conference at Bangalore where officers from all the 28 Passport Offices and the officials of the Ministry of External Affairs including Shri J.C. Sharma, Additional Secretary were present.

The Conference took several decisions to further streamline the issue of passports and reform procedures for the convenience of the general public.

It was decided to greatly widen the distribution network of passport application forms so that these are freely available from multiple sources like Banks, Post Offices, Bookshops etc. at a nominal cost.

The Indian passport booklet would soon be made with state of the art technology. Passports with a scanned image and entries printed by computers, instead of a pasted photograph and hand-written particulars, would soon be issued first from RPO, Delhi and subsequently from other Passport Offices.

Official visit of H.E. Dr. Surin Pitsuwan,
Foreign Minister of Thailand,
to India
8-10 July, 2000

On the invitation of Minister of External Affairs, His Excellency Dr. Surin Pitsuwan, Foreign Minister of Thailand is visiting India from July 8-10, 2000 accompanied by senior officials and a group of Thai businessmen. The visit is expected to improve mutual understanding and cooperation on bilateral, regional and international issues of mutual interest. Thailand is currently Chairman of ASEAN PMC and ARF which are meeting later this month in Bangkok. Apart from talks with Minister of External Affairs, Shri Jaswant Singh, His Excellency Dr. Surin Pitsuwan’s programme includes a call on the President and the Prime Minister. A meeting with Shri Nitesh Kumar, Minister of Agriculture is also scheduled. Two bilateral Agreements relating to (I) Cooperation for Utilisation of Atomic Energy for Peaceful Purposes; and (ii) Investment Promotion and Protection Agreement, are expected to be signed during the visit. A meeting organised by Confederation of Indian Industry (CII) will provide the opportunity for business interaction. New Delhi July 4, 2000.

At the invitation of the Prime Minister, Shri Atal Behari Vajpayee, the Prime Minister of Nepal , H.E. Mr. Girija Prasad Koirala, will pay an official visit to India beginning on the 1st August 2000. Prime Minister Koirala will have talks with Prime Minister Vajpayee and also call on President K.R. Narayanan. Apart from Delhi, Prime Minister Koirala is likely to visit other cities in India. The details of the programme are being worked out through mutual consultations.

New Delhi
July 4, 2000
