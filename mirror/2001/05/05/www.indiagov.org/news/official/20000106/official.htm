The Republics of Croatia and India signed an Agreement for Cooperation between the Diplomatic Academy of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and the Foreign Service Institute of the Ministry of External Affairs. From the Croatian side, H.E. Dr. Zoran Andric, Ambassador of the Republic of Croatia signed the Agreement while Dean, Foreign Service Institute and Secretary, Ministry of External Affairs Shri Dalip Mehta signed from the Indian side.

The Agreement envisages exchange of materials and scholars to strengthen relations between the diplomatic services of both countries.

The Agreement was signed on January 5, 2000 in Hyderabad House, New Delhi.

In keeping with the tradition of high-level exchanges between the Indian Armed Forces and the Myanmar Armed Forces, Gen. V.P. Malik, Chief of Army Staff and Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff Committee will pay a friendly visit to Mandalay in Myanmar from January 6 to 7, 2000 at the invitation of Gen. Maung Aye, Vice Chairman of the State Peace and Development Council, Deputy Commander-in-Chief of the Myanmar Armed Forces and Commander-in-Chief of the Myanmar Army. A delegation of senior military officers, including representatives of the Indian Air Force and Navy will accompany the COAS.

Gen. Maung Aye, accompanied by a high-level delegation, will pay a friendly visit to Shillong in India as the guest of the COAS from January 7-8, 2000.

It may be recalled that the then Chiefs of Army Staff, Gen. B.C. Joshi and Gen. S. Roychowdhury, had visited Myanmar in May 1994 and March 1997 respectively and the then Chief of Air Staff, Air Chief Marshal S.K. Sareen. In April 1996, Lt.Gen. Tin Ngwe, Commander-in-Chief of the Myanmar Air Force, had visited India in December 1996 and Vice Admiral Nyunt Thein, Commander-in-Chief of the Myanmar Navy visited India in November 1999.

During this exchange of visits, the two sides will discuss measures for enhanced cooperation between the armed forces of the two countries in areas, including training and border management between their forces deployed along their common border.

In view of the fact that Gen. Maung Aye is Chairman of the National Trade Council, the National Industrial Development Committee and the National Agricultural and Economic Committee and has wide-ranging political, military and economic responsibilities, the Government of India will avail of the opportunity of Gen. Maung Aye's visit to Shillong to undertake an informal and wide-ranging exchange of views with the Myanmar side on matters of mutual interest. The Government will be represented by Shri Murasoli Maran, Minister of Commerce & Industry, Shri R. Kumaramangalam, Minister of Power and senior representatives of the Ministries of Commerce, Defence, External Affairs, Home Affairs and Power together with representatives of the States bordering Myanmar. The two sides will discuss issues concerning border security, measures for economic cooperation in border areas, border trade as well as enhanced interaction in the fields of agriculture, industry and science & technology.
