Joint Statement by Indo-Canada Working Group on Counter-terrorism

The third-meeting of the Indo-Canada Working Group on counter-terrorism took place in New Delhi on February 15 - 16, 2000.

The Working Group mechanism between India and Canada was established during the visit of Canada's Minister of Foreign Affairs, Mr. Lloyd Axworthy to India in January, 1997, in order to further enhance cooperation between India and Canada in combating terrorism. India hosted the first meeting of the Working Group in April, 1997 in New Delhi. Canada hosted the second reunion in Ottawa in November, 1998.

The two sides expressed satisfaction at their ongoing cooperation in the area of counter-terrorism, and agreed to enhance such cooperation bilaterally as well as in multilateral forums. They expressed their conviction that terrorist acts are criminal acts and could not be justified for political, ideological religious or any other reasons.

The next meeting of the Working Group will be held in Canada at a mutually convenient date.

New Delhi
February 16, 2000
