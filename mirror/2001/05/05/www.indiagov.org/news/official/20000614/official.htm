In response to questions regarding Mr. Nawaz Sharif’s recent statements to the Pakistani media on Pakistan’s aggression in Kargil last year, the official spokesman said that Mr. Nawaz Sharif’s statements only reconfirm what has been known all along that the Pakistan army had planned this misadventure, that it were Pakistan’s regular troops and not so called mujaheddin that had been sent across the Line of Control in the Kargil Sector. 

The second, and more worrisome aspect is that of deceptions, more particularly and tellingly, against the Armed Forces and the people of Pakistan themselves by the present regime attempting to perpetuate the untenable myth of so called mujaheddin, of not disclosing the actual casualties suffered by Pakistan's military units themselves, thus denying them the elementary right of trust and honour which is due of all military forces anywhere in the world. 

New Delhi
June 14, 2000. 

Honourable Minister of Human Resource Development, Dr. Murli Manohar Joshi arrived in Damascus on June 12, 2000 to represent Government of India at the funeral of late President of Syria, Hafez Al-Assad. He was met at the airport by the Syrian Prime Minister, Mr. Mohammed Mustafa Mero and other members of his Cabinet. On June 13, 2000 Dr. Murli Manohar Joshi, along with the accompanying members of the delegation, paid his respects to the deceased leader at the People's Palace, overlooking Damascus city. He also met Dr. Bashar al-Assad, the late President's son to personally convey deep condolences on behalf of the Government and people of India. He also expressed the Government's resolve to continue with, and further consolidate, the existing friendly ties between India and Syria and wished Dr. Bashar and the Syrian people a prosperous and peaceful future. The delegation left for India on June 13, 2000.
