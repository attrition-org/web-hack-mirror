Prime Minister of Australia to visit India
10-11 July 2000

Australian Prime Minister John Howard will make an official visit to India on July 10-11, 2000.  This is the first official visit to India by an Australian Prime Minister since 1989.

During his visit Prime Minister Howard will have contacts with the President, the Vice President, Prime Minister and other senior Indian Ministers.  The object of the visit is to further advance Australian-Indian cooperation in all respects.

Australia's links with India have grown considerably over the past 10 years especially after the opening of the Indian economy.  Australia recognises India as an increasingly important player in regional and international affairs and is keen to pursue with us an enhanced dialogue on economic and strategic issues of mutual interest.  Given the size of India's emerging market and the complementarities between the two economies there are significant opportunities for Australian investments in India especially in the IT and telecommunications sectors, which the
visit is expected to promote.

In 1999 Australia's total trade with India amounted to US$ 2.1 billion. Australian investments in India are currently estimated to be in excess of US$ 1 billion.

New Delhi
June 19, 2000
