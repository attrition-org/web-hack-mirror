External Affairs Minister, Shri Jaswant Singh's Statement in Parliament on March 2, 2001

In the face of universal concern and despite protests and cautions against taking any such step, the Taliban appear bent upon committing a grave wrong, indeed, a sacrilege to humanity, to the civilisational and cultural inheritance of all mankind by wanting to destroy the incomparable and unique statues of Buddha at Bamiyan. It is tragic that this act of vandalism, the most extreme amongst the many other acts of destruction of statues, artifacts and archaeological treasures of Afghanistan is being pursued despite a global outcry against it.

This regression into medieval barbarism is precisely what India, amongst many other countries, has been cautioning the world against for so long. The international community has unequivocally condemned and rejected the Taliban's ideological orientations which have been responsible for the Taliban territories emerging as the world's principal centre of international terrorism, illicit drugs and violation of human rights, especially those of women. 

Even at this late stage, we would like to convey that the destruction of these precious manifestations of the cultural heritage of humankind should stop. If the Taliban do not wish to retain this inheritance, India would be happy to arrange for the transfer of all these artifacts to India where they would be kept safely and preserved for all mankind, in the full knowledge and clear understanding that they are, in the first place and above all, treasures of the Afghan people themselves. 

New Delhi, 
March 1, 2001 

Launch of Passport Application Sale and Collection through Speed Post 
Centres

In the continuing drive to improve the delivery of passport services to the public the Ministry of External Affairs and the Department of Posts have come together in a common endeavor to enhance the accessibility of passport services.

From 5th March 2001 speed post centres in 47 locations in different cities will start  accepting completed application forms. These centres would also provide  application forms for new passports to the public. These 47 centres will augment the  existing 28 passport offices and the 21 Passport Application Collection Centres and  at one stroke will nearly double the number of places where people can deposit their  filled application forms in the country. Processing and dispatch of passports will be done by the passports offices as at present.

This new facility will be inaugurated by the Hon'ble Shri Ram Vilas Paswan, Minister  for Communications and Shri Ajit Kumar Panja, Minister of State for External  Affairs at New Delhi on March 5, 2001 at 11.30 AM.

With this new initiative the distance to be traveled by applicants for obtaining an  application form or submitting it for a passport will be reduced in large population  centres. In. the next phase 57 additional speed post centres would be enabled to  accept application forms as well thus increasing the out reach of passport services       considerably. 

The collaboration with the Department of Posts already includes delivery of  passports through speed post services. In this way applicants submitting completed  application papers at a speed post centre near their homes will get a passport  delivered at home and would not have to visit the passport office at all if they so  choose.

As the computerisation of passport offices is progressing several passport offices  already have their own websites which would allow a status check on the passport  applications. This network would gradually cater to all the 20 computerised passport  offices. Tele-enquiry services at the passport offices are also being revamped. With  these services in place people would not have to necessarily go to passport offices  to enquire about the status of processing of. their passport applications.

In this exercise, the availability of forms submission of applications and the delivery  of passports would be effectively decentralized while the processing, which is a  specialised field and requires considerable expertise, data base and hardware, will  remain centralised in the 28 passport offices. These services are also being augmented through an expansion of machine writing of passports project, the creation of data bases for old passports and the establishment of connectivity between passport offices for easier communication and cross checking. 

The present cooperation between the Department of Posts and the Ministry of External Affairs will see these Joint services start at Tiruvanthapuram, Kollam, Thiruvalla, Kottyam, Kochi, Alwaye, Thrissur and Kozhikode in Kerala;

at Bangalore, Mangalore, Udipi, Hubli, Dharwad, Belgaum, Bellary and Gulbaroa in  Karnataka; 

at Mumbai GPO, and Andheri in Mumbai; at Navrangpura, Pindi, Bazar, Ahmedabad SPC and Ahmedabad GPO in Ahmedabad; 

at Kolkata GPO, Bidhan Nagar, Kolkata and Siliguri in West Bengal; 

at SPC New Delhi, New Delhi HO, Delhi GPO, Lodhi Road HPO, Krishna Nagar HO, Ashok Vihar HO, Delhi GPO, Indraprastha HO, Ramesh Nagar HO and  Sarojini Nagar HO in Delhi;

at Hyderabad GPO, Secunderabad HO, Khairatabad, Hyderabad Jubilee HO and Humayun Nagar in Andhra Pradesh; 

at SP Business office, IIT PO, Vadaplani PO, Anna Nagar PO, Tirplicane PO and Anna Road HPO in Chennai.

It is the endeavor of the Government to gradually passport application receiving facilities at the district level in every district of India thus reducing the necessity of  travel by the growing member of passport applicants to the passport offices. 22.43 lakh passports were issued in the year 2000 reflecting the growing requirement for extending the passport services througliout the country and bring these services closer to the people.

Resolution Adopted by both Houses of Parliament

Today, in this new millennium, when all civilizations are coming together through dialogue and shared values, we are shocked by the Taliban announcements of intended acts of religious fundamentalism and terrorism against human culture and civilization. They have moved tanks and guns to destroy one of the greatest examples of human creativity. This intent of destroying the two thousand year old statues of Buddha and the Buddhist shrines in Bamiyan would constitute an appalling act of cultural vandalism.

This House unanimously condemns the barbarism and anti-civilisational intent of the Taliban in Afghanistan in the strongest possible terms. We hope the world community and especially the United Nations will take note of this and prevail upon the Taliban regime in Afghanistan to desist from this senseless, destructive act.

New Delhi,
March 2, 2001



The Prime Minister of India, Shri Atal Bihari Vajpayee, has written to the leaders of Japan, South Korea, Cambodia, Sri Lanka, Bhutan, Thailand, Laos, Cambodia, Myanmar, U.S.A., U.K., France, Russia and China and the U.N. Secretary-General about the reported decree of the Taliban leaders in Afghanistan to destroy all statues in that country including the ancient Bamiyan Buddhas, describing this order as a "disquieting development".

The Prime Minister added that the reported decree "represents a further obscurantist regression - an assault on centuries of Afghan tradition and upon an irreplaceable civilisational inheritance of all mankind. We are disturbed to see reports that this demolition is already in progress". 

Prime Minister Vajpayee urged that "the collective voice of humanity be raised against this outrage, and that the Taliban be made to see reason". He also urged that "we should also take up this matter with those countries that have influence with the Taliban, so that they, too, could intervene to seek the withdrawal of the decree". 

New Delhi 
March 2, 2001
