Prime Minister, Shri Atal Bihari Vajpayee, will now leave for his visit to the United Nations and the USA on September 7, 2000.  The re-scheduling of the departure date on the advice of his doctors would have no bearing on the major elements of his programme.  The programme in New York 8th September onwards remains unchanged.  The Prime Minister will, however, not visit San Francisco but proceed directly from New York to Washington D.C. New Delhi
August 30, 2000

The residential premises of two Indian diplomats based in Islamabad, Shri Vipin Handa, Counsellor and Gp. Capt. S.K. Jayaswal, Air Adviser, were intruded upon by the operatives of the Pakistan intelligence agencies today.

In the first incident, five intelligence operatives, claiming to be employees of the Capital Development Authority, entered the house of Shri Vipin Handa at 1540 hours while his wife, teenage daughter and young son were by themselves at home.  They went all over the house, rummaged through their personal belongings and made threatening statements before leaving. They refused to show their identity cards when asked to do so. In the second incident, six intelligence Agents forced their entry into the residence of the Air Adviser at 1545 hours after threatening his servant’s family with a gun.  The entire drama was replayed at the Air Adviser’s residence as well.  These incidents follow a pattern of calibrated and escalating harassment and brazen intimidation by Pakistani authorities of members of the High Commission of India.

The Ministry summoned the Pakistani Deputy High Commissioner this evening and lodged a strong protest deploring the blatant and gross violation of the privacy and sanctity of the residential premises of the Indian diplomats.  The Ministry demanded that measures be taken to ensure that there are no repetitions of such incidents in future and sought adequate security for all personnel of the High Commission of India, Islamabad and their residential premises.
