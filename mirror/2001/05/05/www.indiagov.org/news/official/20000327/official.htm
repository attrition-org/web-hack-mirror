Prime Minister Shri Atal Bihari Vajpayee spoke to the President of the Russian Federation Mr Vladimir Putin over the hotline today. The Prime Minister conveyed his warm felicitations and greetings over Mr.Putin's impresive victory in the elections. He said that the entire Indian nation was happy over his election and conveyed his best wishes to Mr.Putin in his high responsibilities.

Prime Minister recalled his conversation over the hotline on January 27, 2000. He mentioned that bilateral relations between India and the Russian Federation are time-tested and based on continuity, trust and mutual understanding.

Prime Minister reiterated his invitation to Mr. Putin to visit India on a State Visit at the earliest opportunity. He said that the people of India would accord him a warm welcome. Prime Minister also mentioned that we looked forward to the signature of the Declaration on Strategic Partnership between India and the Russian Federation during his visit.

President Putin thanked Prime Minister for his felicitations. He gratefully accepted the invitation to visit India at the earliest opportunity and said that this would allow both sides to further consolidate their bilateral relations.

New Delhi
March 27, 2000

Visit of H.E. Mr. Bulent Ecevit, Prime Minister of the Republic of Turkey

At the invitation of Prime Minister of India, H.E. Mr. Bulent Ecevit, Prime Minister of the Republic of Turkey, accompanied by Madame Rahsan Ecevit and a high-level delegation will pay a State visit to India from March 31 – April 2, 2000. A business delegation is accompanying the Turkish Prime Minister to participate in the fourth meeting of the Indo-Turkish Joint Business Council (JBC) also to be held during the visit.

High level contacts have been a feature of India-Turkey relations. Prime Minister Turgut Ozal’s visit in 1986 started the current phase of interaction. Prime Minister Rajiv Gandhi visited Turkey in 1988. President Kenan Evren visited India in 1989 which was reciprocated by President S.D. Sharma in 1993. President Suleyman Demirel came to India in 1995 and President K.R. Narayanan paid a return visit in 1998. Vice-President Krishan Kant visited Turkey in 1998 to participate in the 75th anniversary of the Turkish Republic.

Prime Minister Ecevit’s forthcoming visit is expected to provide a strong impulse to the strengthening of Indo-Turkish cooperation, particularly in the economic and commercial spheres. The visit also re-affirms the cultural affinity between the two peoples, to which Prime Minister Ecevit’s personal contribution has taken the form of translation into Turkish language of Bhagavat-Gita and Rebindranath Tagore’s ‘Gitanjali’.

Apart from meeting with the Prime Minister, the visiting dignitary is to call on the President and the Vice-President and meet with senior Ministers of the Government of India and the Leader of the Opposition. He will be conferred with an honorary degree of D.Litt. (Deshikottam) by Visva Bharati at Shantiniketan. Prime Minister Ecevit will address business and industry leaders of the two countries at a meeting organized by FICCI.

A number of agreements, including cooperation in the field of agriculture and establishment of mechanism for political consultations between the foreign ministries of India and Turkey, would be signed.

New Delhi
March 27, 2000.

India and the United States are pleased to announce their agreement to commence negotiation of a Mutual Legal Assistance Treaty soon, which we hope to conclude this year.

This proposed MLAT is a natural follow-on to the new Extradition Treaty between India and the United States that entered into force on July 21, 1999.

Conclusion of an MLAT would enhance law enforcement cooperation between India and the United States by providing a streamlined and effective mechanism for assisting one another in the investigation and prosecution of crimes.

New Delhi
March 27, 2000
