In response to a question the official spokesman confirmed that over the last five years, Pakistan had on completely baseless and untenable charges, sought the recall to India of 17 members of our High Commission in Islamabad.  The official spokesman also confirmed that 89 cases of intimidation, harassment, abduction and physical torture of our High Commission officials by the Pakistani agencies were intimated to the Pakistan Foreign Office during the same period.  In all these cases the Vienna Convention of Diplomatic Relations of 1961 and the India-Pakistan Code of Conduct for the Treatment of Diplomatic Personnel of August, 1992, were grossly violated.   Personnel of our High Commission and their family members are routinely subjected to intensive and intrusive surveillance and harassment by the Pakistan agencies. 

The official spokesman also focussed attention on the callous approach of Pakistan towards its humanitarian and diplomatic obligations as is being witnessed currently in its failing to grant consular access to the shipwrecked crew of an Indian cargo vessel, Jan Kallyan, which had caught fire and sank on June 25, 2000, in the Arabian Sea on a journey from UAE to India.  Eleven crew members of the vessel were rescued by a Pakistani fishing boat on June 30, 2000.  These crew members had spent nine days in the sea without food and water.  Pakistan informed us about
the rescue of these crew members on July 6, 2000.  It also conveyed that some of the Indian crew members who were in need of medical treatment had been admitted to the government hospital in Pasni (Pakistan).  The Government of India immediately conveyed its ‘deep appreciation to the Government of Pakistan and, through them, to the crew members of the Pakistani fishing boat involved in the rescue of the eleven crew members of the Indian boat ‘Jan Kalyan’.  In keeping with international practice, the High Commission of India sought immediate consular access to the crew members in order to ascertain their welfare and to arrange for their repatriation to India.  Consular access has not been accorded as yet by the Government of Pakistan.  The agony and suffering of the crew members as well as that of their families in India is, therefore, being inexplicably prolonged.  The continued detention of the crew members in Pakistani custody and the denial, as yet, of consular access displays an inhumane attitude.

New Delhi
August 28, 2000
