The twelfth meeting of the India-China Joint Working Group on the Boundary Question (JWG) was held in New Delhi on April 28, 2000.  The Indian delegation was led by the Foreign Secretary Shri Lalit Mansingh. The Chinese delegation was led by the Vice Foreign Minister Mr. Yang Wenchang.

2. Vice Foreign Minister Mr. Yang Wenchang called on External Affairs Minister Shri Jaswant Singh on April 28, 2000.  Mr. Yang conveyed greetings from Foreign Minister Tang Jiaxuan to EAM and said that since EAM had assumed office, relations between India and China have continued to improve.  EAM reciprocated the greetings and reiterated his invitation to Foreign Minister Tang to visit India.  EAM said that, in the JWG process, the efforts of the two sides should be to achieve results.  This would contribute to the creation of a climate where
further results could be achieved.

3. During the sessions of JWG, the two sides reviewed the development of bilateral relations since the visit of External Affairs Minister Shri Jaswant Singh to China in June 1999.  They expressed satisfaction that the commemorative activities to mark the 50th anniversary of the establishment of diplomatic relations between the two countries have provided an impetus to further development of relations.

4. The two sides discussed boundary-related issues, including clarification of the Line of Actual Control (LAC) and Confidence Building Measures between the armed forces.  The discussions were held in a frank and friendly atmosphere.  The two sides agreed that the Expert Group would meet as often as required to discuss boundary related issues, including, LAC clarifications.  The two sides reiterated their
commitment to seek a fair, reasonable and mutually acceptable settlement of the boundary question.  There were recognition that the two Agreements –The maintenance of Peace and Tranquility along the Line of Actual Control in the India-China Border Areas (1993) and the Agreement on Confidence Building Measures in the Military Field along the Line of Actual Control in the India-China Border Areas (1996), have provided the framework for the maintenance of peace and tranquility in the border areas.

5. The two sides discussed arrangements for the State visit of President Shri K.R. Narayanan to China at the invitation of President Jiang Zemin in May this year.  The Chinese side referred to the importance they attached to the President’s visit.  Both sides agreed that the visit would enhance mutual understanding and co-operation.  The Indian side recalled the invitation to Premier Zhu Rongji and expressed the hope
that the Chinese Premier would visit India at his early convenience.

6. The two sides also exchanged views on regional, multilateral and international issues of mutual interest.

7. The two sides agreed on adopting a forward-looking approach and building a long term, stable, constructive and mutually beneficial relationship on the basis of Panchsheel and on promoting cultural, economic, scientific and technical and people-to-people contacts while continuing to address outstanding issues through dialogue.

New Delhi
29th April, 2000.
