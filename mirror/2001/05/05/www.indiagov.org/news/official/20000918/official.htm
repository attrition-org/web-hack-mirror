1. A strike notice by the All India Passport Employees Association (AIPEA) was received by the Ministry on 9 September, 2000. The Association raised several issues and extensive discussions for more than 8 hours were held between the office bearers of the Association and senior officials on Thursday 14 September and Friday 15 September. A response to all the issues raised by the Association was also formally conveyed to the Association in the evening of Friday 15 September. 

2. Meanwhile the Association held lunchtime demonstrations outside the passport offices all over the country on 14 and 15 September and did not withdraw the strike notice. On Monday, 18 September members of the AIPEA resorted to agitation and dharna outside the passport offices and did not perform their duties. However, the officers at all 28 passport offices attended to their duties. 

3. Due to this, work was affected in most of the 28 passport offices all over the country though some offices functioned normally. 

4. At RPO, Delhi officers and staff from the Ministry of External Affairs were deputed to assist in the operations of the office. Other offices worked on the strength of their officers only while in many offices some staff members also reported for work. 

5. The Association is unwilling to work with a private contractor who has introduced the machine writing of passports at RPO, Delhi. There are some un-founded apprehensions among some passport staff on working with a private contractor who has been given the work of machine writing for a limited period only for RPO, Delhi. The staff has been repeatedly assured that the Government intends to get the machine writing work done through the officials and staff of the Central Passport Organisation at all other Passport offices if, after the required training, the machinery is optimally utilised and the norms of productivity are maintained. 

6. The other issues raised by the Association pertain to their cadre review, review of the recruitment rules and resolution of anomalies in their pay scales. On all these issues dialogue has been held with the Association on several occasions and these are in an advanced stage of consideration in the Government. 

7. The Government is of the view that when it is willing to work with the Association for the resolution of their genuine and reasonable demands, a strike at this stage is neither called for nor justified since it negates the overall objective of providing prompt support and efficient services to the people. 

8. The number of passports issued in 1999 was over 25.8 lakhs from the 28 passport offices in India. The number of passports issued per annum has gone up even though the staff strength has remained inadequate. In 1996, there was a staff of 1858 in the CPO cadre, which grew to 1939 in 1999. In the same time the number of passports issued increased from 19.12 lakhs in 1996 to 25.8 lakhs in 1999. Thus, while the number of passports issued grew by almost 34%, the staff strength rose by a mere 4%. Similarly, revenue earned in 1996 by the passport offices in India was 71.16 crores which grew to 82.2 crores in 1999. This will grow further since the ‘Tatkaal’ scheme was introduced in January 2000 for which no additional staff has been added. 

9. All the passport offices are expected to work normally from Tuesday 19 September 2000. 

New Delhi
September 18, 2000
