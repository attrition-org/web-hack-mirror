Serious incidents of harassment and intimidation of our High Commission personnel and their families by Pakistani agencies had taken place during the past few days.  The spokesman drew attention in particular to an incident which had taken place on September 2, 2000 involving an attempt at molestation of minor daughters of staff members of the High Commission by an intruder who had entered the compound of the house of one of the High Commission’s staff members.  The staff member and his  wife had gone to a hospital.  His daughters aged 8 and 6 and the 7 year old daughter of another staff member who lives in the same area were playing the driveway of the house.  At around 5 p.m. an intruder entered the compound and took two of the girls to one corner of the garden and attempted to physically misbehave with them.  The girls resisted and ran inside the house.  At this stage, the intruder left the house.  The girls informed the staff member and his wife of the incident when they returned home.

The spokesman said there is little room to doubt that the intelligence officials of Pakistan, who were keeping a surveillance over the house did nothing to prevent the incident.  It also appears that the intruder not only had the knowledge of the absence of the staff member and his wife but was also sure that they would not return home early.  The role of the Pakistani intelligence officials was therefore extremely
questionable. 

The spokesman confirmed that an FIR about this heinous crime was lodged by the High Commission with the local police authorities.  The Pakistan Deputy High Commissioner was summoned to the Ministry of External Affairs yesterday.  He was informed that the Government of India took the gravest view of this incident.  Further, that the Government of India expected that the perpetrator of this heinous crime, irrespective of his identity, be arrested, prosecuted and punished according to law. The same demand was conveyed to the Pakistan Foreign Secretary by our
High Commissioner in Islamabad during the course of a meeting today. Pakistan Foreign Secretary and the Deputy High Commissioner expressed regret over this reprehensible act and said that the Pakistan authorities will carry out a full investigation of the incident. 

In another incident on September 3, 2000 a staff member and his family were proceeding by car from Islamabad to Taxila when they were stopped at a Police Check Post, questioned and taken to a Police Station where they were detained for 40 minutes.  The spokesman also recalled that the residential premises of two senior Indian Diplomats were intruded upon by the operatives of the Pakistan intelligence agencies on August 30, 2000.  These high handed actions of the Pakistan authorities were in complete violation of the Vienna Convention and the Bilateral Code of
Conduct for the Treatment of Diplomatic Personnel. Government have lodged strong protests against these actions of the Pakistan authorities.

New Delhi
September 05, 2000
