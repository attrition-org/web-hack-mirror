Prime Minister Vajpayee today received a group of relatives of the Prime Minister of Fiji Mr. Mahendra Chaudhry. They expressed concern at the situation in Fiji and, in particular the fate of Prime Minister Chaudhry, who is being held at gun point together with some other members of the Fiji Government in an attempted coup d’etat, since Friday, May 19. Prime Minister Chaudhry’s relatives conveyed their deep anxiety about his physical safety and well being and sought PM’s help to ensure the Fiji Prime Minister’s early release from detention.

PM conveyed his deep disquiet regarding recent developments in Fiji. He reiterated his view that any move to oust the legitimately elected Government in Fiji would be unacceptable to India. He said that India condemned the undemocratic and criminal nature of the events that have occurred in Fiji. He also expressed surprise that the coup leaders appear to enjoy police protection and freedom of action in Suva. They are in violation of the principles of the Harare Declaration and the Millbrook Action Programme of the Commonwealth. PM directed EAM Shri Jaswant Singh to take up the matter urgently with the Commonwealth Secretary General and the British Government.

Concerted international pressure is necessary in order to restore the legitimate government in Fiji. India is ready to play its part. Disruption of inter-racial harmony and loss of property since the coup attempt is a matter of concern and it is hoped that the crisis can be rapidly brought to an end with the restoration of the legitimately elected government.

New Delhi

May 23, 2000
