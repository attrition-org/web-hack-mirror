H.E. Lyonpo Sangay Ngedup, Minister for Health and Education, Royal Government of Bhutan, will pay an official visit to India from April 4 to 9, 2001. This will be his first official visit to India after assuming the current charge in June, 1998. 

The visiting Minister's itinerary includes a visit to Mumbai, where he would be calling on the Governor of Maharashtra.  During his stay in Delhi, he would be calling on the Hon'ble Minister for Human Resource Development and Hon'ble Minister for Health who will host a dinner and lunch respectively for him.  The Foreign Secretary will also host a dinner in honour of the Minister.

The Minister will also take the opportunity to visit some of the premier Institutes in the field of health and education.  These include, inter-alia, All India Institute of Medical Sciences, Apollo Hospital, Jawaharlal Nehru University, Delhi University and Indian Institute of Technology, Mumbai in the field of IT Education.

Lyonpo Sangay Ngedup is no stranger to Delhi as he is an alumnus of St. Stephen's College and Jawaharlal Nehru University.  Starting his career as a career diplomat he rose to the position of a Cabinet Minister in 1998.  He also became the Chairman of the Council of Minister for one year, under rotation, from July 1999 to July 2000.

Government of India has a very extensive cooperation programme with the Royal Government of Bhutan encompassing support to various sectors in Bhutan.  Besides providing support for educational infrastructural projects in Bhutan, India provides around 100 scholarships every year to Bhutanese students in medicine, engineering, nursing, agriculture, law, etc.  Bhutan's only degree college - Sherubtse College - is affiliated to Delhi University.  Around 13 lecturers are deputed to this college under TCS of Colombo plan.

India has also extended its cooperation in the development of medical infrastructure in Bhutan.  Two, major hospitals with capacities of 150-bed at Mongar and 350-bed at Thimphu are being developed with the assistance of the Government of India.  Two 20-bed hospitals have recently been completed at Tashiyangtse and Lhuntse in Eastern Bhutan with India's assistance.  Another programme with the objective to control the dreaded disease of Malaria in Bhutan is also under implementation under India assistance.

New Delhi
April 4, 2001
