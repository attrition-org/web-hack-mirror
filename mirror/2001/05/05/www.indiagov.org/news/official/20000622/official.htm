1. Prime Minister will be visiting Italy and Portugal from 25 to 30 June, 2000. In Italy he will be accompanied by the Finance Minister. Prime Minister will hold talks with Prime Minister Amato and attend a Business Meeting organised by Confindustria and FICCI. He will also meet President Ciampi in Rome.

2. Prime Minister’s visit to Italy will be the first Head of State/Head of Government level visit exchanged between the two countries since that of Mr. Romano Prodi, the Prime Minister of Italy to India in January, 1998. Italy is an important interlocutor for India, both politically and economically. It is a significant trading partner and also an important investor in India. The political dialogue between the two countries has been strengthened in recent months with the visit of the External Affairs Minister to Rome in January, 2000 and that of Mr. Franco Danieli, Italian Minister of State for Foreign Affairs to India in March-April, 2000.

3. Prime Minister will subsequently visit Portugal from 27 to 29 May, 2000 for the first-ever India-EU Summit and for a bilateral visit. The Summit will be held on 28 June. The EU side will be represented by Mr. Antonio Guterres, Prime Minister of Portugal and Mr. Romano Prodi, President of the European Commission. Prime Minister will be accompanied by the External Affairs Minister, the Finance Minister, the Minister of Commerce and Industry and the Minister for Information Technology. It is expected that a Joint Declaration will be issued at the conclusion of the Summit. The Declaration will serve as a broad vision statement of India-EU relations and provide a blue-print for upgrading the relationship to a qualitatively higher level. The Summit will also finalise an Agenda for Action which will encompass joint initiatives in the political and economic fields. The India-EU Summit will be preceded by an India-EU Business Meeting organised by CII and the Portuguese Chamber of Commerce and Industry on 27 June, 2000. About 30 representative Indian companies and over 200 of European ones are expected to participate in this meeting. The Prime Minister will be accompanied by the Finance Minister, the Commerce and Industry Minister and the Minister for Information Technology.

4. Prime Minister will also pay a bilateral visit to Portugal from 28 to 29 June 2000 at the invitation of Mr. Antonio Guterres, Prime Minister of Portugal. He will hold talks with Prime Minister Guterres and President Jorge Sampaio.

5. Prime Minister’s visit to Portugal will further consolidate the friendly relations which exist between the two countries. The President had visited Portugal in September, 1998. This has been followed by the visits of Mr. Jose Lello, Portuguese Minister of State for Foreign Affairs to India in February, 2000 and of the External Affairs Minister to Portugal in March-April, 2000. The close understanding which exists between the two countries is reflected in the fact that the first-ever India-EU Summit takes place during the Portuguese Presidency of the European Union.

New Delhi
June 22, 2000
