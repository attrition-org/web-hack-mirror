Visit of the President of the United States of America

At the invitation of the President of India, President Clinton of the United States will visit India from March 20, 2000 for approximately five days. The visit of the US President is expected to pave the way for a qualitatively new and closer relationship between the two democracies.

New Delhi
February 1, 2000

At the invitation of President, Shri K.R. Narayanan, the Indonesian President, H.E. Mr. Abdurrahman Wahid, will be paying a State visit to India on February 8 and 9, 2000. During his visit, he will be meeting the Vice-President Shri Krishan Kant, the Prime Minister Shri Atal Bihari Vajpayee, the Speaker of the Lok Sabha Shri G.M.C. Balayogi, the External Affairs Minister Shri Jaswant Singh, the Minister of Commerce and Industry Shri Murasoli Maran, the Minister of Human Resource Development Dr. Murli Manohar Joshi, the Leader of the Opposition, Smt. Sonia Gandhi and other dignitaries. 

A large delegation of 65-70 businessmen will accompany the President of Indonesia. An interactive business session and Luncheon Meeting has been arranged by the Federation of Indian Chambers of Commerce and Industry (FICCI).

Government of India attaches great importance to this visit, in the context of recent changes in Indonesia including a peaceful democratic election, the first in over 30 years, in Indonesia. The visit will provide an opportunity to renew and revitalise our relations with the 4th largest country in the world with India shares a maritime boundary.

New Delhi
February 1, 2000
