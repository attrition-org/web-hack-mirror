India-European Union Troika Senior Officials’ Meeting 

On May 15, 2000, the India-EU Troika Senior Officials’Meeting was held in New Delhi. Ambassador Joao Salgueiro, Director General for External Policy, Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Portugal, and Mr. Lalit Mansingh, Foreign Secretary led the EU and Indian sides at this meeting. The EU side comprised representatives of the EU Presidency (currently Portugal), European Commission and the EU Council secretariat. 

The main focus of this meeting was on finalising the agenda and joint declaration ‘Prospects for India-EU relations in the 21st century’ for the forthcoming India-EU Summit, which will be held in Lisbon on June 28, 2000. Prime Minister will lead the
Indian delegation to the Summit. The EU side will be represented by Mr. Antonio Guterres, Prime Minister of Portugal (as President of the Council of the EU), Mr. Romano Prodi, EC President, Mr. Javier Solana, EU High Representative for
Common Foreign and Security Policy and Mr. Chris Patten, EC External Relations Commissioner.  Prime Minister will also pay a bilateral visit to Portugal on June 29, 2000 after the India-EU Summit concludes. 

Other issues discussed at the meeting between Mr. Lalit Mansingh and Ambassador Joao Salgueiro include India-EU relations, including political exchanges, civil society dialogue, economic, commercial & technological co-operation, co-operation in
specific areas such as environment, human rights, international terrorism, drug trafficking and international security issues. 

An India-EU Business Meeting is being organised on June 27 by the Confederation of Indian Industry, at which around 30 Indian companies and 150 European ones are likely to participate. This Business Meeting would include sectoral presentations
on areas of Indian expertise, such as IT, pharmaceuticals, biotechnology and financial services. 
  
New Delhi, 
May 15, 2000
