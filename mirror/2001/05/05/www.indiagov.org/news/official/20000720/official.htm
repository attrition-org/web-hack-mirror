Official Visit to India by Foreign Minister of Korea
July 30 – 31, 2000 

Mr. Lee Joung Binn, Minister of Foreign Affairs and Trade of Republic of Korea (ROK) will pay an official visit to India on July 30 – 31, 2000 at the invitation of External Affairs Minister, Shri Jaswant Singh.

During the visit, he will hold talks with the External Affairs Minister Shri Jaswant Singh.  The agenda will include bilateral, regional and global issues of mutual interest.

The Foreign Minister of Republic of Korea is expected to call on President Shri K.R. Narayanan and Prime Minister Shri Atal Bihari Vajpayee.

India has warm and friendly relations with the Republic of Korea. The shared values of democracy and human freedoms offer a sound basis for a cooperative relationship between India and Republic of Korea in the 21st century.

In recent years, the two sides have exchanged several high level visits.  In February 1996, the then President Kim Young Sam visited India at the invitation of the then President Shri Shankar Dayal Sharma.  The then ROK Prime Minister Kim Jong Pil visited India in February 1999 to inaugurate the India Engineering Trade Fair organised by CII, in which ROK was the partner country.  ROK Minister of Trade Han Duck Soo visited India in May 2000 for the Third India-ROK Joint Trade
Committee meeting.

From the Indian side, the most recent visit was that of the then Minister of State for External Affairs Smt. Vasundhara Raje in January 1999.

The visit of Foreign Minister Lee will help in further consolidating and strengthening our bilateral relations.

New Delhi
July 20, 2000
