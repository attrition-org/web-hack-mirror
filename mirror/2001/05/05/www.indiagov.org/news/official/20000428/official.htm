At the invitation of the Principal Secretary to Prime Minister and National Security Adviser, Shri Brajesh Mishra, the Secretary of the Security Council of the Russian Federation, His Excellency Mr. Sergei Ivanov visited New Delhi from 26-28 April 2000. Mr. Ivanov was accompanied by a composite high level delegation which included His Excellency Mr. Illya Klebanov, Deputy Prime, Minister of the Russian Federation, His Excellency Mr. Mikhail Fraikov, Minister of Trade of the Russian Federation, and other senior officials.

2. During his visit, Mr. Ivanov called on the Prime Minister, the External Affairs Minister and the Raksha Mantri. He held detailed discussions on bilateral, regional and international issues with the Principal Secretary to PM and National Security Adviser.

3. During his call on PM, Mr. Ivanov handed over a letter from the Russian President Putin. The letter refers to the importance of Indo-Russia relations for stability and security in Asia and the whole world. It also calls for cooperation in combating international terrorism, drug trafficking and religious and political extremism. Mr. Ivanov also conveyed the proposal for His Excellency Mr. Vladimir Putin, President of the Russian Federation to pay a State Visit to India. PM mentioned that Mr Putin will be an honoured guest and that he would be accorded a warm welcome. The visit is expected to take place within the first ten days of October 2000.

4. A Protocol on Cooperation between the National security Council of India and the Security Council of the Russian Federation was signed during Ivanov's visit. This Protocol formalises institutionalised cooperation between the National Security Councils of the two countries.

5. The Protocol recognises the importance of cooperation in 

(a) strengthening the national security of the two countries through political means; 
(b) improvement and development of ways and means of cooperation in international, economic, defence, information, ecological and other fields of security;
(c) enhancing mutual cooperation in areas relating to suppression of crime, international terrorism, religious and political extremism, trafficking of arms, drug trafficking and separatism which undermines national sovereignty and territorial integrity; and 
(d) existing areas and allowing for development of new areas of interaction. For achieving these objectives, the National Security Councils of the two countries would form one or more Joint Coordination Groups. The NSA of India and the Secretary, Security Council of the Russian Federation will remain in regular and frequent contact with each other.
 
April 28, 2000
New Delhi
