Visit of HRH Princess Masna, Ambassador at large, Brunei Ministry of foreign Affairs to India from  April  26-28 2000

On the invitation of Minister of State for External Affairs, Her Royal Highness Princess Hajah Masna, Ambassador-at-large in Brunei's Ministry of Foreign Affairs, is visiting India from April 26-28, 2000. She is accompanied by her husband, 12 year old son and the Permanent secretary in the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, H.E. Dato Paduka Haji  Osman. The visit is part of the ongoing high level exchange of visits between leaders of the two countries and is expected to promote better understanding and cooperation between us. HRH Princess Masna will hold talks with Shri Ajit Kumar Panja, Minister of State for External Affairs, in which bilateral relations in all its aspects will be reviewed, including regional and international matters of mutual interest. 

Princess Masna will also call  on Vice President and Minister of External Affairs. 

A visit to Agra is planned during her stay in India.

April 27, 2000
New Delhi

The Twelfth Session of the India-China Joint Working Group on the Boundary Question (JWG-XII) will be held in New Delhi on April 28-29, 2000. The Indian delegation will be led by Foreign Secretary Shri Lalit Mansingh. The Chinese delegation will   be led by W Yang Wenchang, Vice Foreign Minister of the People's Republic of China. 

JWG-XI took place on April 26-27, 1999 in Beijing, 

EAM visited China in June, 1999. During that visit, there was agreement on promoting CBMs,  having a Security Dialogue, commemorating the 50th anniversary of establishment of diplomatic relations (April 1, 2000) in a befitting manner and expanding trade and economic relations.

JWG-XII takes Place against the backdrop of the first ever India- China Security Dialogue held in Beijing on March 6-7 2000, the sixth Meeting of the India-China Joint Economic group in Beijing on February and the commemoration of the 50 th anniversary by both sides through exchange of messages, organizing of receptions by the two Foreign Ministers and the respective Embassies in the two capitals, bringing out of special First Day Covers,    and Preparations for President Shri K.R. Narayanan's State visit to China in May 2000. 

Besides discussing boundary related issues, the two sides will review 
recent development in bilateral relations and exchange views on regional and international issues.

April 27, 2000
New Delhi
