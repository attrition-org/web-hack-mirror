The Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Republic of Maldives, Mr. Fathulla Jameel paid an official visit to India from March 3-8, 2000.

During his visit, Mr. Jameel called on the President, the Prime Minister and the Finance Minister and held official discussions with the Minister of External Affairs. The talks covered bilateral and regional matters.

The 4th session of the India-Maldives Joint Commission for Economic and Technical Cooperation took place on March 6 & 7, 2000 under the chairmanship of the respective Foreign Ministers. The meeting reviewed the progress of implementation of the decisions of the 3rd meeting of the Joint Commission in 1995 and identified further steps and proposals to strengthen the process of bilateral economic and technical cooperation.

The Maldives side noted with appreciation that the Indira Gandhi Memorial Hospital at Male has been functioning well and is a flagship project which symbolizes the firm foundation of the close and cooperative relations between India and the Maldives. The Maldives has requested India to facilitate the further deputation of Indian experts to assist in the functioning of the Hospital. The Maldivian side also expressed appreciation for Indian assistance in the setting up of the Maldives Institute of Technical Education, which provides vocational training to young Maldivians.

The area of human resource development is a priority for the Maldives. In this connection, it was agreed that steps would be taken to put up an institute of Hotel and Catering Services in Male, with Indian assistance.

The Maldives side provided the Indian side details about plans to develop Infrastructural facilities and community development programmes in outlying atolls and sought India's collaboration in relevant areas of island development. The Indian side conveyed its willingness to assist.

The two Ministers emphasized the role of information technology in national development and agreed to devise programmes in this area.

In order to enhance existing levels of trade and investment, it was decided to set up a sub-group to identify specific measures in this regard.

The two sides felt that commercial joint ventures need to be encouraged and decided to examine the possibility of cooperation in the field of deep-sea fishing. It was also agreed to facilitate contact between the respective tourism industry associations.

During his visit, Mr. Jameel addressed a meeting of the CII and outlined the availability of investment facilities in the Maldives. The CII President, Mr. Rahul Bajaj, announced that CII would be holding a 'Made in India' exhibition in the Maldives later this year.

The visit of Mr. Jameel reaffirms the close and friendly relations between India and the Maldives and the keenness of the leadership in both countries to strengthen these ties through substantive economic cooperation.

New Delhi
March 7, 2000
