INAUGURATION OF WEBSITE ON INDIAN DIASPORA

A Website on Indian Diaspora was inaugurated by Shri Promod Mahajan, Minister for Parliamentary Affairs & Information Technology on 19th December, 2000. Dr. L.M. Singhvi, M.P, Chairman, High Level Committee on Indian Diaspora and Shri U.V. Krishnamraju, Minister of State for External Affairs were also present. The address of the website is  http://indiandiaspora.nic.in

2. The website has been created in order to invite comments suggestions
 from general public and NRIs/PIOs on different aspects for consideration by the High Level Committee on Indian Diaspora. In due course the website will also give relevant and useful information to NRIs/PIOs.

3. The Committee was constituted by Government of India with effect
 From September 1, 2000 and is headed by Dr. L.M. Singhvi, M.P and formerly High Commissioner of India to UK, with the rank of Cabinet Minister. The other members of the Committee are Shri R.L. Bhatia, MP and formerly Minister of State for External Affairs, Shri J.R. Hiremath, IFS(Retd) and Shri Baleshwar Agrawal, Secretary General, Antar-Rashtria Sahyog Parishad, New Delhi. Shri J.C Sharma, Additional Secretary(NRI&PV), Ministry of External Affairs is the Member Secretary of the Committee.

4. The terms of reference of the Committee are as follows:-

(a) Review the status of Persons of Indian origin (PIOs) and Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) in the context of constitutional provisions, laws and rules applicable to them both in India and the countries of their residence;

(b) Study the characteristics, aspirations, attitudes, requirements, strengths and weakness of Indian Diaspora and their  expectations from India;

(c) Study the role that the PIOs and NRIs may play in the economic and social and technological development of India;

(d) Examine the current regime that governs the travel and stay of PIOs in India and investments by PIOs in India, and recommend measures to resolve the problems faced by NRIs in these areas;

(e)  Recommend a broad but flexible policy framework and country specific plans for forging a mutually beneficial relationship with the region or PIOs and NRIs, and for facilitating their interaction with India and their participation in India's economic development.

5. The Government has all along attached considerable importance to the
 India diaspora and their relations with the motherland. The concerns, need and problems of the overseas Indian communities and their potential for contributing to India's socio-economic and technological transformation and development are matters of priority concern. Encouragement of cultural and emotional bonds and strengthening of the same is a shared objective of the overseas Indian community and the Indian Government. The Committee is expected to look into the entire range of issues concerning overseas Indian community and view to suggesting policy and organizational frameworks in this area.
 

New Delhi
December 19, 2000


At the invitation of the External Affairs Minister, Foreign Minister of Jordan, Mr. Abdel Elah M. Khatib, visited India from 18 – 19 December, 2000.  During his stay in India, he called on the Vice President and the Prime Minister.  Discussions between Foreign Minister Khatib and the External Affairs Minister were wide-ranging and there was close identity of views on important issues like Middle East Peace Process, Iraq, etc.  Both the Ministers also discussed ways and means to further consolidate and diversify bilateral relations.  During the visit, a Memorandum of Understanding on Foreign Office Consultations was signed.
 
 
 

New Delhi
December 19, 2000
