Joint Press Statement 
on the conclusion of the official visit to India 
by H. E . Mr. Irakli Menagarishvili, Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia

At the Invitation of the External Affairs Minister of India, H.E. Mr. Jaswant Singh, the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, H.E. Mr. Irakli Menagarishvili paid an official visit to India from May 9-12, 2000. 

2. The Foreign Minister of Georgia was received by the Vice President of India, H.E. Mr. Krishan Kant and the Speaker of the Lok Sabha. H.E. Mr. G.M.C. Balayogi. Detailed discussions were held between the two Foreign Ministers on bilateral, regional and international issues of mutual interest and concern. The meetings were held in an atmosphere of mutual trust and friendship.

3. During the meetings, the Sides recalled that Indo-Georgian relations were close and friendly. They reiterated that considerable opportunities exist to further expand and deepen their bilateral cooperation. In this context, the Sides noted that the necessary politico-legal basis should be worked out at the earliest to enable their cooperation to be further consolidated in the long term to mutual benefit.

4. During the course of the discussions on regional and international issues, the Ministers briefed each other on the current situation in South Asia and the Caucasus. They recalled the new challenges facing the international community from separatism, terrorism, religious extremism, drugs trafficking and arms trade. The Sides called for joint effort by the International community to combat these new challenges. They expressed support for each other's national sovereignty and territorial integrity as established by law and as enshrined in their respective Constitutions. The Sides also reaffirmed their determination to cooperate bilaterally and in international fora to address the problem of terrorism. In this context, Georgia supported the proposal by India for adoption of the Comprehensive Convention on International Terrorism and felt that it deserved the support of the international community.

5. The Sides underlined the need for expediting the reform of the UN and its Security Council. In this context the Georgian Side expressed its support for India’s aspiration to occupy her rightful place in the reformed Security Council.

6. The Indian Side reiterated the invitation for a visit to India by the President of Georgia, H.E. Mr. Eduard Shevardnadze, on a mutually convenient date.

The visit would provide a major impetus to the development of bilateral relations.

7. A Protocol on Consultation between the Ministry of External Affairs of the Republic of India and the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia was signed by the two ministers after their talks. This would facilitate the Sides to hold regular consultations between their ministries of Foreign Affairs and assist in the tasks of expansion of their relations in the bilateral sphere and as also appreciate their views on regional and international issues.

8. H.E. Mr Irakli Menagarishvili thanked Mr Jaswant Singh for the warm hospitality accorded to him and to his delegation members. He invited the External Affairs Minister of India to pay an official visit to Georgia. The invitation was accepted with great pleasure. Dates for the visit would be decided through normal diplomatic channels.

New Delhi
May 12, 2000
