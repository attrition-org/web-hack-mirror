Shri Ajit Kumar Panja, Minister of State for External Affairs, will be paying official visit to Argentina from August 27-30 and Trinidad & Tobago from August 31-September 3, 2000.  He will be accompanied by senior officials, in the Ministry of External Affairs.

The visits to Argentina and Trinidad & Tobago will be the first to be undertaken by Shri Panja since assumption of charge as Minister of State for External Affairs.

With a land mass almost equal to India, Argentina is the third largest economy in Latin America (after Brazil and Mexico) and is by far the largest economic partner of India in the region.  Bilateral trade between the two countries in 1999-2000 reached US$ 565 million, largely in favour of Argentina.  However, over the years India has been able to greatly diversify its range of exports which currently include motor vehicles, pharmaceuticals, garments, machine tools, etc.  A number of
Argentine companies are also looking into the possibility of investing in India’s infrastructure development, particularly the energy sector. 

During his visit Shri Panja will be reviewing the entire gamut of bilateral and multilateral relations with the Argentine Foreign Minister, Mr. Adalberto Rodriguez Giavarini.  He would be also having discussions with the Argentine Health Minister, Dr. Hector Jose Lombardo, regarding the problem of registration of Indian pharmaceutical companies in Argentina.  The visit will be utilized by MOS (EA) to explain to the new socialist government of President Fernando De La Rua, which came into power in Argentina in December last year, India’s position on numerous issues of common interest and concern including the surge of cross-border terrorism sponsored by Pakistan.  During the visit an agreement on cooperation between the Foreign Service Institutes of India and Argentina will also be signed.

India’s ties with Trinidad & Tobago go back to the mid-nineteenth century, when the first indentured labourers from India landed in that country and started working on sugar plantations.  Currently nearly 42% of Trinidad & Tobago’s 1.3 million strong population is of Indian origin.  The two countries have traditionally enjoyed excellent bilateral relations.  Prime Minister Basdeo Panday was the chief guest in the Republic Day Function in India in 1997.  Prime Minister Atal Bihari Vajpayee paid a return visit in February 1999 and was warmly received.  During these two visits the two countries signed a large number of bilateral cooperation agreements which provide the necessary framework for cooperation.

During his visit to Trinidad & Tobago Shri Panja will be meeting President Arthur Robinson, Prime Minister Basdeo Panday, Foreign Minister Ralph Maraj and several other dignitaries.  He will be also addressing the Institution of International Relations of the University of West Indies.

New Delhi
August 25, 2000
