The Central Passport Organisation (CPO) of the Ministry of External Affairs has launched an ambitious programme of comprehensive computerisation of all the 28 Passport Offices.

2. A giant step towards modernisation of India’s passport would be taken on 13 July 2000 when Shri Ajit Kumar Panja, Minister of State, would release the first Machine-Written-Machine-Readable Passports. These state-of-the-art passports would have scanned image and photograph of the passport holder and the present passport with pasted photograph would be gradually phased out.

3. The Machine-Readable Zone in the Passport would have the personal particulars of the passport holder in alpha numeric form and this would make entry and exit at the Immigration Counters quicker across the globe.

Computerisation of Passport Offices

4. 20 of the 28 Passport Offices in the country have already been computerised by the National Informatics Centre (NIC).  All the eight remaining Passport Offices would be computerised in the next twelve months.

5. The Central Passport Organisation (CPO) has a website:http://passport.nic.in which was launched on 14th September 1999.  The other Passport Offices would also soon have their own websites where status of the passport application can be checked. 

6. To further streamline the processing procedures at the Passport Offices, a comprehensive computerisation programme has been approved at the estimated cost of around Rs.30 crores.  The project management has been entrusted to NIC.

7. The proposed computerisation of Passport Offices would facilitate the following tasks: 

i. An online status Index checking through creation of central index by
networking all the Passport Offices;
ii. Facility to math photograph for suspect cases;
iii. Efficient planning, monitoring and control of passport issuing activity;
iv. Future provision to connect POs with Indian Missions abroad to respond to queries instantaneously.

8. This comprehensive computerisation is to be implemented in the Passport Offices with seamless integration with the working of the Offices. The project would, therefore, be implemented in stages which would have the following components:

i. Transfer of historical data of approx. 1.6 crore passports from Index Cards to CD-ROMs;
ii. Machine writing of passports with a scanned photograph and signature of the applicant;
iii. Computer connectivity of all 28 Passport-Offices;

Work on the first two components has already begun in RPO, Delhi. Once the software, hardware and procedures are tested satisfactorily, these would be gradually replicated in other Passport Offices starting with Bangalore.

Vision

9. The goal is to set up a modern, transparent and efficient passport issuing service of which the general public can be genuinely satisfied and illegalities and irregularities avoided.

The Government of India greets, with a sense of relief, the decision of Fiji’s Great Council of Chiefs to release Prime Minister Mahendra Chaudhry and his Cabinet colleagues from detention in Fiji’s Parliament building where they have been held hostage since May 19.  We expect that this first step will lead to the restoration of a democratic and constitutional government within the framework of Fiji’s Constitution of 1997.  It is also our expectation that the Commonwealth’s principles outlined under the Harare Declaration and the Millbrook Action Programme will continue to be adhered to.

New Delhi
13th July, 2000.
