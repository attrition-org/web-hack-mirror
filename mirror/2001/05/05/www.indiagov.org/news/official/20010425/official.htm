The First Bangladesh, India, Myanmar, Sri Lanka and Thailand –Economic Cooperation (BIMST-EC) Transport and Communication Experts’ Meeting was held in New Delhi on 23-24 April, 2001.  Experts in the field of Transport and Communication from these five countries attended the meeting.  The meeting was inaugurated by Foreign Secretary Smt. Chokila Iyer who stressed India’s firm commitment to BIMST-EC objectives and emphasized that the success of its cooperation in all fields is tied to the availability of a sound infrastructure in transport and communications.  She underscored the importance of multiple modes, network continuity, harmonious standards and procedures and environmental friendliness, in this context.  Secretary (Shipping) Shri M.Pinto who also spoke at the inauguration, focussed on the priority to inland water transport for fostering transport linkages.  The concept paper prepared by lead country India was commended by the participants for identifying areas of common concern and pragmatic strategies.  BIMST-EC delegates made presentations relating to the scenario of transport and communications in their countries.  The following priority areas were identified for fostering cooperation:

Transport and cross-border facilitation:  Constraints and solutions:  Streamlining of documentation and banking procedures, harmonisation of tariffs and customs, creating of warehousing facilities and evolving streamlined procedures for facilitating cross-border movements.

Multimodal transport logistics:  BIMST-EC should share their experiences in institutional and human resource development in this area.  Compatible technologies should be adopted and legislative and regulatory regimes should be facilitated.

Infrastructure development:  Resource mobilisation & institutional issues:  Existing operation routes should be optimally utilised.  Mismatches in vehicles due to traction and axle loads should be removed.  Chief Executives of Railways of BIMST-EC countries should meet, and the ALTID (Asian Land Transport Infrastructure Development) project should be implemented.

Maritime transport:  Shipping, inland water transport and ports:  Possibilities of forming joint ventures between the shipping lines of the member countries should be explored, along with cooperation in the HRD of seafaring manpower.

Human Resource Development:  Each country will prepare a status paper of the training facilities in their country.  India offered to organise training programmes, workshops and seminars for highway engineers of BIMST-EC countries under the aegis of the National Institute for Highway Engineers, Delhi.

Communication Linkages and Networking:  Each country will identify national focal points for establishing linkages between regional and national activities. 

On the basis of the discussions, the participants have drawn up agreed conclusions and a report which spells out specific areas for future action to strengthen cooperation within BIMST-EC in transport and communications.
 

New Delhi
April 25, 2001.
