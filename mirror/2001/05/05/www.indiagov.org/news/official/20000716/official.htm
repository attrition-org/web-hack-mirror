In a brilliantly planned and executed military operation, nicknamed Operation "Khukri", during the last 36 hours, 221 UNAMSIL troops (mainly from India) and 11 UNAMSIL Military Observers from various other countries, who were in stand off at Kailahun in Sierra Leone for more than 10 weeks (since 02 May), were extricated from Kailahun to Daru. 

2. Extensive diplomatic efforts by the UN and India, and political initiatives at the highest level to persuade the RUF to lift the siege having been unsuccessful and peaceful means having been exhausted, the use of force to secure the freedom of movement of UNAMSIL Peacekeepers and Observers had become unavoidable. The 'go ahead' for the military option was given by the UN Secretary General Mr. Kofi Annan and the UN Department of Peacekeeping Operations.

3. The Military Operations supervised personally by Maj. Gen. V.K. Jetley, UYSM from Daru commenced at 0600 hours on 15 July 2000, for which the preparations and build up had been completed earlier.

4. The operation was a complex one which involved:

a) Securing a link-up between our columns from Daru and Kailahun at a mid-way place in area Pendembu.
b) The total distance is approximately 72 kms. and in this area, RUF had over 1000 rebels and in the area of Pendembu, was their Brigade Headquarters.
5. The operation was planned in two phases: 
a) Phase 1 (13-14 July) Preparatory Phase
i) To establish an Air and logistics base at Kenema.
ii) Build up reserves i.e., 18 Grenadiers, at Daru.
b) Phase 2 (15-16 July) Execution of Plan
i) Commenced 0600 h (Sierra Leone time) on 15 July 
ii) Involved dropping heliborne troops at three places to get control of Road Daru-Kailahun. These places are - Kenema, Geihun and Three Bridges Area. Approximately 300 troops were helidropped by our Air Force helicopters. The returning helicopters also picked up Military Observers and certain troops in their flights.
iii) Also involved our Attack Helicopters engaging in RUF positions at Pendembu-Kailahun-Manowa Ferry besides some other places
iv) Composite Columns of Mechanised Vehicles (BMPs) and Infantry, commenced simultaneous move from both directions of Kailahun and Daru at the same time.
v) 'RUF' put up resistance as best as they could but because of three factors, operations proceeded smoothly and rapidly:
aa) Surprise and speed
ab) Good orchestration of operations
ac) Extensive air cover by our Air Force helicopters
vi) By evening 4pm, Pendembu was attacked by our troops from the South and captured by 6pm and link-up effected.
6. There was a small supporting operation by a Ghana Company along with our BMPs, between Kenema and Daru at 'Sregbara'. It cleared one road block.

7. It is assessed that by the evening, our troops will be safely back at Daru. We had two cases of shrapnel injuries to the commando, who were immediately evacuated and provided medical assistance. They are fine.

8. The Force Commander, Maj. Gen. Vijay Jetley, had located himself at Daru for the operations and repeatedly flew over the operational area, to monitor the progress of the operations.

9. The UNAMSIL operation was basically carried out by the Indian troops who were:

Indian Troops

i) 18 Grenadiers and 5/8 GR, approximately 1100 personnel
ii) Special Forces (Para Commandos) - one team
iii) Mechanised Infantry elements
iv) Air Force - 3x Attack Helicopters, 3x MI-8 and 4x Cheetah Helicopters
Other UN Troops
i) Nigeria - 2x Companies - kept as reserves at Daru
ii) Ghana - 1x Company
iii) UK - 2x Chinook Helicopters (did only one sortie)
iv) Sierra Leone Government - MI-26 helicopters (flew up to Daru only)
11. In this operation, RUF have taken heavy losses and own forces have recovered one MMG, eight LMGs, seven rocket launchers and one truck load of ammunition abandoned by the RUF at Pendembu. In addition, two of our one Ton vehicles, earlier taken away by RUF have also been recovered. As part of the effective Air operations, own Attack Helicopters destroyed three RUF vehicles carrying their reinforcements at Manowa Ferry.

12. These operations prove the mettle of the Indian soldiers, who have done the country proud!

New Delhi
July 16, 2000
