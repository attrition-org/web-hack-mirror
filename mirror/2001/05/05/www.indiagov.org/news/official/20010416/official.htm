Visit of H.E. Mr. Jens Stoltenberg, Prime Minister of the Kingdom of Norway (19-23 April, 2001)

H.E. Mr. Jens Stoltenberg, Prime Minister of the Kingdom of Norway will be visiting India from 19th to 23rd April, 2001. The Norwegian Prime Minister will be accompanied by his wife Mrs. Ingrid Schulerud and a high level official delegation. He will also be accompanied by a large business delegation.

During his visit, the Norwegian Prime Minister would meet Rashtrapatiji, Vice President and Prime Minister. He will inaugurate the India-Norway Power Meeting, a Seminar on Peace Keeping and the Norwegian Film Festival in New Delhi. The visiting dignitary will also visit Bangalore for India-Norway IT Seminar in addition to visiting the Software Technology park of India and Infosys. An Agreement between Indian Institute of Management, Bangalore and the University of Oslo will be signed in his presence.

The Norwegian PM will in addition to Delhi and Bangalore, visit the historic cities of Agra and Fatehpur Sikri.

New Delhi
April 16, 2001 

The Ministry of External Affairs along with the Indian Institute of Public Adminstration (IIPA), New Delhi is organising a training programme from 16 to 20 April, 2001 for the officers of the Central Passport Organisation (CPO), who manned the 28 passport offices all over the country. The training programme is a major institutionalised step towards human resource development. The title of the programme is "Managing Orgainsational effectiveness in regional passport offices".

2. The training programme will be inaugurated by the Minister of State for External Affairs, Shri U.V. Krishnam Raju, at 1000 hrs on April 16, 2001.

3. The delivery of efficient passport services is the prime objective of the Ministry and the Central Passport Organisation. A series of initiatives have been undertaken to modernise the rendering of passport services in the country and to make this process user-friendly. These include extensive computerisation, machine writing of passports and decentralisation of the acceptance of passport application forms to bring the service closer to the door steps of the applicant.

4. In any service oriented organisation the human element is of paramount importance. The Central Passport Organisation will be assessed and judged by the public opinion on the basis of its efficacy, promptness and its ability to deal with people in a courteous manner. Focussed attention is, therefore, being given to human resource development and training for officials and staff of the Central Passport Organisation.

5. This 5-day programme, which is being inaugurated on 16 April, 2001 focuses on motivation, time management, behavioural aspects, public relations etc. It would give the passport personnel an opportunity of appreciating new methods of job design and provide ideas for improving productivity. It would also strengthen appreciation, among the personnel, of the technology of passport processing and other recent innovations made in the central Passport Organisation.

6. This training programme has been possible as a result of the partnership established between the Ministry of External Affairs and the Indian Institute of Public Administration, New Delhi. The vision is to ensure that any visitor to the passport office returns satisfied and the CPO personnel give service with a smile.
