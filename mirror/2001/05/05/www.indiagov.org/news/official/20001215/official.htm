The first meeting of the BIMST-EC “Expert Group on Technology” is being held in New Delhi on 18 – 19 December 2000.

BIMST-EC is a regional economic grouping consisting of Bangladesh, India, Myanmar, Sri Lanka and Thailand formed in June 1997.

India took over the Chairmanship of BIMST-EC from Bangladesh this year at the third Ministerial meeting held in New Delhi in July 2000.

 “Technology” is one of the six sectors prioritized for cooperation by the BIMST-EC countries.  The other five sectors are Trade & Investment, Transportation & Communication, Energy, Tourism and Fisheries.  India is the lead country for two of these sectors viz., “Technology” and “Transport and Communication”.

The meeting of the Expert Group will commence by an address from Hon’ble Bachi Singh Rawat, Minister of State for Science and Technology, as well as welcome remarks from Shri S.T. Devare, Secretary (ER) in the Ministry of External Affairs and Prof. V.S. Ramamurthy, Secretary, Department of Science and Technology.

The Group consisting of experts from member countries will finalise an Action Plan for the “Technology Sector”.

The technology cooperation in BIMST-EC aims at chartering a new and environmentally safe path of sustainable economic development through effective use of science and technology.

Joint Press Statement

Mr. C.M. Shafi Sami, Foreign Secretary of Bangladesh, visited New Delhi from December 13-14, 2000 for Foreign Office consultations between India and Bangladesh.  He was assisted by the High Commissioner of Bangladesh to India Mr. M. Faruque Mohammed and a delegation consisting of representatives from the Ministries of Foreign Affairs, Home Affairs, Commerce, Communications, Water Resources, Land Records & Survey and the National Board of Revenue, Government of Bangladesh.  The Indian delegation was led by the Foreign Secretary, Shri Lalit Mansingh and included Shri M.L. Tripathi, High Commissioner of India to Bangladesh and representatives of the Ministries of External Affairs, Home Affairs, Shipping, Water Resources, Finance (Dept. of Revenue), Commerce & Industry, Roads & Highways, Railways, Survey of India and the State Governments of Assam, Meghalya, Mizoram, Tripura and West Bengal. 

 During his stay in Delhi, the Foreign Secretary of Bangladesh called on Shri Jaswant Singh, Minister of External Affairs, and Shri Ajit Kumar Panja, Minister of State for External Affairs and discussed matters of mutual interest.  Both Ministers reaffirmed India’s firm commitment to the further strengthening of the friendly relations between the two countries.

 The Foreign Secretaries of India and Bangladesh held extensive discussions on matters covering the entire range of the multi-faceted relations between India and Bangladesh.  They also exchanged views on regional and international developments.

 In terms of the decision taken by the Prime Ministers of the two countries in Dhaka in June 1999, they reviewed the measures for restoration of multi-modal communication links between the two countries.  The sides noted with satisfaction the smooth operation of the Calcutta-Dhaka bus service and agreed to expedite the early introduction of a bus service between Agartala and Dhaka.  They welcomed the restoration of the Petrapole-Benapole rail link.

The two sides reviewed ways and means of further consolidating and enhancing mutually beneficial trade and economic cooperation, promotion of cooperation in the areas of water resources, science & technology, the liberalisation of visa arrangements, cultural exchanges, and shared concerns about the growth of terrorist activities in the region.  They agreed on the need to convene the Joint Economic Commission in Dhaka at an early date.

Detailed discussions were held on pending matters relating to the India-Bangladesh Land Boundary Agreement of 1974.  Both sides agreed to cooperate with a view to achieving early resolution of all outstanding boundary-related matters.  For this purpose, the Foreign Secretaries decided to set up Working Groups to deal with the completion of demarcation, and the exchange of enclaves and territories in adverse possession.  The Working Groups would systematically address these matters and report to the Foreign Secretaries on a regular basis.  Both sides also discussed the possibility of free and unfettered movement in the Tin Bigha Corridor.

Both sides recalled with great appreciation the acts of good-neighbourliness by the people and Governments of India and Bangladesh in providing assistance to nationals of either country during the recent floods.  They agreed on the need to enhance cooperation in strengthening early flood warning systems.

The two sides noted with great satisfaction the successful implementation of the Treaty on the sharing of the Ganga waters at Farakka.  They noted that the Joint Rivers Commission would be meeting in Dhaka in January 2001.

The discussions also covered proposed high level visits from either country and the schedule for upcoming meetings relating to border management, border trade, customs, transport operators, inland water and trade.

The talks took place in an atmosphere of great cordiality, reflective of the warm and friendly relations between the two countries.

New Delhi
December 15, 2000

H.E. Mr. Lakshman Kadirgamar, Minister of Foreign Affairs of Sri Lanka, visited India and held wide-ranging talks with the Minister of External Affairs on December 15, 2000.  The two Ministers discussed areas of mutual interest including regional cooperation. 

The two Ministers noted that the Senior Officials had drawn up a detailed programme of SAARC, Technical Committee meetings to be held over the next three months.  They agreed that these meetings should continue.  On the strength of the outcome of their work, which would provide an agenda, the possibility of a Standing Committee meeting could be considered.  

The two Ministers discussed the problems faced by fishermen from both countries.  They highlighted the humanitarian aspect of the problem faced by poor people for whom fishing is the only means of livelihood.  They agreed that the issue should be dealt with in a spirit of compassion and understanding.  They further agreed that all pending cases of fishermen in custody should be expedited.  Sri Lanka would be sending a team to India in order to study the legal procedures involved.  Minister of External Affairs said that the team would receive full cooperation in India.  

Minister of External Affairs reiterated India’s concern at firing on Indian fishermen and emphasized the need to avoid such incidents.  

Discussions between the two Foreign Ministers also touched on progress in the implementation of the India – Sri Lanka Free Trade Agreement and in the activities of the India – Sri Lanka Foundation.  

They expressed satisfaction at the excellent state of bilateral relations between India and Sri Lanka.   India supports the unity, sovereignty and territorial integrity of Sri Lanka and a negotiated political settlement of the conflict there for a lasting peace which would meet the aspirations of all elements of Sri Lankan society.
