A high-level delegation from Sri Lanka, led by Mr. Lakshman Jayakody, Minister of Cultural and Religious Affairs, met the Union Minister for Agriculture, Shri Nitish Kumar today. The two Ministers and their delegations held talks covering issues relating to fishermen and fishing boats of either country in the custody of the other.  Mr. Jayakody was accompanied by Mr.M.T.K. Nagodawithana, Acting Director General, Sri Lanka Department of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources.

Mr. Jayakody had earlier come to Delhi in July 2000 as a Special Envoy of the President of Sri Lanka. 

The talks focussed on the problems faced by bona fide fishermen from both countries.  Mr. Jayakody highlighted the humanitarian aspect of the problem faced by poor people for whom fishing is the only means of livelihood.  It was agreed that they should be dealt with in a spirit of compassion and understanding and their release should not be delayed beyond the period required for completing necessary procedures. The Indian side appreciated the recent release of 7 Indian fishermen by Sri Lanka.  The Sri Lankan side noted with appreciated that orders had been
issued for the immediate release of some Sri Lankan fishermen in Indian custody; the Indian side agreed to review and expedite other pending cases.  The agriculture Minister conveyed India’s concern at the firing on Indian fishermen and urged that steps be taken by Sri Lanka to minimize such incidents. At the suggestion of Sri Lankan side, it was agreed to consider the setting up of a monitoring mechanism which could periodically review problems concerned with fishermen in custody in both countries. The talks took place in a spirit of cooperation, reflective of the friendly relations between the two countries.

New Delhi
September 23, 2000
