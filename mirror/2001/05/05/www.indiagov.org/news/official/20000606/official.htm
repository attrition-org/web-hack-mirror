Vice President to Visit
Bulgaria, Slovakia and Egypt

Shri Krishan Kant Vice President of India will pay a State visit to the Republic of Bulgaria, Slovak Republic and Arab Republic of Egypt from June 8th  to June
21st, 2000. The Vice President will be accompanied by Smt. Suman Krishan Kant and high level official delegation including Members of Parliament and senior
officials    

Bulgaria
The Vice President will be visiting the Republic of Bulgaria from 9th to 11th  June, at the invitation  Mr. Yordan Sokolov, the Chairman of National Assembly.
During his stay he will be holding  wide-ranging  talks with H.E. Mr. Petar Stoyanov, President of the Republic of Bulgaria, Mr.Yordan Sokolov, the Chairman of
the National Assembly, H.E. Mr. Ivan Kostov, Prime Minister, and H.E. Mr. Petar Jotev, Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Economy, on important global
and regional issues and ways and means to expand our mutually beneficial interaction. This contact with the top leadership of the Republic of Bulgaria is expected
to  give a major impetus to the development and consolidation of our mutually beneficial bilateral relations in the new millennium. 

While in Bulgaria the Vice President will visit  places  of historical and cultural importance. He will visit the Monument of unknown soldier, Ancient Plovdiv, St. Cyril & Methodos Modern  Foreign Art Gallery and St. Alexander Nevski Cathedral. The Vice President will hold interaction with Prominent Indologists, teachers and students at the Sofia University. 

Slovakia

Shri Krishan Kant will  pay a State visit to Slovak Republic from 13th to 16th June upon an invitation by the President H.E. Mr. Rudolf  Schuster. He will hold
discussions with H.E. Mr. Rudolf Schuster,  President, H.E. Mr. Mikulas Dzurinda, Prime Minister, and H.E. Mr. Josef Migas, Chairman of National Council
(Parliament) which will provide an opportunity to review and revitalise our relations and would give a very significant momentum to all round  bilateral cooperation
not only in the economic and commercial areas but in political, cultural and scientific sectors also. 
 
During his stay Shri Krishan Kant will visit Bratislava City Centre, Bratislava and Devin Castle, Uncut Crystal Factory & Museum and Trencianske City Center &
Castle besides attending  the Indian Cultural  show at Slovensky Ludovoumelecky Kolektivvenue theatre. Shri Krishan Kant will also lay a wreath at the Memorial 
of Slovak National  uprising. 

Egypt

Shri Krishan Kant will visit Egypt from 17th to 21st June. He will represent India at the two-day summit meeting of G-15 countries, which will be inaugurated  on 19th June at Cairo. He will attend the inauguration of the G-1 5 Trade Fair on 18th June. 

The  G-15  Summit will be devoted  to North-South  and South-South dialogue, poverty issues and issues pertaining to development, democracy and security. The Vice President will also participate in a retreat  with the  Heads of States and  the Heads of Governments of G-15 countries. The Vice President  had also 
participated in the G-15 Summit meetings at Kualamapur (1997) / Cairo (1999). 

The  Summit level group for South-South consultation and cooperation known as the the G-15, came into existence during the Ninth Non-Aligned Summit in
Belgrade in September 1989. The intention was to create a functional and compact group of developing countries that could focus on South-South economic
cooperation supported by political engagement at the  the highest level. The setting up of the G-15 coincides with the beginning of what is, in retrospect, a clairvoyant
response to the challenges and opportunities that have since  been posed to developing  countries. 

The Vice President will return to Delhi on 21st June, 2000. 

New Delhi
June 06, 2000 

The Government of India is deeply disturbed about reports of a coup in the Solomon Islands yesterday and the kidnapping of the Prime Minister and Governor General by armed militants attempting to seize power by force.

We strongly condemn such illegal activity against legitimate governments anywhere, whether in the Solomon Islands or Fiji.

The Government of India calls upon the international community, and in particular the Commonwealth, to protect the fundamental political values enshrined in the Harare Declaration and the Millbrook Action Programme and to act in a concerted way to restore the legitimate government in both countries.

New Delhi
June 06, 2000
