At a function held at 1100 hrs at Hyderabad House on April 1, 2000 Minister of State for External Affairs, Shri Ajit Kumar Panja received, on behalf of the government of India a First Day Cover released by the Department of Post of India in commemoration of the 50th anniversary of the establishment of diplomatic relations between India and China. The First Day Cover was presented by Shri S. C. Dutta, Member, Postal Services Board.

During the visit of External Affairs Minister Shri Jaswant Singh to China in June 1999, the two sides had agreed to commemorate the 50th anniversary of the establishment of diplomatic relations in a befitting manner.

India recognized the People's Republic of China in December 1949. Diplomatic relations were established on April 1, 1950.

The Flag cover designed by the Department of Post of GOI carries the national flag day of the two countries and special Postmarks depicting historical symbols, i.e., the Dharma Chakra of India and the Great Wall of China. The First Day Cover also carries commemorative text in the Hindi, English and Chinese language. The Department of Post of India has also made a special arrangements at Dak Bhawan to help people post the First Day Cover on August 1, 2000.

The relation of a First Day Cover by the Department of Post coincides with the release of a First Day Cover by China Post in Beijing.

April 01, 2000
