Joint Press Release  
On the Visit of H.E. Lyonpo, Jigmi T. Thinly Foreign Minister of  Bhutan to India 
from April 17-26, 2000 

H.E. Lyompo Jigmy Y.  Thinley, Foreign Minister of Bhutan and Mrs. Thinley were on an official visit to India from April 17-27, 2000. The visit took place in response to the invitation extended by H.E. Shri Jaswant Singh, India's Minister for External Affairs. This was the first official visit of H.E. Lyompo Jigmy Y.  Thinley to India. The Foreign Minister's visit to India is a good-will visit with a view to further strengthen the close ties of friendship and cooperation between the two countries. 

During  his visit H.E. Lyonpo Jigmy Y. Thinley called on the President, H.E Lyompo K.R. Narayanan and the Prime Minister, H.E. Shri Atal Behari Vajpayee H.E. Lyonpo Jigmy Thinley also met with his counterpart H.E. Shri Jaswant Singh, Minister for External Affairs, H.E.Shri Ajit Panja, Minister of State for External Affairs, H.E. Shri M.M. Joshi, Minister for Human Resource, and development and other senior officials in the Government of India. Both sides reviewed the wide-ranging cooperation between the two countries and  satisfaction at the timely progress or the on going projects, particularly, in the hydel sector. The two sides also discussed other issues of mutual  interest and concern. Both sides agreed to Continue to closely consult and cooperate with each other on these issues.

The foreign Minister also visited places of historic, cultural, technological environmental interests in south India. H.E. Lyonpo Jigmy Thinley  conveyed his appreciation to the Government of India for the excellent arrangements and hospitality extended to him and members of his delegation during visit. H.E. Lyonpo Jigmy Y.Thinley also extended an invitation to H.E Shri Jaswant Singh, Minister for External Affairs to visit Bhutan in the year 2000, which was accepted.
 

During the visit two sides agreed to host a Bhutan Exhibition in India in October-November  2001. The exhibition will further strengthen the shared heritage of history, culture and geographical proximity, which have created strong ties of friendship between Bhutan and India. The proposed theme of  the exhibition will be the 'The Living Religious and Cultural Tradition of Bhutan' and will focus mainly on: 

1. Buddhism in Bhutan 
2. Buddhist Ritual performance
3. Arts,  crafts and Architecture of Bhutan 
4. Performing  Arts of Bhutan 

This will  be the  first major exhibition of Bhutan held in India after more than a decade and provide a rare opportunity to the people of India to share in the living  religious and cultural tradition of Bhutan. It will depict the  Mahayana Bhudhist heritage of Bhutan as propagated by Guru Rimpoche (the great  Indian Saint known as Padmasambhava) and other great Buddhist of Bhutan.

The special highlights of the Exhibition would be the re-creation of a typical Bhutanese Lhakhang (chapel), preparation of ritual cakes and ritual performance of using instruments, display of the thirteen aspects of Bhutanese traditional arts and crafts known as zorig chusum, demonstration of thangka painting and textile weaving, folk and mask dance performances by the artists from the Royal Academy of Performing Arts. The exhibition will be held in New Delhi and Calcutta for a period of two weeks in each location.

New Delhi
April 26, 2000
