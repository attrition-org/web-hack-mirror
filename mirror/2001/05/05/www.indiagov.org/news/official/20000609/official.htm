Kailash Manasarovar Yatra, 2000

The first batch of the Yatris of Kailash Manasarovar Yatra, 2000, which left New Delhi on June 2, is presently at Gunji. They were to cross Lipulekh Pass into Tibet on June 10 but could not proceed further from Gunji on June 8, 2000 due to continuing rains in the region. The Yatris will proceed further as soon as weather conditions permit. All the Yatris of the first batch are safe and in good health.

The second batch of the Kailash Manasarovar Yatra departed from New Delhi for Kathgodam on June 8, 2000. The batch will be reaching Bageshwar tonight, as scheduled. 

The third batch of the Kailash Manasarovar Yatra will report in New Delhi on June 13, 2000 as scheduled.

New Delhi,
June 9, 2000

Two officers, one JCO and 18 other ranks of the Indian Battalion earlier confined to a RUF brigade headquarters in Pendembu pending their re-joining their unit have last evening been made hostage and also disarmed.  Simultaneously, the RUF leadership has now made a set of demands to the UNAMSIL.  This is entirely unacceptable.  Soon after this development, the government conveyed its very serious concern to the UN Secretary General who has assured that the immediate release of these Indian troops is the highest priority for the UN as this is a challenge to the UN itself.

New Delhi,
June 9, 2000.
