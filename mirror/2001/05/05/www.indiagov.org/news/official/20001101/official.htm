Mr. Nguyen Dy Nien, will lead the Vietnamese side.  On the margins of the JCM, EAM will call on the President, the Prime Minister, the Deputy Prime Minster of Vietnam and the General Secretary of the Communist Party of Vietnam.  Meetings with some other Ministers are also planned depending on the time available.

3. The 3rd session of the India-Lao PDR Joint Commission on Trade, Economic, Scientific and Technological Cooperation, will be held on the 9th of November, 2000 at the Lao capital city of Vientiane.  H.E. Mr. Somsavat Lengsavad, Deputy Prime Minister and Foreign Minister of Laos will lead the Laotian delegation for the JCM.  During his stay in Laos, EAM will also call on the President and the Prime Minister of Laos.

4. EAM will also be representing India at the first Ganga-Mekong (Suvannaphoom) Linkage meeting which is being held in Vientiane after the Joint Commission meeting.  Foreign Ministers from Mekong basin countries, namely, Thailand, Cambodia, Vietnam, Laos and Myanmar are expected to attend this meeting which has an objective of further strengthening linkages between India and Mekong basin countries in the fields of tourism, education and culture to begin with.  It will be recalled that the project was jointly announced in Bangkok during the 33rd ASEAN-PMC meeting last July.

The G-15 Foreign Ministers met in Mexico City from 30-31 October 2000, to review implementation of the decisions taken at the Tenth Summit in Cairo in May this year, to prepare for the Eleventh Summit to be held in Jakarta, Indonesia next year and assess the response of the G-8 countries to the concerns of developing countries, as reflected in the outcome of the recent Okinawa Summit.

Mr. Ajit Kumar Panja, Minister of State for External Affairs, led the Indian delegation to the Mexico meeting, which comprised Shri S.Devare, Secretary (Economic relations), Ministry of External Affairs, H.E. G.S.Iyer, Ambassador of India to Medico, Smt. Suryakanthi Tripathi, Joint Secretary (Economic Development and Multilateral Economic Relations),Ministry of External Affairs and Dr. Mrs. Nandhini Iyer Krishna,
Director (MER), Ministry of External Affairs.

The meeting considered a wide range of issues including poverty alleviation, trade and investment, environmental issues, information and communication technologies, incentives for small and medium enterprises reform of the international financial architecture and human resource development.  Over 14 Ministers attended the meeting.  The President of Mexico, Emesto Zedillo Ponoe de Leon, who received the heads of delegations, stressed the crucial role that the G-15 could play as a
strategic forum based on South South cooperation.

Mr. Panja, set the tone for discussions on two of the crucial issues on the agenda of the Mexico meeting, notably poverty alleviation and human resource development. Acknowledging that poverty eradication was among the most daunting challenges facing the international community, Mr. Panja highlighted the range of policies and programmes India had adopted at the domestic level to combat poverty.  He stressed that sustained economic growth was crucial to combat poverty and that the efforts of
developing countries at the national level needed to be reinforced by corresponding efforts at the international level to render the multilateral trading regime and the international monetary and financial systems more responsive to the objectives of poverty and development.Mr. Panja's call to evolve a common definition of poverty, that was based not merely on monetary baselines but a more holistic approach that
addressed issues such as individual calorific intake or consumption basket, struck a responsive chord in many delegations.  Where progress was recorded in combating poverty he also stressed that it was imperative to take necessary measures to sustain such gains and prevent re-slippage below the poverty line.

On the issue of human resource development, Shri Panja pointed out that HRD was crucial in the new knowledge-based global economy and needed to be seen not just as a tool to improve literacy or vocational skills but to empower entire sections of the population.  He stressed the crucial importance of providing compulsory primary education for all.  Mr. Panja also argued for a special emphasis on the education of the girl child and called for a special fund to be set up to counter the complex socio
economic or familial obstacles to the education of the girl child.  He pointed out that the experience of developed countries revealed the catalytic impact of educating the girl child, which was reflected in higher productivity, health etc.  Mr. Panja also called on international funding agencies to support domestic outlays on education.  In recognition of the importance India attached to human resource development, Mr. Panja also pointed out that since 1964 India has been training over 1500 officials from developing countries every year in diverse fields, and further indicated support for the setting up of a G-15 Human Resources Training Programme.  He statement was widely appreciated with many delegations urging further discussions of these issues at the next G-15 Summit.

Shri Panja also stressed that if the G-15 was to be effective it would need to evolve a mechanism for follow up and implementation of its Summit decisions.  This call received wide support and acclamation. Shri Panja also urged the G-15 to concentrate on consolidating their identity and effectiveness as a strategic player on the international
arena rather than discussing further expansion of membership and replicating the attributes of other universal membership developing country groupings such as the G-77.

After two days of negotiations the G-15 meeting adopted a statement on the key issues on the agenda, which fully reflects India's interests and positions.  The meeting endorsed the establishment of a G-15 Human Resources Training Programme, the details of which will be worked out.It also agreed that the theme of the Eleventh Summit would be 'Development and the Digital Divide' and that a task force on
Information and  Communication Technologies would be established to facilitate substantive preparations on this issue.
