The Government of India is concerned about developments in Fiji following the Court of Appeal's ruling of Ist March, 2001. The Court of Appeal had ruled that the Constitution of 1997 is the supreme law of Fiji and that its Parliament had not been dissolved in May last year, but merely prorogued. In the light of this ruling the dismissal of Mr. Mahendra Chaudhry as Prime Minister without convening Parliament and giving him an opportunity to test his majority on the floor of house appears to be arbitrary. Similarly, the appointment of Mr. Laisenia Qarase as caretaker Prime Minister, when his earlier interim government had been declared by the Court of Appeal to have "failed to establish that it is the legal Government of Fiji:, reflects scant respect for the rule of law.

In principle we welcome the decision of the President of Fiji to hold early elections within the framework of the 1997 Constitution. However, we note that the time frame of August for holding the elections fails beyond the constitutional requirement of 51 days.

On the eve of the CMAG meeting in London, the Government of India would urge the international community to impress upon the authorities in Fiji to adhere to its Constitution not only in letter but in spirit also. By extending, in terms of the Constitution of 1997, equal and non-discriminatory treatment to all its citizens, Fiji would regain for itself the right to a place in the Country of Nations, including in the Commonwealth.

New Delhi 
March 19, 2001
