Summary of Press Briefing by the Official Spokesperson
4
May 3, 2001

Forthcoming visit of Foreign Secretary Smt. Chokila  Iyer to Bhutan:

  • Foreign Secretary, Smt. Chokila Iyer wiil be paying an official visit to Bhutan from May 7-9, 2001. This would be her first visit abroad after assuming the charge of Foreign Secretary. The visit is part of ongoing regular bilateral consultations between the two countries on issues of mutual interest. In 2000 the then Foreign Secretary Shri Lalit Mansingh had visited Bhutan in March and then in December.
  • India and Bhutan have very close and problem free relations. The relations are further sustained by regular visits and extensive exchange of views at official level and at the highest level. India has a very extensive cooperation and development assistance programme with Bhutan encompassing various sectors with special focus on infrastructure and the social sectors. Our cooperation in the hydel sector has been mutually beneficial, providing  resources for Bhutan's development and power for India. The 336 MW  Chukha hydroelectric project, commissioned in 1988, is functioning well. India is also extending assistance to Bhutan for the construction of Kurichu Hydroelectric Project (60 MW) and Tala Hydroelectric Project 1020 MW. The first out of the four units of Kurichu Project was commissioned last month. The Tala project is expected to be commissioned by 2004-2005. Some new projects which could be taken up for joint development are under discussion.
  • Both sides share concern on the presence of ULFA-Bodo militants in Bhutan. In July 1999 the National Assembly of Bhutan passed a resolution authorising the government to engage in a dialogue to persuade the militants and take action under the National Security Act against those found to be maintaining links with the militants. In July 2000 the National Assembly adopted another Resolution, reiterating the decision of July 1999 but also authorising the Government to use force to expel the militants, if other options failed. Both Governmens remain in consultation with each other on how best to address this problem. 
In response to questions, the Spokesperson said the following:
  • Asked whether the Ministry of External Affairs Statement marked a shift from its earlier objections to Washington's National Missile Defence (NMD) the spokesperson said that the Statement speaks for itself. India welcomes the announcement of unilateral reduction of nuclear forces by the US, as an example. India also welcomes moving away from the hair-trigger alerts associated with the prevailing nuclear orthodoxies. India notes with appreciation that the US seeks dialogue, consultation and cooperation with the country concerned towards fulfillment of this vision. India believes that there is a strategic and technological inevitability in stepping away from a world that is held hostage by the doctrine of MAD to a cooperative, defensive transition that is underpinned by further cuts and a de-alert of nuclear forces. India has always stood for a multilateral compact that results in an   elimination of all nuclear weapons globally.
  • Asked to comment about abrogation of the Anti-Ballistic Missiles (ABM) treaty and its security implications, the Spokesperson said that the ABM treaty is a bilateral treaty between the US and the Russian Federation. He added that India is not for any unilateral decision but for consultations and cooperative action. The Spokesperson recalled the comments of the Russian Federation Foreign Minister Mr. Igor Ivanov saying that Russia has welcomed the Bush' statement and that the US and Russia should work together to develop a new foundation for world security in the 21st  century. Hoping that this would create the prerequisites for constructive dialogue and consultation on the issues of strategic stability in the interests of Russia and US and in the interests of the entire world. Mr. Ivanov also pointed out that Russia's idea on deep cuts in nuclear arsenals is in harmony with the approach of the US administration. Mr. Ivanov added that It is principally important that the US President has underlined that his administration does not intend to take unilateral steps or to put on the table already prepared variants, but is planning to consult with its allies, friends, with other States, including with Russia to take into account their opinion at the time of taking decisions. 
  • Asked why India was so enthusiastic about the US National Defence Policy the Spokesperson said that we had responded to a highly significant and far reaching statement by the Bush administration. It dwelt on the broader issues of transforming the strategic parameters on which the Cold War security architecture was built. He added that it is going to have a much larger global impact and will have bearing on international security. India had specially welcomed elements that are already articles of our long standing policy such as deep reductions, moving away from hair-trigger alert, international action towards elimination of nuclear weapons and a coordinated and consultative transition to a new security paradigm. 
  • Asked about the agenda for discussions between Mr. Ivanov and Shri Jaswant Singh the Spokesperson said that India and Russia have long standing time tested relations and will discuss the full gamut of bilateral,  regional and international issues. The content of Indo-Russian relations encompasses political and security dialogue, trade and economic relations, science and technology, defence cooperation and cultural contacts. This content is constantly enriched with new elements. We look forward warmly to the visit of Foreign Minister Mr. Igor Ivanov. 
  • Asked about the UNGA report on the situation in Afghanistan the Spokesperson said that the report while describing the situation in Afghanistan made reference to the recent visit of Prime Minister Shri Atal Bihari Vajpayee to Iran. It also drew reference to the Indo-Russian Joint Working Group on Afghanistan and the visit of the UN Secretary General Personal Representative on  Afghanistan Mr. Frances Vendrell to India.
