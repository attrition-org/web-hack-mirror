Links to the rest of the world....

The following are other links in the system dynamics field which may be of interest. While we are making these links available, we are not responsible for the maintenance and content of these pages. There is a range of quality of models and information throughout these pages, and we are not endorsing any one over another.

The links below primarily deal with system dynamics, STELLA modeling, and education. If you'd like to suggest links that may be of interest in these fields, please let us know at webmasters@sysdyn.mit.edu. Now, here are some links to the rest of the system dynamics/systems thinking world:

  • The System Dynamics Society maintains their own webpage containing information about society membership, publications, and an email discussion group.
  • Serendip, where threads on systems thinking and system dynamics can be found.
  • The Maryland Virtual High School of Science and Mathematics has links to many student projects, including models built with STELLA.
  • The CC-STADUS Project WWW Page. (Cross-Curricular Systems Thinking and Dynamics Using STELLA)
  • Günther Ossimitz has written a "mega list of links" to resources for system dynamics and systems thinking in both German and English. He also has links for software, projects, and consulting firms.
  • Waters Center for System Dynamics at Trinity College of Vermont
  • Japanese Chapter of System Dynamics Society (JSD)
  • The Beer Distribution Game web site (maintained by the Center for Organizational Learning) contains instructions for playing the beer game. The beer game is an interactive simulation of a distribution system which shows how business cycles can occur. It is widely used in business schools and corporations to help managers cope with the complexity of dynamic environments and introduce them to the concepts of system dynamics.
  • Tom Fiddaman of Ventana Systems has compiled a comprehensive site for system dynamics and system dynamics modeling. Resources consist of papers to download, links to software companies, other general links of interest, and Tom's Model Library. You can even download his MIT dissertation.
  • John Wiley & Sons are proud to be the publishers of System Dynamics Review on behalf of the System Dynamics Society. You can visit the home page of the journal and view current abstracts at http://www.interscience.wiley.com/jpages/0883-7066/
