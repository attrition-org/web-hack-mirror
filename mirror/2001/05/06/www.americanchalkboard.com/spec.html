Product Standards & Specifications
  1. Face sheet 24 or 28 gauge porcelain enamel steel consisting of :
    • Deposition coat of 2.0 - 2.5 mils on front of steel.
    • Deposition coat of 1.5 - 2.0 mils on front of steel.
    • Porcelain Enamel steel writing and erasing coat system totaling 3.0 - 4.0 mils over front surface for chalkboards and 3.5 - 4.5 mils for markerboards.
    • Firing temperature must be no less than 1200° for chalkboards and 1500° for markerboards.
    • Hardness of writing surface to be no less than 6.5 on MOH's scale.
    • Writing surface shall be uniform in color and texture.
    • Reflectance factor shall be no more than 20% or less than 15% and is guaranteed not to vary as a result of wear.
  2. Core material shall be 1/2" thick industrial grade particle board or industrial grade fiberboard with aluminum moisture retardant backer sheet laminated with suitable adhesive to prevent delamination.
  3. Trim system to be specified. Porcelain enamel chalkboard / markerboard / Remarkable Board surfaces are available in laminated or unlaminated surface sheets prepared under rigorously controlled factory conditions.
  4. Lamination of all materials to be factory type only, with special formulated adhesives. Hand lamination is not acceptable.

  1. Easily washable with most mild soaps and detergents. The surface is not affected by most strong cleaners and it is basically nonstaining due to the nonporous surface. Porcelain enamel steel surfaces are ideal for all magnets and magnetic impregnated materials used as teaching aids. AllianceWall® porcelain enamel surfaces are 100% noncombustible and have 0-0-0 fire hazard classification.
  2. Markerboards feature the AllianceWall® porcelain enamel semi-gloss surface which gives the full color value to dry erase markers with negligible marker wear and produces clear, sharp marker lines for maximum contrast and control of glare. The non-porous surface wipes clean with a dry cloth or eraser.
