FA Communication Plan for Support Services

1.      Purpose of the Plan.  This plan is provided for the purpose of identifying how we intend to keep all interested parties informed on a regular basis of the activity, status and accomplishments of the Support Services Functionality Assessment (FA) which affects the following functions:  Food Services, Military Family Housing (MFH), Bachelor Housing (BH), Morale Welfare and Recreation (MWR), and parts of Social Services (Fallon and Port Hueneme/Point Mugu Family Service Centers, Casualty Assistance Calls Program, Funeral Honors Support, and Retired Activities.)  It is intended to be an informative yet flexible tool to be routinely used throughout the 24-month study, which began 1 June 2000.

2.  Background.  The purpose of the Support Services FA is to examine functional areas under Support Services in order to find economic savings in service delivery while maintaining or improving service to our customer.  The FA process was approved by CINCPACFLT as a substitute for the more rigid and time-consuming A-76 Study.  It is intended to gain the savings anticipated from an A-76 competitive bid process in a faster time frame with less disruption to the work force and more opportunity to provide continuous quality service to our customer.  The FA was chosen as it allows the entire work force to contribute by sharing ideas, participating in Process Action Teams (PAT’s) and being involved throughout the process.  The degree of tasking will vary but everyone may participate in one way or another. 

 3.      The Oversight Team.  The FA Oversight Team is comprised of the Team Leads for each function, staff from the CINCPACFLT Manpower Field Office (CMFO) who are assisting or taking the lead on coordinating completion of some projects, and Dr. Mary Rotterman the overall lead for this FA.  Program Managers who are not represented in the following table also attend the meetings periodically, i.e., Mr. Butch Bautista, Acting PM, Food Services; Mr. Robert Culp, PM Military Family Housing; Captain Steven Sonntag, PM MWR; and Captain David Kennedy, Acting PM, Social Services.  At the time a contractor is hired to support the FA, they will also attend.   The Oversight Team meetings are facilitated by Ms. Damaris Wilson .

The overall charter of the Oversight Team is to guide all the functions through the FA process and focus on improving program efficiency and added customer value.  Specific responsibilities include: tracking the overall progress of the FA, ensuring all functions follow similar and agreed upon formats where appropriate, facilitating the consolidation of all the information collected into one document for the Support Services FA write-up, and leading the cross-functional PATs once the To-Be Organization development begins.  This Team is supported directly by function-specific teams responsible for data collection, program and process descriptions, and idea generation for the To-Be organization. 

4.  Intended Audience.  The communication plan is intended to be of use to a wide variety of people including:

Staff of the 5 affected functions
Commanding Officers
CNRSW Staff in other departments
CINCPACFLT interested program managers and FA officials
Higher authority interested parties (i.e. CNO, N46)
Union Officials
Support Services Customers

5.  What is in the Plan?  The Communication Plan has two major components internal and external.

a.  Internal Communication. The internal communication component consists of informing the work force of what is happening and providing answers to questions or concerns raised by employees.  It includes: distribution of a monthly Support Services FA Newsletter which includes answers to employee questions about the FA; distribution of  FA meeting minutes; visits and other communication by the FA Team with the employees at their work sites; publishing and updating the FA POA&M; and posting information on the CNRSW Support Services FA website, once developed. 

(1)    Support Services FA Update Newsletter.  This monthly newsletter will address overall progress of the Support Services FA and any special issues including changes in the FA POA&M.  It will also include answers to any questions formally raised by employees regarding the FA.  It is intended to reach all affected staff.  The newsletter will be forwarded to each Team Lead for dissemination to all staff and will be posted on the CNRSW Support Services FA website, once developed.  The following addresses the vehicle for staff to submit formal questions/concerns about the FA.

(a)     Anonymous Suggestions/Questions/Concerns from employees:   Employees will have the option of faxing or mailing anonymous comments, questions, concerns, and ideas, directly to M. Rotterman, Regional FA Coordinator. (Fax:  619-556-7631; Mailing Address: Family Housing Welcome Center, Bldg. 3544, Attn: Dr. Mary Rotterman, N90.A, 2625 LeHardy St., San Diego, CA 92136-5182).  Dr. Rotterman can also be reached by e-mail at: rotterman.mary@ns.cnrsw.navy.mil.  All submissions will receive a response on the next monthly newsletter and, if the staff member leaves their name/#, a direct response will also be provided.

(b)   Suggestions from employees on how to improve the newsletter are most welcome and can be addressed to Damaris Wilson at DSN 526-9646, COM (619) 556-9646, or wilson.damaris@ns.cnrsw.navy.mil

(2)  FA Minutes.  The FA Oversight Team and function-specific Teams meet on a regular basis.  The minutes reflect the contents of the meetings and will be posted on the CNRSW Support Services FA website, once developed.

(3)  Regular Visits/Communication with Employees. The purpose of these contacts is to provide a more personalized update to employees on what is happening on the FA front and to give employees an opportunity to have their questions and/or concerns addressed.  Each function will establish a mechanism to keep affected staff directly informed about the FA and to encourage staff to express concerns and ideas related to the FA.

(4) POA&M.  The FA process involves a series of steps, each one of which has multiple smaller steps.  The steps address the following and must be completed within the 24 months timeframe for the Study.

1. FA Planning (includes forming Teams, reviewing instructions and establishing the communication plan)
2. Writing up As-Is Organization (includes information on staffing, processes, workload, costs)
3. Benchmarking (looks for best practice information regarding key processes)
4. Developing To-Be Organization (includes customer feedback, re-engineering processes across functions)
5. Determining To-Be Performance Measures (addresses how we will measure success under the new organization)
6. Calculating To-Be Workload
7. Developing To-Be Staffing
8. Costing To-Be Organization
9. Performing Cost-Benefit Analysis
10. Writing Implementation Plan
11. Implementing Plan

The POA&M details these steps and provides the target completion date and current status. The POA&M is a roadmap to track where we are in accomplishing the tasks.  Over time, dates may be revised due to special circumstances or direction from higher authority.  Changes and progress will be reported in the monthly Support Services FA Newsletter.

(5)  Support Services FA Web Page.  A CNRSW Support Services FA Web Page will be developed to enable the posting of key information as described above.  Once the Web Page is in place, the address will be included on the monthly newsletter.

b.  External Communication.  External communication is any communication required outside the function.   It includes routine progress reports up the chain of command as well as the forwarding of issues or proposed changes that require higher authority approval to implement.  It also includes but is not limited to other CNRSW Departments, Commanding Officers, CINCPACFLT, CNO N46, other regions and the general public.  Major components include distribution of FA minutes, briefings to the CNRSW Executive Steering Committee (ESC), reports on progress to CINCPACFLT, and possible press releases to the general public.

(1)  Minutes of FA Leadership Team Meetings.  Routinely the Support Services FA Oversight Meeting minutes will be forwarded to the ACOS for Support Services and distributed to the CNRSW Chief of Staff, CNRSW Business Manager, CNRSW Strategic Sourcing Office, Human Resources and Union representatives.  Upon request, they will also be forwarded to installation Commanding Officers and any other individuals.  As noted previously, the minutes provide the reader information about the overall direction of the Team as well as the status in completing the POA&M steps.

(2)  Briefs of the NAVREG SW ESC.  In order to keep the chain of command informed, the ACOS for Support Services and appropriate staff will provide periodic updates to the ESC on progress being made.  Information Briefs will afford the ESC an opportunity to better understand what the FA Oversight and function-specific Teams are doing, accomplishments to date including internal changes transparent to the customer, challenges worthy of ESC involvement, and issues pertinent to customer services.

(3)  Higher Authority Reporting.  The FA Oversight Team will assist with the completion of quarterly progress reports to CINCPACFLT.  The reports will document progress or issues related to the POA&M, and any immediate cost savings or program improvements that have occurred as a result of the FA. 

(4)  PAO Announcements.  Occasionally the FA Team may have announcements worthy of dissemination outside of normal military channels. Such announcements will be coordinated through the CNRSW PAO Officer in a format stipulated by PAO. Team members will refrain from any direct contact with newspapers or other public media channels and will direct any and all inquiries to PAO.     

6.  Updates.  The Communication Plan will be routinely reviewed by the FA Oversight Team to ensure it is meeting the requirement of effectively keeping all interested parties informed.  Ideas on how to improve this Plan and/or feedback on its effectiveness are encouraged from all and should be directed to Dr. Rotterman at: rotterman.mary@ns.cnrsw.navy.mil

This Plan is based on the Communication Plan written by COMNAVREGNW Community Programs staff.  We thank the Northwest staff for sharing their work with us.
