FA UPDATE (GENERAL)

  • There was no FA Newsletter in February. Information covered in this newsletter addresses FA actions that occurred in both February and March.
  • Mr. Derek Noether of Booz·Allen and Hamilton (BA&H) has assumed the role of Project Team Leader for Housing and Food Services. Ms. Polly Croteau and Ms. Tina Korol will continue to work with the Bachelor Housing, Family Housing, and Food Services teams.
  • A fourth Strategic Planning session was held on 16 January. Team Members finalized the Guiding Principles and Strategic Goals, and identified three subgroups to develop strategies to reach each of the three Strategic Goals for the directorate. The subgroups' work will be reviewed at the next meeting in February. The minutes from the meetings convened to date can be viewed via the CNRSW website at www.cnrsw.navy.mil. Click the FA Information link under Support Services.
  • Two Strategic Planning sessions were conducted. The members from the CNRSW Support Services' Directorate, CINCPACFLT, and BA&H were in attendance. The sessions were facilitated and documented by BA&H. On 27 February 2001, the leadership team reviewed the results of the goal groups' efforts and came to agreement on the phraseology of the three Strategic Goals.
    • Goal 1: We will deliver our products and services to meet our customers' needs.
    • Goal 2: We will create a well-trained and empowered staff.
    • Goal 3: We will identify, develop, and continually improve our products and services.
    On 27 March 2001, the leadership team reviewed the results of the goal groups' efforts and came to agreement on the strategies and objectives for each strategic goal. With this agreement, the Strategic Plan is now ready for implementation. The next strategic planning session will address implementation planning including evaluation of which objectives should be addressed as part of the FA.
  • The BA&H Team has continued to populate the FA Database with data that will be used for baseline analysis and future To-Be scenarios. The database currently includes most of the APF and NAF revenues and expenses for FY99 and FY00. The database also includes detailed facility information, staffing information, and other support function specific data. As new information is gathered, it will continue to be populated within the database.
  • During February and March 2001, members of the FA Leadership Team visited the Metro and OTH sites to gather more information and document the As-Is processes. These efforts are projected to continue through May. Specific goals include: developing process maps that reflect baseline processes; verifying and collecting workload data, including workload fluctuation patterns, for the baseline period; and identifying key issues and concerns within each of the functional areas. The latter information will be used to help focus redesign efforts in the To-Be design phase of the FA
    • li>The concept of cross-functional services for certain key areas (e.g., marketing, IT) are being examined. The goal would be to increase consistency and efficiency across the functions.

FA ACCOMPLISHMENTS

  • The first FA Customer Survey distribution effort was completed and SCANTRON generated the general report to be included in the CINCPACFLT Balanced Score Card Report as well as the more specific report for use by managers. Overall the scores were very high. For further information on the survey please contact your supervisor; at minimum, the FA Team Leads have copies that can be distributed.
  • The FA Leadership Team has put together the first quarterly report (Balanced Score Card Report) that will be forwarded to CINCPACFLT on or around 30 March, 2001. This report will serve as an update on elements being studied under the FA process. The areas addressed include: customer satisfaction survey results, expenses and revenues for FY 99 and FY 00, staffing levels, and selected patron count information for the different support areas.

PROGRAM MANAGER'S CORNER

Bachelor Housing. As we gain momentum in the FA process...want to take this opportunity to pass on my appreciation to all site managers and their staff. This massive effort could not be accomplished without your support in the collection and validation of data. Your input is critical to the end product. Thank you for your support and involvement in the process.

Food Services. Master Chief Wheeler, Food Services PM, and the representatives for Booz Allen and Hamilton visited almost all Regional Commands during the months of February and March. The "AS IS" data collected and other benchmarking ideas will be invaluable in completing the FA process. Thank you to all the commands and galley personnel.

Military Family Housing. Military Family Housing held a regional directors meeting at the end of February in Ventura to discuss the Change Management issues, the status of the FA, and the relative processes associated with family housing. The latter topic yielded 18 major processes utilized throughout the region that will be further examined to determine which will have the most financial impact on MFH operations.

Morale Welfare and Recreation. Captain Sonntag, MWR PM, conducted Site Visits to NAF El Centro, NWC Seal Beach, NAS Fallon and NB Ventura County. The visits included touring MWR facilities, talking with employees and briefing both the MWR Site Managers and Commanding Officers on the current status of the MWR programs, including MWR's role in the Support Services Functionality Assessment. At the request of the Site Manager, Captain Sonntag conducted a Town Hall meeting with MWR employees during his visit to NB Ventura County.

Social Services. Social Services key personnel will be hearing from BA&H regarding initial interviews (with CACP/FHP and the Regional RAO Office) and follow-on interviews with the two FFSCs. Although attention has been focused on the larger functions in the last couple of months, this will be changing in April.
