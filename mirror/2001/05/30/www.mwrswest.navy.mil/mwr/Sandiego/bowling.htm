NOTICE TO PATRONS

HIGH COSTS COMPEL MWR TO INCREASE BOWLING AND BILLIARDS FEES

Effective June 1, 2001, fees charged by MWR for bowling and billiards at the Admiral Robinson Bowling Center and Sea ‘n Air Lanes will be in increased in accordance with the schedule provided below. The charging of higher fees has become necessary due to rising labor costs, higher wholesale costs for goods and supplies, and higher utilities rates that are either already being charged or are projected to be charged to MWR activities in the San Diego metropolitan area.

Since the beginning of the California utilities crisis, MWR has worked hard to keep prices low for our patrons by conserving energy, reducing labor, and pursuing other cost-conscious initiatives. To date, we believe we’ve been successful in holding our prices steady at a time when comparable programs and services in the commercial sector have been forced to raise their prices, scale back their services, or go out of business. However, increased fees must now be charged to at least partially offset the substantially higher costs that are being passed along to us, including the planned doubling of current electricity rates for all on-base MWR activities on 1 October of this year. Because law mandates that all Category C MWR programs be self-sufficient, price increases are also being implemented in other San Diego area MWR programs, as well, including Golf, Marinas, RV Parks and Food and Beverage. Despite these increases, a recent market analysis has shown that all of the programs in which we are raising our prices are still charging fees/prices that are much lower than those charged for comparable programs and services in the commercial sector.

As of June 1, the following prices will be charged for Bowling and Billiards at the Naval Station San Diego Admiral Robinson Bowling Center and NASNI Sea n’ Air Lanes:
