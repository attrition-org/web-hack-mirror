NOTICE TO PATRONS

HIGH COSTS COMPEL MWR TO INCREASE MARINA FEES

Effective June 1, 2001, fees charged to the patrons of the Fiddler's Cove and Pt. Loma Marinas will be increased in accordance with the schedule provided below. These pricing adjustments include the first increase for slip fees since 1991 and the first increase in live-aboard fees since 1994. Although MWR has worked hard for many years to keep prices low for our patrons, the charging of higher fees has finally become necessary due to rising labor costs, higher wholesale costs for goods and supplies, increased energy consumption resulting from the operation of the new Marina clubhouse at Fiddler's Cove, and substantially higher utilities rates that are being charged to MWR activities in the San Diego metropolitan area.

Since the beginning of the California utilities crisis, MWR has worked especially hard to keep prices low for our patrons by conserving energy, reducing labor, and pursuing other cost-conscious initiatives. To date, we believe we've been successful in holding our prices steady at a time when comparable programs and services in the commercial sector have been forced to raise their prices, scale back their services, or go out of business. However, since December of last year, we've experienced a 400 per cent rise in monthly utilities costs which, along with current projections for additional cost increases in the future, preclude our ability to continue to offset our program costs through efficiencies alone. Increased fees must be charged to at least partially offset the substantially higher costs that are being passed along to us. Because, by law, all Category C MWR programs are mandated to be self-sufficient or they must be eliminated, price increases are also being implemented in other San Diego area MWR programs, as well, including Golf, RV Parks, Bowling and Food and Beverage. Despite these increases, a recent market analysis has shown that all of the programs in which we are raising our prices are still charging fees/prices that are much lower than those charged for comparable programs and services in the commercial sector.

As of June 1, the following prices will be charged at Navy marinas in the San Diego area.
