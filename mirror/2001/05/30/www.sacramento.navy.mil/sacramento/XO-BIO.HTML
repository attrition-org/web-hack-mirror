LIEUTENANT COMMANDER SHAWN W. LOBREE, U.S. NAVY

 

     Lieutenant Commander Lobree grew up and attended high school in Miami, Florida, before attending Florida State University where he graduated with a degree in Economics in 1986.  He received a commission via the Naval Reserve Officer’s Training Corps Unit at Florida A & M University.  Lieutenant Commander Lobree earned a Master’s Degree in Information Technology Management from the Naval Postgraduate School in 1998.

 

     Upon commissioning, Lieutenant Commander Lobree was assigned to USS FAHRION (FFG-22) from September 1987 to October, 1990.  He qualified as a Surface Warfare Officer in 1988, when FAHRION deployed to the Arabian Gulf and participated in Operation Earnest Will, the escort and protection of Kuwaiti tankers.  FAHRION also deployed to the Baltic Sea and the Great Lakes during this tour. Lieutenant Commander Lobree then reported to shore duty where he served as Aide to the Commandant, Naval District Washington, DC from November 1990 to October, 1992.  He then reported to Surface Warfare Officer’s School where he attended the Department Head Course from November 1992 to June 1993, graduating with distinction.

   

     Lieutenant Commander Lobree reported to USS BARNSTABLE COUNTY (LST-1197) and served as First Lieutenant from June 1993 until the ship was decommissioned in June 1994. During his tour BARNSTABLE COUNTY deployed to the Arabian Sea and Arabian Gulf, participating in Operation Desert Storm and Operation Restore Hope, humanitarian assistance to Somalia.   Lieutenant Commander Lobree then reported to Amphibious Squadron SIX in July 1994 as Operations and Plans Officer.  Embarked in USS NASSAU (LHA-4), PHIBRON SIX deployed to the Mediterranean Sea and participated in Operations Deny Flight and Provide Promise in support of the U.S. role in the Balkans conflict.   Lieutenant Commander Lobree then reported to the Naval Postgraduate School where he completed graduate education from January 1996 to March, 1998.  He then reported to the staff of Commander, SIXTH Fleet in April 1998 and served as the Fleet Scheduler and Assistant Surface Operations Officer until February 2000.  Embarked in USS LASALLE (AGF-3), COMSIXTHFLT served as the commander for all naval forces participating in Operation Allied Force, the Kosovo campaign. Lieutenant Commander Lobree reported as Executive Officer of USS SACRAMENTO (AOE-1) in June, 2000.

 

     Lieutenant Commander Lobree is authorized to wear the Meritorious Service Medal, the Navy Commendation Medal, the Navy Achievement Medal, the Navy Unit Commendation and the Joint Meritorious Unit Award in addition to other campaign and unit awards.
