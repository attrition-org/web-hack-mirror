CAPTAIN DAVID H. BUSS, U.S. NAVY

Captain Buss graduated with distinction from the U.S. Naval Academy in 1978 and is the 24th Commanding Officer of USS SACRAMENTO (AOE-1), homeported in Bremerton, Washington.

Following designation as a Naval Flight Officer in July 1979, Captain Buss reported to VA-42 for initial training in the A-6 Intruder. He served with the "Fighting Tigers" of VA-65, completing Indian Ocean, Mediterranean and North Atlantic deployments in USS Dwight D. Eisenhower (CVN 69) from 1980 through 1983. After a tour as a FRS instructor with VA-42, Captain Buss served as Aide and Flag Lieutenant for Commander, Carrier Group Eight, completing deployments in USS Nimitz (CVN 68). He then reported to the staff of Commander, Medium Attack Wing One, serving as Bombardier/Navigator Readiness Officer from 1988 through 1990. As Operations Officer and Maintenance Officer with the VA-36 "Roadrunners", Captain Buss completed a deployment in USS Theodore Roosevelt (CVN 71) and participated in Operations Desert Storm in 1991. He then served as the Senior Naval Operations Staff Officer at the Joint Warfighting Center, at Hurlburt Field, Florida from 1991 through 1994. Captain Buss served as Executive Officer of VA-34 and subsequently assumed command of the "Blue Blasters" in September 1995. The "Blue Blasters" were one of the last A-6 Intruder squadrons in the Navy, deploying in USS George Washington (CVN 73) to the Adriatic Sea in support of NATO operations in Bosnia and to the Arabian Gulf in support of Operation Southern Watch in 1996. Following squadron command, Captain Buss completed the Naval Nuclear Propulsion training program and reported as the Executive Officer, USS Nimitz (CVN 68) in October 1998. He assumed command of USS Sacramento (AOE-1) in April 2000.

Captain Buss has accumulated more than 3800 total flight hours of which more than 3400 are in the A-6 Intruder. He flew 41 combat missions during Desert Storm and has more than 880 carrier landings. He was the recipient of the 1991 COMNAVAIRLANT Naval Flight Officer of the year and 1991 Captain Michael J. Smith "Intruder of the Year" awards. Decorations include: the Distinguished Flying Cross (Combat V); the Defense Meritorious Medal; the Meritorious Service Medal (two awards); the Air Medal (nine awards); the Navy Commendation Medal (five awards); the Navy Achievement Medal; and various campaign and unit awards.
