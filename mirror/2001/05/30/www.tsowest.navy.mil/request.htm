Trial Service Office West

 

 

 

 

Request for Service Letters are required by both the Trial Service Office (Trial/Prosecution and Command Services) and the Naval Legal Service Office (Defense and Personal Representation) in order to obtain assistance.  The command is also responsible for requesting services for individual defendants in courts-martial as well as administrative boards.  Please select from one of the letters provided below and forward as appropriate to either the Trial Service Office (TSO) or the Naval Legal Service Office (NLSO).  The following checklist is a synopsis of what is required by TSO and the NLSO.  For a complete explanation, the TSO Instuction for requesting services (provided below) should be reviewed.

When requesting trial services, the following protocol should be followed:

  1. All cases should be sent to the TSO prior to preferral so that a prosecutor may review the charge sheet;
  2. After a prosecutor has assisted you in drafting the charges, you should forward a request for services letter to the TSO. That letter should include the original charge sheet and three copies, four certified copies of the convening order, and three copies of the evidence;
  3. You should then forward a request for services letter to the NLSO along with two copies of the charge sheet, two copies of the convening order, and two copies of the evidence; and
  4. When drafting a request letter, be sure to include you unit’s unclassified movement schedule.
