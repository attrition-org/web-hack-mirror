Like any military command, Naval Security Group Activity Denver has many command policies that serve to strengthen the chain of command, foster camaraderie and improve morale, good order and discipline. We would like you to be aware of some of these policies before you arrive.

Uniforms
Return to Policies

The Commanding Officer sets uniform policy in accordance with Area Coordinator directives and conducts personnel inspections twice a year in almost every uniform authorized in the Denver area. You should arrive in Denver with a full sea bag with all uniforms in good condition. Pay particular attention to cold weather items such as coats and gloves. Watch caps are not authorized, ear muffs are. A pair of rubber overshoes may save a pair of street shoes from a long walk from the parking lot. Dungarees, ball caps, and wash khakis are only authorized within the base confines, to and from work (no stops), and on Fitzsimons Army Garrison. Except for short visits to pick up LES's you must wear your uniform whenever you are in the ADF or Joint Services Admin Bldg. Your image reflects on the command and the U.S. Navy. Make it a good one. Wear your uniform with pride.

Physical Readiness
 Return to Policies

Most people have to acclimate to Denver's altitude. The command allows 60 days for members to acclimate. After that, you will be tested if it is in the middle of a cycle. You should take an unofficial test prior to the end of the sixty day period to ensure you are ready for the official test. All personnel are weighed and measured upon arrival. If over Navy standards you will be placed on remedial PT until you meet the requirements. Remedial PT is held at 0600 three times a week and a test is conducted on Friday, weather permitting. If you pass the test and the weight requirements you may be excused from the remedial sessions but still have to test each Friday. All personnel are required to PT three times a week in accordance with Navy directives and keep a log of your activities regardless of your physical readiness status or duties assigned.

Collateral Duties
Return to Policies

Like all Navy commands there are far more duties than there are Sailors so we have to assume collateral duties. Most of these are assigned to E5 and above but there are many opportunities for junior Sailors to be on standing committees as well. Collateral duties are expected to be executed with the same diligence as every other duty assigned.

Evaluations and Fitness Reports
Return to Policies

The Commanding Officer NSGA Denver is the reporting senior for all Sailors at NSGA Denver. He delegates this authority to the Executive Officer and Department Heads for certain paygrades. Input from operational duties is considered along with military duties in determining grades and promotion recommendations but the final authority is ultimately the Commanding Officer. Sailors at NSGA Denver are very competitive for promotion.

Awards
Return to Policies

Sailors at NSGA are eligible for both Navy and Joint Awards and it is possible to get both at the end of a tour. Sailors being considered for awards will have their entire record considered. Any adverse information such as NJP or PRT failure may impact the decision.

Off Duty Employment
Return to Policies

Denver is an economically robust area and part time jobs are abundant and pay well. While the extra income may be necessary to pay bills, in many cases Sailors take part time employment to pay for a new car or stereo. The Command feels that this is short sighted and the time would be better spent pursuing a college degree or volunteering in the community. In any case no NSGA Sailor will accept part time employment that would bring discredit upon the Service. Any part time employment is undertaken with the full understanding that military duties come first and no accommodation will be made to schedules in deference to part time employment.

Drugs and Alcohol
Return to Policies

Random urinalysis is conducted monthly. Illegal drug use is not tolerated nor is alcohol abuse.

Sexual Harassment and Racial Discrimination
Return to Policies

Not tolerated. All newly reporting Sailors must attend Navy Rights & Responsibilities Workshop upon arrival where you will be briefed in detail.

Chain of Command
Return to Policies

The chain of command is the most basic of military tenets and its integrity, strength, and responsiveness are no less critical here than at any other command. Each of us, no matter what our rank or positions, has a responsibility to maintain the chain of command and use it to its fullest extent.
