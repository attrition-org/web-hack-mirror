Quality Of Life

In the last year NSGA Denver has become home to dozens of plans for quality of life improvement.  Currently a new commissary and exchange is being constructed to accommodate the needs of our military patrons stationed at Buckley Air Force Base.  Open date for the new facilities is Late 2001.

There are many things to do in the Denver area in your off time.  The facilities provided here range from skiing to night life.  For a better idea on what the area offers please refer to the Denver City Search web site.  Here you will have resources to find out more about the area.  Another resource for you to find information is your sponsor.

Here is just a few more tidbits of information about Denver.

Contrary to popular opinion and those misleading travel posters seen everywhere, Denver is not in the mountains. You can, however, see them from Denver! No matter what your interests are, there is literally something for everyone located within easy reach of the Metro area. Because the climate is generally mild year-round, recreational activities normally stopped in the winter due to weather (jogging, bicycling, etc.) are not affected. Be forewarned, however, that a nice 65 degree Monday in December may be followed immediately by a chilly 25 degree Tuesday!

As you would expect, skiing is a major recreational activity (not to mention a large industry) in Colorado. You don’t have to travel far to enjoy some of the best skiing (and snowboarding) not only in the Rockies, but perhaps in the world! There are excellent downhill skiing resorts such as Keystone, Breckenridge, Arapahoe Basin, Copper Mountain, and Winter Park all within 80 miles of downtown Denver. Farther away, but still easily accessible are areas such as Aspen, Vail, Steamboat Springs and Crested Butte. Many lift ticket discounts are available.

Denver and the surrounding areas have more than 200 parks and 20,000 acres of mountain paths, with bike and jogging paths, tennis courts and gold courses. The Highline Canal is a 71 mile route that skirts the City of Denver.

Although Colorado is landlocked, boating is popular. Sailing is available at Chatfield Dam and Cherry Creek Reservoir. Within several hours drive are Horsetooth Lake near Ft. Collins, Carter Lake near Loveland, Grand Lake, Lake Granby, Lake Dillon and a number of private reservoirs. Hunting and fishing are also very popular in Colorado. For information contact the Wildlife Division Region at (303) 297-1192.

SPORTS

If sports is your game, then chances are good that you’ll love Denver!! Denver offers a wide range of activities from baseball and football, to basketball, hockey, golfing, soccer and skiing. Whether you like to participate or watch, there's something for you.

FOOTBALL – "Broncomania" is perhaps the major affliction of Denver area residents. If you don't have it by the time you arrive, you are guaranteed to catch it soon thereafter. You won't have to worry about season tickets a Mile High Stadium...unless you have the money to purchase "rights" from another season ticket holder. The Denver Broncos have sold out every game for the past twenty-six years and the waiting list for season tickets is reputed to be several thousands. But don't fret...there is always someone at work who may be willing to sell their tickets for that week's game (especially if it looks like the game will have a foot of snow and 20 below in blizzard conditions!!)

BASEBALL - Coors Field, a state-of-the-art, open air, natural grass ballpark at 20th and Blake Streets in downtown Denver is always listed as "sold out" for each and every game. However, tickets are always available, ranging from the expensive seats near the field to the $4.00 "Rockpile" seats behind center field which are sold on game days. Many opposing teams have complained that Coors Field is a "launching pad" for homeruns, mainly due to the thin air and usually hit by a Rockies player. A routine fly ball in many ballparks is often a 400 foot homerun in Coors Field. Shutouts are not the norm!

BASKETBALL & HOCKEY - Professional basketball fans will enjoy the excitement that the Denver Nuggets bring to Colorado.  They share the Pepsi Center with the Colorado Avalanche, Denver's National Hockey League team and winner of the 1996 Stanley Cup in just their first year in Colorado.

Denver also offers dog racing, golf, bicycling, tennis, running clubs, rodeo, professional soccer, drag racing and just about any other sporting event you can think of.

CULTURE

When the sun goes down in Denver, the excitement builds up. Denver has always had an active and varied nightlife, but the city is also a surprising cultural center. From the Colorado Symphony to the Denver Center Theater Company, to sell-out rock concerts and intimate cabarets, there is always something for every taste in after-dark entertainment.

The Denver Performing Arts Complex is recognized as the second largest in the nation. Situated on four square blocks downtown, the Center rises under an architecturally imposing 76-foot high roof. The complex includes the Helen G. Bonfils Theater Complex, Boettcher Concert Hall, the Auditorium Theater, the new Buell Theater and a parking garage. Major Broadway plays are a frequent visitor.

There are a number of dinner theaters in the Denver area offering a wide variety of entertainment from serious drama to lightly comedy to melodrama.

With the revitalization of Lower Downtown (known as LoDo), the variety of clubs and restaurants can almost overwhelm even the casual visitor.

Some of the tourist attractions in Denver are as follows:
 

  • The Botanic Gardens, Denver Zoo and the Colorado State Museum

  • The Colorado State Capital

  • Seven Flags Elitch Gardens
a family amusement park located in lower downtown Denver.

There are many children’s organizations such as Boys Club of Denver, Boy Scouts of America, Camp Fire Council, Colorado Children’s Chorale, Denver Girls Inc., 4-H Clubs, Girl Scouts, etc.
