INTRODUCTION

This document will provide you with some general information on the greater Denver area.  All newcomers are urged to contact their sponsors without delay: to advise them of your travel plans, your expected arrival date, and other pertinent information. ARRIVAL IN DENVER

Depending on how you plan to travel to Denver, the following information is provided to assist you in reporting to NAVSECGRUACT Denver. Remember to communicate with your sponsor to provide an arrival date and time. This will smooth your check-in process.

ARRIVING VIA COMMERCIAL FLIGHTS

If you fly to Denver, you will arrive at the Denver International Airport (DIA), approximately 15 miles from NAVSECGRUACT Denver. You should notify your sponsor of your airline, flight number and estimated time of arrival so that he or she can meet you at the airport. If desired, you may rent a vehicle from one of the numerous rental agencies found in the main terminal. Should you arrive at DIA and rent a vehicle, the following directions are provided to assist you in finding NAVSECGRUACT Denver and Buckley Air Force Base.

You will exit the airport and will be driving on Peña Boulevard. You will be on this road for approximately 10 miles. Look for the exit sign for "Airport Road" / "Aurora". Take this exit. Follow this road.  To proceed to NAVSECGRUACT Denver, continue on Airport Road to the next intersection (Airport Road and 6th Avenue).  Turn left onto 6th Avenue and the gate for Buckley Air Force Base will be approximately 1 mile on your right.

ARRIVING VIA INTERSTATES 70, 76 OR 25

CHECKING IN

Upon reporting to Denver, your sponsor will bring you to the NAVSECGRUACT Denver Admin Department located in the Joint Service Admin Facility (JSAF), Bldg. 26, on Buckley Air Force Base. After in processing, your sponsor will take you to our servicing Personnel Support Activity Detachment (PSD). Our PSD is located a couple of miles from NAVSECGRUACT Denver at 20600 E. Highway 30, phone: (303) 677-6258.

When you go with your sponsor to PSD, you must have in your possession your service record, pay record, and original travel orders with all endorsements and receipts (including airline tickets). You will be interviewed and your PCS travel claim will be completed at that time. Once you have been processed through PSD, you will return to the NSGA Admin Department and will be ready to start the NAVSECGRUACT Denver check-in process.

You should bring both your medical and dental records with you when your sponsor brings you to Buckley Air Force Base. This applies to only active duty medical/dental records. You (subject to change in the future) may hold your family members’ records. Your sponsor will take you to check in with the 821st Medical Squadron (821 MDS) on Buckley. You will deliver your records to them. This clinic is staffed 0700 - 1630 and handles only active duty personnel. You can reach the clinic at (303) 677-6474.

If you are married (any paygrade) or are a single E-5 or above, you will now be eligible for 10 days no-cost house-hunting TAD. Following this TAD, you will commence the formal check-in process. Your sponsor will initially bring you to the NSGA Admin Department located in the JSAF on Buckley Air Force Base, where you will commence your check-in and then take you to our PSD.

After completion of the PSD check-in, you will return to the NSGA Admin Department where you will receive your house-hunting TAD orders. Following this TAD, you will return to the Admin Department and commence the remainder of the formal check-in process. At times, the approval to allow you to commence work may take several days. In these cases, you will be assigned to X-Division. You will be required to muster each day. During this time, you will be allowed to take care of any personal matters you were not able to take care of during your house-hunting TAD.

If you are single, E-4 and below, you will commence the check-in process by having your sponsor bring you to the NSGA Admin Department located in the JSAF on Buckley Air Force Base.

After checking in with the Admin Department, your sponsor will set up and appointment for you to go to our PSD. Once you’ve been processed through PSD, your sponsor will bring you back to the Admin Department to commence your formal check-in.

The approval to allow you to commence work in your assigned department may take time. In these cases, you will be assigned to X-Division. You will be required to muster each day. During this time, you are encouraged to take care of any personal matters associated with getting settled in. You will be afforded ample time to take care of your personal business.

Once approval to commence work has been received, all Sailors will be required to go through security indoctrination: currently held on Mondays and Thursdays. After this briefing, your sponsor will take you to your future department where you will start the site check-in process and continue with command check-in. Your sponsor is responsible for taking you to the people and places you need to see.

MEDICAL/DENTAL INFORMATION

For active duty members assigned to NAVSECGRUACT Denver, military care is provided through the 821st Medical Squadron (821 MDS), a small Air Force medical clinic located on Buckley Air Force Base. This clinic is open from 0730 - 1630, Monday through Fridays. Military sick call is held from 0715 - 0830.

This clinic is not yet adequately staffed to also support family members, so, at present, they only see active duty personnel. Family members are referred to civilian medical providers contracted under the TRICARE Prime network. The managed care support contractor (MCSC) that coordinates and pays for medical care in this area (the TRICARE Central Region) is the TriWest HealthCare Alliance. TRICARE was implemented in this region on 1 April 1997.

If you have family members that accompanied you to Denver, to ensure a smooth enrollment in TRICARE here, do the following: (1) When visiting PSD, verify that your each of family member’s enrollments in DEERS is current and accurate; (2) Contact the TRICARE MCSC from your previous region via telephone to disenroll from their region; (3) Visit the TRICARE Service Center at the former Fitzsimmons Medical Center to complete enrollment of your family members in the TRICARE Central Region.

Active Duty dental care is provided to Sailors through Dentrust Dental, a contracted dental office. Your sponsor will help you drop off your dental records at Dentrust. This office is located on 6th Avenue, about a half a mile from the 6th Avenue Gate to Buckley Air Force Base. Family members eligible for dental care fall under the TRICARE Family Member Dental Plan (TFMDP) through United Concordia. Numerous dentists in the Metro Denver area participate in this plan and the choice of provider is left up to each service member.

HOUSING

The Denver area offers a large variety of housing options. Many people from the command live close to the base, while others live as far as 75 miles away.

BUY OR RENT? This is often a dilemma for members coming to Denver. The housing market in Denver is very good for both buyer and seller. Denver has finally evened out from the "oil-bust" days and homes are keeping their value.

RENTING - There are many condos, town-homes, duplexes and single family homes for sale and rent, as well as a large array of apartment complexes. The rental market for apartments is currently good in Denver. You will find a large variety to choose from unless you have a pet. Many apartment complexes will not rent to people with pets.  The average rent for a two bedroom apartment in the local area is approximately $800 - $1,000 per month.

BUYING A HOME - Most single family homes range from $140,000 to well over $250,000 and beyond. There is also an abundance of condos and town-homes to purchase from $85,000 to $150,000 and beyond. Condos and town-homes are much harder to sell in Denver than single family homes.

LOCATION

Command members live all over the Denver Metro area and beyond.  Approximately 85% of command members live in Aurora, with the remainder living in Denver and up to 75 miles away from the command. Members buy homes in areas including Littleton, Highlands Ranch, Parker, Englewood, Franktown, Elizabeth, Castle Rock, Bennett, Monument, Arvada, Falcon and Colorado Springs.

In DENVER you will find an array of housing lifestyles - high rise condos, city-styled town-homes and older single family homes. The city is a mixture of styles providing urban and suburban environments. There are in-town neighborhoods and plenty of contemporary single and multi-family housing options.

AURORA offers family-oriented communities. Aurora has been identified as one of the fastest growing cities in the United States. Aurora dates back to the early 1900’s but most of the city is new. Aurora maintains all the charm suburban living affords, yet it also offers all the advantages of living in a major city. Aurora's residents have an array of mixed housing. Easy access to any of Denver’s major highways gives Aurorans quick commutes to work or play. Cherry Creek and Aurora Reservoirs provide boating, sailing, fishing, swimming and other outdoor activities.

SOUTHEAST of DENVER, ENGLEWOOD and GREENWOOD VILLAGE are known for their well-groomed neighborhoods located near the growing Denver Technology Center (DTC). This area has garnered national attention for its rapid growth and its well planned, park-like environment. HIGHLANDS RANCH offers a wide variety of professionally designed homes in all price ranges. Cherry Creek School is the premier school district when it comes to education and programs. The curriculum of the district is a balance of traditional skill development and new instructional approaches. Excellent programs for the gifted and talented are also offered.

LITTLETON is characterized by small, distinctive neighborhoods and a family-oriented atmosphere. Littleton is located in the southernmost section of the Denver Metro area. Its school district has one of the lowest dropout rates in the state and 75% of its high school graduates go on to college. Although Littleton is a "bed-room" community, it still provides almost 20,000 jobs in its many major commercial areas.

JEFFERSON COUNTY consists of four major cities...LAKEWOOD, ARVADA, WHEAT RIDGE and WESTMINSTER. Education is a high priority in Jefferson County. The school district has a national reputation for rich, innovative, and varied programs. Jefferson County is home to some of the most magnificent scenery found anywhere. Rolling grassland, craggy rock formations, tumbling mountain streams and lofty pines present a vastly diverse montage.

GENERAL INFORMATION

Denver was born during the great "Pikes Peak or Bust" gold rush of 1859 when some small flakes of placer gold were found where the South Platte River meets Cherry Creek. In its first few years, the city survived a flood, several major fires, Indian attacks and even raised an army that defeated an invading force of Confederates during the Civil War. With the discovery of more gold in the mountains, Denver became a boomtown. Saloons, gambling halls, and wagon trains lined the mud-filled streets and every outlaw, lawman and desperado in the West made at least one visit to the Mile High City. The turn-of-the twentieth century brought respectability, and the wealth of the mountains was poured into parks, fountains, statues, tree-lined streets and elaborate mansions. Denver became the most elegant city in a thousand-mile radius. The energy boom of the 1980’s, and the growth of new industries, such as cable, with the ever-present tourism industry continue to maintain Denver's economic position in the country. Today, the city is known for its modem architecture and design and for its beautiful blending of parks, gardens and neighborhoods.

Denver is one of America’s fastest-growing cities with about 500,000 people living within the city limits and over 2 million in the metro area. Denver has a young, active and well-educated population. The median age in Denver is 29.5 with 35% of the residents between 18 and 35. Denver has the second highest number of college graduates per capita in the U.S. (after Washington, DC), and has the highest educated downtown workforce. Use of the city’s 200 parks is so high that a speed limit was instituted...for bicycles.

The cost of living in Denver is about 103.3, slightly above the national average of 100.0, according to a national survey conducted by the American Chamber of Commerce Researchers Association (ACCRA). The two factors, which vary most from the U.S. average, are health care and utilities. Health care costs are about 20% above the national average while the cost of utilities is about 5% below the national average. Of course, NSGA Denver personnel and their families are covered under TRICARE as of 1 April 1997 and are thus not impacted by the high health care costs.

Denver International Airport (DIA) is the first new airport to be built in the United States in over 20 years. Located just 24 miles from downtown, DIA lights up the night with a striking, opaque fabric roof made with Teflon-coated fiberglass. DIA is 53 square miles, twice the size of Manhattan Island, New York.

Since the 1980’s Aurora’s land area has more than doubled, making it Colorado’s third largest city, covering more than 126 square miles. Aurora’s population is around 250,000. Much of Aurora sits above Denver with a view of the mountains to the west. It is approximately 20 minutes from downtown Denver, and contiguous to Buckley. Other areas close to the site are Englewood, Parker and Highlands Ranch, to name a few.

Education plays an important role in the lives of Denverites, with the Denver Metro area ranking second highest among U.S. cities (behind Washington DC) for the median number of quality institutions for all ages and interests.

CLIMATE

Nothing is more misunderstood than the Denver climate. Located far from any moisture source, Denver has a mild, dry climate with over 300 days of sunshine a year; more hours of annual sun than San Diego or Miami Beach. The city receives approximately 15 inches of precipitation a year, about the same as Los Angeles.

SPRING in Denver has warm, sunny days, while the nearby mountains still have snowcaps and skiing. Evenings can be cool, and the city sometimes receives a late-spring snow shower. However, if snow does fall, it melts almost immediately.

SUMMER brings warm, sunny days with very low humidity. Temperatures seldom go above the low 90’s, and evenings are cool, providing natural air conditioning.

FALL is one of Denver’s most delightful seasons. In the Rockies, fall comes early, with the colorful turning of the Aspens from mid-September through the end of October. Down on the plains, Denver enjoys "Indian Summer" throughout October and November with temperatures often in the 70’s and 80’s. With artificial snow, ski areas can open as early as Halloween, while Denver enjoys fine golfing weather.

WINTER means bright, sunny days and surprisingly comfortable temperatures. Denver’s average daily high in February is 45 degrees, just one degree cooler than Washington, DC. Snow falls so rarely in Denver that the city's golf courses remain open all year. When snow does fall, it melts quickly due to the city's abundant sunshine.

NOTE: Denver’s air pollution is clearly visible as a brown cloud. Air pollution is generally more severe in the fall, winter and spring. While the smog used to be confined to the north of Denver, there is increasing "smog creep." However, the east and southeast still remain the most smog free and, therefore, the most desirable living areas. Denver has increased its fight against smog by encouraging car pooling, banning wood-burning fireplaces, etc. This measure has helped.

ALTITUDE

If this is your first time in the Metro Denver area, you may experience a more rapid heartbeat, shortness of breath, chapped lips, tiredness, and quick sunburn. This is due to the fact that Denver is one mile higher than sea level. Compared with sea level, you are more than a mile closer to the sun, so the air is thinner and clearer. It may take a few days or even weeks to acclimate to the altitude. You will also experience the "fun" of cooking in Denver. With the altitude, water will boil at 202 degrees rather than the 212 degrees, so that three-minute egg may seem a bit mushy after only three minutes! Once you understand the differences, cooking becomes easier (it just takes longer)!!

TAXES

The State of Colorado is like everywhere else in the world....they have taxes! The basic tax rate for the state of Colorado is 3.8% sales tax on almost all purchases. Denver and Aurora sales tax is currently 7.5% of your purchase. Certain cities, towns, and counties (and even a new shopping mall) impose an additional tax which ranges from 0% to 4.5%. For items to be delivered, tax is assessed at the delivery point, not at the rate where purchased. State income tax is 5%. Overall, state and city taxes are equal or less than most metropolitan areas of similar size.

RECREATION

Contrary to popular opinion and those misleading travel posters seen everywhere, Denver is not in the mountains. You can, however, see them from Denver! No matter what your interests are, there is literally something for everyone located within easy reach of the Metro area. Because the climate is generally mild year-round, recreational activities normally stopped in the winter due to weather (jogging, bicycling, etc.) are not affected. Be forewarned, however, that a nice 65 degree Monday in December may be followed immediately by a chilly 25 degree Tuesday!

As you would expect, skiing is a major recreational activity (not to mention a large industry) in Colorado. You don’t have to travel far to enjoy some of the best skiing (and snowboarding) not only in the Rockies, but perhaps in the world! There are excellent downhill skiing resorts such as Keystone, Breckenridge, Arapahoe Basin, Copper Mountain, and Winter Park all within 80 miles of downtown Denver. Farther away, but still easily accessible are areas such as Aspen, Vail, Steamboat Springs and Crested Butte. Many lift ticket discounts are available.

Denver and the surrounding areas have more than 200 parks and 20,000 acres of mountain paths, with bike and jogging paths, tennis courts and gold courses. The Highline Canal is a 71 mile route that skirts the City of Denver.

Although Colorado is landlocked, boating is popular. Sailing is available at Chatfield Dam and CherryCreek Reservoir. Within several hours drive are Horsetooth Lake near Ft. Collins, Carter Lake near Loveland, Grand Lake, Lake Granby, Lake Dillon and a number of private reservoirs. Hunting and fishing are also very popular in Colorado. For information contact the Wildlife Division Region at (303) 297-1192.

SPORTS

If sports is your game, then chances are good that you’ll love Denver!! Denver offers a wide range of activities from baseball and football, to basketball, hockey, golfing, soccer and skiing. Whether you like to participate or watch, there's something for you.

FOOTBALL – "Broncomania" is perhaps the major affliction of Denver area residents. If you don't have it by the time you arrive, you are guaranteed to catch it soon thereafter. You won't have to worry about season tickets a Mile High Stadium...unless you have the money to purchase "rights" from another season ticket holder. The Denver Broncos have sold out every game for the past twenty-six years and the waiting list for season tickets is reputed to be several thousands. But don't fret...there is always someone at work who may be willing to sell their tickets for that week's game (especially if it looks like the game will have a foot of snow and 20 below in blizzard conditions!!)

BASEBALL - Coors Field, a state-of-the-art, open air, natural grass ballpark at 20th and Blake Streets in downtown Denver is always listed as "sold out" for each and every game. However, tickets are always available, ranging from the expensive seats near the field to the $4.00 "Rockpile" seats behind center field which are sold on game days. Many opposing teams have complained that Coors Field is a "launching pad" for homeruns, mainly due to the thin air and usually hit by a Rockies player. A routine fly ball in many ballparks is often a 400 foot homerun in Coors Field. Shutouts are not the norm!

BASKETBALL & HOCKEY - Professional basketball fans will enjoy the excitement that the Denver Nuggets bring to Colorado.  They share the Pepsi Center with the Colorado Avalanche, Denver's National Hockey League team and winner of the 1996 Stanley Cup in just their first year in Colorado.

Denver also offers dog racing, golf, bicycling, tennis, running clubs, rodeo, professional soccer, drag racing and just about any other sporting event you can think of.

CULTURE

When the sun goes down in Denver, the excitement builds up. Denver has always had an active and varied nightlife, but the city is also a surprising cultural center. From the Colorado Symphony to the Denver Center Theater Company, to sell-out rock concerts and intimate cabarets, there is always something for every taste in after-dark entertainment.

The Denver Performing Arts Complex is recognized as the second largest in the nation. Situated on four square blocks downtown, the Center rises under an architecturally imposing 76-foot high roof. The complex includes the Helen G. Bonfils Theater Complex, Boettcher Concert Hall, the Auditorium Theater, the new Buell Theater and a parking garage. Major Broadway plays are a frequent visitor.

There are a number of dinner theaters in the Denver area offering a wide variety of entertainment from serious drama to lightly comedy to melodrama.

With the revitalization of Lower Downtown (known as LoDo), the variety of clubs and restaurants can almost overwhelm even the casual visitor.

VEHICLE REGISTRATION

EMISSIONS TESTING

Colorado state law requires that registered vehicles undergo an emissions inspection in the same month of license plate expiration. You will have to have your car tested at an Envirotest Center. The cost is approximately $25.00. If you wish to operate a motor vehicle on board any military installation in the Denver area, it must have valid emissions test sticker displayed on the windshield, even if your car is registered in another state. New vehicles purchased in the state of Colorado are exempt from emissions testing for the first four years.

LICENSE PLATES

To obtain Colorado plates, you must have verification of your motor vehicle's motor and serial numbers, your title or registration and an emissions inspection to get the license plates at one of the many vehicle registration office located in each county. FEES are based on weight, age and taxable value of vehicle. The ownership tax on your car could range from $100 to $300 or more per year. To be exempt from this tax you can obtain a non-residency certificate. Some license plate offices are located as follows:

AUTOMOBILE INSURANCE

Automobile insurance is MANDATORY in Colorado. Every owner of a motor vehicle registered in the state must have liability insurance. Evidence of liability insurance must be carried in the car while in operation. Failure to show proof of insurance if stopped by the police in Colorado can result in loss of your license on the spot. Play it safe and always have valid current vehicle insurance. Please note that Colorado’s auto insurance rates are generally higher than most other states.

SAFETY BELTS/CHILD CAR SEATS

Colorado’s safety belt law states that every child under four years old and weighing less than 40 pounds must be in an approved child car seat.

VOTER REGISTRATION

To register to vote, you must be a U.S. citizen, at least 18 years of age before the next election and a resident of your county and precinct for 32 days prior to the next election date. You can register to vote when you register your vehicle or apply for your driver’s license. In most cases you can register at any city and town clerk or a county election department.

TELEPHONE

US West provides telephone service to the Metro Denver area. Residential service averages $20-25 per month. US West does not offer out-of-state long distance service. This is handled by such companies as AT&T, MCI and Sprint. When you call for initial service with US West, you will be asked if you desire long distance service. You will have to call the long distance service of your choice and arrange for this service separately. Your monthly bill will reflect the US West charges as well as your long distance service, but you only pay US West. A deposit for long distance service may be required by US West. If your home is already wired for service, hookup can be done in a day or so. If not, installation of your phone may take from seven to ten days. For new service in the Metro Denver area, call 1-800-244-1111.

UTILITIES

GAS and ELECTRICITY are provided to most of Metro Denver by the Public Service Company of Colorado. However, you will find smaller utility companies providing service in the outlying suburban areas. According to a recent study, Denver utility bills ranked lower than 26 major cities in the nation and 24.6% below the national average.

WATER is provided to the Metro Denver area by the Denver Water Department which has extensive holdings throughout the Rocky Mountain Region. Rates for water service and sewage compare favorably with the rest of the country. For additional information, contact Denver Water Customer Service at(303) 893-2444.

TRASH REMOVAL

Garbage collection is included as a city service in the city of Denver. In all other metro areas, you must pay for the service. There are many private contractors available.

PETS

Dogs and cats must be vaccinated yearly against rabies, and, in most areas, dogs must be licensed annually. Pets brought into the Metro Denver area must either be vaccinated within 30 days or have been vaccinated within the past twelve months. Most communities have strict leash laws. There are numerous veterinary clinics throughout the Metro area as well as 24-hour animal hospitals. Please check with a Vet in your area for more information.
