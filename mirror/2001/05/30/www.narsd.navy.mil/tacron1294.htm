1.        RESCHEDULES / FLEX DRILLS [RS/Flex]:        Reschedules (RS) and Flex Drills will only be used whenever personnel cannot participate in the regular DWE.  Only CO, XO, or your Department Head may approve RS, or Flex drills.

(a)     RS/Flex.   Reschedules and Flex drills are for personal and “Needs of the Navy”.  Examples are training opportunities, AT conflict, ENWGS planning, or Gaining Command support.  RS/Flex drills must be pre-approved (by CO, XO, DH) no later than 1200 on the Friday before the Drill Weekend.

(b)     Timing.   RS/Flex drills generally will be performed on an alternate NAR drill weekend.  RS or Flex drills may be performed at other times to satisfy the needs of the Navy, but require special approval by the XO or CO.  The NAR PM will not grant an off-weekend drill without squadron approval.

(c)     Procedures.   Use the Chain of Command.  Contact your Department Head first; he will assign make up IDT dates as well as work and/or to be performed (In the absence of the Dept. Head, contact the Assistant DH, XO, or CO in that order).  The Department Head will then contact the Manpower Officer (preferably by e-mail).  The Manpower Officer will close the loop with an e-mail or phone verification that your RS is scheduled

 

2.        ANNUAL PHYSICALS: Annual Physicals are now done on the first drill weekend of the month only.  Check in prior to 0730 at NAVAIRES medical on the day scheduled.  Call in muster with Department Head at 0745.  All reservists age 28 and above completing a long form physical, will be tested for cholesterol, triglycerides, and fasting blood sugar.  1) No alcohol within 72 hours prior to exam.  2) No food or drink (except water) within 12 hours prior to exam.  3)  Remove contact lens within 48 hours prior to exam and bring eyeglasses.  EACH UNIT MEMBER IS RESPONSIBLE FOR ENSURING THAT ANNUAL PHYSICALS ARE CURRENT. 

3.        MEDICAL NOTE: Vaccination for yellow fever will be from 1000-1100 at the Branch Medical Clinic, building 601, on Saturday of the drill weekend.  Call  (619) 545-0620 for more information.

 4.        URINALYSIS TESTING: Members notified to provide urinalysis samples during a given month must report as ordered.  Members failing to do so will receive “U’s” in mustering and be subject to disciplinary measures as determined by the CO.

 5.        ZERO TOLERANCE: For more than a decade, the Navy has been committed to enforcement of it’s “ZERO TOLERANCE” policy on Drug abuse.  Navy drug screening labs utilize state-of-the-art equipment, which continue to produce forensically accurate and legally defendable results.  Sailors who use drugs will be caught.  Drug abuse is incompatible with Navy core values and will not be tolerated.  Recently, a few sailors within NAR San Diego failed their urinalysis screening and are currently being processed for separation. Don’t become a statistic.

6.        COMPUTER SECURITY/OPERATION: TACRON 12 allows us to use its personal computers and standard software programs to do our work.  TACRON 1294 is assigned its own password to enter the system.  Only authorized users have this code.  USE ONLY FLOPPY DISKS IN TACRON 12 MACHINES.   The software standard for TACRON 1294 is the Office 97 suite that includes: Microsoft Word, Microsoft Excel, and Microsoft PowerPoint.  Save all files on 1.44 MB HD diskettes.

7.        PAY PROBLEMS: Personnel should resolve their pay issues by contacting Reserve Services at 545-3270.  Further problems should be addressed to the assistance center at 1-888-297-7194, fax: 1-901-874-2779 or www.ppac@pensnet.navy.mil.

8.        BOQ / BEQ RESERVATIONS: Out of area (greater than 50 miles) members who need a room for the drill weekend MUST make berthing reservations through the NAR supply department at least two week prior to the drill weekend.  Those failing to request a reservation at least two weeks in advance will have to arrange berthing at their own expense.  Members may request a room by signing the berthing log in the NAR supply office or by phone, COMM: (619) 545-4550, DSN: 735-4550, TOLL FREE: (800) 828-9202 ext. 4550, by FAX: (619) 545-5190, DSN FAX: 735-5190, or on-line at sdberthing@cnrf.nola.navy.mil.  Members MUST confirm their reservation three days prior to their stay by contacting NAR supply during working hours, 0700 – 1530.  Those wishing to cancel a reservation must do so at least two days prior to the drill weekend.

·         Members may make reservations up to one year in advance at the NAR supply office.

·          Any member arriving after midnight should give advance notice by calling the NAR supply department or the BOQ / BEQ to prevent cancellation of your reservation.

·         Members may request a BOQ / BEQ reservation for any of the following: Regular Drill, or RS.  The NAR supply department will assist in obtaining a reservation for members but makes no guarantees.

9.     ADVANCEMENT REQUIREMENTS (ALL E-6 & BELOW): In order to ensure proper service record entries, any advancement requirements (normally given to PSD/ATD) must be turned in to the Retention Department.  Do not turn in these items yourself.  Retention shall send a representative each weekend to PSD/ATD on your behalf, ensuring these items are entered into your service record.  Training manuals for advancement are now available online only at http://www.advancement.cnet.navy.mil. Remember that personnel in special programs (APG, AIA, RESCORE-R) must still complete courses as required by their enlistment contract.

10.     COMMUNITY VOLUNTEERS: TACRON 12 is currently involved in both a weekly project and a monthly project.  Every Wednesday from 1230-1345, the unit provides tutoring and general teacher assistance to Penn Elementary School students.  The school is located in Paradise Hills.  The point of contact can be reached at 437-3671.  Additionally, TACRON 12 is providing assistance for the “Habitat for Humanity” project, where homes are built for underprivileged communities. Fifteen (15) volunteers are taken every last Wednesday of the month for an 8-hour workday.  Support for these TACRON 12 volunteer efforts is encouraged for all TACRON 1294 hands. 

11.     FAMILY SERVICE CENTER UPDATE: Family Service Center has now changed its name to Fleet & Family Support Center.  FFSC programs are now available for every military member as well as their families.  Programs include General Life Skills, Personal Financial Management, Transition and Spouse Employment Assistance.  Reservations are required for most programs.  These programs are FREE.  Locations are NB San Diego (619) 556-7404, NB Point Loma (619) 553-8306, NASNI (619) 545-6071.  FFSC monthly schedule can be found at www.cnrsw.navy.mil/fsc/fsc.htm.

12.     “MY BOSS IS A PATRIOT” PROGRAM:  Anyone interested in recognizing their manager/supervisor for their support of your involvement in the reserves may participate in the “My Boss is a Patriot” program.  A certificate will be presented to your boss in a ceremony either here or at your work.  More information and applications can be found online at http://www.esgr.org.
