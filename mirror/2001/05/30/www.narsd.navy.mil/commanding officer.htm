Commanding Officer

Captain R. Kent Hudgens

     A native of Richmond, Virginia, Captain Hudgens was awarded an NROTC scholarship to attend the University of North Carolina, in Chapel Hill.  He graduated in May 1977, with a Bachelor of Arts degree in English and was commissioned as an Ensign in the United States Navy.

     He immediately commenced aviation training at the Naval Aviation Schools Command in Pensacola, Florida and was designated a Naval Aviator 16 months later after completing advanced jet training in Beeville, Texas.  Captain Hudgens remained in Texas for an additional 15 months as a flight instructor in VT-26.

     Reporting to VF-101, NAS Oceana, Virginia in January 1980, Captain Hudgens received training in the F-14A Tomcat.  He transferred to VF-143 upon completion of training, served aboard USS DWIGHT D. EISENHOWER (CVN-69) for two major deployments, and was awarded first place honors in the 1981 East Coast Fighter Derby. 

     Captain Hudgens resigned his active duty commission in March 1983, and affiliated with the Naval Reserve as a Selected Reservist in VF-1486, where he served as an F-14A instructor pilot at NAS Oceana.  Captain Hudgens elected to be recalled to active duty in August 1989, as an officer specializing in the training and administration of reservists.

     Captain Hudgens completed a department head tour in VF-301 at NAS Miramar and then transferred to NAS New Orleans where he served as the Operations Officer.  He was then reassigned to the staff of Commander, Naval Reserve Force as Commanding Officer, Staff Enlisted Personnel and Director of Administration.

     Captain Hudgens transferred to NAF Washington in May 1996, as the Executive Officer.  In April 1998, he became the Staff Director for the Deputy Assistant Secretary of the Navy for Reserve Affairs.  Captain Hudgens assumed command of Naval Air Reserve San Diego in August 2000. 

Captain Hudgens’ personal decorations include the Legion of Merit, the Meritorious Service Medal (two awards), the Navy and Marine Corps Commendation Medal and the Navy and Marine Corps Achievement Medal (two awards).

     Captain Hudgens is the proud father of two children, Tyler and Philip, who reside in northern Virginia.
