Reporting Aboard

When arriving in San Diego, report in the uniform of the day to the Naval Station San Diego, located at 32nd St. when in port the ship resides at a variety of piers. Contacting the USS KINKAID, (619) 556-4480, or Naval Station Information, (619) 556-1011, ahead of time will help reduce confusion. If the ship is underway, report to TPU (Transient Personnel Unit) also located at the Naval Station San Diego, Bldg 56.

Driving Instructions:

Directions:
The Naval Station San Diego can be reached by taking one of the Interstate Highways; I-5, I-8, I-15, or I-805; or US Highways; 54, 92, or 96 to I-5.

From the North:
Take the 28th St exit towards National Ave/San Diego
Drive left at the fork in the ramp
Turn right onto S. 28th ST; continue for about 0.3 miles
Turn left onto Harbor Drive
Turn right at the second light.

From the South:
Take the Harbor Drive exit and continue past the stop light.

Driving on Base:
To drive on base at the Naval Station, San Diego, you will need a DoD decal. You can get a decal or temporary pass at the Pass & Decal Office. You will need a valid driver's license, current vehicle registration, a military ID card, and proof of insurance.

Housing:

If you need information regarding your housing situation, the Family Housing Office is an important stop. They provide assignment and referral assistance for all personnel attached to San Diego commands. There are 8,300 government owned and leased homes available throughout San Diego and waiting lists exist for all sites. Personnel should plan to rent in the civilian community until military housing is available. Housing referral specialists will help you locate homes to but or rent which best suit your needs. The Chief of Naval Operations requires all military members to check in with the Housing Referral Service Office prior to negotiating any off-base rental or lease agreement.

Government Housing:
All officers and enlisted personnel accompanied by their bona fide family members are eligible to apply for Military Family Housing. To apply for quarters, you need a local housing application form or DD Form 1746 (Application for Assignment to Housing), a copy of orders showing your detachment date and certification of family members. Application may be made in person or submitted by mail. Those submitted by mail must be confirmed within 30 days of reporting to San Diego.

Your control date (effective date of application) determines your placement on the housing waiting list. The control date will usually be the date of detachment from your last permanent duty station, if the application is received within 30 days of your arrival in San Diego.

Personnel who apply for MFH will receive a written acknowledgement indicating the approximate waiting period for quarters. The waiting times quoted are approximate times only and can fluctuate rapidly.

You (or a member of your immediate family) must confirm an advance quarters application within 30 days of reporting for duty of arrival in the San Diego area. Failure to confirm will result in the cancellation of your application.

Inquires into MFH should be directed to the Family Housing Office.

Civilian Housing:
The Military Housing Office maintains a free listing service with over 5,000 rental and for-sale properties. The service is available to all married and single personnel as well as DOD civilians. Referral Specialists provide current information on typical rental costs, deposit fees, location, public transportation availability, crime rates, school statistics and other facts about civilian housing in San Diego. A counselor can interview you to help you determine your personal and family needs regarding affordable housing in San Diego.

Family Service Center:

The Navy Family Service Center, located at Naval Station 32nd Street, welcomes military families relocating to the San Diego area. All services offered to married couples and their families are also available to the single sailor. The Relocation Assistance Program helps service members and their families who are coming to the San Diego area. Specialists provide individual assistance and everything you need to know about relocating, but didn't know who to ask. For more information on the wide variety of services offered by the Family Service Center, call (619) 556-7404.
