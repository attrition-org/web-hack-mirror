Star Trek: Away Team
[Game Was: Purchased]

While Activision was kind enough to send a copy my way, I picked up a copy of the boxed version since the gold master copy will be going out to a reviewer. Of course the fact that the copy had no manual with it didn't help any either. Yes, I feel naked without my manual... no matter how small. ;) As always, lets start with the basics...

Install:

There are no install size options here, and not even a mention as to how much space the game will take up once installed. I really dislike install programs such as this. What does it take to give me an option or two or at least let me know how much information you're going to dump on my drive.

The game eats up 402 MB of space which is about average for today's games. The game installed without incident.

Manual:

The 5.5 x 7.5 inch, 41 page manual has a paper cover to match the pages within. Kind of a cheap out, but at least there's a manual, right? For those that want a more in-depth manual, one is offered via acrobat, so the four of you that actually open that up should have a grand time.

The paper manual included goes over game mechanics as well as backgrounds for the entire 17 member crew. A single page of backstory sets the stage, while weapons, skills, equipment, and enemies are also covered. A small section is dedicated to Multiplayer as well.

The game comes on one CD and is enclosed in a plastic jewel case.

Game:

While the game is played in Real Time, they have implemented a pause feature that works quite nicely. When one of you crew members takes a hit off screen, the action is paused immediately so you can access the situation. (You can toggle this on and off under the options menu.) The way its integrated into the playing system is excellent and gives the game a great pacing. Not quite as fast as say, Fate of the Dragon, which makes for a nice change of pace.

The graphics are first rate and the controls are pretty easy to get used to. While no tutorial mission is technically available, you do start off with Commander Data (Voice done be the actual actor, Brent Spiner.) walking you through the first mission and some of the second.

The sounds are right out of the series, starting from the Install Program and taking you right into the game. Very nicely done. There are missions inside Space Stations, Ships, Out side on alien planets and more. There is a large variety of weapons and equipment, along with crew members having special abilities (Such as a Vulcan Mind Meld or Vulcan Nerve Pinch) in order to get the job done.

After the first mission, you have the opportunity to chose which crew members you plan to beam down as the away team. The Mission Briefing's are done nicely as well and let you know what skills and equipment you'll need to get the job done.

The maps I have seen so far have been extremely detailed and quite large. The isometric view makes it easy not to get lost however. Crew Members can run, walk, sneak and you can even see the enemies field of view (ala Commandos). As you would expect in this type of game, you'll need to use a combination of stealth, tactics, and brute force to get the job done.

Topping it off with an in mission save, this is two in a row for Activision. It looks like Star Trek Voyager: Elite Force is not the only Star Trek game that's a "Must Have' anymore.

64 MB RAM
Quad-speed CD-ROM drive
500 MB Disk Space

Fate of the Dragon
[Game Was: Purchased]

Real Time Strategy fans are in for a treat today. Two games are being offered and both look quite good. My guess is, most will go with the topic that appeals to them first and wait for the other to hit Value of the Week. With two demos available (Single play and Multiplay) there's a lot known about this one. If you've tried the demos already, you're sure to know whether or not the game is for you. As always, we'll start with the basics...

Install:

The following size options are available upon installation:

Maximum Install: 340 MB
Typical Install: 270 MB

I went with the maximum install and the game installed without incident. Nice to see a game that's not eating up a huge amount of your drive. BTW: I keep getting asked how big my drive is, since I put so many games on it. I have two drives... a 60 GB and a 40 GB. I use the 40 for games that always stay on my system and then I use the 60 for games I'm currently playing. It gets a lot of install and uninstall action.

Manual:

No weight awards here, but at least we're bigger than a Jewel case. The 5 x 7 inch, 60 page manual seems to do a decent job of handing out the game mechanics for you. It has a nice amount of backstory available, which helps set the stage for the game.

Units, Buildings, Resources and Equipment are all explained in detail.

The game comes on one CD and is enclosed in a plastic jewel case.

Game:

As I mentioned earlier, with the release of not one, but two demos, most gamers know what to expect. If you passed on this, Think Age of Empires II, done with an Asian theme. They have added some very nice touches to make the game a bit different, such as playing with two maps (One of your tow and one of the whole battlefield.), introducing Sacrifices, Disasters, and some other odds and ends that make the game unique in small ways.

Trade and Diplomacy play a part in the game as well and you'll have plenty to research, which is always appealing to me. There are difficulty settings which you can chose and of you finish all missions on "hard" a new level of difficulty is revealed, called "Nightmare". 

When starting a campaign, you select from among three portraits as who to play as. A stand alone Tutorial is available, however the Campaign starts you off with its own training mission as well. The graphics are very nicely done and should keep most RTS fans happy.

I made a big deal of the very nice 100% cotton (Is there anything else? If there is, it doesn't touch my body.) long sleeve, comfy shirt and put it on. His eyes lit up and he keeps telling me how nice I look. Ah the riches.

Many have been asking about my wife and how I was lucky enough to marry such a pretty lady, being as ugly as I am. It's kind of an interesting story, so I guess I'll share it with you. Those of you looking for gaming information can stop reading here, as there won't be any at this point.

It was November of 1989. My partner was getting married and all I kept telling him was, he was signing his death warrant. I had been married extremely young for a short period of time and swore I would never do it again. The only positive thing out of the entire experience for me was the two gorgeous children I have.

I went to the wedding alone, since there were a lot of common friends among us. In New York, it's not uncommon for guests to cost a couple about $100 a head to have at a wedding, so I told him since I wasn't dating anyone special at the time, I would go alone. Many of my friends took my lead, since that meant more of us could go. It was a great time.

I went to the Church first only because it was a few blocks from where I lived. As I stood and watched the bridesmaids come down the isle, I was bowled over by this beautiful brunette. She was breath taking.

As you leave the Church, a line forms so you can congratulate the Bride and Groom. As I got to my partner, instead of congratulating him I said, "Hey. Who's the brunette? You know I love brunette's and you've never told me about her?!"

"She's engaged and she's my wife's best friend. Stay Away.", he replied.

During the reception I didn't pay much attention to her since she was "off limits" but I had a great time and plenty of adult beverages. We had a designated driver, so over indulging wasn't a problem and most of us did indeed. As it all came to an end, I saw my beautiful brunette coming down a staircase with no one else around. I walked over to her, drunk as a skunk and slurred...

"You're the most beautiful thing here tonight."

Classy, huh? She smiled at me, said thank you, and off she went. Two weeks went by until my partner was back to work. As soon as he was, I asked a bunch of questions.... I found out that her name was Mary Jo and she was supposed to be married already, but they had called off the wedding. It was on hold at the moment and they weren't getting along very well. Super.

In general conversation, I would ask my partner about Mary Jo and came to find out that she was a single woman again. I told him I wanted to be set up with her but my partner refused. I explained to him that out of respect to him and his wife, I would never do such a thing, but he was adamant. As time went by (Many months), I kept asking about her and he stood his ground. Finally, one day I called him at home and his wife answered the phone. We were very friendly and just started talking about things in general. Finally, I said "Do you know Jerry won't let me meet Mary Jo?"

My friends were shocked when I told them I was getting married, since they knew how dead set against it I was. I never believed in "Soul Mates"... because anyone can fall in love with multiple people. However, I truly believe Mary Jo and I are from one soul. The day I married Mary Jo, which will be 9 years come this September, I thought I was deeply in love with her. Little did I know I hadn't even scratched the surface. The Moon Project
[Game Was: Purchased]

Earth 2150 was one of the better Real Time Strategy games ever to grace my PC. I really liked being able to set up a Home base, while heading out and taking on other missions. The graphics fed the shallow gamer in me and there was enough here to keep me busy for quite sometime. While some may see this as "more of the same", there is enough here for fans of the original to consider the purchase. As always, we'll start with the basics...

Install:

The game offer the following install sizes:

Full Install: 635 MB
Full Spawn Install: 342 MB
Minimum Install: 258 MB
Minimum Spawn Install: 258 MB
Typical Install: 342 MB

I went with a full install and the game installed without incident.

Manual:

I often talk about the Trade Marked "Gone Gold In Store Lift Test". Grizzled veterans of gaming know exactly what that means... Picking up a box and feeling how heavy it is, so you have an idea of what to expect when it comes to manual and any goodies which may be inside. It's like buying a Crumb Cake and looking for the heaviest one since that one most likely has more crumbs. ;)

I'm happy to say this game passes with flying colors. The 5.5 x 8.5 inch, 72 page manual does an excellent job of defining the gaming mechanics, units, upgrades, buildings and adding to the backstory, as well as adding in a timeline. Very well done.

What gives this game the majority of its weight however, is that a Free Prima Games Strategy Guide is included! Not a "mini-manual", but a full fledged, 160 page manual covering the entire game. Super.

The game comes on one CD and is enclosed in a plastic jewel case.

Game:

As I mentioned earlier, there is certainly a feel of "more of the same" here. However, with a pretty quick release since the original (Released 06/13/00), this is certainly a well developed addition to the series. 

I thought there would only be one campaign focusing on the Lunar Corporation, however there are 3 full campaigns for all three sides once again. The European Dynasty comes with three full tutorial missions outside of the campaign, while the other two come with a single tutorial mission.

All in all there are about 31 missions (Excluding the tutorials.) among the three campaigns, which is a lot of bang for your buck. Graphically, the game looks almost identical to the original, however 2150 was excellent from the start.

We didn't wait four years for this and then have to say "What? This is more of the same!". There's a lot here for a simple 9 month wait and well worth your time if you enjoyed the first game.

Star Wars: Battle for Naboo
[Game Was: Purchased]

Fond memories of Rebel Assault dancing in my head moved me to grab this one. One of the very first games to take full advantage of the newest piece of hardware out for PC's... the CD-ROM, it had some great gameplay as we flew around as Rookie One.

Following in the same footsteps, Battle for Naboo brings us some good looking Star Wars action. As always, lets start with the basics...

Install:

As you pop in the CD, you'll see the familiar Lucas Arts Splash Screen that pops up for installing and playing a game. The install program offers two different install options, which I found kind of odd...

Automatic Installation: 150 MB
Manual Installation: 150 MB

I went with the manual and the game installed just like any game. Everything was pretty automatic as it was, but I assume the "automatic" option does offer directory changes and the like.

The game installed without incident.

Manual:

I was a bit disappointed here. Lucas Arts doesn't usually put in wonderful manuals with their software, but they're not all that bad either. Here, we're given a Jewel Case manual of a mere 15 pages. The game is a very straight forward and doesn't really need much in the way of documentation, but some backstory would have been nice.

The game comes on one CD and is enclosed in a plastic jewel case.

Game:

As with Rebel Assault, there is no save system here. You must finish the mission in order to move on to the next, with the game automatically saving what levels you have completed. As you move forward with the game, you can play old levels, trying to better your score.

You may also use different craft, as you unlock them, on the earlier levels. That's got some fun aspects to it if the crafts get faster and more powerful as the game goes on. Taking a 10th level vehicle into the first level and wiping everyone out quite quickly could be loads of fun. :)

The game supports Keyboard Controls, Mouse, Game Pad, as well as Joystick. The configuration is customizable and very easy to use. The graphics are excellent as expected and this looks to be a nice little diversion.

Pentium II or Athlon class 233 MHz or higher.
64MB RAM
8MB PCI or AGP 3D Hardware Accelerator required.
Quad-speed CD-ROM drive

I already have news of another title that has hit gold status and I think my snooping around will turn up one or two more today or tomorrow. I would love for it to be this busy all year long, but lets face it... If it was, we would all be broke. Which games to buy is a tough call with some great games hitting shelves. However, it all comes down to what type of game you like the most, which can make it a bit easier.

I've been so busy with Gone Gold, I haven't been able to do much surfing. I took this morning to do a little surfing and it was a great surprise to see our site mentioned on CGO, one of my daily stops. I have to admit, I still get a kick out of seeing links to Gone Gold on sites that don't often link to other sites. The link from Old Man Murray was one of the biggest surprises. :)

If we ever make a mention in their print magazine (Which keeps getting better.), that will bring the site full circle... with mentions in all three major magazines. The little goals that make me happy. ;)

I was posting news last week and sent an ICQ message to a fellow webmaster, letting him know about a game that went gold. He said "It's nice to see the old Rich LaPorte back.". It was then that it hit me... I am feeling more like myself more and more. It's almost as if I see a finish line and I've finally made it. 

While my disabilities are permanent, the illness could have been, but it seems to be a battle that I've won and it feels great. I'm sure my section of the site suffered during it all, especially the hard days, and I want to thank all of you for sticking with me and encouraging me to carry on. For now,

Let me get to work.

Safe Tour,
