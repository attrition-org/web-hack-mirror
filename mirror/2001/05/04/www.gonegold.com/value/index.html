We have a fine mixture of titles this week, from a number of sources, with some of the bargains particularly interesting.

Value of the Week

Cossacks: European Wars - $27.99 @ Best Buy -

A fine strategy review site, PC Strategy Games, typically uses 3 writers in their appraisal of games. All 3 rated Cossacks in the 90's, with an average of 92%, characterizing the title as "...a worthy addition to the RTS genre that covers a fascinating period (17th & 18th centuries) in European history. Enjoy it - and learn something at the same time."

For titles listed today, be sure to check our  comprehensive Gold Guide for up to date review synopses and other useful information on 470+ games.

Other Newer Titles of Note:

Evil Islands - $29.99 @ Best Buy -

EuroGamer found this "a huge, varied and utterly addictive game; ...if you're a role-playing fan looking to loose yourself for a few weeks, this could be just the thing" (80% rating).

Majesty: The Northern Expansion - $14.90 @ Compuexpert, till Tuesday noon -

This add-on pack is essential for lovers of the original, with Game-Over awarding 71% - "...although it has only 12 quests, those quests should give you 50 hours or more of gameplay." [The inclusion of the "LaPorte Lodge", makes this a "Must Have". ~Guess Who]

Clive Barker's Undying - $29.99 @ Circuit City -

This wonderful title was given a Pure Gold award (90% rating) by James Wink in his review. He felt the game successfully "walks a fine line between horror and being a typical shooter," with a "story progression that manages to surprise and raise itself well above the mundane." Do a price match at EB, use their online rebate for $10, and bring it down to a net of $20.

Tribes 2 - $39.99 @ Circuit City; $34.90 @ Compuexpert -

Gamezilla gave this one a 90% rating. "If you like team warfare and like online gaming, then this game was custom made for you. If you enjoy first person shooters, but only single player, then you won't have much fun." Make the fun a little cheaper by using a price match at EB, and a $10 online rebate.

Ultima Online: The Third Dawn - $27.99 @ Best Buy -

Reviews aren't particularly favorable overall with this one, with Gamespot awarding 69% - "...although the switch to a 3D engine may be seen as an attempt to attract new players, Third Dawn is nonetheless best suited for Ultima Online current users." Interestingly, 146 reader ratings accompanying the review page average only 45%.

Riddle of the Sphinx - $14.99 @ Best Buy -

Earning a 75% rating (Unrefined Ore) in our review, this was viewed as having considerable of merit. "As contrasted with some of today's graphically rich and gameplay hollow PC games, ROTS provides many hours of intriguing and suspenseful Pyramidic exploration."

Under $20 Bargain Bin - 

Battle Isle: The Andosia War - $14.90 @ Compuexpert -

Don't let this one slip by. As PC Strategy Games, giving an 85% rating indicates: "Inspired gaming hybrid that seamlessly links RTS and turn based playing styles. The whole package oozes quality and bears all the hallmarks of a real labor of love."

Traitors Gate - $14.99 @ Best Buy -

One of the best adventure titles of the last year or so, this "Thief-like" approach was awarded a B+ by Just Adventure, who felt it was "nicely designed, having all the best elements of adventure gaming - detailed pre-rendered scenes, stunning and accurate looking cut scenes, a well developed storyline, and puzzles that are thought through when devised so that they carry the story forward rather than bringing it to a grinding halt."

Lands of Lore III - $7.99 @ EBGames -

I replayed this a few weeks ago, and appreciate the rating (75%) and review from Game Power: "A common complaint of gamers is that too many titles are released nowadays that are nothing but eye candy without gameplay. Not this baby. LOL III is loaded with gameplay, but is hobbled by graphics that don't quite come up to snuff. Get past those, and you've got quite a treat in store"

Sid Meier's Antietam! - $9.99 @ EBGames -

This follow-up to Gettysburg was enjoyed by the Computer Games Online reviewer: "Antietam! does the same wonderful job at recreating the "feel" of battle that Gettysburg! did so well. Yes, you get tension, stress, and frustration in a way few other wargames can replicate, but you also get thrills and a strong sense of satisfaction at a job well done, at a plan that comes together just right" (80% rating).
