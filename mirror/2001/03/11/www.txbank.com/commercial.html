Rate Information for all Interest Bearing Checking Accounts

All Commercial Checking Accounts (interest and non-interest earning) are analyzed monthly to determine if the account’s Investable Balances are sufficient to cover the Cost of Services provided.  The Investable Balance is the average collected balance less the regulatory reserve requirement.  The Investable Balance is multiplied by the Earnings Rate (the 90 day Treasury bid rate on the first business day of the month being analyzed) to determine Earnings Credit.  The Earnings Credit is used to offset the Cost of Services.

No Service Charge will be incurred if the Earnings Credit is sufficient to offset the cost of Services.

Commercial Checking Account customers can request to have their check images on a CD-ROM monthly.  For more information on how to obtain this service, please contact a loan officer or new accounts representative today.

RATE INFORMATION FOR ALL INTEREST BEARING CHECKING ACCOUNTS

At our discretion, we may change the interest rate on your Account.  Interest will be compounded every month.  Interest will be credited to your account every month.  We use the daily collected balance method to calculate the interest on your account.  This method applies a daily periodic rate to the principal in the account each day.

ACCURAL OF INTEREST OF NONCASH DEPOSITS.  Interest begins to accrue no later than the business day we receive credit for the deposit of noncash items (for example, checks).  We may require not less than 7 days, notice in writing before each withdrawal from an interest bearing account other than a time deposit.
