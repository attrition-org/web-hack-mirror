LABOR MARKET ANALYSIS

The Labor Market Analysis is an annual study conducted to help improve the match between the labor needs of employers and the skills of job seekers by providing current, localized occupational information. The program is produced locally through a partnership between the Madera County Private Industry Council and the State of California Employment Development Department (EDD), Labor Market Information Division (LMID).  The program is produced as part of a state-wide project called the California Cooperative Occupational Information System (CCOIS).

The report which is a comprehensive study of  74 occupations presents the most important findings from the recently completed project.  It identifies the region's strengths and weaknesses, contains specific local information on training and experience requirements, wages, benefits, and employment and industry trends. The data is collected by survey from local employers and training providers.  The document is also accessible in portable document format (PDF) at http://www.calmis.cahwnet.ca.gov


Adobe Acrobat Reader free software is required to download and view PDF files.

The Madera County Private Industry Council and the State of California Employment Development Department - Labor Market Division have made a substantial effort to ensure the accuracy of the information contained in this report.  We are committed to exceptional standards of research and to high quality labor market information for local users.  Labor market research is often impacted by a variety of factors in a short period of time.  Therefore, the data must be viewed as a photograph which captures a picture of reality at a specific period in time when the research was done.

We encourage you to use the data with confidence, due to the commitment to quality and years of research that are provided by the state of California Cooperative Occupational Information staff.

Any questions regarding the data contained in this report should be brought to the attention of the Madera County Private Industry Council.  Please contact Nivia deOliveira, Research Analyst  at (559) 673-7031 or ngreen@maderacoe.k12.ca.us.
