Federal Relations

 

 

May 26, 1999

The Honorable Tom Bliley
Chairman
Committee on the Commerce
U.S. House of Representatives
2409 Rayburn House Office Building
Washington, DC 20515

Dear Chairman Bliley:

I write on behalf of the Association of American Universities, the American Council on Education, and the National Association of State Universities and Land-Grant Colleges to commend you for the introduction of H.R. 1858, The Consumer and Investor Access to Information Act of 1999. Data and information are the foundation of scientific and scholarly research and teaching. Preserving the ability of universities, their faculties and students to produce, analyze, integrate, and disseminate data is essential to sustaining the productivity of this nation’s integrated system of academic research and education. This research and education system has played a key role in U.S. leadership in science and technology and U.S. higher education is one of our most robust industries, drawing millions of students worldwide.

At the same time, universities, their faculties and students will benefit from policies that preserve the economic incentive to produce valuable new collections of information. As both producers and users of databases, universities have a vested interest in policies that will protect the proprietary and information integrity of databases while assuring that research and educational programs can effectively access and use databases and their elements for the creation and dissemination of new knowledge. This legislation accomplishes these objectives by responding to the needs of database producers and consumers with effectively balanced provisions.

We look forward to working with you as this promising legislative initiative moves forward.

Sincerely,

John C. Vaughn
