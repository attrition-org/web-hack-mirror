Commerce Department Announces Internet Grants Availability

The Commerce Department recently announced its plans to award approximately $17 million in grants to state, local and non-profit entities to extend the benefits of the Internet and other information technology services to all Americans, especially those in underserved communities. Applications are due by March 11, 1999.

Since 1994, the Commerce Department’s Telecommunications and Information Infrastructure Assistance Program (TIIAP) has provided more than $118 million in matching grants to organizations that develop innovative, practical projects that use advanced telecommunications and information technology. Administered by the National Telecommunications and Infrastructure Administration (NTIA), the TIIAP has funded projects focusing on innovations in education and training, support for the creation of more responsive public institutions, enhancement of economic development in rural and disadvantaged areas, and increased public access to health care. For example, in 1997, Pennsylvania State University received a grant in order to develop a video teleconferencing system that allowed remotely located nurses to provide home health services to diabetics in Philadelphia County. The University of Illinois, Urbana received funds to increase the level of Internet access to low income communities in their area. The University of Tennessee Medical Center at Knoxville received a grant to develop a tele-medicine system that provides care for trauma patients by enabling image and voice communication among ambulances, rural emergency departments, and a Level I Trauma Center. And the University of Utah at Salt Lake City received $369,951 to implement a video network for communities in rural Utah that provides services, such as physical and occupational therapy, to children with special needs.

According to NTIA, grant applicants are encouraged to consider the use of advanced network technologies to enhance the quality and efficiency of services delivered through non-profit networks. Also, they are encouraged to demonstrate an involvement in cross-cutting projects utilizing partnerships among five application areas-community networking, education, culture, and lifelong learning, health, public safety and public services.

NTIA will hold a series of regional, technical assistance workshops to discuss the TIIAP funding priorities, application requirements, and lessons learned from previous TIIAP grantees. The workshops will be held on January 26 in Albuquerque, New Mexico, January 29 in Memphis, Tennessee, February 3 in Boston, Massachusetts; and February 11, in Chicago, Illinois.
