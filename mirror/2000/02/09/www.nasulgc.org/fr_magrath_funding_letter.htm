Federal Relations

Letter from NASULGC President C. Peter Magrath to House and Senate leaders on behalf of member insitutions urging sustaining funding for research and student financial assistance programs.

 

August 3, 1999

 

The Honorable Thomas A. Daschle
United States Senate
509 Senate Hart Office Building
Washington, D.C. 20510

Dear Senator Daschle:

I am writing to you on behalf of the 203 research-intensive public universities, including the University of South Dakota and South Dakota State University that comprise the National Association of State Universities and Land-Grant Colleges. As Congress continues to deliberate the FY2000 budget, the NASULGC institutions urge you and your colleagues to sustain the federal investment in research and student financial assistance that has been a crucial element in the prosperity and growth experienced by our nation over the past fifty years.

The institutions listed below have a long tradition of serving our nation, states, and communities as partners in education and economic development. We have always done so in partnership with the federal government, which has provided critical resources throughout the long history of public higher education. We are now deeply troubled that the current fiscal contraints outlined in the Balanced Budget Act will seriously weaken the partnership. Significant reductions in the federal commitment to research and financial aid programs could impair the ability of our institutions to generate the research advances and skilled workforce that have been linchpins in our nation’s economic growth and development.

In this era of economic prosperity and projected budget surpluses, we believe that now is the time to bolster the federal investment in our future. Faculty and scholars on our campuses are on the verge of discoveries that will help propel our nation even more forward in the next century. Advances in such areas as biomedical and health sciences, information technology, and defense technologies would be severely compromised under the current budget regimen. But if the budget restraints can be eased, we will have the vital resources to enable us as a nation to continue our economic development and prosperity.

We appreciate your thoughtful consideration of our concerns and look forward to working with you on this critical issue.

Cordially,

 

C. Peter Magrath
President
