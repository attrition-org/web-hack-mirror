GLEN CANYON RECEIVES GRANT FROM EXXON FOR P.A.R.K.S. PROGRAM

Glen Canyon National Recreation Area received a grant for science education made possible by an innovative partnership between the National Park Service (NPS), Exxon Corporation, the National Park Foundation (NPF), and the National Science Teachers Association (NSTA). Glen Canyon was one of 36 National Parks chosen from a competitive process that was open to all 378 National Parks.

The Parks as Resources for Knowledge in Science (P.A.R.K.S.) program builds on NSTA’s "Building a Presence for Science" initiative, which aims to improve science education by helping implement the National Science Education Standards.

Throughout the three-year duration of the P.A.R.K.S. program, Ohio State University will conduct assessments to ensure program effectiveness and facilitate replications. Upon the completion of the P.A.R.K.S. program, NSTA will publish a handbook on how to incorporate the national science education standards into education programs. The publication will be available to all National Parks and the education community.

The National Park Foundation is the official nonprofit partner of the National Park Service. Created by Congress in 1967, the Foundation raises support from corporations, foundations and individuals to preserve and enhance America’s National Parks. Over the past five years, NPF has raised more than $42 million in direct support for the National Parks.

In September of 1998 the Exxon Corporation announced a pledge of $1.5 million to the PARKS program for the enhancement of science and environmental education programs. This grant, which will be distributed to 36 National Parks over a three-year-period, will promote science literacy, provide opportunities for students and teachers to use National Parks as hands-on learning laboratories and strengthen students’ appreciation of National Park resources. Exxon’s pledge to NPF is part of the corporation’s ongoing commitment to education. In 1998, Exxon Corporation and the Exxon Education Foundation together provided more than $25 million to support education initiatives in the United States.

With more than 53,000 members nationwide, the National Science Teachers Association is America’s largest organization committed to excellence and innovation in science teaching and learning for all. Through conventions, award-winning publications, journals and professional development opportunities, NSTA keeps science educators current in teaching, science content, research and available resources and technology.
