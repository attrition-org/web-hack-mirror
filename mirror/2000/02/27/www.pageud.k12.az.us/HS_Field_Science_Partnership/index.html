PUSD Field Science Partnership


A partnership between Page High School (Page, AZ) and
The National Park Service at Glen Canyon National Recreation Area 

 

  

In May 1996, the Page Unified School District requested funds from the Arizona Community Foundation to begin the development of a partnership with the National Park Service at Glen Canyon National Recreation Area (GCNRA).  At the same time, National Park Service officials submitted funding requests to the National Park Foundation and the "Parks and Classrooms" program to provide matching funds for the development of the partnership.  The primary goal of this partnership would be to increase student learning for district students while at the same time enhancing research activities within the Recreation Area.

     This program involves The National Park Service and a specialized Page High School course that is funded by the Arizona Community Foundation, the National Park Foundation, the National Park Service, and a recent grant from the Exxon Corporation through the National Park Foundation.  This grant is part of the "Parks As Resources For Knowledge in Science" program and will be used to integrate the National Science Education Standards into the Field Science Partnership. 

The class is currently involved in two projects:

  1. Bacterial monitoring on Lake Powell for total coliform and E. coli.

  2. Monitoring of an endangered fish, the razorback sucker in two local golf course ponds. 

Objectives met as a result of this partnership include:

Employment of a National Park Service Educational Specialist to provide coordination between participating agencies and school district instructors.

Use of professionals from outside the district in both learning and evaluation activities.

Conducting of research in partnership with National Park Service personnel and other agencies involved in natural resource management in the area.

Reporting of data to various audiences including interested scientific community members, funding entities, other students, and the Internet.
