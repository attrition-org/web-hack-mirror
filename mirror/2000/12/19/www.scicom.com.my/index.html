Four Palestinians killed on Sunday's confrontations with the Israelis
Palestine, Politics, 12/18/2000

Confrontation have been escalated on Sunday in various areas of the West bank and Gaza despite of the news which talked about Palestinian, Israeli and US consultations on resuming the negotiations. These news coupled with the escalation of the Israeli terrorism against the Palestinians.

The Palestinians Eyad Mahmoud Dawoud ( 27) and Ahmad Abdul Jalil Qassas ( 33) were killed by the Israeli fire bullets in the confrontations which took place in Rafah city, near Salah eddine gate on the Palestinian borders. As a result five Palestinian were hit, one of them was seriously wounded and they were admitted to the hospital.

Eye- witnesses said the Israeli occupation solders were opening fire randomly against the Palestinian citizens and that a special unit from the Israeli forces assassinated the activist at " the Fatah" movement Samih al-Murabi at Qalindya camp. Al-Murabi is 27 year old. The Palestinian Muhammad Shalash ( 18) was killed by the guns of the Israeli settlers near Ramullah.

Meantime, Palestinian demonstrations broke into several Palestinian areas and then the Palestinian youths started to strike Israeli positions by stones in Khan Younis, at the crossroad to Ghoushe Kteive settlement, in Hebron ( al-Khalil) and near the settlements and they damaged an Israeli military vehicle and two coaches carrying armed settlers near Ghoushe Kteive. They also attacked Jewish settlements in Jerusalem, and Gaza and wounded a settler near Jenin.

Meantime, Palestinians detonated an explosive near Eibal mountain when Israeli military vehicles passed by and attacked an Israeli on- foot patrol near Ramullah.

Meanwhile the Israeli forces continued their bombardment of the Palestinian cities and villages and missiles on Sunday were dropped on positions in al-Khalil ( Hebron), and Raheel dome and also in Bethlehem and Gaza and the Israeli forces carried out a vast detention campaign in various parts of the west bank and Gaza.

On the other hand, news reports said that the Palestinian President Yasser Arafat announced that a Palestinian delegations and another Israeli one will head for Washington within the few coming days to make consultations with the American administration on resuming the negotiations.

To this effect, the Israeli foreign minister Shloumo Bin Ami talked about a possible settlement with the Palestinians over al-Aqsa mosque.

He explained there should be a definite solution for the question of al- Aqsa mosque. He continued that the Palestinians and the Israelis will discuss in Washington the two issues of halting the Intifada and means of pushing forward bilateral talks.

Bin Ami added that what has been so far discussed between the two sides were Camp David ideas, and the red lines that israel still persistent on. He ruled out any Israeli concessions in the issue of having sovereignty over Jerusalem.
