Sheldon Jackson College is a special place for
Alaska Natives

If your heritage is among the indigenous peoples of The Great Land, we think you will be happy at Sheldon Jackson.

  1. Sheldon Jackson is a small college.  If you choose to attend one of the University of Alaska campuses, be prepared for big classes.  SJ's enrollment is 250 students!  One-on-one attention at SJ.  Walk downtown to Sitka. 

  2. Sheldon Jackson has generous financial assistance for Alaska Natives.  In addition to BIA and Native Corporation dollars for college students, SJ has scholarships that prefer Native students.

  3. Sheldon Jackson College was founded for Alaska Natives.  The school was founded in 1878, making it the oldest one in Alaska.  Originally led by John Brady (who later became first Governor of the Alaska Territory and is buried in Sitka), SJ was a vocational school for Tlingit boys, an elementary school, a high school, a junior college, and is today -- 120 years later! -- an accredited college offering two-year and four-year programs in:
    • aquaculture
    • business
    • elementary education
    • fisheries
    • human services
    • interdisciplinary studies
    • liberal arts
    • marine biology
    • natural resources
    • outdoor recreation
    • seafood technology
    • secondary (HS) education

  4. Serving Alaska Natives is an explicit principle of the SJC Mission.  We have an active Alaska Native Club which participates with local Native organizations.  Our teachers are sensitive to Native ways of learning.  Tutors are available if you need class help.

  5. SJC enrolls students from many Alaska Native culture groups.  Even though Southeast Alaska (where Sitka is located) is home to Tlingit, Haida, and Tsimshian, SJ enrolls all Alaska Native culture groups.

  6. The Sitka community has abundant Native services, for example:
    • Mt. Edgecumbe HS
    • SEARHC Hospital
    • Alaska Native Brotherhood and Sisterhood
    • Sitka Tribe of Alaska
    • Southeast Alaska Cultural Center
    • Sheldon Jackson Museum
    • SJ's Stratton Library Native Studies Collection
