On the other hand, Sheldon Jackson is an extraordinary college - in at least four ways:

#1 Well, how many colleges do you know of on an island in Alaska?!

  • SJ sits directly on the Pacific Ocean;
  • … amidst the 17-million-acre Tongass National Forest;
  • … surrounded by islands, mountains, and Mt. Edgecumbe volcano! (Don't worry! Edgecumbe has been dormant for 9000 years!)
  • Sitka was established by Tlingit Indians, then governed from 1799 until 1867 by Russians. October 18, 1867, Sitka: US "buys" Alaska from the Russians … 586,412 square miles for 2 cents/acre.
  • Average Sitka temperatures range from 34 to 62. A coastal, temperate climate.
  • Average Sitka rainfall range from 3.2 inches to 14.7 per month.
Alaska has…
    • World's largest concentration of bald eagles
    • World's largest carnivore: Kodiak brown bear
    • World's largest temperate rainforest (the Tongass, in which Sitka is located)
    • America's highest mountain: McKinley (Denali) at 20,320 feet
    • America's largest national park: Wrangell-St. Elias
    • 6640 miles of coastline. (Including islands, 33,900 miles of shoreline!)


#2 99-00 annual tuition of $6850!

50% below the national average for private colleges.
(And we also give $500,000 in scholarships.)

Each student pays a different amount to attend Sheldon Jackson, because each student's family financial situation and grades are different. As well, 90% of our students receive financial aid of some sort. To learn how much you will pay at SJ, complete the Free application for Federal Student Aid.

#3 Enrollment of 270!
We will argue that being a small college improves a Sheldon Jackson College education, e.g.:

      • Classes of 10 not 110.
      • Way more personal attention from your profs. (This really helps when it comes to looking for a job, because your teachers will provide your recommendations.) … from your academic advisor … your student dorm assistant … the financial aid director …
      • No long lines at registration, to reserve a racquetball court, or checkout a kayak.
      • Courses rarely fill up . . . you really can complete a bachelor’s degree in four years.

We want students to go to college. Seems to us that too many colleges boast about who they keep out rather who they educate … like a hospital that only accepts wealthy patients with minor health problems, then boasts about its healing successes. Too many colleges are more about wealth than wonder
