Financial Aid at Sheldon Jackson

College is expensive, especially a private college. Understanding how financial aid works could mean the difference between attending the college of your choice and having to attend the cheapest one. So, as ridiculous and convoluted as college financial aid has become in America today, do your best to plow through the following information. It will open doors for you.

Financial aid for collegians comes from four "pots":

Pot #1: The federal government

 

  • Pell Grants (not repaid)
  • College Work-Study (on-campus job)
  • Perkins, Stafford, and PLUS loans (must be repaid after graduation)
  • Students or parents must complete a Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA Online) in order to be considered for money in this "pot"
  • Monies given are based totally on the collegian's "need" (the family's finances)

  Pot # 2: The state government

If you are an Alaska resident, the following aid programs are available to you:

  • Alaska Student Loan (must be repaid after graduation)
  • Winn-Brindle Loan (for those entering fisheries; forgivable)
  • Rural Teacher Loan (for those from a rural education district who return to a rural district to teach; forgivable)
  • Contact the Alaska Commission on Postsecondary Education (800/441-2962) for more information.

Unfortunately, if you are not an Alaska resident, it is unlikely that you will be able to use your home state's financial aid at Sheldon Jackson. Most states do not allow their state tax money to leave their states. Check with your high school guidance or transfer counselor about your state, however.

Pot #3: Sheldon Jackson College

 

In addition to having one of the lowest tuitions for an accredited private, liberal arts college in the US ($7250 for two semesters!), SJ gives four scholarships:

A. The Alaska Scholarship (for Alaska residents)

  1. If your HS (or college if you're a transfer student) GPA is 3.2 or above, you'll receive a full-tuition ($7000) scholarship from us.
  2. If your HS (or college if you're a transfer student) GPA is 2.75 to 3.19, you'll receive a half-tuition ($3500) scholarship from us.

B. The Honor Scholarship (for residents of states other than Alaska) - The College has 20 Honor Scholarships of $3000 each. These are given "first come, first served," using the date of the enrollment deposit to prioritize.

C. The Referral Scholarship - An elite group of SJ "friends" are each given a $3500 scholarship that they may "refer" to a new SJ student. This elite group is comprised of dues-paying SJ alumni, VIMs, work groups, Humpback, and College Trustees. We give 20 of these Referral Scholarships, again, "first come, first served," using the date of the enrollment deposit to prioritize.

D. The Legacy Scholarship

  1. Any SJ student who is the dependent of an SJ degree-holder (HS, JC, or College) receives a $7000 scholarship.
  2. Any SJ student who is the dependent of a former SJ student (anything less than a degree-holder) receives a $3500 scholarship.
  3. If one "family" has more than one member at SJ concurrently:
  • If there are two, each receives a $2000 tuition discount.
  • If there are three, each receives a $3000 tuition discount.
  • If there are more than three, each receives a $4000 tuition discount.

 Pot #4: Other

 

In addition to these three "pots o' gold," there may be other financial available to you that we don't know about, for example:

    • If you're Alaska Native, be sure to check with your village and regional corporations.
    • If you're Presbyterian, the Presbyterian Church (USA) has scholarships dedicated to Presbyterians. Contact the Office of Financial Aid for Studies, 100 Witherspoon Street. Louisville, KY 40202-1396. [502/569.5745]
    • If you're not Presbyterian but active in your home church, many denominations have aid available for collegians.
    • Your local Rotary, Elks, Moose, et al. clubs often give scholarships.
    • You or your parents' employer may have an aid program. Many corporations do.

Conditions for being considered for financial aid at Sheldon Jackson

At SJ-again, unlike most other colleges-we do not require a separate application to be considered for financial aid. Generally we ask that you fulfill only two conditions to be considered:*

    1. Be admitted to SJ.
    2. Complete a Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA Online) (FAFSA).

Awarding policies

  1. The College always awards students federal and state aid before we award SJ money, because our scholarship endowment isn't unlimited. This is why we require the FAFSA.
  2. No college is allowed to give the student who receives federal and/or state funds more than the government says they "need." "Need" is calculated by subtracting the student's "estimated family contribution" (EFC) as determined by the FAFSA from the college's costs.
  3. The renewability of SJ scholarships is handled by the Honor's Day Committee. In the spring, the College has a Honor's Day celebration. At this time, students submit an application for scholarships to the Honor's Day Committee. This Committee determines awards for returning students, based upon the funds available to the College at that time. New students who received scholarships from SJ will most likely continue to receive them, if they have done well academically, and they been contributing members of the SJ community.
  4. The Alaska, Honor, Legacy, and Referral Scholarships, and the Visit Incentive tuition discount are all taken from the College's own revenues. Therefore, because we are not a wealthy school, we cannot "stack" these awards. So, for example, if you receive a Legacy Scholarship, we probably cannot also give you the Visit Incentive tuition discount.

So, let's summarize:

  1. Each student pays a different amount to attend college, because each student's finances and abilities are different.
  2. You do not know what it will cost you to attend Sheldon Jackson College until you've received your Award Letter from us.
  3. We cannot send your Award Letter until you are admitted to SJ and have completed a Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA Online).
