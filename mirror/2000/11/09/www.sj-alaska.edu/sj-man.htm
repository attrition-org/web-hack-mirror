Sheldon Jackson was a passionate, robust man:

  • After Princeton, he became a Presbyterian missionary and oversaw church work in the western U.S.
  • He traveled 30,000 miles/year (in the mid-1800s!), raising thousands of dollars for schools (by 1892, he had started 31 schools!) and churches (350!).
  • He gathered Native art throughout his travels because, as he wrote a friend, "...in a few years there would be nothing left to show the coming generations of natives how their fathers lived."
  • A close friend of President Benjamin Harrison.
  • First General Agent of Education in Alaska.
  • Jackson made 33 trips to Siberia, importing nearly 1300 reindeer for Alaskan Eskimos.
  • At his death at age 75, he had made 26 trips to Alaska.
  • Short on height (5'2") but clearly long on energy and commitment.

Sheldon Jackson is a college:

  • SJ was founded as an "industrial and training" school for Tlingit boys in 1878.
  • John G. Brady was the Presbyterian missionary who established the school. (He later became Alaska's first Governor).
  • The school evolved as Native needs changed: from vocational training into a grade school, then a high school. In 1944 it became a two-year college open to non-Natives.
  • We began offering four-year programs in 1976.
  • Today Sheldon Jackson is fully accredited by the Northwest Association of Schools and Colleges, authorized to offer courses in Alaska by the state's Postsecondary Commission, and certified by the National Association of State Directors of Teacher Education Colleges.
