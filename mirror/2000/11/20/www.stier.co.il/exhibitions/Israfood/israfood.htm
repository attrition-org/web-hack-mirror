Guests and Visitors:

ISRAFOOD is open to trade only. Traditionally the ISRAFOOD exhibition is well attended by buyers for the following:
Retail and wholesale food suppliers and distributors such as grocery stores, supermarkets, mini-markets, convenience stores, large food chains, food malls and other food distributors.
Dining establishmentssuch as restaurants, bistros, pubs, coffee houses, diners, fast food outlets, food bars, pizzerias, cafes and bakeries.
Catering institutions and institutions with dining facilitiessuch as kibbutzim, guest houses, wedding halls, convention facilities, municipal and national institutions, hospitals, cafeterias and other institutional dining halls.
Hotels including holiday villages, seminar halls and retreats.
Israeli trade will be invited by (120,000) personal invitations. We expect trade attendance from neighbouring countries including Jordan, Egypt, the Palestinian Autonomous Areas, Turkey, Italy and Greece.

Various Instructions for Exhibitors:

  • Public entrance: Registration area is in building 32 and building 27.
  • The height of the stand must not exceed 2.50 meters. Exhibitors wishing a display higher then the maximum height must receive written authorization from the organizers. Note: any such authorization is subject to a surcharge by the organizers.
  • Unconstructed space may be rented in specific areas of the exhibition halls. The minimum size of such space must be 40 sqm.
  • Unconstructed space does not include: partitions, fascia signs, spotlights, furniture, electrical supply.
  • Prior to the setup of Unconstructed space, a final plan/layout must be submitted to the organizers for approval. The recommended period for submission of the final plan is at least 60 days prior to booth set up.
  • Fees/prices listed on the brochure have been calculated as of December 1999. The organizers reserve the right to change these rates without prior notification; however this will not affect those that have already submitted their application and reserved their exhibition space prior to such changes.
