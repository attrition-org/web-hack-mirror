Rogue Spear vs SWAT3 - It seems the hot topic on the forums lately is about the games Rogue Spear and SWAT3. I love both games, but I am going to break them down for you. these are my opinions and in no way reflect reality, lol. I will separate them into topics, enjoy.
  • Multiplay - Well since SWAT3 has no multiplay capabilities yet this is a no brainer, Rogue Spear wins. I will interject my thoughts though. SWAT3 was built for multiplayer according to Rod Fung (the producer of the game) and will offer many ways to play it, from co-op to team based fights. I think it will rock, what could be more fun that shooting a friend through a wall and yelling at him to "GET DOWN".
  • Weapon Selection - I have seen people stating that SWAT3 does not have the great weapon selection Rogue Spear does, well I am glad it does not. Rogue Spear is based on a multi-national organization, while SWAT3 is the LAPD. It would be nuts for the SWAT team to have access to hundreds of weapons, but be warned, SWAT3 is easily modded and you can expect some awesome mods for it in the future. I heard rumors of an ARMM one. There is no winner for this category, both have good weapons for what they are.
  • Maps - Rogue Spear has awesome maps, I have to say that first. The outside and inside locations are fantastic. SWAT3 does not really have any outside locations since they are based on an organization that mostly works in a city. But the maps that SWAT3 has are just as well done as the ones in Rogue Spear. So this category is another tie, and if you doubt me about SWAT3's levels then try some of the later missions, they are large and very well done, mainly the convention center.
  • Weapon Realism - Rogue Spear has more realistic weapons while SWAT3 has more realistic ballistics. SWAT3 does not seem to take into effect the affects movement and injuries have on your aim. But then again a SWAT unit moves slow on purpose so that that are ultra safe and accurate with their strikes. But overall Rogue Spear wins this category.
  • Game Speed - Rogue Spear is a slow game compared to others like Quake and Unreal Tourny, but it is still faster than SWAT3.....sometimes. SWAT3 has two modes, stealth and dynamic. Stealth is used to move your team slowly so you have time to think and react to your situation and environment. Dynamic mode is for when the s**t hits the fan and you are in the heat of battle. Dynamic mode is very close to the speed of Rogue Spear so there is once again no winner in this category.
  • Environment Interactivity - This is how your surroundings react to your actions. Once again SWAT3 wins. Bullets can penetrate certain structures, mirrors crack, bullets ricochet off metal, rounds and explosions leave marks on items and walls, flashbangs burn items if carelessly thrown near a flammable object, and I freaked when I shot a terrorist and I found him by following his blood trail. Rogue Spear has a very interactive environment also but it is not as immersive as SWAT3.
  • Additional Items - There is a huge number of items in Rogue Spear as well as SWAT3, but overall SWAT3's items come off more realistic. The use of chem-lights is awesome, not to mention the fact than CS gas makes the enemy cough and react slower, flashbangs actually blind the suspect and he recoils from the explosion. Rogue Spear has more destructive items while SWAT3 has better executed ones. I have to give this category to SWAT3. Here are my reasons. You can hear the pins fall from your flashbangs and CS gas, you can see the glow of the chem-lights, you can blow doors realistically with breaching charges, and you can cuff hostages as well as the bad guys. It is rather rewarding to slap some cuffs on someone as they tell you where to shove your night-stick, hehe.
  • Graphics - SWAT3 beats the living crap out of Rogue Spear on this one. The lighting is done perfectly, shadows, mirrors, explosions, blood, it is all done with a high level of detail. You even drop your used clips on the ground after use. The modeling of the characters in the game is superb, the mouths move, eyes look around, eyes get wide when in scary situations, and people look like real people. You even recoil from the rifles firing, it is visible, as is the smoke and flash of the weapon. The stunning graphics have to be seen to be appreciated.
  • Sound - SWAT3 wins again. The sounds in this game are remarkable. People scream, insult, praise, and act like real people. I have had people that I handcuffed warn others to run, I have also had others tell me not to mess up their Rolex with the cuffs. This game rocks in the sound department. It also supports directional sound.
  • AI - The AI in Rogue Spear leaves a lot to be desired. I have shot one tango only to have another come to the same spot like it is his turn to be killed, this happens all the time. This will not happen in SWAT3. The enemy will react differently each time you encounter them, some even hide in closets. Some will be aggressive and some will not. Each character in SWAT3 has its own AI which is independent of the other characters. Your team members will actually cover your six instead of following you like robots as the ones in Rogue Spear do. Tell your guys in SWAT3 to cover you and they will take up positions to do so, and they will do it right! Tell them to breach and clear a room and they do that right also, they even lean around corners. Sometimes they take to long to fire at an enemy that is not cooperating, but usually they do better than you do. I have also seen a terrorist use a hostage as a human shield. SWAT3 wins this category without a doubt.
  • Animations - While Rogue Spear has better death and running animations, SWAT3 wins in this department also. The reloading and holstering of weapons, throwing items, opening doors, facial expressions, and body movements are far superior to Rogue Spears. I will admit there is nothing cooler than slowing Rogue Spear down after you shoot a guy and watch him spin to the floor.
  • User Support - RSE supports their products well but they do not allow mod creators the ability and options to be free to create mods for the game. You can change some things in Rogue Spear but you are very limited in what you can do. RSE has denied releasing any tools for creating custom maps. I know you can add items and tangos to a map, but I am referring to programs like the one released with Half-Life. You need to release or at least have the capabilities in your software to allow your audience to create additions for the game and help it thrive in such a competitive field as computer gaming. SWAT3's addon which will be out soon according to Sierra may come with a map creation program. I think they will release this, this is the company that released Half-Life and that had one. Sierra has seen how games that can have additions created by the uses make a more enjoyable and longer lasting product. So in conclusion, SWAT3 wins this category also.
My last thoughts...I like Rogue Spear a lot and have dedicated much of my time to it. As far as Sierra goes, I honestly have never liked them, but the release of Half-Life and now SWAT3 has redeemed them in my eyes...not as programmers but as publishers of games. All in all I think SWAT3 is a superior product to Rogue Spear, but you will have to kill me or blackmail me to remove Rogue Spear from my drive and stop playing it. I enjoy both games and recommend that any gamers who likes first person shooters do the same, both are great products.

SWAT 3 and Me - I purchased SWAT 3 by Sierra Studios yesterday evening and I have to tell you, I got no sleep last night. This game is so good that when the multi-play patch comes out in January or February it will be in direct competition with Rogue Spear for the "King of First Person Tactical Shooter Games". The graphics in the game are the best graphics yet in any game in my opinion. The sounds are awesome, as is the game play. You have to keep in mind, this game is as real as it gets. Rogue Spear feels like an arcade game after you have played SWAT 3. The lighting in the game is great, cut a closet light on and the light and shadows mimic real life perfectly. Shine your flashlight in the mirror and you will have to look twice to make sure you are playing a game and not watching a movie. When you change weapons or items, the animation of the movements is perfect. Gone are the look and feel of textures laid over models like Rogue Spear, now you can actually see the holster and armor. You actually put your weapons away and get them back out. Death animations are done nicely also, realistic blood and damage from the rounds is also done great. Here are a few things that have happened to me so far in the game, and I played each mission over ten times, and they were never the same, I never knew what to expect, here it the scoop...
Day 1...I get a call to look into a possible sniper on a major freeway. Going to the house I find his wife just out of the shower, she is wearing only a towel. I cuff her and move on while she is yelling at me and calling me names. I proceed to the attic, on my way up dispatch tells me he was seen in the attic window. I proceed with caution...as I lean around the corner at the top of the stairs I see him. He also sees me and fires a shot off from his hunting rifle. I can not get a clean shot so I fire a rain of rounds through the wall towards his direction, I hear a satisfying grunt as he begins to scream for mercy. Remember, this mission was never the same, sometimes he ran from me. One time he was not home, and he came home as I searched the house.
Day 2...I am issued a warrant on a hit and run suspect, his son lives with him and I am to use extreme caution. I get to the house and proceed in the building through the den. His son sees me and pulls out a pistol, as he does this his father runs down the hall (his father is unarmed). The son fires a round at me and I hit him with three shots to the chest. As the son screams his father turns around and runs to his dying son. When he gets there he grabs the pistol his son had and aims it at me, after yelling at him to drop it he eventually complies and I cuff him. Another time I cuffed the son and he screamed "Dad, get out of the house!".
You also create an operative and that is the character you play in the game. Mine is a male caucasian, with blue eyes, and even has my name on his jacket...it is etched across his back. The training is done superb also. A killhouse is provided along with a wealth of mission to practice. The killhouse makes the one on Rogue Spear look like a cardboard box. I have to tell you, go get this game, it is very realistic and my wife and I were both tripping at how good the graphics are. Get ready for the most realistic first person tactical game you have ever played, because SWAT 3 is it.
