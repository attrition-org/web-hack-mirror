The Lumberton School District offers children in grades kindergarten through eight the opportunity to receive a first-class education in central Burlington County. With an average addition of 90-130 students per year, Lumberton has one of the most rapidly growing school districts in South Jersey. Enrollment for the 1999-2000 school year is approximately 1350 students.  The district’s youngest students, grades K-2, learn in the Florence L. Walther School (enrollment 475).  Students in grades 3-5 are educated at the Bobby’s Run School (enrollment 440), which is located adjacent to the Lumberton Middle School on Dimsdale Drive.  Students in grades 6-8 receive instruction at the Lumberton Middle School (enrollment 410).  The Lumberton Schools educate students under the leadership of Frank J. Logandro, Superintendent of Schools; Thomas Fanuka, Business Administrator, and Lorraine Marinelli, Ed.D., Assistant Superintendent.  Questions may be directed to the Board of Education Offices, PO Box 8, 30 Dimsdale Drive, Lumberton, NJ 08048, (609) 267-1406.
