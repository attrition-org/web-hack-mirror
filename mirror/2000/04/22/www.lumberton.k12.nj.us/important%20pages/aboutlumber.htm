Lumberton School District, Lumberton Township

Burlington County, New Jersey

The Lumberton School District offers children in grades kindergarten through eight, the opportunity to receive a first-class education in central Burlington County. With an average addition of 90-130 students per year, Lumberton has one of the most rapidly growing school districts in South Jersey. Enrollment for the 1999-2000 school year will reach approximately 1,350 students in the district’s three schools by December, 1999.

Florence L. Walther School

56 Chestnut St., Lumberton, NJ, (609) 267-1404

The district’s youngest students, grades K-2, learn in the Florence L. Walther School. With a focus on developing the child’s self-esteem, curriculum provides academically stimulating, yet fun, lessons. The goal is to help these youngsters learn and grow as a whole person, and know that everyone can be successful. Classes are self-contained, with the exception of special subjects. Approximately 470 students are enrolled for the 1999-2000 school year. Mr. John Baily is the Walther School’s Principal.

Bobby’s Run School

32 Dimsdale Drive, Lumberton, NJ, (609) 702-5555

Students in grades 3-5 at the Bobby’s Run School, located adjacent to the Lumberton Middle School on Dimsdale Drive, expand their horizons with a host of challenging academics. The goal is to allow the students to learn with confidence, fostering cooperation and collaboration to help all students succeed. Students in grades 3 and 4 are taught in a self-contained format; students in grades 5 are semi-self contained, taught by one primary teacher and teacher "teams" for selected subjects. Approximately 450 students are enrolled for the 1999-2000 school year. Ms. Janet C. Coulter is Principal of the Bobby’s Run School.

Lumberton Middle School

30 Dimsdale Drive, Lumberton, NJ, (609) 265-0123

Lumberton Middle School provides students in grades 6-8 academic challenges, social development and the opportunity to explore extracurricular activities. Grade 6 students are taught in a "teaming" environment, rotating classrooms and teachers for a number of subjects. Grade 7 and 8 students change classrooms and teachers with each academic course. Approximately 400 students are enrolled for the 1999-2000 school year in the Lumberton Middle School. Ms. Patricia A. Hutchinson is Principal.

Curriculum

Technology is a major focus in the Lumberton School District. Lumberton continues to lead the majority of South Jersey school districts in incorporating technology into the schools and into the curriculum. Currently each classroom in every school has two student computers, as well as one teacher workstation. Additional computers will be added to classrooms this summer. The goal is to end up with six computers for students, along with one teacher workstation, in every district classroom.

Children in younger grades receive regular computer time to become familiar with how it works, learn basic interactive programs, and e-mail partners in neighboring schools across the country. Older students perform Internet research, develop spreadsheets, learn word processing, and eventually develop multimedia presentations, which incorporate sound, video and graphics. Many of the students enjoy cross-grade technology instruction through the year.

All students from grades 1-8 participate in many academic specials. Children in the Florence L. Walther School enjoy instruction in Art, Music, Library/Media Center, Physical Education, and Health. Bobby’s Run Students receive instruction in Art, Computer Lab, Music, Library/Media Center, Physical Education, Health and World Languages. Middle School students also add to the list Critical Thinking/Creative Problem Solving, Cultural Diversity, Drama/Public Speaking, Family and Consumer Science, Journalism and Study Skills.

Support Services

The district also offers a number of special services for all district students, including Basic Skills Instruction, Counseling Services, English as a Second Language, Speech and Language Services and Gifted and Talented/Enrichment. A district Child Study Team offers Special Education and Preschool Disabilities support. All students are offered hot and cold lunches daily through contracted services with PMG Services. The district also has on staff full- and part-time custodians and a full-time nurse at each school.
