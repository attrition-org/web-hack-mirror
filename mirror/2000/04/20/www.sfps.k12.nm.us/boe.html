The primary responsibility of the Board of Education is to establish educational policies and select a Superintendent of Schools.

  The Board shall be composed of five (5) members, residents of the District, who shall be elected for a term of four (4) years from March 1st succeeding their election.

Board of Education Goals

1.  Develop a culture of self respect and respect for each other utilizing the diversity of Santa Fe Public Schools community.

2.  Ensure that each student demonstrates mastery of academic and technological literacy and exhibit effective social skills.

3.  Make Santa Fe Public Schools the center of our community through increased support for youth and families.

4.  Provide our students safe, disciplined, and nurturing learning environments.

5.  Reduce the student drop-out rate.

6.  Improve process and procedures associated with financial and asset management.

7.  Broaden the funding base and increase resources for the Santa Fe Public Schools.
 

                                                                                                                                    Board Goals Adopted on December 12, 1998

Board Meeting Highlights
April 18, 2000

  • The Board signed the Partnership Agreement between the Downtown Rotary club of Santa Fe and the School-to-Careers.
  • Superintendent Garcia updated the Board on the Beverage Contract.  Superintendent Garcia recommended that the District do not go with an exclusive beverage contract but to keep working with the local Coca-Cola and Pepsi bottle companies and expand the services with other providers.
  • The Board of Education voted unanimously to table the Public Pronouncement Policy indefinite.
  • Mr. Mike Harris and Ms. Karen Wening provided the Board an update on the Capital Outlay Projects.
  • Ms. Bobbie Gutierrez presented the Textbook Adoption recommendations for the 2000 - 2001 school year.  The recommendations will be brought back to the Board for approval at the May 2, 2000, Regular Board Meeting.
  • Ms. Gina Hickman, Chief Financial Officer, presented to the Board the March Budget Report.

How You Can Participate

Public Participation at Board Meetings is welcomed.  Public comments provide the Board with new insights and fresh ideas.

   The public may address the Board at its Regular Meetings during a segment called "Public Forum".  Community members may sign up prior to the Board Meeting if they wish to address the Board.   The Board requests that comments be limited to four minutes.
