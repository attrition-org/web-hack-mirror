Wireless Networking Taking Shape
Posted Tuesday, February 08, 2000
The high school has taken the first steps toward creating a school-wide wireless data network. Using Apple Computer's "Airport" system of transmitters and wireless network cards, iMacs and iBooks equipped with wireless cards will be able to access the district network from anywhere in the building without plugging into a wall jack. Network access means internet connection and access to network printers and servers. Since iBooks can run on battery power, they will be totally "wireless." Our goal is to expand network access beyond computer labs and limited locations in classrooms.

ACT Prep Software Now Available!
Posted Wednesday, January 05, 2000
The media center has multiple copies of the computer program ACTive Prep, which helps students prepare to take the ACT test. These copies may be checked out from the media center to use on your home computer. The program comes on CD-ROM and is very multi-media intensive, so you will need a fairly recent and powerful PC with Windows 95/98 or an iMac. More information is available in the media center.

New iMacs
Posted Thursday, December 30, 1999
We have just received a shipment of new iMac and iBook computers. Ten of the new iMacs will be assigned to the Industrial Tech Lab. They will be able to run Mac and Windows programs. Another twelve iMacs will replace old computers on teacher desks. Five iBooks will be available for teachers to checkout for use with their students or to take home overnight. We will be trying out Apple's new "AirPort" wireless networking on the iBooks. The computers will run Apple's new Macinstosh Manager security and network management system. The computers are being set up now and should hit the lab and classrooms by mid-January.
