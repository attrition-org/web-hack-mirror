Distance Training via Internet (DTvI)

(APT AII Pilot Project)

 

In 1996, the Ministry of Posts and Telecommunications (MPT), Japan, began investigation of a plan for the Pilot Project of Distance Training via Internet (DTvI). The project aims to develop human resources in the area of telecommunications in the Asia-Pacific region.The Pilot Project was approved as one of the APT/AII Pilot Projects at the High-Level Development Meeting on APT/AII  held in June 1997 in Bangkok, Thailand. The Pilot Project has been udner implementation by MPT, Japan since November 1997.

 
One characteristic of the DTvI is that understanding is improved by the use of graphics and sound. By using these when creating training materials, the final product naturally becomes more simple and concise. However, the downside of such simple and concise materials is a long loading time because of the heavy use of graphics and sound. Considering the different computer terminal and network environments in different country, it was decided to prepare variations for each function and level of training materials.

 

In March 2000, MPT, Japan  has installed one of the DTvI  learning module, called CALSurf, at APT Internet Information Server. In CALSurf, Graphics and sound are fully used, and individual users' data including their progress and achievement are managed by the server. Currently, following five learning course-wares have been added to CALSurf module:

1.     Easy Learning of Satellite Communication System
2.    Easy Learning of Optical Communication Methods
3.    Basic knowledge of the Personal Handy-phone System
4.    Internet for Small Office
5.    Music DO-RE-MI.
