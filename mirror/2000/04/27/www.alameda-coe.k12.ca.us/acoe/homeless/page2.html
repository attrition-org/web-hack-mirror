OVERVIEW

  • Refine the thrust of this program
  • Connect homeless children and their families to services and programs quickly so that we can be responsive to school access and success
  • Coordinate a network of local and social services agencies to assure assistance with health issues, such as immunization and school meals programs
  • Provide connections to basic skills programs and employment for parents
  • Implement parent education and training programs in health, economics, and basic skills raining for educators that includes homeless sensitivity and services
  • Provide professional developement in teaching reading and math
  • Review and act to remove policies thay may act as barriers to the enrollment of homeless children and youth
  • Coordinate existing programs for children that include Head Start, special education, talented and gifted programs, programs for adolescents, vocational education, housing agency programs
  • Be instrumental in implementing tutoring and mentoring year round
  • Support before, after, and summer school programs
  • Support developmentally appropriate early childhood education
  • Continuously evaluate the program as a formative action evaluation to provide needed services. Start with a needs assessment survey of parents, teachers, and care providers in all homeless educational facilities
  • Supply needed educational materials, transportation, and meals to the level of any school site
  • Provide and assure an advocate or liaison on each site
  • Establish and review criteria for identification of homeless youth
  • Resolve the issue of student records, i.e., entering identification and assessment, leaving, transferring destinations, and portfolio assessment to assure educational equity
  • Hold and facilitate regional and interagency forums and meetings
  • Support the Unified Family concept
  • Provide extraodinary or emergency assistance, as necessary, to enroll and retain homeless students in school
