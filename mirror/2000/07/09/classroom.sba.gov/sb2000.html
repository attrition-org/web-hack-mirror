The SBA is proud to be a sponsor of the
SMALL BUSINESS 2000 PBS series

SMALL BUSINESS 2000 is a weekly, half-hour, program on public television. The purpose of the program is to inspire and help people succeed in business. SMALL BUSINESS 2000 is the only "how-to" series on television about starting and growing a business. The series, built around case studies, takes the viewer on location where a successful business owner talks about how they started, how they manage and how they grow their business.

The series is aired on about 200 public television stations around the country. It is distributed by the PBS Adult Learning Satellite Service to over 5000 colleges and universities in the US to be used as case studies in business courses. The United States Information Agency also broadcasts the program around the world. In Latin America, the series is in Spanish and airs on over 1500 cable stations.
