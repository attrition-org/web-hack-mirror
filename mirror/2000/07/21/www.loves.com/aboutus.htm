Love’s Country Stores began 36 years ago when Tom and Judy Love decided to start a chain of filling stations and bought a location in western Oklahoma. After they had opened 40 units, they added convenience foods. Every few years something new was added to the locations. This meant that innovation was a trademark, adding services when the time was right to continue their success.
© 1964 Musket Oil Company is incorporated and three months later our first unit is opened in Watonga, Oklahoma. This filling station is called "Deep Rock".
© 1972 The first convenience store opens in Guymon, Oklahoma. The combination of a convenience store with a gasoline station is new to the southwestern part of the U.S. at this time. Another original idea is self-service gasoline. This store is called "Mini Stop Country Store". The customer response is positive and the decision to expand is easy. During the next ten months, construction starts on six more convenience stores.
© 1973 O.P.E.C. embargoes oil shipments to the U.S. Fuel shortages happen across the country and Love’s (along with the other independent marketers) have serious difficulties obtaining adequate supplies of fuel. During this time Love’s stays true to a commitment to remain open seven days a week, 24 hours a day. A lot of customer goodwill is earned during this period.

The convenience stores are renamed "Love’s Country Stores".
© 1978 A sandwich concept is built inside a convenience store and called "Fresh Daily Deli". As the name suggests, sandwiches are prepared daily for each customer. This becomes a standard in every Love’s Country Stores location.
© 1981 With 90 successful convenience stores, Love’s now moves to the interstate highways. The first Love’s Travel Stop opens in Amarillo, Texas. It is roughly twice the size of a convenience store and includes truck fueling in the sales mix. The Amarillo travel stop is a hit and this concept becomes the growth vehicle for Love’s.
© 1991 The Sales and Marketing Executives International elect Love’s Country Stores Corporation of the Year.
© 1993 Branded fast food is introduced when a Love’s Travel Stop in Oklahoma City opens a Taco Bell Express. Customers like the choice of a nationally branded fast food in addition to the Fresh Daily Deli. More branded food concepts are opened in Love's stores.
© 2000 In it's 36th year of serving customers, Love's opens the 60th travel stop in Dallas, Texas.
