Bequest Doubles OCU CD Collection, Brings Priceless Recordings to University

For years, Oklahoma City resident Bill Blevins enjoyed performances by Oklahoma City University's Oklahoma Opera/Music Theater Company. Upon his death on May 30 this year, he returned the gift of music with an enormous collection of album and CD recordings, books, videos, scores and librettos.

"What this gift has done for our library is amazing," said Mark Parker, dean of the OCU School of Music. "Prior to receiving this gift, our CD collection numbered 500. It has now tripled."

Also included in the collection are an album of "42nd Street" signed by the cast members, OCU alumna Leona Mitchell's first album and other OCU-related memorabilia.

Blevins had a life-long passion for classical music and contributed generously to many aspiring artists and students of opera and musical theater. In addition to the music collection, the Bill Blevins Endowment is being established at OCU.

Blevins was chief of pathology at Midwest City Hospital from 1969 until his retirement in 1998. He earned a bachelor's degree from Oklahoma State University and his medical degree from the University of Oklahoma. He also obtained a Ph.D. in chemistry and a Ph.D. in microbiology from Baylor Hospital in Texas.

Two Students Claim State Pageant Titles

In its 96-year history, Oklahoma City University has produced three Miss Americas and 27 state pageant winners. The University's winning tradition continues this year with Katherine Hill, a May 2000 OCU graduate and 1998 Miss OCU winning the Miss Minnesota 2000 crown; and Jill Pennington, an OCU graduate student in vocal performance capturing the Miss Nebraska 2000 title. They will compete for the Miss America title in October.

Symphony Orchestra Receives National Award

For its strong commitment to the works of 20th century composers, the Oklahoma City University Orchestra has received the second place Award for Adventurous Programming by the American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers (ASCAP) and the American Symphony Orchestra League. The award was presented on June 16 at the American an Symphony Orchestra League's national conference in Boston, Mass.

OCU's repertoire for the 1999-2000 season included works by John Adams, John Corigliano, Stephen Paulus, Christopher Rouse, Michael Torks, Joseph Schwantner and Ellen Taaffe Zwilich. Mark Parker, dean of the Margaret E. Petree College of Music and Performing Arts' School of Music and the Orchestra's director, explained that the season was conceived as a celebration to usher in the year 2000.

"The OCU Symphony Orchestra has made a commitment to music of living composers," Parker said. "We have often premiered music of the University's past Composer-in-Residence Ray Luke and our current Composer-in-Residence Edward Knight. The Orchestra looks forward to readings of new works by student composers, as well as professional composers."

OCU competed against outstanding schools from across the nation, including the Manhattan School of Music, the first place winner; and Yale, which took third place.

"Students in OCU's Symphony Orchestra, while trained in the classics, receive an extensive education in music of our time," Parker said. "The students are not the only ones who benefit from this exposure to so much of today's music. While many university orchestras are lucky to have 100 people in the audience at any given concert, the audience at OCU regularly numbers over 350."

Stars Name New Women's Basketball Coach

Janell Jones has been named the head women's basketball coach at Oklahoma City University. The university conducted a nationwide search and selected Jones after interviewing a number of candidates.

Jones has been an assistant coach at OCU the last two seasons, in which the Stars won back-to-back NAIA National Championships. Prior to that, she was an assistant coach at Oral Roberts University, and was a successful high school coach at Midwest City Carl Albert. While at Carl Albert, her team won the Class 5A State Championship and Jones coached Page Sauer, who is now playing in the WNBA.

"I'm excited to have the opportunity to continue working with the great group of players that we have at OCU," Jones said. "We have an outstanding nucleus of returning veterans and some exciting new recruits that will help us sustain the accomplishments of the past two years."

OCU returns four players who started during the two championship seasons. Two Oklahoma All-State selections, Millwood's Kiesha Watson and Carl Albert's Sara Kincannon, have already signed to play for OCU next season.

"We believe Janell has been a major part of the success of our program the past two seasons and we're confident that she will continue to lead OCU to prominence in the future," said athletic director Dr. Joseph "Bud" Sahmaunt.

Three OCU Students Receive FTE Fellowships

Three Oklahoma City University students are among 37 undergraduate students in the United States selected to receive Partnership for Excellence Fellowships from the Fund for Theological Education

Students Stephanie Hughes-Tremper of Oklahoma City, Jeanne Pate of Altus, Okla., and Peggy Risenhoover of Norman, Okla., were selected for their superior academic achievement and exceptional gifts for ministry.

As fellows, they will receive a monetary award and continued support by FTE as they determine whether to pursue theological education in preparation for ministry. The program is designed to encourage their formation as ministers for the churches.

OCU Dance Alumni Selected for International Competition

Four of the 24 finalists for the 2000 Leo's Jazz Dance Choreography Competition are dance graduates of the Oklahoma City University School of American Dance & Arts Management. They are Rick Twombley, Beth Dukleth, Lauren Gaul and Kim McCollum.

"Considering the international scope and prestige of the competition, I am very pleased at the high representation that our graduates have among the finalists," said John Bedford, dean of the OCU dance school. "I credit the dance faculty for providing dance majors with exposure to top choreography while they are students, giving them a firm foundation on which to develop their abilities."

The 2000 Leo's Jazz Dance Choreography Competition will take place August 2-6 at the University at Buffalo, New York. Gold, Silver and Bronze Leos, along with cash prizes, will be awarded.

The choreography of all 24 finalists will be performed during the Jazz Dance World Congress, August 2-6, in the Center for the Arts, University at Buffalo, New York. A distinguished jury will choose the winners of the three Leo awards.
