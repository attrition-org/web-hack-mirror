Maritime Telecommunications
Network (MTN) 

As a subsidiary of ATC TELEPORTS is a leading provider of domestic and international satellite services. The company owns an operates more than 160 antennas accessing most major satellite systems from U.S. teleport locations in Arizona, California (under construction), New Jersey, Massachusetts, Texas, Virginia and Washington. The teleports provide complete engineering, management and technical assistance for full-time and occasional-use video, Internet, data and voice applications.

MTN uses the teleport facility located in Holmdel NJ for the core of its maritime business.  It is in close proximity to the largest U.S. telephony carrier interconnection hub - 60 Hudson Street, New York. 

Through this vast antenna network, MTN provides services worldwide and gives our maritime customers the ability to connect with the closest proximity and least cost to the final destination.

In addition, the Holmdel facility has successfully completed the implementation of the world's first commercial OC-3 asymmetric satellite service for direct Internet backbone access. This allows MTN to provide cost effective Internet access to it's cruise line and offshore customers.

With an outbound data delivery rate of 155.5 Mbps and 34 Mbps for the return, this service is the largest commercial asymmetric Internet satellite link to date. Due to ATC Teleports' proprietary design, this service offers the most efficient Internet over satellite service to date with effective IP throughput measured at greater than 99%.

The OC-3 network is incorporated as part of the direct high-speed multiple OC-12 Internet backbone that was developed by ATC Teleports and its partners. The Satellite International Private Line (SIPL) hub for the network is collocated at ATC Teleports' Holmdel, New Jersey facility. This new technology is providing the fastest and most direct service to the U.S. Internet backbone for International ISP's, with only 500 milliseconds of delay. This network connects the customer directly onto the backbone at the teleport, as opposed to utilizing fiber connection from the teleport to a local Internet POP, offering more flexibility in data rates, bypassing the need for local loop connections and greatly reducing the costs for bandwidth. ATC Teleports has a second SIPL hub at the Glenwood, New Jersey facility, offering customers a wide range of satellite options. IP Platforms are available at the Dallas and Washington DC facilities.

By utilizing proprietary network technology, designed in-house, ATC was able to achieve the largest data rate per unit of satellite space segment. The OC-3 service also offers the most effective Internet throughput, currently not matched by any other operational satellite asymmetric design.
