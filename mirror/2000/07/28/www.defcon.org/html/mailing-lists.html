MAILING LISTS
  • DEF CON Stuff This is a general discussion list covering the entire scene, and gets about 20 posts a day.  To subscribe mail majordomo@mailsrv.dis.org and in the body of the message on its own line subscribe dc-stuff for the general discussion list.
  • Access All Areas Offers a more European look at the scene, and is generally low traffic. Mail majordomo@access.org.uk with the words subscribe aaa-list in the body of the message.
  • Firewall Wizards is an excellent moderated list run by Marcus Ranum.  Mail majordomo@nfr.net with the words subscribe firewall-wizards in the body of the message.
  • Bug Traq is one of the original high traffic security mailing lists.  It is a full disclosure forum, and originally covered more unix issues than windows ones.  Send an e-mail message to listserv@securityfocus.com with a message body of: SUBSCRIBE BUGTRAQ Lastname, Firstname. 
  • The Cryptography Mailing List covers all things crypto from policy to algorhythms.  Mail majordomo@c2.net with the words subscribe cryptography in the body of the message.
  • NT Security List hosted by ISS is fairly high bandwidth with topical conversation on current NT (and related) security issues.  Mail majordomo@iss.net with subscribe ntsecurity in the body of the message.
  • The VPN mailing list is low traffic, with a specific focus on VPN technologies.  Mail majordomo@listserv.iegroup.com with the words subscribe vpn [your email address] in the body of the message.
  • NT Bug Traq is a moderated NT mailing list that is a bit better than the ISS list.  It is generally not a full disclosure mailing list, so sometimes the full details are not allowed through.  Mail listserv@listserv.ntbugtraq.com with the words subscribe NTBUGTRAQ [your name] in the body of the message.
  • NT Security is a wide open discussion about Windows security issues.  This is the corollary to NT Bug Traq, run by the same folks.  Mail listserv@listserv.ntbugtraq.com with the words subscribe ntsecurity [your name] in the body of the message.
  • Info Sec News [ISN] is full of security related articles and news contributed by its members.  Mail listserv@securityfocus.com with the words subscribe isn Lastname, Firstname in the body of the message.
  • Security Focus News [SFN] SF-NEWS is Security Focus weekly summary mailing list! This list is designed to take notable events covered by the Security Focus web site on a weekly basis. Topics include BugTraq, jobs, incidents, pools, and events. Send an e-mail message to listserv@securityfocus.com with a message body of: SUBSCRIBE SF-NEWS Lastname, Firstname 
  • Defaced is a low volume mailing list that circulates news on defaced web sites.  To subscribe email majordomo@attrition.org with a message body of "subscribe defaced".
  • Risks is a digest that describes many of the technological risks that happen in today's environment.  They also have a news group and digests available.  To subscribe email risks-request@csl.sri.com with a message body of "subscribe".
  • The Cu Digest [or Computer underground Digest] is a more-or-less weekly digest/newsletter/journal of debates, news, research, and discussion of legal, social, and other issues related to computer culture.  To subscribe email cu-digest-request@weber.ucsd.edu with a message body of "sub cudigest".
  • VIRUS-L is an electronic mail discussion forum for sharing information and ideas about computer viruses, which is also distributed via the Usenet Netnews as comp.virus. Discussions include (but not necessarily be limited to): current events (virus sightings), virus prevention (practical and  theoretical), and virus related questions/answers. The list is moderated and digested.  To subscribe email listserv@lehigh.edu with a message body of "subscribe virus-l firtname lastname".


There are plenty of mailing lists out there that relate to the topics we are all interested in. Some have very high volume, and others don't. I suggest using a mail filter to seperate inbound mailing list traffic to specific folders so you don't get crushed by volume.  If you know a good mailing list and want it added here please email me (dtangent@defcon.org) with the required information.
