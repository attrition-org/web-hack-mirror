History of WHRHS

The Watchung Hills Regional school district was formed in 1955 as a bell-wether program for academic excellence. The district currently serves Watchung Borough, Green Brook, Warren and Long Hill Townships; communities of business and professional families who commute to nearby research and corporate complexes and the area cities and universities.

The three ridges of the Watchung Mountains and I-78 span the heavily wooded 32 square mile district, 25 miles Southwest of New York City. Modern connecting units of the 85 acre campus serve 1140 students in grades 9 - 12.
