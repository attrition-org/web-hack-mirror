Arizona Department of Corrections

NO ESCAPES IN 1997

The number of escapes from within an Arizona prison's secure perimeter in 1997 - zero.

The number of escapes from a work crew outside a prison's secure perimeter in 1997 - zero.

The total number of prison escapes in any form from an Arizona prison in 1997 - zero.


For the first time in the history of the Arizona Department of Corrections, not one prison escape occurred in an entire calendar year. In fact, no escapes have occurred for a continuous period of more than fifteen months.


"Preventing escapes is one of our greatest challenges," said Deputy Director of Prison Operations Charles Ryan. "Collectively, the employees of the Department have heightened their awareness of attention to detail as it pertains to the public's safety. It was because of our employees that we experienced zero escapes for the first time in our history," Ryan said.


Arizona State Prison Complex-Florence Warden Meg Savage was reminded of the July 9, 1997 escape attempt by death row inmate Floyd Thornton that resulted in the deaths of Thornton and his wife. Rebecca Thornton fired numerous rounds of gunfire from outside the prison's fence at correctional officers and inmates working in Florence's vegetable garden. She was shot and killed by one of the officers just as she fired her weapon at her husband, who was running toward the fence in an apparent effort to escape.


"The Thornton incident demonstrated how the heroic response by some of our staff members prevented the escape of one or more of the most dangerous criminals in Arizona," Savage said. "'1997's zero escape total is a direct reflection of the caliber of staff we have in the Department of Corrections. We know for a fact that on several occasions, escape attempts were thwarted by observant and conscientious staff," Warden Savage stated.


The last time any escape of an Arizona Department of Corrections inmate occurred was October 4, 1996 when an inmate jumped from a transportation van while en route to the state prison in Safford from St. Mary's Hospital in Tucson. Coincidentally, this last escapee, Jesus Espinosa, became 1998's first escape apprehension when Department of Corrections investigators arrested him at a Tucson apartment on January 5, 1998.


"Zero escapes in an entire calendar year is a great milestone of which all of us in the Arizona Department of Corrections can be especially proud," Corrections Director Terry Stewart said. "This was not the result of just good fortune. It is a direct result of the dedication the employees of the Department of Corrections have to maintaining safe and secure prisons for the people of Arizona," Director Stewart said.
