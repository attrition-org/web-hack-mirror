VICE PRESIDENT/CAMPUS PRESIDENT

COLUMBUS, NEBRASKA 

Central Community College, a fully accredited community college founded in 1966, is seeking candidates for the position of College Vice President of Academic and Training Programs and President of the campus in Columbus, Nebraska. 

The college is a multi-campus community college with major campuses at Columbus, Grand Island, and Hastings, Nebraska.  Head count enrollment is approximately 30,000, with major emphasis on comprehensive educational programs.  Students are the number one priority in the college that utilizes competency-based instruction and planning.  The college has placed special focus on assessment of student learning. 

Responsibilities:   The Vice President/Campus President is the chief administrative officer, directly responsible to the College President.  This position is responsible for campus and community relations; all campus functions, including budget preparation and administration, instruction, student services, plant operations, campus staff, and grants; and all other related campus activities.  The Campus President in Columbus has college-wide responsibility for all academic programs and training activities and assessment of student learning for both. 

Qualifications:  Applicants should possess an earned doctorate degree from an accredited institution (ABD will be considered); experience in academic/vocational/technical education; a strong commitment to the community college philosophy; experience in senior managerial positions (three years dean level or above, preferably at a community college); experience with business, industry; high interest in regional development and innovation; and an understanding of individualized instruction is helpful because many instructional programs are individualized.  Preference will be given to candidates having a minimum of ten years of combined teaching, educational administration, business, and technical experience. 

Employment Conditions:   The successful applicant is expected to be a resident of the Columbus community, active in the greater Columbus community (9 county region) and the college Foundation.  An excellent fringe benefit package is provided.  This is a level 14 position and salary is commensurate with individual’s background and experience. 

Deadlines:   Closing date for applications and nominations is June 30, 2000.  Application materials must be submitted on or before July 10, 2000. 

Application:   Submit a letter of application including resume, credentials, and salary requirements to:  Douglas L. Adler, Human Resources Manager, Central Community College, P.O. Box 4903, Grand Island, NE.  68802-4903, or phone 308-398-7327.
