Central Community College Foundation Office
 
 

The primary goal at Central Community College is to make quality education available to as many as possible. In support of that goal, the Central Community College Foundation exists to expand educational opportunities and enhance educational programs and facilities at the College.

The Foundation's mission is simple: To provide monetary and advisory support for students, faculty and College programs and to make the needs of the College visible to the community and to business and industry.

To accomplish that mission the Foundation conducts fund raising activities and accepts charitable gifts of cash, securities and other property of value. Foundation staff also provides consultation and assistance to persons who choose to support Central Community College by naming the CCC Foundation as a beneficiary in wills, life insurance policies and a variety of planned giving instruments.

Funds accumulated by the Foundation are used to provide scholarships for students, as enhancement grants to faculty members, and to provide new or improve existing equipment and facilities at Central Community College.

Gifts to the Foundation, a not-for-profit 501(c)(3) corporation, are tax deductible to the extent allowable by law. The Foundation maintains donor records and files on all current and past charitable donations.

Over the years, thousands of individuals and organizations have contributed to Central Community College and the CCC Foundation. All those who have ever provided financial support are included on the Donor Honor Roll, which is published in the Foundation's Annual Report each year.

Levels on the Donor Honor Roll reflect the total contributions of the individual or organization over the years, ranging from the Campus Club, for those who have contributed $99 or less; to the Board of Governor's Club--Platinum, for those whose gifts and pledges exceed $100,000.

.

Many donors enhance their personal contributions through corporate matching gifts. Donors are urged to check with their employer to see if they have a program of matching charitable contributions by employees to educational institutions .

.

A variety of Planned Giving instruments are available that make it possible for a person or a family to secure their personal financial needs while, at the same time, providing gifts to charitable institutions such as the CCC Foundation.

The Foundation staff stands ready to provide estate planning assistance and consultation on bequests and wills, charitable gift annunities, charitable lead trusts, life estate contracts and other planned giving opportunities.

.

Numerous memorial scholarship funds have been established with the CCC Foundation in memory of loved ones. The Foundation maintains all such memorials and administers the scholarship funds in accordance with the wishes of those who established the memorial.

.

Over the past seven years the CCC Foundation has awarded more than $1.3 million in scholarships to students all across the 25-county Central Community College area. During the 1998-99 school year scholarships totaling over $350,000 were awarded to approximately 500 students.

Several permanent annual scholarships have been established over the years. These scholarships are awarded each year based on the criteria of each scholarship fund.  In addition, thousands of designated scholarships are donated each year by various communities, schools, organizations and individuals from across the state and the nation.

.

In 1988, the Central Community College Foundation held assets of just over $500,000. By year-end 1999 net assets had grown to over $5.5 million.

This growth was made possible by the many generous donors who have supported the Foundation's fund-raising events, activities and programs over the years.  Asset growth bears a direct correlation to the level of support the Foundation is able to provide to Central Community College.

A Board of Directors oversees Foundation activities and sets policy. The Board is limited to 30 regular members representing all areas of the 25-counties served by CCC. Board members are elected to three-year terms. Also included on the Board are three ex-officio members representing the CCC Board of Governors and ex-officio members representing the College administration and the three College Campuses.
