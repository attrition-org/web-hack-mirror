This Web site will help eligible Washington residents choose a health plan and provider with one of four Washington State health care programs: Basic Health, Children's Health Insurance Program (CHIP), Healthy Options,  and Public Employees Benefits Board (PEBB).  The Department of Social and Health Services (DSHS) and Health Care Authority (HCA) oversee these programs. Each program has its own eligibility requirements and benefits package, and works with health plans throughout the state to offer health care to eligible residents. In turn, these health plans contract with providers to give you the medical care you need.

If you are not certain you are eligible for Basic Health, CHIP, Healthy Options, or PEBB and would like to apply or get more information, please call the toll-free numbers listed below.

Basic Health provides medical benefits to any Washington State resident who meets income guidelines, and is not eligible for Medicare or institutionalized at the time of enrollment. Members pay premiums on a sliding scale based on their income, age, family size, and health plan choice. Additional benefits for eligible children are available in the Basic Health Plus program.

For information, call 1-800-826-2444.
The Children's Health Insurance Program (CHIP) is for children under 19 who don't have other medical insurance and whose family income is too high for Medicaid.  The CHIP managed care program offers eligible children the same benefits as the Medicaid program Healthy Options.  There are low premiums and copayments.

For information, call 1-800-562-3022.  (TTY/TDD users call toll-free 1-800-848-5429).

Healthy Options is the name of the DSHS Medicaid managed-care program for low income people in the state of Washington. Healthy Options offers eligible families, children under 19, and pregnant women a comprehensive medical benefit package with no premium or cost-sharing.

For information, call 1-800-562-3022.  (TTY/TDD users call toll-free 1-800-848-5429).

The PEBB program provides medical and dental benefits to all eligible state employees as a benefit of employment. In addition, K-12 school district employees and retirees and local government employees may also choose to join the PEBB program.

For information, call 1-800-700-1555 (employees) or 1-800-200-1004 (retirees).
