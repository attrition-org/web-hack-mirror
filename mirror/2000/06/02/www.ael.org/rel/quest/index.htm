QUEST

QUEST is a five-year, collaborative research project to better understand how schools can create continuous school improvement that leads to increased student learning. QUEST does not promote specific programs, but instead offers a set of core beliefs to guide schools on a never-ending journey of collective inquiry.

QUEST's framework for continuous improvement provides resources for those wanting to better understand the theory base.

Processes used by schools engaged in the QUEST project involve the broader school community in setting a vision and establishing directions and goals.

QUEST sponsors workshops and conferences, as well as onsite training. Inquiry, reflection, dialogue, and storytelling are the major vehicles used in these conferences. To find out how to become a QUEST school, contact sattesb@ael.org.

QUEST is but one of many vehicles for school improvement. QUEST staff collaborate with other regional educational laboratories through the School Change Collaborative, headed by the Northwest Regional Educational Laboratory.
