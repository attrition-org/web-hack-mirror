Regional Educational Laboratory

For more than 30 years, the AEL, Inc., has served as one of 10 federally supported Regional Laboratories across the country. The Laboratory's current service area includes Kentucky, Tennessee, Virginia, and West Virginia.

The Regional Lab's work embodies the essence of AEL's mission--to link the knowledge from research with the wisdom from practice to improve teaching and learning.

The Regional Laboratory

  • conducts research and development
  • provides information exchange
  • designs and conducts professional development
  • offers evaluation and planning

A special focus of the Laboratory's efforts is rural education. AEL has been selected by the U.S. Department of Education as the Regional Educational Laboratory with a rural specialty.

Applied Research and Development Activities

Among AEL's applied research and development (R&D) activities are

  • individual state-focused R&D
  • processes to help local school communities continuously improve
  • teacher action research
  • rural education

Information Exchange

Information exchange at AEL includes both giving and gathering information. The Resource Center offers free searches of the ERIC database and the Internet, access to specialized periodicals, research syntheses, and information search packages on timely topics.

AEL also provides information through its newsletter The Link, Policy Briefs, occasional papers, and other publications that result from its work. In addition, Laboratory staff are available to provide consultation on-line or by telephone.

Facilitating information gathering in the region is a key staff function. AEL resident directors link with particular groups and associations concerned about education. Also, an annual education issues forum, hosted in each state, keeps AEL informed about issues in the region, helping the Laboratory provide timely information and assistance.

Professional Development

Professional development opportunities have been mainstays of the Laboratory's work over the years. Training is offered in two AEL-developed school improvement processes--Questioning and Understanding to Improve Learning and Thinking (QUILT) and Interdisciplinary Teamed Instruction. AEL provides professional development materials on such topics as authentic assessment, curriculum integration, and teaching marginal learners.

Evaluation and Planning

As a research and development organization, AEL values data-based processes and decision making. Evaluation of programs and systematic planning for future activities are large parts of each Laboratory-developed project. In addition, the Laboratory's expertise in planning, research, and evaluation is made available to educators and others throughout the region on a contractual basis.
