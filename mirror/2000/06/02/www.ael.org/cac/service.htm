Types of Services Offered
The Region IV Comprehensive Center works at the national, state, and district level. For example:

On the National Level

  • Coordinate the national Safe and Drug-Free Schools Conference
  • Convene directors of the Goals 2000 Parent Assistance Centers
  • Present and facilitate sessions at the regional Improving America's Schools Conference
  • Collaborate with the national network of other technical assistance providers such as the Mid-South Regional Resource Center in Kentucky, the Southeastern Equity Center, or the Mid-Atlantic Center.
On the State Level
  • Assist state department of education staff in developing consolidated plans
  • Convene compensatory program coordinators from the six states in Region IV
  • Collaborate to offer state Schoolwide Conferences
  • Train statewide school support teams
  • Assist in defining "Adequate Yearly Progress"
  • Conduct training for Title II coordinators on developing performance indicators
On the District Level
  • Train clusters of teachers serving migrant students
  • Assist in developing assessment strategies for ESL/LEP students
  • Train administrators in the local consolidated planning process
  • Recommend best practices and research-based models for Title I schoolwide planning
  • Conduct training for reading coaches
  • Provide training for teachers on parent involvement
