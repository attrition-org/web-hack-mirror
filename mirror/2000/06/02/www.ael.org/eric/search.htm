Searching the ERIC bibliographic database used to be off limits to ordinary humans. Now, we've got lots of Web and CD-ROM options. But if you choose, you can still have someone else search it for you.

Here are your options:

  2. CD-ROM Options for Searching ERIC
  3. Let Us Search ERIC for You

Web Options for Searching ERIC

ERIC Bibliographic Database
  • The ERIC Search Wizard guides you with ease through a sophisticated search strategy for searching the ERIC bibliographic database.
  • ERIC's Personal Librarian Turbo-search option is one of the best ERIC search engines on the Web and includes the entire database back to 1966.
  • Other ERIC search options--here's the list, all in one handy location.

Other ERIC Searchable Resources

  • Test Locator contains records on more than 10,000 tests and research instruments.
  • Lesson Plans for Teachers contains more than 1,000 unique lesson plans which have been written and submitted by teachers from all over the United States.
  • National Parent Information Network (NPIN) provides information to parents and those who work with parents and fosters the exchange of parenting materials.
  • Gateway to Educational Materials (GEM) provides one-stop, any-stop access to high quality Internet lesson plans, curriculum units, and other education resources.
  • Education Resource Organizations Directory (EROD) helps you identify and contact organizations that provide information and assistance on a broad range of education-related topics.

CD-ROM Options for Searching ERIC

In an innovative partnership with the private sector, ERIC now offers a CD-ROM version of the database that almost any school, and even individuals, can afford. This version of the database--the NISC DISC--is powerful and easy to use. You can purchase a yearly subscription (four updates) for $100. The current disk covers the education literature from 1985 to the present. The archival disk covers 1966-1984 and is separately available.

Details about other ERIC CD-ROM products are listed in Ready Reference #20, at the ERIC Processing and Reference Facility.

By the way, Ready References give a lot of detail about ERIC's structure and history. Check them out if you search the database frequently, or if you are teaching a class on research.

Let Us Search ERIC for You

Our clearinghouse staff would be glad to help you with your search, or conduct the search for you. Just send us a message with your question, and we will be glad to respond at no charge. Another option is to send your question to the ERIC system's special question-answering service, AskERIC. The full ERIC database (1966- present) is now available, and AskERIC is no longer limited to 100 citations. If you need information on the theory or practice of education, the AskERIC information specialists will respond to you within two working days with appropriate ERIC database citations, full-text ERIC Digests, and Internet resources. You will receive personalized attention and resources that are tailored to your individual information needs.
