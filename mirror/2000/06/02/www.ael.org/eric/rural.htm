    ERIC Digests are brief (1,500-word) summaries of the education literature on specific topics, including reference lists for readers who want to know more. Digests are informative, easy to read, and free (you can download them here or order printed copies by contacting the clearinghouse). Our current list of ERIC Digests related to rural education is shown below:

    • Attracting and Retaining Teachers in Rural Areas, by Timothy Collins (1999), EDO-RC-99-7

    • Building School-to-Work Systems in Rural America, by Hobart Harmon (1998), EDO-RC-97-7

    • Charter Schools, by Margaret Hadderman (1998), EDO-EA-98-2

    • Charter Schools: An Approach for Rural Education?, by Timothy Collins (1999), EDO-RC-98-3

    • Homeless Children: Addressing the Challenge in Rural Schools, by Yvonne M. Vissing (1998), EDO-RC-98-1

    • Improving Rural School Facilities for Teaching and Learning, by Sarah Dewees (1999), EDO-RC-99-8

    • The National Information Infrastructure: Keeping Rural Values and Purposes in Mind, by Craig B. Howley and Bruce Barker (1997), EDO-RC-97-4

    • Parent and Community Involvement in Rural Schools, by Stan Maynard and Aimee Howley (1997), EDO-RC-97-3

    • Perspectives on Rural Child Care, by Betty A. Beach (1997), EDO-RC-96-9

    • A Practical Look at Comprehensive School Reform for Rural Schools, by Topper Sherwood (1999), EDO-RC-98-2

    • The Role of the Rural Community College in Rural Community Development, by Jonathan D. Holub (1996), EDO-JC-96-02

    • Rural African Americans and Education: The Legacy of the Brown Decision, by Patricia S. Kusimo (1999), EDO-RC-98-4

    • Education and Development in Poor Rural Communities: An Interdisciplinary Research Agenda, by Carolyn S. Carter (1999), EDO-RC-99-9
