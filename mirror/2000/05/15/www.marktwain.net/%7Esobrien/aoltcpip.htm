ACCESS TO AMERICA ONLINE VIA TCP/IP
TCP/IP is a communications format for transferring data on a network. America Online's software lets you use TCP/IP (if it is available on your computer) instead of your modem to connect to America Online at no extra charge. Connecting to America Online with TCP/IP is often faster than the usual modem-based connection process, and it will not interfere with your use of other TCP/IP tools like Netscape(c) or Mosaic(c).

Note: You must already have an EXISTING America OnLine Account before you can access AOL or know someone who has an Account you can use
To connect to AOL via TCP/IP (Windows):

1.  Establish your PPP connection (Windows 3.x) or Dialup Networking      connection (Windows 95)
2.  Do Not start Netscape, but instead start your AOL Software.
3.  Click Setup on the Sign On Screen.
4.  Click Edit Location.
5.  Select TCP/IP from the Network drop-down box.
6.  Click Save.
7.  Click OK to return to the Sign On screen.
8.  Select Guest as the screen name.
9.  Click SIGN ON and it will automatically connect using the TCP/IP Network      connection.
10. Enter the screen name and password in the appropriate boxes and you will be       connected to AOL.
To connect to America Online via TCP/IP (Macintosh):

1. Establish your PPP connection.
2. Select TCP Connection from the Locality pop-up menu on the Sign On screen.
3. Sign on to America Online using TCP/IP.


If you do not already have the software that is needed to access AOL you can contact them at: 1-800-827-6364 and ask for whichever format you need. Macintosh or IBM compatible on diskette or CD-ROM
