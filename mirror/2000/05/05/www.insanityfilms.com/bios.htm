Jason Francois; Writer, Producer, Director, blah, blah, blah.

Jason Francois is described by many as a born storyteller. "They used to call it exaggerating, but once you put it onto paper and give it a title, they call it a story". OK, so he's in no way a liar...he's just got an over active imagination. Haunted by this overactive and sometimes twisted imagination, he headed for Vancouver, British Columbia in the summer of 1993 to find a focus for his excess energy and a certificate for his wall. In Canada, he wrote and directed the short film "The System" in 1993, whose main character inspired the off-beat styled characters that have made their way into most of Jason's finished scripts as well as the many "almost there" scripts in his personal library. Damaged Goods is the first of many off-beat feature films written, produced and directed by Jason within the next few years and follows closely to what he calls "simple cinema"; adhesion to the basics of good storytelling without all of the unneeded extras (explosions, T and A, etc.). He also has done time as an Assistant Director on short films; most recently the 35mm short, fantasy film "Mushroomers"; produced by Liquid Reality Entertainment and Directed by Carl Raimondi.

Jason also brings to all projects his lifelong accomplishments in the visual arts as well as strong leadership and personal skills.

 

Michael Donovan; Co-Producer and Actor

Michael Donovan has worked professionally in the entertainment industry for the past eight years in many capacities. He has accumulated many acting credits working in the film and television as well as a principal lead role in the high-profile CD interactive game Phantasmagoria II: A Puzzle of Flesh which saw it's international distribution in 1997.

Michael Donovan's extensive acting credits have afforded him a rare view of the inner-workings of film production from many different levels, which was beneficial to the making of "Damaged Goods".
