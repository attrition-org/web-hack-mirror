 What is ISDN?
 ISDN, which stands for Integrated Services Digital Network, is a system of digital phone connections that is being promoted by Telekom Malaysia. This system allows data to be transmitted using digital connectivity, as opposed to the analog connectivity of home telephone lines (called PSTN). ISDN lines consist of two channel, each of which can transmit data at 64kbps. Thus, data transmission for ISDN can be as high as 128Kbps, that makes ISDN very useful for Internet surfing and downloading.

What are the things I need to get ISDN Internet Access service?
 You will need the following:
1. ISDN telephone line connection from Telekom.
2. ISDN TA/Modem (available through Navatech)
3. ISDN Internet Access account (available through Nasionet and authorised agents)

 Where can I get ISDN connection?
 ISDN is limited to certain areas, although most in Malaysia (especially the Klang Valley) can apply for ISDN. An ISDN line can be installed in your home or office.

 Who installs the ISDN line?
 Currently, only Telekom Malaysia can install ISDN. The procedure is that your must apply to get an ISDN line into your house or office, Telekom Malaysia will assign you an ISDN telephone number.

 Can I still get an ISDN line if my area or building supports Maxis or Time Telecom network?
 This depends on your location. We can find out for you if you give us your current telephone number and address.

 Do I need to have a physical line installed in my house or office for ISDN?
 Yes. You need to have a telephone contractor lay a physical line into your house or office, NavaTech can assist you in this, by introducing contractor.

 Can I use my analog telephone with the ISDN line?
 Certain modems (like the ZyXel which Navatech is promoting) allows you to both surf the internet and use your analog phone at same time. This is because ISDN has 2 channels for carrying data. Hence, one can be used to connect to the Internet, while the other can be used to make and receive phone calls.

 Will I dial up to 1511 or 1515?
 No. These two numbers are for analog service only provided by TMnet and Jaring. You will dial a 7 or 8 digit number to use the Internet gateway of Nasionet (NOT Jaring or TMnet).
