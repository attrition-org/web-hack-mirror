Playing golf was never meant to be (COOL) just for the accomplished players, nor was it meant to exclude them. Rather it should encompass all players regardless of their ability. The National Youth Golf Association (NYGA) has developed a venue for junior golfers that provides an opportunity for both the accomplished and the first time player to compete on an equal playing field. By modifying the USGA’s handicap index system for youth golfers, participants are provided with an official NYGA tour index. Their index allows them to compete with golfers in the same age group, regardless of ability. The National Youth Golf Association is able to accomplish this by incorporating the USGA probability report, the course slope/rating and the NYGA tournament handicapping system. National Youth Golf Association members are able to compete with other members within their age division and have the opportunity to win every time they play.
