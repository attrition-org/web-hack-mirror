How can I contact my Member of Provincial Parliament (MPP)?

I can be reached at my constituency office at P.O. Box 535 1143 Mill St. Manotick, Ontario K4M 1A5 or by phone at 613-692-2403 or toll free at 1-800-267-1020 or you can fax me at 613-692-0869.

Contact information for all MPPs is available in the Members section of the Ontario Legislative Assembly site.

How can I contact a Cabinet Minister (Executive Council Member)?

A list of the Cabinet Ministers (Executive Council Member) appears in the Members section of the Ontario Legislative Assembly site. Contact information is available from the list of members.

What is the E-mail address for the Premier?

The Premier does not have a direct E-mail address, but messages can be sent to the Office of the Premier from the Messages for the Premier page on our Web site.

Where can I find Internet E-mail addresses, postal addresses, and phone numbers for Ontario Government staff?

Internet E-mail addresses (if available), postal addresses, and phone numbers for Government of Ontario employees are available from the PACE Directory (Public Access Corporate Enquiry). Similar information can be obtained from the Citizens' Inquiry Bureau.

Where can I get a copy of Acts (Statutes), Bills, Hansard, Ontario Gazette and Regulations?

Current versions of Acts (Statutes) and Regulations (which provide the detailed information regarding the activities mentioned in the Statute) are available for purchase from Publications Ontario. Copies of the Statutes, current to July 31, 1996, are available on the Publications Ontario Web site. Copies of the Regulations current to at least July 31, 1996, are also available on the Publications Ontario Web site. To ensure that a Bill has become an Act (Statute), you can check the Status of Legislation page in the Documents section of the Ontario Legislative Assembly site.

First Reading and Second Reading copies of a Bill, currently before the House, are available in the Documents section of the Ontario Legislative Assembly site. Third Reading copies are available for purchase from Publications Ontario.

Hansard is available online on the Ontario Legislative Assembly site. Print copies are available for purchase from Publications Ontario.

The Ontario Gazette is available for purchase from Publications Ontario.

Where and how do I get a Birth, Death, Marriage or Name Change certificate and what is the cost?

To obtain copies of birth, death, marriage and name change certificates for events occurring in Ontario contact the Office of the Registrar General of the Ministry of Consumer and Commercial Relations.

Proper registration of an event will take 10-14 weeks from the date of the registration. If an event has NOT been properly registered with the Office of the Registrar General, then certificates or any services relating to the event CANNOT be provided, until proper registration is complete.

The application forms to obtain certificates are available by phoning the number below and having them mailed or faxed to you, or by visiting any of the offices. (Please note the forms will only be faxed to an Ontario fax number.)

Application forms can be sent in by mail or fax (807-343-7459), or brought into one of the offices. If faxing applications in, provide type of credit card for payment (Visa or Mastercard), the credit card number, the expiry date, and the full name on the credit card.

Premium same day service is available for certificates for:

  • births from 1930 to the present
  • deaths from 1980 to the present
  • marriages from 1965 to the present
  • not available for name change certificates

Where can I find tourist information for Ontario?

For tourist information for Ontario see Ontario Travel Information.


How can I find information on ski conditions in Ontario?

Skiers can obtain this information by dialing 1-800-ONTARIO. Toronto area callers can access the ski condition report by phoning 314-0998. Callers requesting information in French can receive the report by calling 1-800-268-3736. Bilingual counsellors are available Monday through Friday from 9 a.m. to 6 p.m., and Saturdays, Sundays and holidays from 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.

How can I find the travel distances between major Ontario cities?

You can use the Distance Table to quickly calculate the distance between major Ontario cities in miles or kilometres.

Where can I find information about road conditions on Ontario Highways?

Winter road condition information is available from the Ministry of Transportation Web site.
Also, for up-to-date information on current highway conditions due to weather or construction, please call the Ministry of Transportation's Road information Service (toll-free from within Ontario) at 1-800-268-1376 or in the Toronto calling area (416) 235-1110.
People with hearing disabilities who have access to the telecommunication device for the deaf (TTY) can call (905) 704-2426.
The COMPASS site has information on the traffic conditions of Highway 401 in Toronto and the QEW in Mississauga.

Where can I purchase the directories, books, reports and other information published by the Ontario Government?

These items are available for purchase from Publications Ontario.

How do I conduct a business name search?

At the present time it is not possible to conduct this search using our site on the Internet. Searches may be requested in person or by mail from the Ministry of Consumer and Commercial Relations, Companies Branch, 393 University Ave., 2nd Flr ., Toronto, ON, M5G 2M2, or through the Ontario Business Connects workstations. Information is available by calling 416-314-8880 or 1-800-361-3223 Toll Free in Ontario.

How do I register my business name?

There are several ways to register a business, including on-line registration. Registration information is available from the Ontario Business Connects Web site.

How do I find information on how to start a small business and loans for a small business?

Where can I find information about Employment Standards (including Domestic Workers, Homeworkers, Hours of Work, How to File a Claim, Minimum Wage, Overtime Pay, Parental Leave, Pregnancy Leave, Public Holidays, Severance Pay, Termination of Employment, Vacation Pay and Vacations with Pay and Employment Facts for Ontario Students)?

Where can I find information about rent control and landlord and tenant issues?

The Ontario Rental Housing Tribunal resolves disputes between landlords and tenants about rights and responsibilities under the Tenant Protection Act, including rent control, and landlord and tenant issues. Information is available at the Ontario Rental Housing Tribunal Web site. The Tribunal provides information to the public through a network of 20 local offices across Ontario. The Tribunal can be reached 24 hours a day by calling 1-888-332-3234 (Toll free from within Ontario).

Where can I find information about the Family Responsibility Office and how can I contact it?

Ministry of the Attorney General provides information about the Family Responsibility Office as part of their site.

Where can I find information about employment opportunities within the Ontario Government?

The Ontario Government does not advertise jobs on our Web site. Positions within the Provincial Government are listed in publications available at your local Ontario Government office as indicated in the Blue Pages of your telephone directory in the Ontario Government section. Applications and resumes can be sent to the Human Resources Branch office for the Ministry you are interested in working for.

Where can I find information on the Ontario government's Summer Employment Programs for youth and students?

Information on the Summer Experience Program, the Ontario Rangers Program, and the Ontario Government Regular Summer Student Hiring is available at the Summer Employment Programs Web site.

Where can I find information regarding the Ontario Student Assistance Program (OSAP)?

For information, to check the status of your application and loan forgiveness process or to see if an institution is approved for OSAP use the Ontario Student Assistance Program (OSAP) Web site.

Where can I obtain a copy of the latest Government of Ontario Information & Technology Standards - the official publications for standards, guidelines, technical reports, and preferred practices?

Current versions are always available on the Internet, with direct access to the GO-ITS Web site.
