We just sold all-hotels.co.uk for £2,500
The business who purchased it are attached to all-hotels.com. They didn't like the fact that we had purchased all-hotels.co.uk and had parked it to best-hotel.com (along with 8 other great domain names) and asked a Scottish court to issue an interdict. This is the equivalent of the English injunction. Consequently Nominet suspended the name pending future court action.
This would have meant that the name would have stayed registered to Best Hotel but couldn't have been used until the interdict was overturned.
We felt this was just a formality and instructed a solicitor to start proceedings to have the interdict lifted. In the meantime, however, we contacted all-hotels.com and suggested that they could purchase the name for a reasonable compensation - after all - we had built up considerable goodwill with it already and also suggested that when we win the case to lift the interdict it could prove costly to both parties. We suggested the name was worth £7,000.
They eventually agreed to pay a maximum of £2,500. We didn't have the time to take this further - we'd have preferred to keep the name - but the compensation helped.
Our main point, which we feel would have resulted in the interdict being overturned was that all-hotels.com had been trading for at least 2 years prior to us registering all-hotels.co.uk - why had they waited so long - names are issued on a first come first served basis - and a business which reportedly generates £75,000 per month were being a bit silly not tying up the name earlier.
We didn't register the name to "pass off" as all-hotels.com Best Hotel and Inn Directory is better anyway (grin) and as we feature hotels from all over the world it seems reasonable that we could call ourselves All Hotels if we want to.
