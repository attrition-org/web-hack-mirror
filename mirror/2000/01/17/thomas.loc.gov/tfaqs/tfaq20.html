Q20. What's this about legislation imposing a tax or long distance telephone charge on internet access?

Any report of pending or actual Federal Legislation imposing a tax or long distance telephone charge on internet access is a hoax.

No such legislation exists or has been introduced.

Often this hoax is associated with a bill "602P" or "602p". The numbering schene for identifying a particular Federal bill, resolution or law never includes an ending "P" or "p".

You can read about this hoax at the US Dept. of Energy Computer Incident Advisory Capability (CIAC)site.

Also, in response to erroneous reports that the FCC is planning to impose per minute usage charges on customer access to Internet Service Providers (ISP's), you can view these FCC Fact Sheets:

No Comsumer Per-Minute Charges To Access ISPs
The FCC, Internet Service Providers, and Access Charges

Additionally, the US Postal Service in a recent Press Release officially disclaims any validity to the rumor of bogus legislation imposing the payment of an Internet e-mail fee.

Legislation has been introduced in the current Congress that calls for a reduction or elimination of telephone taxes -- to wit:

H.R. 1234
SPONSOR: Rep Miller, Gary (introduced 03/23/99)
A bill to amend the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 to repeal the excise tax on telephone and other communications services.

S. 94
SPONSOR: Sen McCain, John (introduced 01/19/99)
A bill to repeal the telephone excise tax.

Other legislation has been introduced that specifically calls for prohibiting tax on Internet access. For example:

H.R.1291
SPONSOR: Rep Upton, Fred (introduced 03/25/99)
A bill to prohibit the imposition of access charges on Internet service providers, and for other purposes.

S.328
SPONSOR: Sen Smith, Bob (introduced 01/28/99)
A bill to make permanent the moratorium on the imposition of taxes on the Internet.

S.1043
SPONSOR: Sen McCain, John (introduced 05/13/99)
A bill to provide freedom from regulation by the Federal Communications Commission for the Internet.
