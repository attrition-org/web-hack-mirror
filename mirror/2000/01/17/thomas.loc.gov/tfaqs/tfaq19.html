Q19. Any suggestions for citation models of documents viewed from THOMAS?

The University of Memphis Government Publications Department has a page entitled: "Uncle Sam: Brief Guide to Citing Government Publications," which includes a section on citing online government publications.

At the bottom of the University of Memphis page mentioned above, there are "Hot Links" to several additional online electronic style guides.

They give the basic citation form for online sources as:

Author, Title (edition), TYPE OF MEDIUM. Date of publication. Supplier. Available: [online address]. [Access date.]

An example for the Congressional Record:

Ford, Rep. [TN] "Tribute to Rhodes College Mock Trial Team." Congressional Record. ONLINE. 25 May 1995. THOMAS. Available: http://thomas.loc.gov/home/r104query.html [2 February 1999].

An example for a Congressional bill:

U.S. House. 104th Congress, 1st Session, H.R. 1950, Newborns' and Mothers' Health Protection Act of 1995, as introduced in the House. ONLINE. THOMAS. Available: http://thomas.loc.gov/home/c104query.html [2 February 1999].

An example of a Committee Report:

U.S. House. Committee on the Judiciary. Proposing an Amendment to the Constitution of the United States with Respect to Tax Limitations (to accompany H.J.Res.62). (H. Rpt. 105-50). ONLINE. THOMAS. Available: http://thomas.loc.gov/home/cp105/cp105query.html [2 February 1999].

(End of suggestions for citation models)

In the last several years, several online style guides have been published in paper. Perhaps the best known is:

Li, X., & Crane, N. B. (1996). Electronic styles: A handbook for citing electronic information (Revised edition). Medford, NJ: Information Today. [ISBN: 1-57387-027-7, $19.99]
