Q18. Can you tell me about the 106th Congress?

  • These are the Members of the 106th Congress:
    The U.S. House of Representatives
    The U.S. Senate

  • This is the Party makeup of each Chamber:
    The U.S. House of Representatives consists of 221 Republicans, 211 Democrats, 1 Independent.
    The U.S. Senate consists of 55 Republicans and 45 Democrats.

  • This is the one Independent serving in Congress:
    The Honorable Bernard Sanders, Representative from Vermont, is the one Independent serving in this U.S. Congress.

  • This is how many women are serving in Congress:
    There are currently 58 women in this U.S. Congress: 49 in the House of Representatives and nine in the U.S. Senate.

  • These are the longest serving Members of Congress:
    In the Senate, Strom Thurmond of South Carolina has the longest continuous service: He began his Senate service on November 7, 1956 (2nd session, 84th Congress).

    In the House, John Dingell of Michigan has the longest continuous service: He began his House service on December 13, 1955 (1st session 84th Congress).

    Carl T. Hayden of Arizona holds the all-time record for length of service in Congress. He served a total of 56 years, 10 months, and 28 days (in the House from February 19, 1912, to March 3, 1927, and in the Senate from March 4, 1927 to January 3, 1969.

  • This is the current salary level of all Members of Congress and Senators:
    All Members of Congress and Senators currently earn an annual salary of $141,300. The Speaker of the House earns $181,400. The Majority and Minority Leaders earn $157,000.
