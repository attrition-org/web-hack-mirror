Q3. How do I download a bill, a committee report or a section of the Congressional Record to my computer?

When you link to the text of a bill or the text of a committee report, you will see a list of options across the top of the page.

GPO's PDF version of this bill
Reference to this bill in the Congressional Record
Link to the Bill Summary & Status file
Download
The exact appearance of the image may vary.

Under Bill Text searching, If you click on Download, THOMAS copies an HTML ASCII version of the bill text to you browser. You can then use your browser's save-file feature to save the file to your computer.

Under Committee Report searching, if you click on Download, THOMAS initiates an ftp (file transfer protocol) session, sending your browser an ASCII version of the text. You can then use your browser's save-file feature to save the file to your computer.

If you click on GPO'sPDF version, you will receive a .pdf version, which you can save through your browser's save-file feature and which you can read through the Adobe Acrobat reader. If you do not already have Adobe Acrobat, you will be given the opportunity of downloading it for free.

The primary difference you will notice between the ASCII and the PDF version is that the PDF version will look like the original nted version of the bill and will include line numbers, whereas the ASCII version is in plain text.

Also THOMAS provides download capability for the text of articles in the Congressional Record.
