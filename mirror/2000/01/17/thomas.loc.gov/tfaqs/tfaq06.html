Q6. How do I link a particular part of THOMAS to my own web site?

Bear in mind that since THOMAS provides access to information about federal legislation which is in the public domain, no special permission is required to use (e.g. photocopy, build permanent links to, etc.) the data, or the THOMAS web site itself.

The URL addresses you see when you execute a search, are temporary addresses and will expire in about 30 minutes. However, you may wish to establish a permanent link to a bill, a committee report, a list of bills sponsored by a member of Congress, or some other search result without having to re-execute the search every time. Detailed information about how to do this can be accessed from the THOMAS homepage by clicking on About THOMAS and at the next screen clicking on About InQuery. There it is explained in the Web Developer's Guide on How To Make Direct Links to THOMAS Documents.
