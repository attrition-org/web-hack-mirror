Q2. How do I find actual text of legislation passed before 1989 and other historical information?

There are probably no web sites with a comprehensive, full text database of legislation introduced into Congress before 1989. THOMAS does have summary information under Bill Summary & Status for legislation introduced since 1973. This does not contain full text of bills.

From THOMAS you can also access Annals of Congress, 1789-1793, early precursors to the Congressional Record.

For government research or possible information on obtaining a print copy of the text of a legislative document prior to 1989, via a Federal Depository Library (FDL) nearest you, visit the FDL Program web site. Or, you can contact the Gov't Printing Office at any of the following. (Caution: The Gov't Printing Office web site, GPO Access, is a large omnibus site and you may experience long delays.)

  • toll free phone: 1-888-293-6498
  • web site: GPO Access
  • email: gpoaccess@gpo.gov

The United States Code, the body of federal law into which bills signed into law eventually find their place. is online from several sources, e.g. the House of Representatives Internet Law Library and GPO Access. There are no links available in THOMAS or any other internet site which map a given public law to its place in the United States Code.

Also, the U.S. House of Representatives Clerk's Office provides a very useful Congressional archives searching tool entitled, Historical Information.
