Q17. Where can I find objective, impartial information on the impeachment process?

A number of Web sites on impeachment have been developed over the last several months, since the topic is clearly relevant to current events. Although many sites are political in nature, there are a few that strive to be non-partisan, describing the impeachment process as mandated by the Constitution. Here is a list of such sites:

  • Federal Impeachment Materials (CIS)
    This page provides bibliographic references to primary source materials of the U.S. Congress on impeachment cases, censure cases, and related Congressional reproval actions. The listings are grouped into Impeachment and Censure sections. Each entry in each section is accessible by name alphabetically, by name chronologically, and by result of initiated action.

  • Backgrounder on Impeachment (Legal Information Institute of Cornell University Law School)
    Deals with the topics: What is Impeachment?; The Impeachment Process in a Nutshell; The Constitutional Authority for Impeachment; What is Censure? Links to Official Sites, Official Documents, Related Topical Websites, and Textual Sources. The Official Documents include the Senate Rules Governing Impeachment (Rules of Procedure and Practice in the Senate When Sitting on Impeachment Trials [PDF]) and the (House Rules Governing Impeachment [PDF]).

  • Impeachment of President William Jefferson Clinton (Grace York, Government Documents Coordinator, University of Michigan Library)
    A superb collection of impeachment materials, including Congressional Research Service (CRS) Reports and Memoranda. Topics covered include: Grand Jury; Historical Materials; House Floor Debates; House Judiciary Committee; Independent Counsel; Jones v. Clinton; Key Players; Procedures; Senate Trial (Articles of Impeachment - Censure - Senate Procedures - Senators); and Related Materials.

  • Impeachment (Michael Nelson, The Presidency A - Z, 2nd ed., 1998, Congressional Quarterly)
    From the highly respected publisher of legislative books and periodicals. Includes the topics: Constitutional Origins, Impeachment Procedures, Impeachment of Andrew Johnson, Case of Richard Nixon, Other Impeachment Cases, Additional Readings.

  • The American Presidency: Impeachment (Grolier Online)
    A very brief write-up of impeachment basics and history.

  • Impeachment: An Introduction (IntellectualCapitol.com)
    Covers topics: What does the Constitution say?; How often has the process been used?; What is an impeachable offense?; What procedures would the Congress follow?; What is the role of the courts?

  • A Citizen's Guide to Impeachment (Alan Hirsch, Esq., Essential Books)
    Although hosted by a partisan source, this in-depth treatment of impeachment in the "Frequently Asked Questions" format has lots of objective information on the process.

  • The Federal Impeachment Process A Bibliographic Guide to English and American Precedence, Historical and Procedural Development, and Scholarly Commentary. (Cornell Law School)
    References for further study on impeachment.
