Library hours, facilities, and services

Part of the Faculty of Law complex, the UBC Law Library is available to the university community, the legal profession, and members of the general public in need of legal materials. It forms part of the campus network of libraries and its holdings are incorporated into the union on-line catalogue.

The law collection consists of 280,000 volumes of both primary and secondary materials including statutes, law reports and subordinate legislation from all Canadian jurisdictions, Great Britain, Australia and New Zealand. The United States's primary materials are now available on-line via Westlaw and Lexis. The library supports its book collection of Canadian authorities through the extensive use of retrieval systems such as QuickLaw and Canadian Law On-Line, accessible in the computer lab. Advance reasons-for-judgment are received from the Supreme and Federal Courts of Canada, from most British Columbia courts

and from major administrative boards. A treaty and document collection supports the study of international law, and basic English-language materials describing the legal systems of foreign jurisdictions enhances research in comparative law.

A specialized Asian law collection, unique to Canadian law libraries, is maintained and comprised of materials in the vernacular from The People's Republic of China, Hong Kong, Japan, Korea and Taiwan. The Law Library holds many legal treatises, periodicals, and government publications, as well as the standard legal indexes (some on CD-ROM), bibliographies, encyclopedias, digests and citators which facilitate research. A substantial collection of microform reprints and audio-visual materials complements the book collection.

The Law Library is situated on three floors and has 560 places for study. Library staff are available to assist users in solving their bibliographic problems, and in providing instruction in basic legal research skills.

Students are provided with e-mail addresses and QuickLaw and Internet access.
