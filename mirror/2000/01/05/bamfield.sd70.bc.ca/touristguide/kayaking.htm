Kayaking:

 

        Barkley Sound, in which Bamfield is situated, offers wonderful a wonderful kayaking experience for people of all ages and skill levels.

Calm Waters for the Novice Kayaker

The inlets around Bamfield, with their calm waters, are ideal for those who have never kayaked before. Full and half day tours are available. Watch for eagles, river otters, seals, and bears along the way.

Coastal Tours for the Intermediate Kayaker...

Trips up or down the edge of Barkley Sound offer more challenging paddling, and the opportunity to take a break on one of the beautiful sand beaches that line the coast.

Open Sea for Experienced Kayakers

A trip to the Deer Group from Bamfield involves crossing open ocean, and should only be considered by very experienced paddlers, or guided tours. The scenery is spectacular, and several of the island have beaches ideal for camping.

Multi-Day Trips ...

Mutli-day trips to the Deer Group are an exciting way for experienced paddlers to see Barkley Sound. The coast offers up to five days of paddling. You can cross Trevor Channel (open ocean) in about one hour, or you can arrange to have your kayaks dropped off by a local service or the Lady Rose (see Transportation for details). For information about camping in Pacific Rim National Park, contact the park at 728-3234. For information about camping on Huu ay aht Band reserve land contact the band at 728-3414.
