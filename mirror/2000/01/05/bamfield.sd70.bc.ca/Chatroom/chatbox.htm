How to use this chat client

Connecting, Basic Commands, Moderator Commands

How to connect:    click here to launch the chat program, and then follow these instructions.

        Once the program loads, click in the box located in the top right corner where it says YourName.  Delete that text, and replace it with an alias, or nickname you want to be referred to as.  Now click the Connect button in the top left area of the window.  It should say after a short time say "Now talking in Channel #BamfieldChat".  You are now connected and in the chat channel.  On the left of your chat window you will see a vertical rectangular box with your alias in it.  If there are no other names in this box, you are the only one in the channel.  Relax, your either early or you aren't planning on meeting anyone.

Basic Chat Commands:

NOTE: when typing commands do not include the "<" and ">" that are in the examples, these simply mean "replace this with".

Here are the most Basic commands you have to know to start chatting :
  -  All commands in Internet Relay Chat begin with a "/".
  -  To "talk" in IRC, type anything without a "/" at the beginning.

To execute commands, type them in the text box at the bottom of the window.

First, DO NOT TYPE /help, for some reason this program will crash and you will have to reload if you type this command.

/nick
Allows you to change your nickname.  Example: /nick Ben would change your name to Ben.

/list
This will list ALL the available channels.  NOTE: Most servers would produce a *very lengthy* list but ours is fairly low-key and located in Denmark (I believe).

/join #channel name (for example /join #mychannel)
If you want to join one of the other channels on the server, use this.  NOTE: We do not own this server, it is free for our use.  So all 99% of the people in other channels will not be related to this sites content or purpose.  If you want to split a discussion or move your discussion once all the members have arrived, type /join #<whatever_you_want> and make your own channel.  Then invite the other people into it by sending individual messages (/msg <user> <message>) to each, or by notifying the entire channel (/notify #<channel> <message>).

/whois <nickname>
Give some specific information about a particular person (hostmark, server used...)

/msg <nickname> <message>
Sends a private message to another person

|#,#
Sets the color of text, Example: |4,0Hello. (| is the character above \ )

/wake <nickname>
Tries to get a users attention by playing a sound on their computer.

/ignore <nickname>
Does not display text sent from <nickname>.  To take off ignore, simply perform the command again.

/ping <nickname>
Displays the amount of time it takes for a message to reach <nickname>

/opass <password>
Obtain operator status.  This is only available to the persons who register the channel.  In this case us.  However if you want to permanently create your own channel, you can do it from the www.leahcim.com page.

Moderator Commands (Channel Operator):

The moderator(s) or channel operator(s) of a channel have special powers over what goes on the channel and its members.  Channel operators are denoted by an @ symbol in front of their nickname in the nickname listing window (Note: the @ symbol isn't included when writing private /msg 's to such individuals).  The first person to enter a channel automatically becomes the channel operator. 

/topic <What ever you could possibly want>
Lets you set a topic for the channel, this text will scroll along the top of the window for everyone connected to the channel.  It will also be displayed to people who do a /list command.

/kick <nickname>
Allows you to boot unwanted or unruly members of the conversation our of the channel.

/OP <nickname>
Gives operator status to another member of the channel

/DEOP <nickname>
Removes operator status from another member of the channel
