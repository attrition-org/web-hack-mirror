Check List

A SERVICE OF:

The following check list has been developed with the hope that its contents will help protect the homes and families that constitute the population of people that make up our fine state. If this checklist saves just one life from fire death, our goal will have been fulfilled.

Fire Prevention in the home begins with a constant and continuing awareness of potential fire dangers. This awareness must be apart of each family member's continuing daily thought process.

We the people of Arkansas live in a state that has been recognized statistically as having the 2nd to 5th highest fire death state in the USA.

IT IS TIME TO CHANGE THAT. THE CHANGE BEGINS WITH YOU AND STARTS RIGHT IN THE HOME YOU LIVE IN with the educating of your family.

The following check list is offered to you FREE of charge from the Arkansas Fire Academy with the hopes that you and your loved ones will live a fire safe life, forever and always.

 

THE THREE KEYS TO FIRE SAFETY

  • 1. The Absolute key to fire safety lies in the word prevention.
  • 2. Prevention is a continuous process that need be applied in all activity / action that the potential for fire injury may occur.
  • 3. Prevention thoughts or application must be a daily reality that we must all be able to recall on our education to prevent fires and safeguard whenever the need appears.

 

HOME SAFETY CHECK LIST

A. Install Smoke alarms _______. Install on every level of house, keeping in mind every family member must be able to hear the alarm. If you sleep with your bedroom door closed, consider installing one inside your bedroom. A good rule of thumb to remember is a smoke alarm will not help, if you can't hear it. So place as many in your home as necessary to sound the alarm for all to hear.

Note: The use of smoke alarms in your home is an absolute requirement for a fire safe home. This simple device will protect you when you are most vulnerable to fire and injury (when you are asleep) or it will warn you of fire danger in an area of the house that you couldn't have recognized without the sounding of its alarm.

 

B. Check your smoke alarm, at minimum yearly _______. (The Arkansas Fire Academy recommends four checks yearly).

Note: Check your smoke alarms at least once a year, make the testing of your smoke alarm a reality that associates its test with an event that you can't forget.

Example: TEST IT on your Birthday.

Note: Never take batteries out of a smoke alarm for any reason, if it is disturbing your cooking LET IT.

NOTE: The Arkansas Fire Academy recommends you check the alarms four times yearly and do it with the help of your children. Make it fun and make it a learning experience.

 

C. Prepare a family escape plan and practice it as often as it takes to educate

every member of your household ________.

-The escape plan or evacuation plan will allow your family to react to a fire situation with swift and confident movement. A well rehearsed plan will help eliminate much of the confusion that is associated with a fire situation.

- Always plan and practice two routes from each room. If one route leads to a window, always insure you have a safe way to get to the ground surface.

Fire Safety Tips

FOR CHILDREN

Teach your children fire safety. (Start at the preschool age)

Teach them to stop drop and roll. (Should fire get on their clothing)

Remember most fires started by children are started by children of preschool age.

Keep matches out of children's reach.

The Arkansas Fire Academy has a children's fire safety house / instructional center. It can be made available to your child's school. Speak to us or discus the use of it with your children's school. We will be glad to help. The state of Arkansas has FIVE such children's fire safety houses. Call the Arkansas Children's Hospital for the location of the one nearest you.

 

FOR THE KITCHEN

Never leave cooking unattended.

Turn pot handles in (so as not to bump them accidentally in passing).

Do not cook while wearing garments that are loose fitting or have loose fitting sleeves.

Never overload electrical outlets (kitchens and utility rooms are common places for this danger).

 

FIREPLACE

Burn only prescribed fuel sources.

Always keep a fire screen in place (anytime the fireplace is in use).

 

IN CASE OF FIRE:

"When smoke alarms sound "

If you are in bed, do not sit straight up.

Roll off the bed, always keeping in mind that smoke and heat rise. Remember you can't breath unless you are below the heat and smoke layer.

Crawl under the smoke to exit the house (use your fire escape plan).

Check each door for heat before opening it. Use the back of your hand. If the door is hot, use an alternate exit route.

Once your family is out of the house DO NOT GO BACK INSIDE for any reason.

  • Call the fire department or 911 from a neighbor's house. Do not attempt to make the call from your burning house.
  • If your are trapped: close the door behind you, stuff the cracks around the door with clothing or sheets. Wait by a window and signal for help, if you can not exit through the window. Note: Window escape ladders can help provide a safe way out in such situations.

Remember delay can be deadly. For this reason you must have a fire escape plan and be prepared to use it immediately. If you have a two-story residents, window fire escape ladders should be available in every bedroom upstairs.

Make sure all family members can unlock doors and open all windows for escape during escape drills.

Get out FAST!

Find a meeting place for all to gather.

Remember to use only cool water on burns. Keep water on the burn for approximately 15 minutes. If blisters appear seek medical help immediately.

Do not go back into the house for any reason.

Fire safety equipment for the home

1.Smoke alarms minimum one per floor. Arkansas Fire Academy recommends all bedrooms, hallway, kitchen, Living area (in particular, if fireplace is in place).

2. Escape ladder for all second-floor homes one per each bedroom. (This is a rope like ladder that hooks on to the window ledge itself.)

3.A minimum of one all-purpose ABC Type fire extinguisher per every three rooms. (Locate accessible to kitchen, laundry room and located near the water heater/ home heat sources.)

4. Fire blankets insure safer travel in traversing your escape route.

5. Home sprinkler systems prevent major property lose in fire disasters.
