ARKANSAS FIRE ACADEMY ADVISORY BOARD MEETING – The next meeting of the Arkansas Fire Academy Advisory Board will be held at the Camden Training Site on April 6, 2000 at 1:00 p.m.

INSTRUCTOR HOUSING – We encourage any department requesting training from the AFA and who has available dormitory space to allow the visiting instructor(s) to stay in your dormitory. We ask this in an effort to keep the overall cost of training down and, at the same time, we hope to be able to deliver more classes overall. The costs of setting our instructor(s) up in a hotel is very costly and we could certainly cut down on that expense by having our staff member(s) join your on-duty personnel during their stay. The end result is that we hope to get those same instructors out to visit you more often.

NEW VOLUNTARY RURAL AND VOLUNTEER FIREFIGHTER II STATE-LEVEL CERTIFICATION PROGRAM – The AFA has developed and is hoping to implement in the near future a new TOTALLY VOLUNTARY program for rural and volunteer firefighters that will lead to a state level certification as a Firefighter II. We are currently seeking funding to implement this NEW program. The program includes training that would be conducted in each of the seven fire training region on a very flexible schedule. Again, this program would be TOTALLY VOLUNTARY, NOT A MANDATORY REQUIREMENT. (This program would be in addition to [not taking the place of] the IFSAC Self Study programs – see item below.)

FIREFIGHTER I AND FIREFIGHTER II SELF STUDY PROGRAMS – The revision continues on the self study programs to update them to the 1997 edition of NFPA 1001 and the 4th edition of Essentials. The revised programs will be re-titled: the Arkansas Fire Academy Firefighter I and II IFSAC Field Training Preparation Programs. We now anticipate a mid-April release date. Once the revised programs are released, personnel enrolling in them will then be tested out of the 4th edition of Essentials.

UNIFIED FIRE SCIENCE DEGREE PROGRAM – The Unified Fire Science Degree Program was approved by the SAU Advisory Board and the Academic Advisory Board of the Arkansas Department of Higher Education. This brings this program another step closer to helping firefighters across Arkansas complete a two-year degree.

INSTRUCTOR JASON WILLS – There have been some ugly rumors floating around the state questioning Jason Wills’ educational and experiential backgrounds. We’re taking this opportunity to dispel these rumors. Prior to working for the AFA, Jason Wills worked as a technician for the Oklahoma Fire Service Training Academy in Stillwater, Oklahoma. He served on the Oklahoma City Document Management Team and was responsible for research and documentation of the Murrah Federal Building Bombing Report. Jason worked at the OSU Department of Environmental Health and Safety and was a firefighter with the Ingalls Fire Protection District in Ingalls, OK, the Kennett Fire Department in Kennett, MO, and Rural Metro at Eaker Air Force Base in Blytheville. He is IFSAC certified as a Firefighter I and II and Instructor I and II. Jason holds an Associates in Arts degree through Mississippi County Community College and a Bachelors of Science in Engineering - Fire Protection and Safety through Oklahoma State University. He currently serves as Chief Instructor for the Firefighter Standards Course and as a volunteer firefighter with the Camden Fire Department. Jason is a member of the Arkansas Firefighter’s Association, National Fire Protection Association, Society of Fire Protection Engineers, OSU Fire Protection Society, OSU Alumni Association, National Fire Academy Association, and SAU Tech Faculty Senate. In all, Instructor Wills has over 9 ½ years of operational fire fighting experience.

UNIFIED FIRE AND EMERGENCY RESPONSE ASSOCIATE IN APPLIED SCIENCE DEGREE PROGRAM - We continue to move forward with our articulation project with other two-year colleges across Arkansas. We spoke recently with Steve Floyd from the Arkansas Department of Higher Education about the benefits of such a program and he asked us to prepare a written proposal to present to ADHE at their annual meeting in February. He seemed very positive about approval being granted for this proposal.

IFSAC RE-ACCREDITATION VISIT – The recent IFSAC re-accreditation visit went very well. We were the first fire training academy to go on line with the new

NFPA Job Performance Requirement format. Arkansas First, just the way it should be! The team members offered us many valuable recommendations that should ensure our re-accreditation at IFSAC’s annual meeting in April 2000 in Lexington, Kentucky.

Volume I Number 3 October 20, 1998

Hey, our third issue and we’re going strong! We’ve had a lot going on in the past couple of months.

THANK YOU - We would like to thank all the departments who have donated pictures for our dormitory rooms. You have provided us with some very beautiful images of your departments and they certainly brighten up the rooms. Again, thank you very much. - Tom Forbes

AFA BUDGET - Things are moving along to improve the budget for the AFA, however, we face a big hurdle in November. The Arkansas Fire Academy’s budget is tied into the budget for Higher Education. If Amendment 4 is voted in, we have been informed that we could lose up to $372,000 in operating budget per year. As we’ve told you before, our budget is already underfunded and this could have a devastating effect upon our ability to serve the firefighters of this state. - Tom Forbes and Jeff Fortney

CERTIFICATES - You may have noticed a quicker turn around in certificates and EMT / CPR paperwork. The reason we’ve caught up so much recently is the addition of two part-time administrative personnel who only work in those two areas of concern.

We intend to go one step further in the near future. We hope to have our satellite site personnel type up the certificates for each class as it is in session and hand them to the graduates at the end of the course. Then the three part forms will be processed through the regular channels along with all attendance records and anything else required to log the information into our registrar database. We feel this will make all of you much happier and at the same time reduce the registrar’s work load. The entire process should be a heck of a lot quicker and more efficient.

This is a system that will require everyone to be completely trustworthy and honest. Training certificates simply cannot be given out unless individuals successfully complete the course(s) the certificates represent. If this becomes a problem, this new approach to getting the certificates out will end.

REMINDER - Sylvia Langley, our Registrar, needs to know thirty (30) days prior to the start of a Firefighter Standards class the exact number of personnel who will be attending and have their applications. This continues to be a problem and departments are still not providing accurate numbers on how many personnel they plan to send. Please help us to help you. Thanks. - Jeff Fortney

POSITIVE CHANGES ARE HAPPENING - If you haven’t been seeing positive change come from the AFA, I believe you should give it another look. I want you all to know the people working within this academy DO WORK HARD for you. I often hear complaints about my staff and their inability to get the work out efficiently. I am here to tell you these kinds of complaints just don’t have a leg to stand on.

INTERESTING FACT #1 - One of the largest and best equipped fire academies found in this nation services 8,000 customers and it has a staff of 142 full time workers to meet their needs.

INTERESTING FACT #2 - The Arkansas Fire Academy has a customer base of close to 15,000 and has a staff of ONLY 20 full time workers and two part time workers. Therefore, I think we are doing well just keeping our business moving along smoothly.

I write this because my employees need to hear praise from you now and then. We all do. I would like you all to help us help you. Just the other day my registrar lady was kept on the phone for thirty minutes fielding a complaint about late paperwork.... THINK ABOUT IT. (That’s 30 minutes she could have spent processing certificates!)

In closing, one year ago when I first arrived at the Academy, the academy was any where from 125 to 150 days behind in paper work. We are now less than 40 days behind overall. We are getting better but don’t expect problems that have been around for years to disappear over night. We are all working on making it better, please HELP US. - Tom Forbes

Volume I Number 2 August 24, 1998

Welcome to the second issue of the Arkansas Fire Academy Communicator! We have several different topics to cover in this issue and are glad we have this opportunity to get them out to you this way.

Fire Trucks Donated to AFA - On August 3rd, the Arkansas Fire Academy received two older model Mack Pumpers donated to the academy by Little Rock Fire Department and the City of Little Rock. These apparatus were donated to the academy through the efforts of Little Rock Fire Chief Rubin Webb and Cy Carney, Little Rock’s City Manager. These two pumpers will be valuable additions to our vehicle fleet during our budget crisis The Macks were turned over to the AFA freshly maintenanced and well equipped for service. Our thanks to the people of Little Rock, Cy Carney, Chief Webb, and the firefighters of Little Rock. - Tom Forbes and Jeff Fortney

Satellite Budget Quest - Many of you have been calling and asking how things are going with the process of gaining operational and maintenance budgets for the satellites. May I start by telling you that we have been getting nothing but good response from our state government. The issue is being worked by a number of different people representing a variety of different legislative sources. The problem is being given much attention for the right reasons. I truly believe the correct people are working the issues and the proper results are right around the corner. In closing, let’s all be patient and trust in the fact that the problem is being worked by people who understand it and have the concern and ability to correct. - Tom Forbes.

EMS Continuing Education Units - Some of you may have heard that Kenny Shaw who was our contact person for continuing education units at Arkansas Health Department -EMS has left AHD-EMS. When he left, the folks at EMS initially planned to discontinue assigning CEUs hours to fire service courses offered through the Arkansas Fire Academy. Jeff Fortney and Rob Connell (AFA EMS Coordinator) have been working with David Moskowitz and Barbara Poole at EMS to continue the program. Following a recent review of our courses, EMS has agreed that those courses which have a definite relationship to EMS will still carry CEUs, the remaining courses will not. CEUs will no longer be printed on AFA certificates but must be submitted to AHD-EMS by completing the appropriate AHD-EMS form to receive credit for them. This was the best deal we could work out with the Arkansas Health Department- EMS at this time. - Jeff Fortney.

Fire Safety House - The Fire Safety House to be stored at the Arkansas Fire Academy in Camden arrived here in April 1998. To schedule training on how to use the Fire Safety House contact Diane Hughes at Arkansas Children’s Hospital at 501-320-2846. To schedule the Fire Safety House for your community, contact Terry Harcrow at 870-574-1521 or Diane Hughes at 501-320-2846. - Terry Harcrow.

AFA Administration Building Open House - We’ve done some remodeling at the AFA’s Admin building to improve our appearance and our working conditions. It’s amazing what a fresh coat of paint, some new blinds, and new tile and carpet can do to brighten a workplace. We’d like to share this with you and are having an Open House on September 17th from 2:00 p.m. to 4:00 p.m. Please join us for refreshments and a visit. - Sue Horton.

Changes to the Associate Degree Program - Some changes are being made to the AAS in Fire and Emergency Response degree program. In an effort to make the degree program more effective, we have revamped the degree plan itself and how semester hours from the Firefighter Standards, EMT, Firefighter I and Firefighter II courses will be applied. Previously, these courses were in the Fire Service electives while Introduction to Fire and Emergency Response and Company Officer I were in the Fire Sservice Core Requirements area. These will be swapped around. Students attending the Firefighter Standards, FFI, and FFII courses will now receive credit for FFI, FFII, and Hazardous Materials in the FS Core Requirements area. Intro and Company Officer I will now be considered electives. EMT, once an elective, may now be used to help fulfill Fire Service Core requirements or be used as an elective if the student already has First Responder. Also, the maximum number of semester hours awardable through the Portfolio Development and Review process will now be thirty (30) semester hours. - Jeff Fortney.

Storm '98 in Pine Bluff are coming back into the office to get back to work. Chief Jacks and his personnel did a marvelous job of hosting this year's convention. We certainly enjoyed the time we spent in fellowship with the firefighters and chiefs and we are eagerly looking forward to Fire Storm '99 in Jonesboro.

Doctor Roger Worsley, Jeff Fortney and I wish to thank the members of the three associations for allowing us to speak with them recently regarding some concerns expressed across the state about the Academy. You provided us with an opportunity to share our thoughts regarding the Academy's current status and our ideas for its future.

We thank you for your input and we hope we've answered your questions. The number one thing you showed us is that we have been lax in communicating to you with what we are trying to do to help bring the Academy into the 21st century. We've started so many projects that we let ourselves get wrapped in trying to get them done and have neglected to keep you in the loop.

That brings us to the purpose of this newsletter. Jeff Fortney and I understand now how important it is for us to communicate with you, hence the name of this letter: AFA COMMUNICATOR. We intend to publish this communication paper every couple of months to supplement the information published in the Firewatch. We hope this will enable us to keep you better informed on our progress and problems. Please share these newsletters with your department personnel and with others.

We hope you pass along copies of the FACT SHEET we passed out at the convention to your firefighters and neighboring departments. We appreciate your assistance in getting the facts out about the academy, its physical and budgetary conditions, and what we are hoping to do to fix these problems. If you need more copies of our FACT SHEET, please call us at the Arkansas Fire Academy at 870-574-1521 and we'll send you some.

You've given us some new ideas to approach the degree program with. Dr. Worsley and Jeff have already had a short meeting on ways to improve the program. We're going to fix it and you are surely going to enjoy the new direction we=re going with it. In a nutshell, we are going to make it easier and expand the programs ability to capture college credit hours from your previous fire training through various sources.

It has taken 30 years for the Academy to reach its current, antiquated condition. We've accomplished a few positive steps to move the Academy forward in the few months that Jeff and I have been here. With your support and your patience we'll be able to accomplish even more. The joint resolution from the Chiefs and Firefighters Associations to ask the Governor to provide the Academy with emergency funding is a fantastic start and we are grateful for that demonstration of your support.

The last and most important note: the most important gain for the Arkansas Fire Academy this last week has been the support of the three fire associations to help us through the difficult times we are in. This alone has greatly improved our chances of success for future improvements.

Have a fire safe day!

Tom Forbes, Director
Jeff Fortney, Assistant Director
