For Canadians Needing IFSAC Certification

 

NEED IFSAC CERTIFICATION?

Are you seeking IFSAC certification as a Fire Fighter II under NFPA Standard 1001? The Arkansas Fire Academy, a division of Southern Arkansas University - Tech (SAU-Tech), in Camden, Arkansas may be the answer to your needs.

Through our Industrial Training Program, we offer our Firefighter Standards course to non-Arkansas residents and industrial personnel. This program is also a vital part of our Associate Degree in Fire and Emergency Response program through SAU-Tech. What does this mean? It means, that in addition to receiving quality, accredited training to the Firefighter II level through IFSAC, you can also earn college credit towards an associates degree by successful completion of this course. SAU-Tech will soon offer education via the Internet. Courses to be offered include the General Education Core Curriculum and some of our Fire Science courses. Once you leave Camden, you could continue your education through SAU-Tech via computer at home or with attendance at an accredited local community college.

FIREFIGHTER STANDARDS

The Firefighter Standards Course is seven weeks long and is structured around the training line items of NFPA Standard 1001. This course includes nighttime training, exercises and drills

PREREQUISITES

  1. Meet Canadian standards for age, health, fitness, education, etc…
    1. Complete 12th Grade or have your GED.
    2. Possess class 3 and 4 driver's licenses (with air brakes).
    3. Driver's abstract - no more than 6 points in last 3 years.
  1. Copy of the results of a medical physical conducted by a physician. (No vision or hearing impairments.)
  2. Copy of physical fitness testing results conducted by Fire Fit.
  3. Current Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR) card. (American Red Cross, "CPR for the Professional Rescuer"; American Heart Association, "CPR Course C" or "CPR for Health Care Provides, modules 1 through 4"; or equivalent.)
  4. Occupational First Aid Level III or First Responder Level III.

 

PRICES

You will be responsible for your transportation from your home to Little Rock (or El Dorado), Arkansas and then back home. We will help you reach the academy from there. (Please keep us posted on ALL travel arrangements.)

It costs $3,140* (U.S. dollars) to attend the Firefighter Standards course. This includes: lodging and linen at our Fire Academy dormitories, 3 meals per day Monday through Thursday and 2 meals on Friday at our SAU-Tech cafeteria; a copy of the IFSTA Essentials manual, IFSAC Seal fee, tuition for 9 credit hours, and course fees.

You will need to provide meals for yourself Friday evening and on the weekend. You are responsible for bringing enough money for the normal expenses you might incur during your stay. There is one small grocery store and a convenience stores within four miles of the campus where you can purchase some food items. Larger stores are available in Camden, approximately 7 miles from campus.

(* - Prices are subject to change based on costs of housing and food.)

 

EQUIPMENT REQUIREMENTS

We recommend that you bring your own personal protective equipment and self-contained breathing apparatus. If not, we can provide personal protective equipment and self-contained breathing apparatus for your use during training.

We do not provide student duty uniforms. We encourage our students to bring their work uniforms from their home departments. If you don't have a uniform, work style clothing (blue jeans, T-shirt, and shirt) will suffice.

 

LIVING ACCOMODATIONS

Our Fire Academy students live in the Fire Academy dormitories right on the campus of SAU-Tech. These dorms are within walking distance of our gymnasium, student learning resource center, cafeteria, and our current Fire Academy office building (which will someday become a student fire station [fire hall]). We currently have three dormitory buildings. One building houses our television lounge
