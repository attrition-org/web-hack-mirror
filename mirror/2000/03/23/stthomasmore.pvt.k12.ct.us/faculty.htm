Dedicated teachers are the cornerstone of our program. All are graduates of excellent universities and many with advanced degrees. In addition, almost all members of our faculty and their families live on campus, either in dormitory apartments or in other faculty housing, thus creating a 24-hour-a-day atmosphere that fosters a sense of interdependence, community and family.

This close proximity allows faculty members to get to know their students, not just as pupils, but as individuals. At Saint Thomas More School, classroom instruction is only the beginning of a teacher's job. Our teachers also inspire, motivate and encourage the young men entrusted to our care. It is their careful attention, their willingness to go the extra mile, that ensures that our students keep up with their daily workload, or catch up if they lag behind.

In addition to their daily contact in the classroom, teachers and their families also interact with students in the dining hall, in the gymnasium and on the playing fields. They supervise evening study hall, coach sports, chaperone dances, monitor the dining hall, take students on trips, organize clubs, and participate in the life of the school in a myriad of other ways. In the Advisor-Advisee Program each teacher works closely with a small group of students, meeting regularly throughout the year. In their role of advisor, teachers are able to keep track of their advisees' progress, offer counsel and advice on issues of importance to them, and maintain open communication with parents.
