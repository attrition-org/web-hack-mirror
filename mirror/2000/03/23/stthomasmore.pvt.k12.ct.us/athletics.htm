he importance of developing strong bodies as well as sound  minds is one of the school's abiding principles.

Afternoon athletics is a long-standing tradition in boarding  schools, and a requirement at Saint Thomas More School. Each  student must participate in a program of physical fitness  and training during the athletic period.

We make a strong attempt to insure each student receives  the opportunity to compete on our interscholastic teams. Some  opt for intramural activities or individual sports, such as  weight-training and swimming.

SUCKS TO BE YOU -->
At  all levels, emphasis is placed on the development of good  sportsmanship, competitiveness, self-discipline and confidence.  Each student's athletic experience is designed to provide  him with a chance to engage in satisfying athletic challenges,  as well as to provide a counterbalance to academic pressures.

Saint Thomas More School competes with other prestigious  New England prep schools as a member of the Southeastern New  England Athletic Conference (SENE) division of the New England Prep School Athletic Conference (NEPSAC).

Physical education is taught by our dedicated coaching staff.  Each boy develops character and skills through personal motivation  and instruction. We also emphasize the development of skills  in activities which our young men hopefully will enjoy throughout  their adult lives.
