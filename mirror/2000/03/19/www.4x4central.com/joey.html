Joey Jones'
1992 Explorer

At the Mud Pits, Bentonville, Arkansas

(Click on photos for Hi-Res versions)


My Explorer was bought in '91 by my parents.  My mother used it all the way through '95 without me even touching it.  After I got my permit to drive I decided that I wanted the Explorer (how convenient) and ever since then I’ve gone crazy fixing it up. 

I ran 31x10.5's for a long time and they were great tires but I hated how I couldn’t go a lot of places because of the low ground clearance.  I had a wreck in March of '98 (3 weeks after my 16th b-day, it wasn’t my fault) and they wrecked the whole front suspension.  After I saw the price of the discontinued factory rims I decided that I wanted to keep them.  So that is why I chose to use the 33x10.5's, they are awesome performers off and on-road.  The 3" Trailmaster body lift provides the room and some say that you can even fit 35’s without bumper modifications, not sure about that one. 

The best thing about the ride besides taking all of my football buddies around, is the glass packs. The puppies are loud and can easily be heard from a distance. Hemie, at Shademaker in Bentonville, AR installed all of the electronic hardware, including the keyless start.  It sounds good with the glass packs and really turns heads whenever it starts and nobody is in the car.
