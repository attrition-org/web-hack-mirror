Terrestrial Ecology Programme

 

Although never common in Britain, the natterjack toad has undergone a marked decline during the last 100 years. Habitat destruction and deterioration have been the major factors behind its decline.

 

Introduction

As we begin the new millennium, one of the greatest challenges in ecology continues to be our understanding of ecosystem form and functioning at varying spatial and temporal scales. With increasing pressures on our environment as a result of climate change and human-induced impacts, our research aims to increase our understanding of the complex human-climate-ecosystem interactions by focusing upon observation and modelling at regional to global scales.

Research Projects

· Remote Sensing of Carbon Fluxes in Forest Ecosystems.

· Modelling Natural Resource Responses to Climate Change (MONARCH).

· Regional climate change impact and response studies in East Anglia and North West England (RegIS).

· Sustainable development of natural resources and biodiversity conservation in Madagascar.

· Monitoring the hydrological cycle of Southern Africa for operational applications using remote sensing.
