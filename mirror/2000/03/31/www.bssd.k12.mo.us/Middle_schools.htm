The philosophical and structural changes made in 1994-95 facilitate the needs of the adolescent student in this changing world.  An interdisciplinary team approach is used to provide a very strong basic skills program.  Middle schools use teams of teachers working together with a common block of class time and planning time.  This approach allows the teachers two important options:  first, the flexibility of time through creative grouping and scheduling, and second, the integration of various subjects using teaching teams.

The middle schools include grades six, seven, and eight.  They are structured physically and academically to promote thematic interdisciplinary, an innovative teaching.

The exploratory classes consist of foreign language, communication, art, computers, applied technology, home economics, physical education, instrumental and vocal music.  The schedules are designed to give students both diversity and increased instruction time.  Teachers too enjoy the benefits of additional team plan times.
