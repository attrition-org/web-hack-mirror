Frequently asked questions:

 

1. What is ITN?

ITN is one of the largest and most respected news organisations in the world, supplying news programming in the UK to ITV, Channel 4 and Channel 5, as well as to the Public Television network in the US. The ITN website, ITN Online (www.itn.co.uk), is at the cutting edge of web journalism, using text, audio and video to deliver compelling multimedia news content to the wired population. Click here to find out more about ITN.

2. How do I navigate the ITN site?

ITN Online is updated 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, bringing you the latest news and information. Our pages are organised into several sections featuring stories about Britain, World, Money, Sport, Entertainment and the Weather. To visit any of these sections simply click on the key words along the left-hand side of your screen.

Wherever you see underlined blue text or the words "Full story", you have the opportunity to delve deeper and receive more information. There are also plenty of interactive links throughout the site. These allow you to hear audio clips with interviews and commentary, or watch video coverage of captivating national and international stories.

3. What is News on Demand?

This is a fabulous interactive section of the ITN site, where you can access several video and audio clips of the latest stories and download the players that you will need to enjoy the multimedia elements of this site. News on Demand also brings you the ITN World News live, five evenings a week at 22.30 BST. Click here to go to ITN's News on Demand.

4. What is the ITN News Ticker?

The news ticker provides a continuous 24-hour feed of national and international stories to those of you who are hungry for headlines. This is a fabulous way to keep you informed throughout the day whether at work or at home. The service can stay on, even if you leave the ITN web site.

5. What is the ITN Desktop News?

This is Britain's first personalised desktop news service by email, providing you with customised news reports from ITN's international news centre in London. This service allows you to tailor the news bulletin to suit your needs, making it as short or as detailed as you would like it to be. Click here to subscribe to the ITN Desktop News.

6. What is ITN Schools?

This section of the site is dedicated to schools and students across Britain. It looks at the top stories of the decade, according to ITN's respected and well-known presenters. Here you can take a video tour of the ITN headquarters and even enter a competition to make your own news bulletin. Click here to go to the ITN Schools Section

7. How do I search for a story?

The ITN site is a rich source of news information. To access the ITN searching facility simply simply type your text and press return on the serach box on the front page.

8. How do I make ITN my home page?

When you start up any internet browser, it opens a default start-up page. To ensure that you go straight to ITN as soon as you log on, simply click on the link below that matches your browser software.
