CERT Warns of Massive DDoS Attack Looming





















contributed by laney





CERT released a statement last Friday warning the public that hundreds of
computers had been compromised and are poised for a massive Internet attack.
Kevin Poule, CERT Incident Response Team Leader, is urging SAs to install
Unix patches in an effort to protect systems from what is believed to be the
formation of a large-scale DoS attack. CERT thinks there are several groups
working separately to develop and execute an attack of this magnitude and
the best remedy is to update and increase security measures.



MSNBC
via ZDNet























 


The Dismal State of Net Security





















contributed by pyle





With the explosion of breaches and defacements in recent months, companies
are facing the ramifications of prioritizing online presence over effective
security measures.  Privacy advocates are demanding more clarity when it
comes to established standards and policies as the theft of both company and
third party information becomes more and more rampant.



eWeek























 


 Qualcom CEO's Unattended Laptop Vanishes





















contributed by apocalypse dow





Qualcom company secrets may no longer be secret after CEO, Irwin Jacob's
laptop disappeared at the Society of American Business Editors and Writers
Conference.  It is believed the laptop vanished while Jacobs spoke to a
group of attendees during which time the computer was left unattended.
Jacobs is considering contacting the FBI regarding the disappearance.



AP

More.com Charged with Privacy Invasion





















contributed by laney





More.com appears to be in direct violation of its own privacy policy and
Missouri Attorney General Jay Nixon has decided to call them on it.  Nixon
filed suit against More.com last week in what may be the first case of this
kind to be brought before a judge.  It surely won't be the last as privacy
concerns plague both companies and consumers.
