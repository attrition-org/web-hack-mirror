Introduction to OPEC Online

 OPEC Online contains unique data and useful links to other Web resources. Changes are detailed in the announcements page. There you will also find official statements and updates about the activities of OPEC, including archives of the official Press Releases.

If you are interested in the latest news from the OPEC Member Countries and other major petroleum regions and developing countries, then you will want to visit the OPEC News Agency (OPECNA). There you will find twice-daily updates on regular features such as spot oil prices.

For an update on OPEC’s recent activities, please read the 1997 Annual Report. You can also read a brief history of the Organization.

At the top and bottom of each page are navigation bars grouping information under the following headings: Welcome, About OPEC, Latest News, Announcements, Publications, Member Countries, Contact OPEC. Clicking on the OPEC Online icon will return you to the home page.

Please check the index for an overview of this site.

Further Information

 The OPEC Member Countries in 1976, established the OPEC Fund for International Development with its own management and governing board. The OPEC Fund provides development loans and grants to non-OPEC developing countries. Its loan commitments to date exceed $3.4 billion.

 What is OPEC ?

OPEC is an intergovernmental organization dedicated to the stability and prosperity of the petroleum market, as enshrined in the OPEC Statute. OPEC Membership is open to any country which is a substantial net exporter of oil and which shares the ideals of the Organization.

OPEC has 11 Countries as Members. The OPEC Member Countries (MCs) currently supply more than 40 per cent of the world's oil and they possess about 78 per cent of the world's total proven crude oil reserves. For more statistics, please refer to our publications.

How does OPEC Function ?

The OPEC MCs coordinate their oil production policies in order to help stabilise the oil market and to help oil producers achieve a reasonable rate of return on their investments. This policy is also designed to ensure that oil consumers continue to receive stable supplies of oil.

The Ministers of the OPEC MCs responsible for energy and hydrocarbon affairs meet twice a year to review the status of the international oil market and the forecasts for the future in order to agree upon appropriate actions which will promote stability in the oil market.

The MCs also hold other meetings at various levels of interest, including meetings of petroleum and economic experts, country representatives and special purpose bodies such as committees to address environmental affairs.

Decisions about matching oil production to expected demand are taken at the Meeting of the OPEC Conference. Details of such decisions are communicated in the form of OPEC Press Releases.

The OPEC Secretariat is a permanent inter-governmental body. The Secretariat which has been based in Vienna since 1965, provides research and administrative support to the MCs. The Secretariat also disseminates news and information to the World at large.

See a list of the Secretariat officials.

The official language of the Secretariat is English.
