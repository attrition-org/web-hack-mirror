Attending Meetings of the OPEC Conference
If you seek accreditation to a Meeting of the OPEC Conference, the following is required:
Journalists must present their passports and press-cards. 
Analysts need their passports
Procedure: 
1) Fill an accreditation form at the venue.
2) OPEC staff will check the details.
3) Photographs are taken by OPEC staff.
4) I.D. badges will be prepared.

NOTE! Only persons visibly displaying their badge will be admitted to the Secretariat and to all open sessions of the Conference.

A comprehensive pack of background information is provided for all journalists/analysts at the accreditation venue. However, you may find this and other information listed on this page.

  • About OPEC

  • OPEC is an intergovernmental organization dedicated to the stability and prosperity of the petroleum market, as enshrined in the OPEC Statute. OPEC Membership is open to any country which is a substantial net exporter of oil and which shares the ideals of the Organization. The official language of the Secretariat is English.
    OPEC has 11 Member Countries. They produce more than 40 per cent of the world's oil and possess around three quarters of the world's proven crude oil reserves. 
    Member Countries are represented at OPEC Meetings by the Heads of Delegation (Ministers), usually Oil or Energy Ministers.

    OPEC Ministers
    The Oil and Energy Ministers of the OPEC Member Countries, otherwise known as the Heads of Delegation, meet twice a year (usually March and September) to review the state of the international oil market and the various forecasts in order to consider what, if any, actions may be needed to promote stability in the oil market.
