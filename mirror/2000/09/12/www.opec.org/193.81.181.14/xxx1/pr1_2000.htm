28th Meeting Of The
Ministerial Monitoring Sub-Committee
Vienna, Austria, 14 January 2000

The 28th Meeting of the Ministerial Monitoring Sub-Committee, chaired by HE Bijan Namdar Zangeneh, Minister of Petroleum of the Islamic Republic of Iran, and with the Membership of HE Sheikh Saud Nasser Al-Sabah, Minister of Oil of the State of Kuwait; Mr. Dahiru Adamu, Chargé d’Affaires of the Nigerian Embassy Austria; and HE Dr. Rilwanu Lukman, Secretary General of OPEC, met at Headquarters, Vienna, on Friday, 14 January 2000.  HE Dr. Ali Rodriguez, Minister of Energy & Mines of Venezuela, also attended the Meeting.

 The Sub-Committee reviewed the Reports submitted by the Secretariat on the monitoring of Member Countries’ production and supply for the period January to December 1999 and on Oil Stocks, and considered presentations given on the current oil market situation.  Whilst voicing overall satisfaction with the level of adherence by some Member Countries to the production reductions agreed in March 1999, discipline which has restored oil prices to more acceptable levels, the Sub-Committee Members urged even greater compliance by all OPEC and non-OPEC producers concerned.  In this vein, cognisant of oil producers’ responsibility to ensure the stability of world oil markets in such a way as to safeguard the interests of both consumers and producers alike, the Members of the MMSC repeated their conviction that strict adherence to the agreed production restraint by all concerned remained an imperative until at least the end of March 2000.  In light of the continuing market volatility and the remaining high stock levels, all those present in the Meeting agreed to strongly recommend to the Conference the extension of the current agreement beyond that period.

 The Sub-Committee agreed to convene again in March 2000, immediately prior to the next Ordinary Meeting of the Conference.

 Finally, the Chairman and Members of the Sub-Committee thanked the Secretariat for its contribution to the work.
