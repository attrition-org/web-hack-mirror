No. 8/2000

 

Speculation cause of oil market volatility - Ministers

Tripoli, 17 July 00 (OPECNA)--Continuous daily speculation in the futures market is the cause of the current volatility in the international oil market, it was stated here on Sunday.

Libyan Secretary of the National Oil Corporation Abdalla Salem El-Badri and visiting Venezuelan Energy and Mines Minister Ali Rodriguez Araque said the hike in oil prices was not the result of market fundamentals.

The ministers, who met here along with the chairman of the Venezuelan Presidential Preparatory Commission for the Second OPEC Summit, Jorge Valero, emphasized their interest in having a stable oil market, free of any erratic behaviour, which was in the interest of producers and consumers alike.

In a press release issued today, the two sides said that, in order to achieve oil market stability, they would take action in coordination with other Member Countries of OPEC to "implement the understanding reached during the last Conference in June".

The release said Valero had briefed El-Badri on ongoing preparations for the OPEC Summit, to be held in Caracas, Venezuela at the end of September.

They also discussed bilateral relations and agreed to strengthen cooperation between the national oil companies of both countries in the fields of oil and gas.

Rodriguez is also president of the OPEC Conference, while El-Badri is Alternate President.
