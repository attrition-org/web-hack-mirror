Four Indian soldiers killed in Valley

SRINAGAR, Sept 12: At least four Indian soldiers and two Kashmiri Mujahideen were killed in an intense gunbattle inside an army camp in occupied Kashmir on Tuesday, an army spokesman said.

However, police sources put the death toll much higher.

In an apparent suicide attack, Mujahideen entered the camp of 34 Rashtriya Rifles at Beerwah town, 35 kilometres north of Srinagar, after throwing grenades and firing on the army picket.

The dawn attack by the Mujahideen lasted several hours. Indian troops cordoned off the area and drew the Mujahideen into an intense gunbattle.

"Two militants were killed when they tried to sneak into our security force camp at Beerwah. Four soldiers, including an officer were killed, while five others have been injured," said army spokesman Major Bharat Shane.

"We recovered hand grenades and ammunition from the militants," Shane added.

Indian army helicopters ferried the injured from the base camp in Beerwah to a hospital in Srinagar.

A top police official said the number of Indian soldiers killed in the suicidal attack was higher than the army was admitting.

"Eleven Indian soldiers, including an officer were killed in the firefight. There were at least six to eight sorties run by army helicopters given the task of shifting the dead and injured to Kashmir's main army hospital in Srinagar," he said.

An emergency meeting was called by army headquarters in Srinagar to discuss the attack, and matters such as bolstering the spirit of the soldiers in occupied Kashmir amid growing attacks by the Mujahideen.

Lashkar-i-Tayyaba claimed responsibility for the operation, saying that only two fedayeen (fighters) had died.

Locals near the trouble spot fled their homes and have not been allowed to return by the security forces.-AFP
