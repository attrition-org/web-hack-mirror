    ISDN stands for Integrated Services Digital Networking,and is available in Oklahoma from Southwestern Bell (phone 1-800-SWB-ISDN for info), GTE, and others. ISDN is a digital line, not an analog line as is regular dial-up modem service. A BRI (Basic Rate Interface) is the consumer ISDN product, which is composed of consists of two "phone lines". These channels can support 64k digital data, and when bonded, reach speeds up to 128k. You can also use these lines for voice phone calls as well, and bond/unbond each channel without dialing back to your Internet Provider.


    ISDN is a very flexible technology, and is great for the power user at home and small company networks. In order to begin using ISDN, you must purchase a ISDN Terminal Adapter also known as an ISDN modem. Prices range from $150-$400, depending on the model and brand you choose. You then need to order a line from your telephone company. Southwestern Bell charges about $100/month for an unlimited ISDN line usage, which could replace 2 regular phone lines, and save money, while allowing you to surf the net much faster than a regular modem (for complete fee schedule, see table below).

    With Total Access you may choose either a 64k unlimited or 128k unlimited ISDN account. Windows 95 and Windows NT both have ISDN accelerator packs to make ISDN setup easier.
