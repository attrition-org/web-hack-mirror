WARHAMMER 6TH EDITION

Warhammer is Games Workshop's flagship game of fantasy battle in the 28mm scale. The newest edition of this classic miniatures combat game has been worked, reworked and tirelessly playtested by our team of artists, miniatures designers, writers and games designers to make 6th edition the greatest version of Warhammer ever produced!

The entire Warhammer system has been reviewed, from the rules to the feel of the background to make leading an army of valiant warriors or bloodthirsty savages in the Old World an even more fun, challenging and exciting way to pass an afternoon around the wargames table.

WHAT'S NEW ABOUT IT?

The latest version of Warhammer manages to address a number of things about the Warhammer rules system that players regularly told us that they wanted to see fixed or presented differently. Examples are flying creatures, chariots, overly powerful characters, increased importance of rank-and file-troops and other crucial issues which we have received feedback from our customers about over the years.

Also, the magic rules have been revised and now run on a dice based mechanic rather than the previous card based system. This prevents players from cluttering up the gaming table with lots of easily lost or misplaced cards. The 6th edition magic rules run more smoothly and make spellcasting a bit more balanced so that it supports armies rather than deciding the outcome of the game. Color Magic also makes a return which should bring a smile to the faces of many of our veteran Warhammer Generals!

Lots of other changes and improvements serve to make Warhammer a better, more efficent and more enjoyable game than it ever has been before!

WHAT ABOUT MY OLD WARHAMMER ARMY?!?

Veteran Warhammer players have nothing to worry about as the latest version of Warhammer will continue to support all existing armies. Polybagged with the North American edition of White Dwarf #247 (Issue #250 in the UK) is a copy of Warhammer 'Ravening Hordes! This compendium of thirteen "get you by" army lists will allow you to play your 5th edition Warhammer army under the 6th edition rules. Please note that these army list are only a stopgap until the proper army book for that force is released at which point the rules in Ravening Hordes will be superceded. Also, the Ravening Hordes supplement is now available online on this very website!

Also, entire new miniature lines are being developed for use in the new game. New versions of classic troop types will be released as well as all-new regiments which have never been seen before! This is a chance to revisit, update and expand all of the Warhammer Citadel Miniatures ranges.

For example, the Orc and Goblin army will be getting a new Snotling Pump Wagon, Squig Hoppers, Black Orcs, Netters, Goblin Wolf Riders, an incredibly fierce new Giant and more! All of the Warhammer armies will recieve this sort of attention in turn.

Warhammer Rulebook
This 288-page rulebook contains rules for playing the game, colour photos, modelling and painting tips, plus scenarios, and an extensive appendix of extra rules and information. It also tells you all about the world of Warhammer - the lands, the people and creatures who live there, and the history of the Old World.

Plastic Models
The Warhammer boxed set contains enough models to represent the core of two armies, so you can begin fighting straight away.

The Empire:
It is the duty of these knights and soldiers to defend the human Empire from the many enemies who would seize these fertile lands for themselves.  They are well-disciplined, and make use of primitive fire-arms and war machines.		 Orcs & Goblins:
These barbaric green-skinned monsters live in the mountains and forests of the Old World. Though ill disciplined, they are savage opponents, and often triumph through sheer force of numbers.
Before you can use the models, you will need to assemble them. Most people like to paint their models, and with a little practice you can soon be the proud General of a colourful and unique army.

Dice
The Warhammer rules use 6-sided dice to randomly determine what happens at points during the game - whether one soldier succeeds in hitting another, whether troops stand their ground or flee in panic, etc. The two odd-looking dice are an Artillery dice and a Scatter dice, which are used when you're firing war machines.

Range Rulers and Templates
Range rulers are used to measure distance for firing weapons and moving troops, while templates show such things as the area affected by a spell, or where a cannonball could land.

Ruined Building
Scenery is an essential part of any Warhammer battle, and this cardboard building will give your troops height to shoot from, and cover to hide behind.

Getting Started Leaflet
This handy leaflet runs you through a couple of examples of play, and explains how to assemble your models.
